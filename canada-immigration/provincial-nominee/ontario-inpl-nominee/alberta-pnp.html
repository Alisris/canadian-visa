Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program

  1. Home
  2. Canada Immigration Programs
  3. Provincial Nominee Program
  4. Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program

Updated: May 10th, 2023


Ontario is the most populous province in Canada. Approximately 40 percent of Canadians live there. The province is home to Ottawa, Canada's capital city, and Toronto, the country's largest city and primary business and financial center. Ontario is the economic, social, and political heartland of Canada. It is the most popular destination in Canada for new immigrants from around the world, making it the most multicultural province in the country. The area includes more than 125 languages and people from over 200 countries. The easiest way to immigrate to Ontario, Canada, is with a provincial nomination under the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP).

What Is the Ontario Provincial Nominee Program (OINP)

The Ontario INP is designed to give select foreign nationals interested in immigrating to Ontario, Canada, the opportunity to apply for Canadian permanent residence through an expedited process. Formerly called the Opportunities Ontario Provincial Nominee Program (OOPNP), the program is designed for prospective immigrants with the specific skills and experience needed in the province.

There are two different ways to go about getting nominated by the OINP. Outside of Express Entry, a potential immigrant can contact Immigration Ontario and apply to the OINP directly. If given a provincial nomination, they can then create a Canada Express Entry profile and add their nomination credentials.

Prospective immigrants can also begin the process by creating an Express Entry profile and an Expression of Interest (EOI) in the province within the system. The OINP can then contact the person through Express Entry and send them a formal Invitation to Apply (ITA) to the immigration program. This will instantaneously increase their score under the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) by 600 points. As a result, the person will likely have enough Canada Express Entry CRS points to receive an ITA during the next Express Entry draw, which takes place every few weeks.

One of the best ways to start this process is to utilize the expertise of a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC). An RCIC can facilitate your application process from start to finish, including thoroughly evaluating you to ensure you're eligible for the OINP, giving you pointers on how to improve your eligibility, creating your EOI profile, filling out your application forms, representing you with certain provincial authorities, help you source and verify your documentation and provide you with the support and guidance you need throughout your application process.

Here's a full breakdown of how to apply for a Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) like the OINP.

Each OINP stream has specific sets of criteria for different types of applicants. However, there are several critical criteria

If You Have a Job Offer in Ontario:

Employer Job Offer: Foreign Worker stream

This stream is for highly skilled foreign workers who want to apply for Canadian permanent residence and have work experience and a job offer on the province's in-demand jobs list.

The OINP Foreign Worker Stream has three specific sets of eligibility criteria - one for the applicant, one for the Canadian employer and one for the job offer. They are as follows:

Job Offer Requirements:

Your job offer in Ontario must be:

  • For a full-time position
    • This means it must be for a minimum of 30 hours a week
  • For an eligible occupation
    • An eligible occupation refers to an occupation in TEER Category 0, 1, 2, 3 of the National Occupational Classification (NOC)
    • Learn more about TEER categories and how they affect your applications here.
  • Paying at the median wage level for that occupation
    • This means that the annual salary for the job is at the average level for that occupation or NOC code in Canada
  • Vital for the running of your employer's business
  • Based in Ontario

Applicant Requirements:

As an applicant to this stream, you must have the following:

  • An EOI
  • An ITA
  • Sufficient work experience or certifications
  • A valid license or authorization in your occupation
    • This is only necessary if that occupation requires it
  • The Intention to live in Ontario
  • Legal status in Canada
    • This only applies if you are using the OINP from within Canada

Employer Requirements

For the OINP to consider your employment valid, your employer must also fulfill specific criteria. They are as follows:

General Requirements

The employer must:

  • Have been actively conducting business through their enterprise for at least three years before applying
  • Have and work in business premises in Ontario
  • Have no open cases of offences committed against the Ontario Employment Standards Act, 2000 or the Occupational Health and Safety Act
  • Have shown to the relevant authorities that they have committed effort to find a suitable applicant within Canada
    • They can show this through a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)

Revenue Requirements

The employer must have met these requirements within the past fiscal year. In addition, the employer's enterprise must have gross annual revenue of at least:

  • 1,000,000 CAD if the business premises or the applicant's workplace is in the Greater Toronto Area (GTA).
  • 500,000 CAD if the business premises or the applicant's workplace is outside the GTA.

Employment Requirements

For the Canadian enterprise to be considered legitimate, the enterprise must employ at least:

  • Five full-time Canadian citizens or permanent residents.
  • Three full-time Canadian citizens or permanent residents working at the exact location where the applicant will report to work.

Scoring Factors:

This stream is scored on numerous factors relating to the nature of the Canadian job offer, language ability and where in Ontario you plan to move. These factors are as follows:

Employer Job Offer: International Student Stream

International students having a job offer in a specialized occupation are encouraged to apply. This stream allows international students inside and outside Canada to apply for job offers in Ontario. Following that, students will be nominated and granted permanent residency by Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). If you want to become an international student in Canada, here's how to apply for a Canadian study permit.

This is a terrific opportunity for students because Ontario is one of Canada's most varied, lively, and thriving provinces. In addition, it's an excellent starting point for their future jobs. While the OINP has stringent requirements, successful candidates will have a fantastic opportunity to get permanent status through the Ontario PNP.

  • An EOI

Global Skills Strategy (GSS)

The GSS is a government-initiated program designed to help attract and retain highly skilled workers in the province. This program helps fast-track the process for those looking to work in Ontario, reducing the time to apply for a work permit from weeks or months to just two weeks. The strategy also provides employers with a 25% refundable tax credit for each new highly-skilled worker they hire, allowing them to reduce their labour costs. Furthermore, the program offers job-specific language training to help new immigrants quickly adjust to their new job roles and positively contribute to the Ontario economy. 

The GSS provides employers with access to a broader pool of highly-skilled workers while reducing the time and costs associated with the hiring process. It offers employees the chance to start a new job in Ontario quickly and provides language training to help them adjust to their new roles more quickly. The GSS is an excellent way for the province to attract and retain talent and is a great tool for employers looking to increase their workforce.

The GSS has two primary categories. Category A is for businesses that require highly specialized foreign workers to allow the business to grow. To be eligible for this category, you must:

  • be offered a minimum annual salary of 80,000CAD 
  • have advanced knowledge in the offered role
  • have an advanced degree or at least five years of work experience.

If You're an Entrepreneur with a Great Business Idea

In the fall of 2015, the Ontario investor program was officially closed. However, a new OINP category was introduced - the Ontario Entrepreneur Program.

Why Entrepreneurs Choose Ontario

Ontario has a plethora of economic development opportunities for business owners and entrepreneurs. Entrepreneurs can benefit from lower tax rates and overheads, an exceptionally talented and skilled workforce, and various provincial funding programs, especially if you are under 30.

Entrepreneur Stream

The Entrepreneur Stream is designed to help foreigners buy a business in the province or to help create a brand new Ontario company or purchase an existing one. Once the business has been established, entrepreneurs can be nominated by Immigration Ontario for Canadian permanent residency under the OINP Entrepreneur Stream.

The Ontario Entrepreneur Streams application process comes in two stages, each with its application process. They are as follows:

Stage 1

This stage focuses on the application process for you (and your business partners). This focus mostly follows the traditional PNP application process. For this phase of the application process, you must:

  • Register an EOI
  • Submit an online application if your EOI is successful
  • Attend a mandatory interview with your business partner
  • Sign a performance agreement.

However, one of the cornerstones of this stage is establishing whether or not your business plan is viable in Ontario and that you can undertake the establishment of this enterprise. As a result, the eligibility criteria for this stage are as follows.

To be eligible for this stream, you must have the following:

  • At least 24 of full-time business experience, either as a business manager or senior owner, out of the last 60 months
  • A minimum personal net worth of:
    • 800,000CAD if you're starting your business in the GTA
    • 400,000CAD if you're starting your business outside of the GTA
  • A minimum personal investment of the business of:
    • 600,000CAD if you're starting your business in the GTA
    • 200,000CAD if you're starting your business outside of the GTA
  • An active role within the company
  • The expressed intent of making a profit through the business
  • To hire at least:
    • two Canadian citizens or permanent residents if you're starting your business in the GTA
    • one Canadian citizen or permanent resident if you're starting your business outside of the GTA

Stage 2

This stage primarily focuses on setting up your enterprise in Canada. For this phase, you must:

  • Receive a temporary work permit support letter and apply for a temporary Canadian work permit.
  • Establish your business in Canada.
    • You will have 20 months from your arrival date to establish your business and submit a final report on the implementation of your business plan
  • Present the necessary documentation to prove your eligibility for permanent residence in Canada, provided your business fulfills all the eligibility requirements

As much of your business acumen and the legitimacy of your enterprise has already been established in Canada, the next step is to ensure you are eligible for life in Canada. As a result, the requirements for stage two primarily focus on your aspects. To qualify for Stage 2, you must:

  • Have CLB level 4
  • live in Ontario for at least 15 out of the 20 months you have to establish your business
  • Meet the terms of your performance agreement

Immigration FAQ’s

1. What Is The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)?

Skilled foreign workers who want to immigrate to Ontario, can apply through the OINP, for a faster immigration process. The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program is for select immigrants, and their family members, who have the specific skills and experience needed by the province.

In addition, the idea behind OINP is that it is designed to enhance economic development in areas such as job creation and job retention for foreign immigrants.

The OINP is largely employer driven, which means that any job that a skilled foreign worker wishes to apply for, must be preapproved by the IRCC, (Immigration, Refugees Citizenship Canada) and all employers must be pre-screened.

2. How Do You Apply For The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)?

There are two ways you can go about applying for OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program):

  • You can directly contact and apply to the Province of Ontario:
    • If you get a Provincial Nomination, you must create a Canada Express Entry Profile and include your Ontario Nomination Certificate.
  • You can begin your process by creating a Canada Express Entry Profile:
    • You can register your interest in the Province of Ontario on your profile. The OINP can then contact you through Express Entry and invite you to apply (ITA) for the immigration program. If you are nominated by Ontario, your CRS will instantly increase by 600 points.

Candidates with enough CRS points will be Invited to Apply (ITA) for the next Express Entry draw.

3. What Are The Minimum Requirements To Immigrate To Ontario As A Skilled Worker Under The General Provincial Nominee Program?

If you want to immigrate to Ontario as a skilled worker for the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) you must:

  • Have a minimum of two years paid work experience within the past five years. The work experience you apply with, must match the job position you apply for in your application. If you have Canadian work experience, you must be able to prove and validate it;
  • Have a job offer from an Ontario employer, for a permanent, full-time position. The job must fall under the following National Occupation Classification (NOC) categories for skilled workers:
    • O: Management positions
    • A: Professional jobs
    • B: Technical jobs
  • • There are two forms required for the application process the:
    • Joint Verification Form, signed by your employer; and the
    • Employer Pre-Screen Form
  • You must have the proper legal status to be in Canada if you are living in the country during your application process.

Your employer must also meet the following requirements:

  • The employer’s place of business must be situated in Ontario;
  • The employer’s business must be in operation for a minimum of three years;
  • The company must have:
    • A gross revenue of approximately $1,000,000, with at least five full time employees; or
    • A gross revenue of approximately $500,000, with a minimum of three full time employees
  • The employer must meet current wage stipulations for the position; and
  • The employer must also prove that the job is necessary for the operation of their company. The company must prove that they have not overlooked Canadian citizens for employment or training opportunities.

4. How Can International Students Apply For Permanent Residence In Ontario?

The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) has three specialized immigration categories for international students. Graduates from approved tertiary institutions can apply for permanent residency status if they meet the correct requirements for each category. The categories are the:

  • Pilot International Master's Graduate Category (applicant does not need a job offer);
  • International PhD Students Category (applicant does not need a job offer); and the
  • Job Offer Stream for international students (job offer is required)

5. What Are The Minimum Requirements To Apply For OINP International Student Programs?

Each of the three immigration streams under the OINP have different requirements to meet if you are an international student.

Pilot International Master’s Graduate Category

If you wish to qualify for the Pilot International Master’s Graduate Category, you must show that:

  • You intend to live and work in Ontario;
  • You did not reside in Ontario for at least one full year, during the past two years;
  • You are not an asylum seeker or refugee claimant;
  • You are proficient in English or French;
  • You are a graduate from an existing Master's program at an eligible Ontario University;
  • You got your Master’s degree less than two years ago;
  • You have enough savings or income to support yourself and any other dependants; and
  • You plan to further your studies on a full-time basis

International PhD Students Category

If you want to qualify for the International PhD students Category, you must show that:

  • You went to an approved publicly funded University in Ontario;
  • You successfully graduated or completed at least two years of intended PhD studies;
  • You graduated with your PhD less than two years ago; and
  • You have the appropriate legal status in Canada.

International Student with a job offer Category

You can apply for the International Student with a job offer if:

  • You completed half of your studies in Canada;
  • You completed at least a two-year degree, diploma or certificate program;
  • You have a full-time job offer for a skilled occupation in Ontario and the job offer is approved by Opportunities Ontario;
  • You graduated with a degree, diploma or certificate, less than two years ago;
  • You have the appropriate legal status in Canada; and
  • You have a Joint Verification Form signed by your employer, and an Employer Pre-Screen Form.

6. What Is The Ontario Corporate Program And How Is It Related To The OINP?

The Ontario Corporate Program is for implementing, assisting and supporting international corporations that are looking to expand in Ontario, by means of purchasing or opening a business in the province. If the corporation is successfully established, some employees of the international corporation can apply for permanent residence under the OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program).

The OINP establishes a performance agreement with the potential corporation. The agreement includes performance goals and estimated time-frames for the Ontario corporation. If these conditions cannot be met, nominations for key staff members will not be processed.

7. What Are The Minimum Requirements For The Ontario Corporate Program?

The minimum conditions for a successful Corporate Program in Ontario are:

  • A maximum of five key foreign employees may be nominated for each successful Corporate Program;
  • A language test in English (that has the required scores) given to all key foreign nationals and submitted results within 24 months;
  • An investment of $5,000,000 or more should be rendered in the new or existing corporation;
  • An existing international business should have a well-established track record for a minimum of three years; and
  • The creation of five permanent job positions for Canadian citizens or Canadian permanent residents.

8. How Can French Speaking Immigrants Apply For Permanent Residence Under OINP?

A French speaking immigration stream is available under the Ontario Immigration Nominee Program (OINP). The French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream enables French speaking foreign nationals to create an Express Entry Profile, whereby they can indicate their interest in Ontario.

French candidates in the Express Entry Pool may be eligible for the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP) or Canada Experience Class (CEC).

French speaking immigrants must meet the following requirements for the OINP:

  • French speaking candidates must have a tertiary qualification; Bachelor's degree, Master’s Degree or PhD. If candidates did not receive their education from Canadian institutions, it must be confirmed that their education is on par with Canada's standards;
  • French candidates must obtain a high score in the Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) in French and English in order to be a successful applicant;
  • Potential applicants should have proof that they have enough funds to settle in the province;
  • French applicants for the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) must have Canadian work experience for at least one year as full-time skilled workers; and
  • French applicants applying under the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP), must have at least one year paid working experience as skilled workers

Take Note: French-speaking candidates who receive a nomination from an Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP), have 30 days to accept the nomination. This nomination will give candidates 600 additional points in the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS).

9. What Is The Ontario Human Capital Priorities Program (OHCPP)?

The Ontario Human Capital Priorities is a stream of the OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program) whereby skilled workers, with a CRS (Comprehensive Ranking System) score of 400 or more, can be chosen to live and work permanently in Ontario.

It is important for potential candidates to create an Express Entry Profile and indicate their interest in Ontario. To be considered for the Human Capital Priorities Stream candidates must meet all requirements, these include:

  • Obtaining a minimum of seven in the Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) in English or French;
  • Graduating with a Bachelor’s Degree or higher;
  • Having one year of full-time work experience, gained in a skilled job position; and
  • Loving everything about Ontario

If candidates meet all the above requirements, they can be nominated for immigration under the Ontario Human Capital Priorities Program (OHCPP).

10. What Is The Ontario Entrepreneur Stream?

Foreign entrepreneurs who would like to create a business in Ontario or purchase an existing company can do so by means of the Ontario Entrepreneur Stream.

If your business plan is approved, you and your business partner (if applicable) may apply for a temporary business visa in order to establish the business. Once it has been established that the business activity will be economically beneficial to Ontario, you and your business partner may proceed to apply for permanent residence under the OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program).

11. How Do You Qualify For The Ontario Entrepreneur Stream?

In order for a potential entrepreneur or business partner to be eligible for the Ontario Entrepreneur Stream (OES), these five factors must be met:

  • Net Worth
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company within the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must have a minimum net worth of $1,500,000;
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company outside the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must have a minimum net worth of $800,000; or
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish a company in the line of Digital Communication or Information Communication (IC), he or she must have a minimum net worth of $800,000
  • Experience
    • The entrepreneur must have a minimum of 36 months’ worth of business experience; and
    • The entrepreneur must have experience as a business owner or work experience in a senior position
  • Job Creation
    • The entrepreneur is expected to employ at least two Canadian citizens or permanent residents on a full-time basis
  • Preliminary Visit
    • The entrepreneur is expected to have made a business-related visit to Canada, twelve months prior to submitting the application.
  • Personal Investment Proceeds
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company within the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must make a minimum investment of $1,000,000 and have a minimum share of 33.3%;
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company outside the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must make a minimum investment of $500,000 and have a minimum share of 33.3%; or
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish a company in the line of Digital Communication or IC, he or she must make a minimum investment of $500,000 and have a minimum share of 33.3%.
