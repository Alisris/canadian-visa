With an average salary of anywhere between 77,000 and 135,000 CAD per year, youll receive a return on this investment in no time. This includes having access to attractive benefits such as free public healthcare and schooling, being able to invest in prime real estate and enjoy the finest quality of life in one of the most diverse, multicultural and welcoming countries in the world.

Using the right immigration consultants who will not only optimize your chances of success can be a make or break deal, and this is the difference between other immigration companies. Our government trusted and expert consultants are highly trained and know their way around the Canadian immigration system. Business Management in Canada

Business Management in Canada is a highly specialized occupation that requires a lot of occupational experience and skill. Canada has a highly competitive mixed market economy that is chiefly powered by its abundant natural resources, well-developed capital markets, huge commercial domestic market with significant spending power, large international trade networks and a highly educated population.

In order to leverage the economic opportunities presented by the competitive elements that Canada possesses, it requires a cadre of incredibly skilled individuals who are well-versed in managing natural, human and capital resources towards the accomplishment of a financial goal.

This is where business managers come into play and Canada, which currently has over 45,000 extant business management jobs, a comparatively significant figure, still requires additional numbers of business management jobs to augment and support the rising tide of Canadian industry.

The need for and significance of business managers varies from province to province. In general the rule of thumb is that both highly and sparsely populated provinces with developing economies require more business managers and offer better average salaries. As a result they offer better average salaries to attract these individuals to their cities and towns.

The following presents a brief list of Canadas various provinces along with the most up-to-date information according to Indeed Canada regarding the average salary (in CAD) for business managers in each.

  • Ontario Average base salary for business managers: $78,157 per year; $5,534 per month; $33,79 per hour
  • Alberta Average base salary for business managers: $69,650 per year; $4,931 per month; $30,11 per hour
  • British Columbia Average base salary for business managers: $79,971 per year; $5,662 per month; $34,57 per hour
  • Manitoba Average base salary for business managers; $62,055 per year; $4,394 per month; $26,83 per hour
  • Quebec Average base salary for business managers; $70,357 per year; $4,981per month; $30,41 per hour
  • Prince Edward Island Average base salary for business managers; $53,592 per year, $3,794 per month; $23,17 per hour
  • Newfoundland and Labrador Average base salary for business managers; $79,884 per year, $5,656per month; $34.53 per hour
  • New Brunswick Average base salary for business managers; $74,316 per year, $5,262 per month; $32.13 per hour
  • Northwest Territories Average base salary for business managers; $82,498 per year, $5,841 per month; $35.66 per hour
  • Nova Scotia Average base salary for business managers; $81,669 per year, $5,782 per month; $35.30 per hour
  • Saskatchewan Average base salary for business managers; $96,050 per year, $6,800 per month; $41.52 per hour
  • Yukon Average base salary for business managers; $73,319 per year, $5,191 per month; $31.69 per hour

We would advise you to regularly consult Canadian online job sites if you want to keep up-to-date with average salary expectations for business managers in Canada so that you can leverage those opportunities.

What Canada Offers Business Managers

Business management is an increasingly valued skill in Canada as the country's industries continue to swell and beneficiate along the product value chain.

The largest industries in Canada such as the Fast Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG), Retail, Information Technology (IT) and primary industries (farming, mining, viticulture, fishing and whaling) require vast natural and human capital resources with extensive supply chains.

To effectively manage all of these potential productive capacities needs the skills, experience, knowledge and pragmatism of highly skilled individuals. And so business managers in Canada have become some of the most sought after specialized workers.

To attract business managers, Canadian businesses offer an array of occupational benefits that cover health, housing, leave, salary/wage, skills development and educational benefits. Therefore, along with working in various sectors, you can also capitalize on these benefits by moving permanently to Canada as a Business Manager.

Express Entry

The Express Entry system is the fastest immigration visa processing program with application processing times as fast as 6 months. If youre a business manager and want to quickly leverage the opportunity to work in Canada's business management sector, then we highly recommend using the Express Entry system as your application pathway.

The Express Entry system was introduced by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) in order to manage Canada's Federal Economic immigration programs and make them more accessible to highly skilled foreign workers applying to immigrate to Canada.

These three programs include: Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP); Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP) and Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

As a business manager, you can either apply through the Federal Skilled Worker Program or the Canadian Experience Class if you have Canadian work experience.

The Express Entry system is a points-based system, which will score you according to the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) on factors regarding your:

  • Age
  • Educational Qualifications
  • Requisite work experience
  • Proficiency in English and/or French
  • Family ties in Canada
  • Police clearance
  • Medical/Health clearance from a physician that is approved by the Canadian government
  • Ability to adapt to life in Canada

Once you have created an Express Entry profile via the official Canadian government site, you will be ranked against others in a biweekly Express Entry draw pool, of which only the highest-ranking individuals will receive an ITA (Invitation to Apply) for permanent residence in Canada.

Federal Skilled Workers Program

Should you decide to use the FSWP to immigrate to Canada as a business manager, then please be cognizant that you need to have the necessary qualifications, skills, or work experiences to contribute to the Canadian economy.

Aside from passing the Express Entry eligibility assessment the other requirements needed to qualify for FSWP includes:

  • Make sure that youre still in a business management position with the same NOC category description as when you made your immigration application
  • Your work experience must be within the preceding ten years
  • The remuneration for your job was in the form of paid wages or earned commissionvolunteer work or unpaid internships don't apply
  • You must have at least a year of continuous work or complete 30 hours per week- full-time or part-time
  • If you worked part-time work, you must still fulfill 30 hours per week
  • If your a student and gained work experience that provided you with paide wages or commission and was continuous for at least a year then you will meet the minimum requirements to apply

If you meet the criteria above then please give the FSWP a try as an immigration pathway of applying to move to Canada!

Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

Suppose you have any prior experience working in Canada in some capacity that involves the grant of a temporary work permit. In that case, you can leverage the opportunity to immigrate to Canada by using the Canadian Experience Class program.

The eligibility requirement for Canadian Experience Class generally include:

  • Have one year of skilled, Canadian work experience in a full-time position that was legally obtained in Canada and the job must be defined by the Canadian National Occupational Classification (NOC) as level 0, 1, 2 or 3.
  • No education requirement for this program. However, you can increase your Express Entry score by earning a certificate, diploma or degree from a Canadian secondary or tertiary school or completing a foreign credential or by getting an ECA
  • You must: take an approved language test for writing, reading, listening, and speaking; meet the minimum language requirement of Canadian Language Benchmark 7 for NOC 0 or A jobs or Canadian Language Benchmark 5 for NOC B jobs.
  • You must legally be allowed to enter Canada through medical and police clearance

How to Apply

Should you decide to apply for FSWP and CEC, the general application process for each program includes:

  • Step 1: Check Your Eligibility: by passing the eligibility assessment that evaluates you age, work experience, language ability, tertiary education, criminal record, medical certification and travel documents
  • Step 2: Calculate Your CRS Score: The best way to do this is via Canadianvisa.org's CRS calculator.
  • Step 3: Gather Your Documents: The required documentation need to support your application can be accessed here
  • Step 4: Create Your Express Entry Profile: Create your Express Entry profile on the Canadian government website
  • Step 5: Get an ITA, Pay Your Fees and Apply: Monitor every Express Entry draw to see what the minimum requirement is, and whether or not youll be eligible to get your ITA. Youll be notified on your profile if you receive an ITA.

Provincial Nominee Programs

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) are immigration visa programs specifically designed to address the labor shortages in each Canadian province.

After meeting the eligibility criteria for a particular PNP and submitting the required documentation for that program, you may receive a provincial nomination. Receiving a provincial nomination grants you 600 CRS points, which greatly enhances your chances of receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA).

Going by the metric of average salaries and job listings, the to 6 provinces that requires individuals that are skilled in business management the most includes:

  • British Columbia: Needed mostly in the burgeoning IT industry that is centered in Vancouver
  • Newfoundland and Labrador: Required to manage the vast maritime resources (whaling and fishing firms) in the Atlantic provinces
  • New Brunswick: New Brunswick aquaculture is a thriving sector that is requires expert business management
  • Northwest Territories: The Northwest Territories is vast region teeming with natural resources that requires skilled business managers to leverage and capitalize on
  • Nova Scotia: Nova Scotia rustic regions are filled with small to medium sized agri-businesses that requires business managers in scale up and distribute their product
  • Saskatchewan: Saskatchewan has one of the highest numbers of farm holdings per capita and as one of the agricultural breadbaskets of the nation is in need of firm management due these businesses strategic importance

How to Apply

If you want to apply to any of these PNP streams, you can do so through either:

  • Direct Application Process: applying for a provincial nomination directly to your chosen province means that you will have to submit a paper application via mail or courier.
  • Express Entry Application Process: This has two pathways which includes submitting an expression of interest (EOI) to the PNP stream youre interested in or applying through an Provincial Nominee Express Entry Stream

If the above interests you then please go ahead and take a chance moving Canada as a Business manager through a PNP.

Start-up Visa

This is a general Business Immigration Visa option that allows you to move to any Canadian province of your choice and establish a business. You will have to meet a set of requirements in order to be successful with your application.

Business management firms that are new are few and far between, with most business managers being staunchly reliant on established firms in order to find gainful employment. This has left a gap in the market for anyone looking to capitalize on this business opportunity.

Canada will reward you for your entrepreneurial efforts as long as you ensure that your business management business is innovative, will create jobs for Canadians, and be able to compete internationally.

While waiting for the approval of your application, you can also apply for a temporary work permit to keep you busy in Canada.

To be eligible to apply for the Start-up Visa program, you must ensure that you meet the following requirements which include:

  • Business requirements: Hold at least 10% of the voting rights attached to all outstanding shares of the business; play an active role in the ongoing management of your business while living in Canada and your business must be incorporated in Canada.
  • Letter of support: You must get a letter of support from a designated organization such as Venture Capital Firms, Angel Investor Groups and Business Incubators.
  • Language requirements: You must score a minimum of Canadian Language Benchmark level 5 in speaking, reading, listening, and writing in either English or French
  • Settlement money: The Canadian government doesnt support recipients of the Start-up Visa and therefore you must provide proof of your individual and family financial self-sufficiency.

How to Apply

To apply for a Start-up Visa program you need to follow these steps:

  • Step 1: Check Your Eligibility
  • Step 2: Get Your Documents Together
  • Step 3: Complete Your Application Form
  • Step 4: Pay Your Fees
  • Step 5: Submit your Application

The Atlantic High Skilled Program

Under the Atlantic Immigration Pilot is a set of immigration streams that includes the Atlantic High Skilled Program. This program is tailored for individuals that are skilled in a variety of high value specialized jobs to immigrate to Canada by obtaining permanent residency status.

How to Apply

To apply for this program, you can apply by meeting the following requirements

  • You must have at least one year of experience working in management, professional or skilled job;
  • You must have a foreign degree or diploma equal to a Canadian credential, for which you will need an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) report done
  • You will need to take an approved language test to prove you are proficient enough in English or French to live and work in Canada; and
  • You need to have proof of funds that meet the minimum required amount to support yourself as well as any dependents regardless of whether they are joining you in Canada or not
