Are you a Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) graduate seeking opportunity, growth, and international experience? Did you know that thousands of business administration jobs in Canada are currently just waiting to be snatched up? And along with these top-notch BBA jobs in areas from finance to human resources come quite handsome salaries - enough to get you rushing for the first flight out to the Great White North. So if you've just finished university and are looking for a BBA job, let's take a look at some of the Highest Paying Jobs in Canada for BBA Graduates.

Top Industries For Business Administration

When looking for a BBA job in Canada, exploring all the avenues to make the best possible career choice is essential. Below are some of Canada's top industries for business administration as well as the primary duties and responsibilities.

Business Management

As a business manager, analyst, or consultant, you will spend most of your time addressing data and procedural issues, ensuring that day-to-day operations run smoothly. Your main aim will be to ensure that the business operates efficiently. You must also recommend ways to improve organizational performance, increase profits and decrease costs. You will, however, need years of experience under your belt before taking on a management role.

Finance

You'll be crunching those numbers as a financial advisor or accountant. Both occupations involve working with people or businesses to ensure financial health. However, as an accountant, you may also be required to handle your client's taxes. You may require additional on-the-job training for certain senior positions and a provincial regulatory board membership.

Human Resources

If you want to work in Canada in Human Resources (HR), you must be a people person. You will be responsible for ensuring that employees are safe and happy. You must do some administration, including keeping employee records, conducting counseling and disciplinary proceedings, and recruiting new employees. You will need to gain some experience as an assistant before taking on a management role, for which you may require a Certified Human Resources Professional (CHRP) designation.

Market Research

If you want to pursue a career in marketing, you'll need to be comfortable with planning and coordination tasks. You will be required to strategize ways to optimize profits and determine the demand for goods and services and market trends through market analysis. Marketing also offers very attractive jobs in advertising, as well as other fields in media.

Sales

Can you sell ice to a Canuck? Have a knack for analysis? Then sales management may be just what you're looking for. Your days will be spent leading a sales team, compiling sales analysis reports, and running training programs to help maximize the revenue of the company you work for. Job opportunities in sales are expected to see continued growth in job opportunities in Canada over the next few years.

Top Earning BBA Jobs in Canada

As you can see, as a recent Business Administration graduate, your options are endless, and you could find yourself working in any of Canada's top industries. But after four years of study, transitioning from student to career life can be daunting, and you may need to know exactly which occupations would be best suited to you. So, let's see what you could earn when you land your dream BBA job in Canada, according to Talent.com.

What Will I Need to Get a BBA Job in Canada?

Why do I Need an ECA?

If you have completed your degree outside of Canada, you will need an ECA to prove that your qualification is equivalent to Canadian standards. This will also be required to claim points when starting your Canadian visa application process. If you intend to complete your qualification or further your studies in Canada, you'll be pleased to hear that Canada has a Post-Graduate Work Program that allows you to stay on and gain valuable work experience and possibly permanent residency! But first, let's take a look at some of Canada's top industries for business administration as well as the primary duties and responsibilities.

How to Apply to Work in Canada in Business Administration

Check Whether You Are Eligible

Before even applying for a Canadian immigration program or visa, you'll have to check whether you qualify to do so. Factors taken into account by Immigration Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) include:

  • Your nationality
  • Your age
  • Your language ability proficiency
  • Your credentials
  • Your employment experience
  • Your salary and access to settlement funds
  • Job offer information

Find Your NOC Code

Canada uses the National Occupational Classification (NOC) to organize job titles under different categories. The updated NOC 2021 came into effect in November 2022, so before you start your application, make sure you know what the code is for your occupation.

Choose a Suitable Immigration Pathway

Generally, most people who come to Canada to work will require a Canadian work permit. As a BBA graduate, you may be eligible to apply to immigrate to Canada through an immigration program. You won't need a work visa if granted Canadian permanent residency.

Express Entry Options

Express Entry candidates can apply for Canadian permanent residency through one of three programs:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP);
  • Federal Skilled Trade Program (FSTP); or
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

As a highly skilled and qualified graduate, you could choose between the FSWP or the CEC. The FSWP is for skilled workers in management and professional jobs and usually requires a university degree. No job offer is needed. The CEC is for those with a job offer in Canada and at least one year of Canadian work experience.

Applying through the Express Entry system could get your visa processed in as little as six months as long as you meet the general requirements of your chosen program and score highly on factors such as your age, language ability in English or French, a job offer (if applicable) and ability to settle in Canada with ease.

Provincial Nominee Program

Canada has 11 PNPs, each targeting regional labor market needs that must be met to boost the economy. To qualify for a PNP, you will need a job offer in your chosen province or territory and meet your PNP requirements. If your job is in demand in your chosen province, you will have a higher chance of being invited to apply for permanent residency. Below are provinces that need business administration graduates.

If you go the Express Entry route, you can check your Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score using our CRS Calculator.

Provinces Where BBA Jobs are In-demand

  • Alberta
  • British Columbia
  • Manitoba
  • Newfoundland & Labrador
  • Northwest Territories
  • Nova Scotia
  • Quebec
  • Yukon

Atlantic Immigration Program

If you've always dreamt of living near the ocean, why not consider one of Canada's four Atlantic provinces as options? You can choose from Prince Edward Island, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, or Newfoundland and Labrador. As discovered previously, there is currently a demand for business administration degree holders in Nova Scotia as well as Newfoundland and Labrador. This means your chances of being successful in your application are very high. You will need a job offer or a post-secondary qualification from an educational institution in one of Canada's four Atlantic provinces to qualify for the Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP).

Canadian Work Permit Option

If you do not qualify for one of the immigration programs and have a job offer in Canada, you could qualify to apply for an employer-specific work permit. This will allow you to work in Canada legally. The permit will generally be valid for the length of your job offer or until your passport expires, whichever date comes first.

Start Your BBA Career in Canada

Whatever you choose to do with your Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) qualification, know that Canada needs your skills. There are both immigration and Canadian work permit options available for BBA graduates to choose from. BBA jobs also usually come with attractive salaries and job opportunities in at least eight of Canada's 11 provinces and territories, making this qualification an excellent option to consider when choosing study programs.
