Top 10 Jobs in Canada for Foreigners Without Experience

by 
Themba Phongolo

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: June 23rd, 2023
5 min read

Have you thought of immigrating to Canada but are not sure that you have the proper work experience for it? If you have, don't worry. With Canada's expansion of work opportunities that address the country's labor shortages, workers with no experience now also have the opportunity to realize their Canadian work-life dreams!

Labor shortages have swelled Canadas job vacancies to the point that jobs in Canada for foreigners are now available across skilled, semi-skilled, and unskilled occupations. To accommodate the increased demand for unskilled work, Canadian job vacancies with without experience descriptions in their occupational eligibility requirements are more prevalent.

Finding jobs in Canada targeting workers with little to no experience can seem daunting. Still, this list of the top 10 jobs in Canada for foreigners without experience should point you in the right direction.

While this list was created to offer inspiration, you won't be able to apply for these positions here. Instead, you can head to job searching sites like LinkedIn, Indeed, or the Canadian Job Bank, where plenty of Canada's vacancies are advertised.

What Are The Top 10 Jobs For Immigrants Without Experience in Canada?

Canada has long been recognized as a land of opportunity, attracting immigrants worldwide. Many individuals come to Canada for high-paying jobs, even if they don't have prior work experience in the country. If you're one of them, here are the top 10 jobs for immigrants without experience in Canada:

CareGiver (NOC 44101)

The demand for caregivers in Canada is constantly growing due to the aging population. As a caregiver, you would assist elderly or disabled individuals in their homes. Although formal education is not mandatory, having relevant certifications and training can increase your chances of landing a job in this field.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Caregiver jobs are most in demand in Manitoba and Ontario.

The annual wage for Caregiver jobs in Canada is 35,100 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Learn how to apply for Caregiver jobs in Canada.

Dishwasher (NOC 65201)

Restaurants and cafeterias across Canada always need dishwashers. This job requires minimal experience and provides an opportunity to work in a fast-paced environment. While it may not be the most glamorous position, it can serve as a stepping stone toward better opportunities in the culinary industry.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Dishwasher jobs are most in demand in Saskatchewan, Prince Edward Island, and Manitoba.

The annual wage for Dishwasher jobs in Canada is 28,470 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Kitchen Helper (NOC 65201)

Like dishwasher positions, kitchen helper roles are available in Canada. As a kitchen helper, you assist chefs and cooks in preparing, cleaning, and maintaining the kitchen area. This entry-level position allows you to gain valuable experience and learn about different cuisines.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Kitchen Helpers are most in demand in Manitoba and Nova Scotia.

The annual wage for Kitchen Helpers in Canada is 28,470 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Learn how to find Kitchen Helper jobs in Canadas food service industry.

Room Attendant (NOC 65310)

The hospitality industry offers numerous opportunities for immigrants without experience. A room attendant is responsible for cleaning and maintaining hotel rooms, ensuring guests stay comfortably. With attention to detail and a strong work ethic, you can excel in this role and potentially advance to higher positions within the hotel industry.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Room Attendants are most in demand in Alberta, British Columbia, New Brunswick, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, Saskatchewan, and Yukon Territory.

The annual wage for Room Attendant in Canada is 35,100 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

General Laborers (NOC 75110)

Various industries, such as construction and manufacturing, require general laborers for tasks that don't require specialized skills. These jobs often involve physical labor and may be physically demanding, but they provide an opportunity to gain experience and potentially move up the career ladder.

According to the Canada Job Bank., General Laborers are most in demand in Alberta, Nova Scotia, Nunavut, Ontario, Quebec, Saskatchewan, and Yukon.

The annual wage for General Laborers in Canada is 50,700 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Exhibit Cleaner (NOC 65311)

Canada has numerous museums, galleries, and exhibition spaces, requiring exhibit cleaners to maintain cleanliness and order. This position involves cleaning and maintaining exhibits, ensuring they are pristine for visitors. While no prior experience is necessary, attention to detail and a passion for art and culture are highly valued.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Exhibit Cleaners are most in demand in Alberta, BC, New Brunswick, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, Saskatchewan, and Yukon.

The annual wage for Exhibit Cleaner in Canada is 35,100 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Seasonal Nursery Workers - Cone Picking (NOC 85103)

In the agricultural sector, seasonal nursery workers are in demand, particularly for cone picking in tree nurseries. While this job may be physically demanding, it offers an opportunity to work in a beautiful outdoor environment and gain experience in the agricultural industry.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Seasonal Nursery Workers - Cone Picking are most in demand in Ontario and Prince Edward Island.

The annual wage for Exhibit Cleaner in Canada is 33,150 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Cashier (NOC 65100)

Retail stores and supermarkets often have openings for cashiers, even for those without prior experience. As a cashier, you would handle transactions, assist customers, and maintain the cash register. This position requires good customer service skills and attention to detail.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Cashiers are most in demand in Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island.

The annual wage for Cashiers in Canada is 27,787.50 CAD according to the Canada Job Bank.

Driver Residential (NOC 73300)

If you have a valid driver's license, becoming a residential driver can be a viable option. This role involves delivering packages or goods to residential areas. While no prior experience is necessary, having a clean driving record and good time management skills are important.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Driver Residential jobs are most in demand in Manitoba and Prince Edward Island.

The annual wage for Driver Residential in Canada is 46,800 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Packaging (NOC 95109)

Packaging jobs are available in various industries, including manufacturing and distribution. These positions involve preparing products for shipment and ensuring they are properly packaged and labeled. Attention to detail and efficiency are key skills for this role.

According to the Canada Job Bank., Packaging jobs are most in demand in Nova Scotia, Northwest Territories, Nunavut, Prince Edward Island, and Yukon Territory.

The annual wage for Packaging jobs in Canada is 29,250 CAD, according to the Canada Job Bank.

Learn more about Canadian jobs that require little or no experience with this helpful guide about jobs in Canada that do not need a degree.

How Can I Find a Job in Canada Without Experience?

Finding a job in Canada without prior experience may seem challenging, but with the right approach, it is possible. Here are some strategies to help you in your job search:

Networking

Networking is crucial in any job search, and Canada is no exception. Attend job fairs, industry events, and professional networking events to connect with employers and professionals in your field. Building relationships and showcasing your skills and enthusiasm can open job opportunities.

Job Search Websites

Utilize job search websites catering to the Canadian job market. Some of the more popular online job portals include:

  • Indeed Canada
  • Monster Canada
  • Glassdoor Canada

Customize your profile and resume to highlight your relevant skills and qualifications.

Volunteering

Volunteering is an excellent way to gain experience and make connections in your chosen field. Look for volunteer opportunities related to your industry of interest. Not only will you gain valuable skills, but you may also come across job prospects through your network of fellow volunteers and organizations.

Learn more about why should volunteer in Canada.

Which Immigration Programs Can I Use to Apply Without Job Experience?

Canada offers several immigration programs that allow individuals to apply for permanent residency without requiring job experience. Here are three programs you can consider:

Post-Graduate Work Permit (PGWP)

If you have recently graduated from a Canadian educational institution, you may be eligible for a post-graduate work permit. This permit allows you to work in Canada for up to three years, gaining valuable Canadian work experience. This experience can significantly enhance your employability and increase your chances of finding a high-paying job.

Find out more about how to stay in Canada using the Post-Graduate Work Permit.

IEC Working Holiday

The International Experience Canada (IEC) Working Holiday program provides opportunities for young individuals to work and travel in Canada. Through this program, you can obtain an open work permit for any employer in Canada. While this program doesn't have specific experience requirements, having relevant skills and qualifications can increase your chances of securing a job.

Learn how to apply for a Working Holiday Visa for Canada in 2023.

Family Sponsorship

If you have a family member who is a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, they may be able to sponsor you for immigration. The Family Sponsorship program allows eligible Canadian citizens or permanent residents to sponsor their:

  • Spouse,
  • Common-law partner,
  • Dependent child, or
  • Other eligible family members.

This program doesn't require job experience, as the sponsor assumes responsibility for your financial well-being.

Find out more about Family Sponsorship programs.

Learn more about the various ways you can immigrate to Canada without job experience with or as an unskilled worker.

What Canada Offers Workers With No Job Experience

Canada provides job opportunities for immigrants without experience and offers several benefits and support systems. Here are some advantages of working in Canada without job experience:

High Wages/Salary

Canada is known for its high wages and salaries compared to many other countries. Even entry-level positions often offer competitive compensation, allowing you to earn a good income and provide for yourself and your family.

Here is a comprehensive breakdown of the median household income in Canada.

Employment Opportunities

Canada has a strong and diverse economy, with numerous industries experiencing growth and requiring a skilled workforce. With the right qualifications and a willingness to learn, you can find employment opportunities in various sectors, including healthcare, technology, finance, and more.

Learn more about the employment opportunities offered by Canadas economic structure.

Find out more about Canada's employment opportunities by learning where to find a job in Canada.


Healthcare Provision

As a worker in Canada, you and your immediate family members are eligible for healthcare coverage through the publicly funded healthcare system. This ensures access to essential medical services, providing peace of mind and financial security.

Find out more about Canadas subsidized healthcare system.

Skills Development Opportunities

Canada values lifelong learning and provides numerous opportunities for skills development. Whether through on-the-job training, apprenticeships, or further education, you can continuously enhance your skills and qualifications, opening doors to better career prospects.

Finding high-paying jobs in Canada without prior experience may require some effort, but it is certainly achievable. Canada provides abundant job opportunities for immigrants without experience.

Learn more about improving your job skills in Canada.

Various options are available across industries, from caregiver positions to dishwashing jobs. By networking, volunteering, and exploring immigration programs, newcomers can unlock their potential and secure high-paying jobs in Canada.

Tips to Find a Job in Canada Without Experience

Remember to showcase your transferable skills, invest in professional development, and take advantage of Canada's supportive work environment and social benefits. By exploring the top 10 jobs for immigrants without experience, utilizing job search strategies, and considering immigration programs, you can unlock your potential and secure a rewarding career in Canada.

To assist you in becoming a permanent Canadian resident without job experience, we recommend using a certified Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC). RCICs will assist you with application deadlines, liaise with Canadian immigration officers, obtain and submit the required documents and pay all requested application fees.

FAQs

What Place in Canada Has the Highest Number of Workers Without Job Experience?

The province of Ontario, particularly the Greater Toronto Area (GTA), has many job opportunities for workers without experience. The GTA is a hub for various industries, including healthcare, finance, and technology, making it an attractive destination for immigrants.

Which Canadian City or Town Offers the Best Opportunities for Immigrants Without Job Experience?

While many cities and towns in Canada offer job opportunities for immigrants without experience, some notable ones include:

  • Toronto
  • Vancouver
  • Calgary
  • Montreal

These cities have thriving economies and diverse job markets, providing a range of options for newcomers.
