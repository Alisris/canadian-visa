Embarking on a journey from Malaysia to work in Canada can be a thrilling yet challenging process. But you're not alone, and this step-by-step guide on "how to apply Canada visa for Malaysian" will assist you in making this transition as smooth as possible.

Canada is known for its high standard of living, diverse culture, and excellent working conditions, making it an attractive destination for Malaysians seeking to broaden their horizons. But before you can start your Canadian adventure, there are some essential steps that you need to follow.

This guide will walk you through the entire process, from choosing the right visa, checking your eligibility, applying for jobs, and submitting your work permit application. Let's delve into the journey of how to earn the privilege of working in the beautiful land of Canada.

How to Get a Canadian Work Visa as a Malaysian

Canada offers different work visas to foreign nationals based on their job skills, work experience, and the demand for those skills in the Canadian job market. As a Malaysian, you need to understand the intricacies of the Canadian work visa system to find the right path for you.

A Canadian work visa application starts in Malaysia, but the Canadian High Commission handles the process. The procedure is straightforward but requires diligence and careful attention to detail. This guide will help you understand the process better and increase your chances of success.

Remember, the key is to be patient and thorough, as the process can take time and requires various documents and procedures. Each step is crucial, and skipping any can lead to delays or even rejection of your application.

Step 1: Choose a Visa That Works Best for You

Before you start the application process, you must choose the type of work permit that suits your situation and needs best. Canada offers Malaysians two types of work permits. They are as follows.

Employer-specific Work Permit

An employer-specific work permit allows you to work in Canada based on specific conditions, such as the employer you will work for, how long you will work, and where you will work. You can get one via programs like the Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP). Your employer will need a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) to hire you.

Open Work Permit

An open work permit is not job-specific. This means you can work for any employer in Canada except those listed as ineligible or failing to comply with certain conditions. The open work permit can be obtained through the International Mobility Program (IMP). Canadian employers do prefer this program because you won’t need an LMIA to be hired by them. The IMP is also known for its quick and easy process, which is great as work permits tend to be complex when doing it independently.

Step 2: Check your Eligibility to Work in Canada

To work in Canada, you must

  • Prove to an officer that you will leave Canada when your work permit expires;
  • Show that you have enough money to take care of yourself and your family members during your stay in Canada and to return home;
  • Obey the law and have no record of criminal activity (you may be required to give a police clearance certificate);
  • Not be a danger to Canada’s security;
  • Be in good health and have a medical exam, if needed;
  • Not plan to work for an employer listed with the status “ineligible” on the list of employers who failed to comply with the conditions;
  • Not plan to work for an employer who regularly offers striptease, erotic dance, escort services, or erotic massages; and
  • Give the officer any other documents they ask for to prove you can enter the country.

The Canadian authorities may ask you to undergo a medical examination and provide a police certificate.

Step 3: Apply For Jobs in Canada

Once you determine your eligibility and choose the right work permit, the next step is to apply for jobs in Canada. The job search may seem daunting initially, but it can be straightforward with the right approach. Here's a handy guide to finding a job in Canada:

Various job portals in Canada are available for job hunting. Learn more about Canada's top job sites. Tailor your resume to Canadian standards, highlighting your skills and experiences that match the job you are applying for.

Step 4: Submit Your Work Permit Application

After securing a job offer, it's time to apply for your work permit. You can apply online or on paper. The online application is faster, easier to check, and ensures your application is complete before submitting it, reducing the risk of being rejected due to incomplete documentation. Learn more about what to do if your visa is rejected.

After You Apply

After submitting your application, it's time to wait. The processing time can vary depending on factors such as the volume of applications received, the completeness of your application, and the time taken to verify your information. Learn more about Canada work permit processing times.

Living and Working in Canada as a Malaysian

Living and working in Canada can be a rewarding experience. This section will cover why Malaysians choose to work in Canada, the differences between working in Canada and Malaysia, in-demand jobs, salary expectations, and the cost of Canadian work permits.

Why Malaysians Choose to Work in Canada

Many Malaysians choose to work in Canada due to the country's high standard of living, diverse culture, and excellent working conditions. The welcoming nature of Canadians and the respect for multiculturalism also make Canada an ideal place for Malaysians to call their second home. 

How is Working in Canada Different from Working in Malaysia?

Working in Canada differs from working in Malaysia in several ways. From labor laws to work-life balance and the overall working culture, you'll find many differences that can be both challenging and exciting.

What Jobs Are In Demand in Canada for Immigrants?

Canada has a diverse economy with many job opportunities for immigrants. Some in-demand jobs include software engineers, medical professionals, sales associates, and business development managers. However, Canada's provinces and territories have in-demand occupations, which you can find below.


What is a Good Salary in Canada?

Salaries in Canada are competitive and vary based on the job role, industry, and location. According to Talent.com, a person in Canada typically earns between 50,700 - 97,500 CAD annually. However, salaries in Canada are negotiable. Learn more about how to negotiate your salary in Canada.

What is the Cost of a Canadian Work Permit?

The cost of a Canadian work permit can vary depending on the type of permit and where you apply. The standard fee is 155 CAD, but it can change based on certain conditions. Learn more about Canadian visa fees.

Applying for a Canadian work visa as a Malaysian can seem overwhelming. Still, the process can be manageable with the right information and guidance. This guide is intended to help you understand the process better and increase your chances of success. Ensure you start your journey to working in Canada from Malaysia correctly! Good luck with your application, and here's to your future success in Canada!

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions and answers about working in Canada from Malaysia.

Is it Easy to Work in Canada?

Working in Canada can be easy or difficult, depending on your perspective and experience. Most Malaysians enjoy working in Canada after adjusting to the culture and work environment. Learn more about Working in Canada.

How to Prove Work Experience for Canada Immigration?

Proving your work experience for Canada immigration involves providing documents that show you have the necessary skills and experience for the job you are applying for. These can include reference letters, employment contracts, pay stubs, and tax forms.
