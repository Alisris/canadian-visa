Top Job Sites To Get Work In Canada

by 
Themba Phongolo
×

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: January 25th, 2023

Are you planning on moving to Canada anytime soon? If you are, then you may have wondered from time to time if you’ll be able to find any work opportunities in Canada in order to support yourself and/or your family.

As a means to put these thoughts to rest, we have compiled a list of the top job sites that you can use to navigate the Canadian jobs market in order to assist you to make an informed choice regarding your work prospects in Canada.

Whether you’re highly skilled or have little to no work experience, we are confident that these job sites will have you covered with regards to providing you with a list of current Canadian jobs you can apply for.

Why Would You Want To Work In Canada?

It’s 2023, and the Canadian labor market is heating up! As one of the most competitive economies in the world, Canada has been increasing its offering of work opportunities steadily over the last few years. Its complement of immigration application streams is one of the most extensive, means tested and rigorous in the world, with over 100 various immigration programs offered.

These programs include a generous number of work-related immigration visa programs and pilots, for example Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP) and Federal Skilled Workers Programs (FSWP) with each program outlining its own unique set of jobs.

Though the list of jobs provided by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) is comprehensive enough to give people who want to move to Canada for work a clear idea of which work opportunities that they should expect to find available, it is by no means a complete list.

Besides using an immigration consultancy company like CanadianVisa as a valuable and authoritative resource to provide you with the most up to date Canadian work opportunities as per immigration programs offered, sourcing other work opportunities via Canadian job sites is also recommended.

In this regard having a clear understanding of currently available jobs in the Canadian labor market will prove valuable to you and to the Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants (RCIC) that’ll assist you when deciding whether or not you’re eligible to apply. Should you find that you’re not eligible, this will save you both time and money.

The task of finding a job in Canada from overseas can be daunting. Firstly, you have to determine whether the employer has a positive labor market impact assessment (LMIA) and then only can you apply for a work permit. You may need to adapt your resume and dive deep into the labor market needs of Canada to narrow-down where your skills are in-demand.

But no need to panic! We’ve not only narrowed-down the top 5 job sites to get work in Canada, but we’ve compiled a list of expert tips on securing your dream job below!

List Of The Top Job Sites To Get Work In Canada?

Indeed Canada

Type: Worldwide employment-related search engine

Special feature: Job aggregator and free to use

Indeed is the most popular job site in the world. The site attracts thousands of employers in Canada but more importantly, Indeed is a job aggregator, this means it pulls jobs from career sites and classified listings and displays them on its own site. So chances are, if there is a job available in Canada, it will be posted on Indeed!

Both foreign workers and newcomers to Canada use Indeed for their job hunting to never miss out on opportunities. Top Canadian employers within various industries such as healthcare, transportation, and warehousing posts regular job listings on Indeed.

Workopolis

Type: Canadian job platform

Special feature: Offer job services in French and English and is free to use

Workopolis is one of the oldest and most well-known Canadian job sites. The job site has a foreign worker job section that displays jobs aimed at workers outside of Canada. Here you can find jobs for live-in caregivers, farmworkers, cashiers, and plenty of other in-demand occupations throughout Canada.

The job listings will indicate whether the employers have LMIA-documents in order to hire foreign workers which removes all uncertainty from the job-hunting process.

Monster Canada

Type: Canadian job platform

Special feature: Video job ads, job fit scoring system, free resume assessment, and free to use

Monster Canada is one of the more advanced job platforms used throughout Canada. The platform comes complete with a job fit scoring system that shows which jobs are most relevant to your skills and interest.

You can also get a free resume assessment by a professional for tips on how to make your resume more attractive to potential employers. How neat?! On top of that, some jobs are posted with a video to show you rather than tell you more about the job before you swipe right to apply.

eJobbo

Type: Canadian job platform

Special feature: Video job application, smart matching system, affiliated with a visa agency, and free to use

eJobbo is a new Canadian job platform with a growing database of employers and recruiters. The platform has a few unique features such as a smart matching system that works intelligently to only show jobs that are most compatible with your skills, experience, education, and other important criteria. Another great feature especially for foreign workers is the video job application that enables you to ‘sell yourself’ on video.

Canadian Job Bank

Type: Canada’s official government job site

Special feature: Job Match, free to use

The Canadian Job Bank is one of the best sites to find a job in Canada, especially if you are an Express Entry applicant. Employers in Canada who want to support a foreign worker's permanent residency application are encouraged to recruit foreign workers through the Job Bank. The job match function connects eligible employers with Express Entry candidates. Candidates in the pool with a valid job offer can earn 50 or 200 points based on the type of job.

Tips On How You Can Secure A Job In Canada?

Don’t Rule Out Rural Areas

Search for jobs in Canada where the competition is low and jobs are plenty like in the maritime regions and other remote areas. Not only are there greater labor shortages in these parts but they have their own immigration pilots aimed at attracting foreign workers.

The pilots include the Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP) and the Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot (RNIP). These Pilots have lists of designated employers who can support your permanent residency application by offering you a job without needing an LMIA! For example, Nova Scotia is a province under the AIP with a list of employers whom you can contact through the provincial government's job sites.

Enlist Professional Help

When looking for a job overseas, you need all the help you can get. A headhunter, recruitment agency, or a career counselor knows more about the Canadian job market than you do.

They have local connections and understand the legal requirements of international work, global mobility, and relocation. If you want to work in Canada they can cast a wide net to find employment opportunities that may not be advertised online or in newspapers.

Apply with a Canadian Style Resume

You’re sending your resume out to a new audience which naturally means you need to adapt it to that audience’s preferences. A Canadian style resume is key to finding and securing a job in Canada. Your resume should be translated into English or French and your cover letter should be customized to every job you apply for.

In-Demand Occupations in Canada

Is your occupation in-demand in Canada? Not sure? Simply, scan our in-demand occupation pages to discover in which provinces or territories your skills are highly sought after. This will help narrow down your job search to a specific location, especially in a country as big as Canada! At the same time, you will increase your chances of finding employment by looking in the right places.

As of January 2023, and until further notice, certain occupations have been prioritized across all provinces and territories (except in the province of Quebec). This means employers who wish to hire foreign workers in the occupations below do not need an LMIA.

LMIA-Exempt Jobs
Butchers, meat cutters and fishmongers-retail and wholesale
Agricultural service contractors, farm supervisors, and specialized livestock workers
General Farm Workers
Nursery and Greenhouse Workers
Harvesting laborers
Industrial butchers and meat cutters, poultry preparers, and related workers
Fish and seafood plant workers
Laborers in food, beverage, and associated products processing
Laborers in fish and seafood processing

Stay Positive and Keep on Keeping On

Nothing good ever comes easy. Don’t be caught by surprise if it's been a few weeks or months without any job offers. Navigating your way overseas is a difficult task no matter how many top job sites you apply through or how aesthetically appealing your resume is. The good news is that it’s not impossible and it has been done a thousand times! The trick is to stay positive and keep your effort level consistent.

FAQS

What are the most common jobs in Canada?

Some of the most common jobs in Canada include nursing, general work, courier/truck driving, welding and electrical engineering.

Do you need Canadian work experience to apply?

If you have prior work experience in Canada you are more likely to be successful in your job application. However, if you do not have work experience, this does not exempt you from applying.

Can you apply for a Canadian job without using a Canadian job site?

Though it is advised that you use a Canadian job site to search for and apply for advertised jobs, you are not exempted from applying through other job sites that host and advertise Canadian jobs.
