Top Jobs in Canada for Mexican Immigrants

by 
Themba Phongolo

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: May 30th, 2023

For some people choosing to work in Canada is just about earning a good salary and obtaining some good international work experience, but for most, its about creating a better life for themselves and their families and ultimately becoming permanent residents of Canada.

There is no shortage of jobs in Canada as long as you are motivated, hard-working, and looking in the right places. Many immigrants go to major cities, mostly because they may have support structures there, such as Mexican communities, and on the surface, there is a significant amount of job opportunities in areas with larger populations.

The important thing to remember is that while there may be more opportunity, there will always be more competition. Cities tend to breed a dog-eat-dog kind of environment. In contrast, if you broaden your options to Canadas other less populated provinces and towns, you may find ample opportunity just waiting to be snatched up.

Canada is known for its high quality of life, free health care system, and focus on equality and diversity. In addition, the Canadian job market is very competitive and offers many opportunities for foreign workers, including Mexican citizens. Many Canadian employers value the experience and education of Mexican workers and are willing to sponsor work visas to help them establish themselves in Canada. To help you make an informed decision about making the move to Canada as a Mexican, we compiled a list of the top ten jobs for Mexican immigrants which are outlined below.

The 10 best jobs in Canada for Mexican Immigrants

Canada is a popular destination for Mexicans seeking work abroad. With a constantly growing job market, a stable economy, and a high standard of living, Canada offers many opportunities for Mexican citizens seeking employment and a better life.

This article will provide a comprehensive guide to the top 10 jobs in Canada for Mexican citizens, an overview of the Canadian job market, visa, and work permit requirements, job search tips, and adjusting to the work culture. Canadian, and resources and support are available to Mexican job seekers in Canada.

Each listed job has a corresponding National Occupational Classification (NOC) code. NOC is Canadas standardized classification system which assigns 5-digit codes and a description to individual or collective occupations in Canada. The classification system was created to assist prospective foreign workers find the occupations theyre eligible to apply for in a more clearly defined and accessible manner.

Computer Programmer (NOC 21230)

Computer programmers are in high demand in Canada due to the increasing demand for information technology. Mexican workers with experience in programming languages such as Java, Python, and C++ can find opportunities at technology companies nationwide.

The highly specialized nature of this occupation in combination with its high demand among Canadian employers ensures that computer programmers are paid a highly competitive salary and receive favorable work benefits about annual paid and sick leave, health, and housing insurance.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for Computer Programmers in Canada is 90,503 CAD.

To find out how to immigrate to Canada as a computer programmer, take a look here.

Registered Nurse (NOC 31301)

Nursing is an ever-growing field in Canada, and Mexican workers with a valid nursing license in Mexico may be eligible for certification in Canada. Registered nurses can find opportunities in hospitals, clinics, and nursing homes nationwide.

Canada is always looking to expand its healthcare system to meet the access demands of its growing population, and therefore there are many opportunities to find employment.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for a Registered Nurse in Canada is 112,216 CAD.

To know more about where you can live and work as a Registered Nurse in Canada, take a look here.

Accountant (NOC 14200)

Accountants are an essential part of Canada's financial sector and work in a variety of industries. Mexican workers with accounting and finance backgrounds can find opportunities at companies nationwide.

With some of the largest Financial institutions in the Western hemisphere, employment benefits for accounts are very favorable, including annual salaries and health benefits.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for an Accountant in Canada is 81,794 CAD.

Civil Engineer (NOC 21300)

Construction and engineering are important industries in Canada, and Mexican workers with civil engineering experience can find opportunities on construction projects throughout the country. Due to the high demand for civil engineers due to Canadas aims to expand its infrastructure, civil engineers in Canada enjoy a handsome average annual pay and beneficial work benefits and incentives regarding healthcare, housing, and auto insurance.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for Civil Engineers in Canada is 107,345 CAD.

To find out more about how you can apply to immigrate to Canada as an engineer, take a look here.

Teacher (NOC 41220)

Education is an important industry in Canada, and Mexican workers with teaching experience can find opportunities in public and private schools throughout the country.

Canadian Educators, in particular, basic/primary and secondary education are some of the most valued in Canadian society. The Canadian government ensures their retention through highly agreeable pay, paid annual, sick, and maternity/paternity leave benefits.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for a Teacher in Canada is 54,126 CAD.

Take a look here to know which provinces in Canada require your services as a teacher the most.

Software Developer (NOC 21232)

Software developers are in high demand in Canada due to the growing demand for information technology. Mexican workers with experience in software development can find opportunities at technology companies nationwide.

Due to the emphasis on ICT jobs as the future of Canadas economic development, Canada retains foreign software developers by providing them with attractive work benefits, especially in pay.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for a Software Developer in Canada is 123,357 CAD.

Radiology Technician (NOC 32121)

Medical technology is an ever-growing field in Canada, and Mexican workers with medical technology backgrounds can find opportunities in hospitals and clinics throughout the country.

Since radiologists are some of the most consummate and reliable medical professionals who provide a highly essential service, they are well taken care of by their employers through favorable work benefits regarding pay, healthcare provision, housing, and education benefits

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for a Radiology Technician in Canada is 106,834 CAD.

Data Analyst (NOC 21223)

Data analysts are an important part of Canada's information technology industry. Mexican workers with experience in data analysis can find opportunities at technology companies nationwide.

Vital for the improvement of the efficacy of business operations, Data analysts are given very attractive work benefits regarding pay, paid annual and sick and accommodating working hours.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for a Data Analyst in Canada is 90,080 CAD.

Project Manager (NOC 10019)

Project management is a valuable skill in many sectors in Canada, including technology, construction, and finance. Mexican workers with experience in project management can find opportunities in companies throughout the country. Although it varies by province and city, project managers are always in-demand and paid handsomely for their services.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for a Project Manager in Canada is 114,331 CAD.

Social Worker (NOC 42201)

Social workers are an important part of Canada's health care and social services system. Mexican workers with experience in social work can find opportunities in non-profit organizations and government agencies throughout the country.

For the services they provide toward improving the well-being of Canadian society, social workers are compensated well in terms of average annual pay, healthcare insurance, and housing benefits.

Expected Salary

According to IndeedCanada, the average base salary per year for a Social Worker in Canada is 86,901 CAD.

As a prospective immigrant social worker to Canada, if you want to know which provinces require your services the most, take a look here.

If youre eligible to apply for any of the jobs mentioned above, you can start your journey to Canada by contacting a certified immigration consultant.

Recommendations on How to Find a Job as a Mexican in Canada

There are several resources that Mexican citizens can use to find job opportunities in Canada:

  • Job searches engines such as Indeed, Monster, and Glassdoor.
  • Recruitment agencies that specialize in international talents, such as Randstad, Manpower, and Adecco.
  • Professional organizations and networks that cater to specific industries, such as the Canadian Society of Engineers or the Canadian Medical Association.
  • Online job boards and forums that cater to international talents, such as WorkinginCanada.gc.ca or CanadaVisa.com.

Where to Find Work as a Mexican in Canada

Mexican citizens will find different provinces appealing to settle in based on their occupational and lifestyle needs and wants. However, generally, the pattern of Mexican settlement in Canada tends to follow where most varying job opportunities are available for them, which is usually:

  • Alberta
  • British Columbia
  • Ontario

If you arent eligible for Canadian permanent residency (PR), you can use work permits to provide you with a path to becoming a Canadian permanent resident.

Other Pathways for Mexicans Immigrate to Canada

There are a variety of immigration pathways (programs, pilots, and streams) that Mexicans can use to apply for permanent residency in Canada. Among those that grant permanent residency in Canada should your application be successful, the Express Entry system and Provincial Nominee Programs are among the most commonly used.

Learn more about how you can become a permanent resident of Canada here

The Benefits of Working in Canada as a Mexican

Safety

Canada is one of the most socio-economically secure and safe countries in the world, ranking within the 15 safest countries in the world according to Global Peace Index rankings from worldpopulationreview.com.

A lot of Mexicans will find value in the safety and security that Canada provides in comparison to their own countries' criminal turmoils regarding the illicit international drug trade.

Competitive Salaries

Highly educated and skilled Mexican professionals, especially in important industries such as ICT and healthcare will come to find their services will receive higher pay on average in Canada than they would in Mexico.

FAQs

Which Canadian City Has The Most Mexican Immigrants?

As per Statcan.com, Toronto, Ontario, has the highest number of people of Mexican descent in Canada. It is followed closely by Montreal, Quebec.

Which Place in Canada Has The Highest Number of Opportunities for Mexicans?

Socio-economic opportunities are available across Canada for most eligible Mexicans. However, generally speaking, the provinces of Ontario and Alberta are where most Mexicans will find the most economic opportunities.
