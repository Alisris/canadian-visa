Top 7 Jobs in Transportation in Canada

by 
Themba Phongolo

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: June 4th, 2024
7 min read

Canada has a bustling transportation sector shaping the nation's movement of goods and people. Canadas transportation professionals are vital in keeping the country connected and thriving. The increasing number of jobs in transportation in Canada highlights the importance of the nation's transportation industry. Whether you're a seasoned bus or truck driver or an aspiring transit operator, there's something for every foreign worker in the top 7 jobs in transportation in Canada.

Ready to explore the diverse transportation careers in Canada? Then, join us in uncovering the top 7 jobs in transportation in Canada and learn about the qualifications and salary prospects for these in-demand roles.

Does Canada Need Transportation Workers?

Canadas transportation sector faces a well-documented shortage of skilled workers. This demand is reflected in recent Express Entry draws, which target specific occupations. For example, on March 13, 2024, Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) held a draw specifically for Transport occupations, inviting 975 candidates with a minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score of 430.

This targeted draw highlights the pressing need for qualified transportation workers across various roles. Trucking, for example, experiences a significant driver shortage, with estimates suggesting a need for over 17,000 additional drivers annually by 2025, according to Canadas Immigration Levels Plan. Similar shortages are projected for aviation, marine, and railway transportation in the coming years.

These trends suggest that Canada's transportation industry will continue to require lots of skilled foreign workers. This allows qualified individuals internationally to explore careers in Canadas growing transportation industry.

What Are the Top 7 Transportation Jobs in Canada?

Canada's vast geography and thriving trade industry relies heavily on a strong transportation network. This network is driven by a diverse workforce operating various modes of transport from land to air and sea. Here's a closer look at seven of the most in-demand jobs in transportation in Canada, with National Occupational Classification (NOC) codes and annual average salaries from Canadas Job Bank.

Transport Truck Driver (NOC 73300)

Job Description

Transport truck drivers support Canada's land freight movement. They haul goods and materials long distances, ensuring timely deliveries internationally across Canadas provinces and territories. Responsibilities include operating tractor-trailers, adhering to safety regulations, performing pre-trip inspections, and maintaining accurate logs.

Qualification Requirements

A high school diploma or foreign equivalent qualification accredited by Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) is generally required. The crucial element is obtaining a Commercial Driver's License (CDL) with the appropriate endorsements for the type of vehicle you plan to operate. Certain Canadian employers may also prefer candidates with experience in the transportation industry.

Annual Average Salary: 66,702.68 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Ontario, Prince Edward Island.

Bus Driver (NOC 73301)

Job Description

Bus drivers are vital in transporting passengers within Canadas cities, towns, and regions. They operate various buses, including school, city, and tour buses. Responsibilities include adhering to schedules, ensuring passenger safety and comfort, maintaining order within the bus, and understanding different road conditions.

Qualification Requirements

A high school diploma or ECA equivalent qualification, followed by a CDL with a passenger endorsement specific to your driving bus. Some Canadian employers may also require experience driving a bus.

Annual Average Salary: 61,360.41 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: British Columbia, Ontario

Rapid Transit Operator (NOC 72024)

Job Description

Rapid transit or subway operators are responsible for safely driving and operating subway trains within a designated network of railway lines. They ensure passenger safety, adhere to schedules, operate the trains according to established protocols, and communicate effectively with passengers and control centers.

Qualification Requirements

A Canadian high school diploma or equivalent ECA-accredited qualification is usually required. Most Canadian employers prefer candidates with a college diploma in railway transportation technology or a related program. Experience working in the transportation industry, particularly rail operations, can be advantageous.

Annual Average Salary: 61,365.12 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: British Columbia, Ontario

Aircraft Pilot (NOC 72600)

Job Description

Airline transport pilots, also known as commercial airline pilots, are highly skilled professionals responsible for the safe operation of commercial airplanes. They transport passengers and cargo on scheduled flights, identify weather conditions, perform pre-flight checks, and communicate effectively with air traffic control.

Qualification Requirements

A high school diploma or equivalent ECA qualification is required, followed by obtaining a commercial pilot's license (CPL) with an Airline Transport Pilot (ATP) rating. This involves rigorous training and flight hours. Pilots must also pass strict medical examinations to ensure fitness for duty.

Annual Average Salary: 133,386.34 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Ontario

Ship's Officer (NOC 72602)

Job Description

Ship's mates, also known as marine mates, are vital crew members on commercial vessels. They work under the direction of the ship's captain. They are responsible for various tasks, including navigation, watchkeeping, cargo handling, and ensuring the safety and security of the vessel and its crew.

Qualification Requirements

A high school diploma or equivalent ECA qualification. However, the crucial qualification is to obtain a Marine Navigation Certificate or a similar qualification issued by Transport Canada. This involves attending a marine navigation school and meeting specific experience requirements at sea.

Annual Average Salary: 101,376.21 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Newfoundland and Labrador

Air Traffic Controller (NOC 72601)

Job Description

Air traffic controllers are the guardians of the skies, ensuring the safe and orderly flow of air traffic in and around airports. They communicate with pilots, monitor aircraft movements, provide instructions and clearances, and manage potential conflicts to prevent accidents.

Qualification Requirements

A specialized Nav Canada training academy qualification and high school diploma or ECA equivalent qualification is required. Air traffic controllers must also meet strict medical and security requirements.

Annual Average Salary: 117,699.67 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Newfoundland and Labrador, Quebec

Automotive Service Technician (NOC 72410)

Job Description

Automotive service technicians specializing in trucks and buses play a crucial role in keeping these large vehicles operational and safe. They diagnose and repair mechanical and electrical problems, perform preventative maintenance, and ensure these vehicles comply with safety regulations.

Qualification Requirements

A high school diploma or equivalent ECA is generally required along with a college diploma in automotive service technician or a related program specializing in heavy-duty vehicles. Some employers may prefer candidates with experience working on trucks or buses.

Annual Average Salary: 74,682.43 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Manitoba, Nova Scotia, Nunavut, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Yukon

How Can I Find a Transportation Job in Canada?

Canada's booming transportation sector offers many career opportunities. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to find a transportation job in Canada.

Step 1: Identify Your Area of Expertise

Consider transportation jobs that excite you. Identify your strengths by asking yourself whether you are mechanically inclined, detail-oriented, or possess excellent communication skills.

Research the specific requirements for your chosen role. College diplomas, certifications, and CDLs are often necessary. Resources like the Canadian Council of Motor Transport Administrators (CCMTA) offer information on CDLs.

Step 2: Do Research Using Online Resources

Explore industry-specific job boards like IndeedCanada, Workopolis, and Transportation Job Board by ITE Canada to find targeted postings. Use resources like Canadas Job Bank and the Canadian government's transportation jobs webpage to find government and transportation-related opportunities.

Step 3: Network and Build Connections

Attend job fairs and transportation industry conferences to connect with potential employers and learn about upcoming opportunities. Explore associations like the Canadian Trucking Alliance or the Canadian Airports Authority. These organizations often connect members with job postings and career resources.

Step 4: Highlight Your Transferable Skills

When applying for jobs, emphasize transferable skills relevant to the transportation industry in your resume and cover letter. This includes skills such as a strong work ethic, problem-solving abilities, and a commitment to safety. Customize your resume and cover letter to showcase skills and experiences that align with the job requirements.

Step 5: Consider Additional Qualifications

Fluency in English and French can be advantageous, particularly for jobs requiring communication with a diverse clientele. Explore additional certifications or training programs relevant to your chosen field. This can enhance your resume and make you a more competitive candidate.

How Can I Work in Canadas Transportation Sector?

Canada's transportation sector welcomes skilled international workers. Securing a Canada work permit is the first step to joining this exciting field. Here's a breakdown of the two common pathways to obtain a Canada work permit:

Temporary Foreign Worker Program

The most common path involves obtaining a valid job offer supported by a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA), which enables you to apply for an employer-specific work permit through the Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP).

This demonstrates the employer couldn't find a qualified Canadian citizen or permanent resident. Use industry job boards and transportation associations like the Canadian Trucking Alliance (CTA) to connect with potential LMIA-approved employers.

International Mobility Program

Some transportation jobs, particularly for recent graduates from Canadian universities or spouses of existing work permit holders, may qualify for open work permits by applying for the International Mobility Program (IMP). The IMP allows you to work for any employer in Canada without an LMIA and use an open work permit to address Canadas specific economic and labor needs.

Now that you better understand the top 7 transportation jobs in Canada, you can begin your dream journey to becoming a transportation worker in Canada.

FAQs

Are There Opportunities For Career Advancement in the Transportation Industry?

Yes, the transportation industry offers ample opportunities for career advancement, with avenues for progression in roles ranging from entry-level positions to managerial and executive levels.

How Can I Prepare For a Career in Transportation?

To prepare for a career in transportation, consider acquiring relevant certifications, licenses, and specialized training programs. Gaining hands-on experience through internships or apprenticeships can provide valuable insights into the industry.

What Are the Challenges And Rewards of Working in the Transportation Industry?

The transportation industry presents both challenges and rewards. Challenges may include long hours, tight deadlines, and understanding regulatory requirements. The rewards come in the form of job stability, competitive salaries, and the satisfaction of playing a vital role in keeping the world moving.
