Immigrate to Canada in Administration

by Robynn. Updated: June 3rd, 2020

Did you know that administrative jobs are in-demand in four of Canadas provinces? Boasting scenic landscapes, bustling and vibrant cities brimming with cultural attractions, attractive salaries, work benefits, free healthcare and education as well as the highest quality of living in the world, isnt Canada the place that you could see yourself building a future in?

With 1 million newcomers set to immigrate to Canada by 2022, the Canadian government is constantly accepting and processing visa applications, in search of only the highest qualities of skilled and semi-skilled workers looking to start a new life abroad.

Because administrative jobs in Canada are in-demand at all skill levels, there are naturally multiple options to choose from out of Canadas +70 different Canadian immigration programs, which means that whether you work in healthcare, education, or your average office, Canada has a place for you. But with all these options available it could be quite confusing, making it difficult to know where to start. So let's take a closer look at how to immigrate to Canada in 2020 as in administration.



Administrative Jobs in Canada


There are currently over 6,000 administrative jobs in Canada for experienced and qualified foreign workers who dream of unlocking their potential by immigrating to Canada. There are administrative jobs available at all levels, including highly skilled positions in government and healthcare administration to semi-skilled vacancies in general office admin such as payroll administrators.

When searching for an administrative job in Canada, it is always best to start by deciding where you would like to live and work in Canada. It is not only important to discover what your life would be like in Canada but also which provinces or territories will be are affordable to live in relation to your income. As mentioned earlier, administrative occupations are in-demand in 4 of Canadas provinces, including British Columbia, Saskatchewan, Manitoba and Nova Scotia. What Do I Need to Immigrate to Canada in Administration?


There are various ways to immigrate to Canada in administration. This, of course, depends on various factors such as:

  • Your skill level or training
  • Your level of work experience
  • Your age
  • Your language ability in English and French
  • Which province or territory you intend to settle in.

Occupations in Canada are classified according to the National Occupation Classification (NOC) system. This will give you a code that tells the government what your job skill level and type is. This will indicate what immigration programs you qualify for. Below are the NOC codes for administration jobs in Canada:

Now that you know your NOC code and skill level, let's take a look at your qualification requirements.

What Qualifications Do I Need?

This will once again depend on your labour industry as well as your job title and skill level.


Qualification Requirements for Administrative Jobs in Canada
Skill Level 0 or A                                                                 Usually requires a university education (some positions may require certification and experience eg. HR manager will require a Certified Human Resources Professional (CHRP)
Skill Level B                         Usually requires a college education, specialized training or apprenticeship (some positions may require certification and experience eg. Recruitment officers will require a Certified Human Resources Professional (CHRP)
Skill Level C                                                  Usually requires secondary school and/or occupation-specific training

It's also more than likely that you will need an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) This will prove that your qualifications meet Canadian standards as well as ensure that you receive Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) points when you begin your Canadian visa application. You can get an ECA from any of the designated organizations listed below:

  • Comparative Education Service - University of Toronto School of Continuing Studies;
  • International Credential Assessment Service of Canada;
  • World Education Services;
  • International Qualifications Assessment Service (IQAS); and
  • International Credential Evaluation Service.

To see if you meet the specific requirements of your chosen province or territory, be sure to contact the relevant regulatory authority.


How Do I Apply for My Canadian Visa?


As mentioned earlier, there are so many ways to immigrate to Canada in administration in 2020 but to make things just a little bit simpler weve broken down our top 4 options to move to Canada as an administrator.

  • The Express Entry System;
  • Provincial Nominee Program;
  • The Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot;
  • Atlantic Immigration Pilot.

1. The Express Entry System

The Express Entry system manages 3 Canadian federal economic immigration programs:

  • The Skilled Worker Program;
  • The Skilled Trade Worker Program; and
  • The Canadian Experience Class Program.

If your administrative occupation is classified at level 0, A or B, you could apply through the Federal Skilled Worker Program if you meet the necessary requirements and score a minimum of 67 out a maximum 100 points. Once you have created an Express Entry profile, your application will be entered into draws twice per month for up to a year or until you receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residency in Canada. Your profile will be ranked according to the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) and if you are among the highest-scoring applicants, you will receive an ITA. The best part, your application could be processed in as little as 6 months!

2. Provincial Nominee Program

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) requires that you have a full-time job offer in a province where your job is in demand. As weve already established, administrative jobs are in demand in four provinces and territories, namely British Columbia, Saskatchewan, Manitoba and Nova Scotia. Each province or territory has its own PNP and requirements that must be met to be able to apply for a provincial nomination (PN). What's so great about a PN you ask? Its worth 600 extra CRS points, which basically ensures that youll get an ITA for permanent residence.

Bonus tip: to speed up processing times, choose an Express Entry-linked PNP (if you qualify) and express interest in a province or territory when you create your profile. This will give the province or territory a chance to choose your profile straight from the Express Entry draw pool, cutting your processing time from 15-19 months (if you apply directly to your province or territory) to 6-8 months which means you could end up in Canada in under a year!

3. The Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot

To apply for Canadian permanent residency through the Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot all you will need is a valid full-time job offer in one of the 11 participating communities in order to immigrate to Canada. Seeing as administrative jobs are in-demand in British Columbia, Manitoba and Saskatchewan, you may want to take a look at these communities:

  • Brandon, Manitoba
  • Altona/Rhineland, Manitoba
  • Moose Jaw, Saskatchewan
  • Vernon, British Columbia; and
  • West Kootenay(Trail, Castlegar, Rossland, Nelson), British Columbia

Make sure that you find out if there are any specific requirements to move to your chosen community and province.

4. Atlantic Immigration Pilot

The Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP) is both a community-driven and employer-specific pilot that helps highly and intermediate-skilled foreign workers with valid job offers immigrate to Canada. So if you want to immigrate to one of Canadas four Atlantic provinces, especially Nova Scotia, you may be eligible to apply through the AIP, whether your skill level or type is 0, A, B or C.
