Top Skilled Trade Jobs In-demand in Canada in 2024

by 
Themba Phongolo

If you’re a foreign skilled tradesperson looking for better opportunities abroad, then you should set your eyes on the Great White North, where there are a lot of in-demand skilled trades jobs in Canada across various sectors. These professions not only offer stability but also provide pathways to lucrative careers and professional growth in Canada. From essential roles like electricians and plumbers to specialized fields such as welding and HVAC technicians, these jobs play a crucial role in sustaining and advancing Canada's development.

Ready to learn about in-demand skilled trades jobs in Canada in 2024? Then, take a look at our list of in-demand skilled trade jobs in Canada for 2024, driving the country's industrial and residential sectors forward.

What Are Canada's Top Ten In-demand Skilled Trade Jobs?


With an aging population and continuous infrastructure development, the demand for skilled tradespeople will remain high for years. If you're considering a skilled trade career, here's a closer look at ten of the most in-demand jobs in Canada, with National Occupational Classification (NOC) codes and annual average salaries from Canada’s Job Bank.

Industrial Electrician (NOC 72201)

Industrial electricians are the backbone of the industrial sector, responsible for installing, maintaining, and repairing electrical systems in factories, power plants, and mines. Their expertise encompasses various tasks, from wiring control panels to ensuring adherence to safety regulations. Industrial electricians typically require a college diploma or apprenticeship program, followed by journeyperson certification.

Annual Average Salary: 102,899.67 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Ontario, Saskatchewan

Welder (NOC 72106)

Welders are the metalworking masters who join pieces of metal using heat. Their skills are crucial in various industries, including construction, manufacturing, and shipbuilding. Depending on the specific welding process (arc, MIG, TIG, etc.), welders can create strong and durable joints for bridges, pipelines, heavy machinery, and more. Welding training can be obtained through college programs, technical schools, or apprenticeships.

Annual Average Salary: 74,832.85 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island

Heavy Equipment Operator (NOC 73400)

Heavy Equipment Operators operate heavy machinery like excavators, bulldozers, and graders in the construction, mining, and forestry sectors. Their expertise ensures efficient earthwork, site preparation, material movement, and road building. Heavy equipment operators typically receive on-the-job training or participate in apprenticeship programs to gain the necessary skills and certifications for specific equipment operations.

Annual Average Salary: 80,165.77 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Prince Edward Island

Plumber (NOC 72300)

From residential bathrooms to complex commercial plumbing systems, plumbers ensure the smooth flow of water and gas. They install, maintain, and repair pipes, fixtures, and related equipment, ensuring adherence to safety codes and regulations. Plumbers usually complete a college plumbing program or apprenticeship to acquire theoretical knowledge and practical skills.

Annual Average Salary: 88,172.69 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Prince Edward Island

Carpenters (NOC 72310)

Carpenters are the woodworkers who build and repair structures of all shapes and sizes, from houses and bridges to furniture and cabinetry. They possess skills in cutting, shaping, and assembling wood using hand and power tools. Carpentry training is offered through college programs or apprenticeships, with journeyperson Red Seal Certification required for independent practice.

Annual Average Salary: 80,137.69 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island

HVAC Technician (NOC 72402)

Keeping buildings comfortable year-round depends on the expertise of HVAC technicians. They install, maintain, and repair heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems in residential and commercial settings. HVAC technicians typically complete college programs or apprenticeships to learn about system operation, troubleshooting, and maintaining optimal comfort conditions.

Annual Average Salary: 88,143.09 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island

Transport Truck Driver (NOC 73300)

The backbone of the national supply chain, transport truck drivers are responsible for delivering goods across long distances by road. They ensure safe and efficient transportation of various commodities, adhering to strict safety regulations and navigating diverse road conditions. Truck driver training programs or obtaining a commercial driver's license (CDL) are essential for this career path.

Annual Average Salary: 66,760.93 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Ontario, Prince Edward Island

Millwright (NOC 72400)

Millwrights are the mechanics of the industrial world. They install, maintain, and repair machinery used in factories, power plants, and other industrial settings. Their work ensures the smooth operation of complex equipment, critical for various production processes. Millwrights typically undergo college programs or apprenticeships to acquire the necessary skills and knowledge.

Annual Average Salary: 87,056.25 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Alberta, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island, Saskatchewan

Automotive Service Technician (NOC 72410)

Keeping vehicles running smoothly requires the expertise of automotive service technicians. They diagnose, repair, and maintain cars, light trucks, and other automotive systems. Their work involves troubleshooting engine issues, repairing brakes, and ensuring overall vehicle performance. Automotive service technicians usually complete college programs or apprenticeships, followed by certification specific to vehicle makes or repair areas.

Annual Average Salary: 74,777.70 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Manitoba, Nova Scotia, Nunavut, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Yukon

Cook (NOC 62200)

While not traditionally considered a skilled trade, cooking requires a unique blend of technical skills and artistry. Cooks prepare and cook meals in restaurants, hotels, hospitals, and other food service establishments. They possess knowledge of food preparation techniques, menu planning, sanitation practices, and customer service. Cook training can be obtained through college programs, culinary schools, or on-the-job training under experienced chefs.

Annual Average Salary: 42,721.54 CAD

Most in-demand provinces: Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island

How Can I Find In-demand Skilled Trade Jobs in Canada?


Canada's booming skilled trades sector offers many opportunities, but the job search process can be overwhelming. Here's a roadmap to finding the perfect in-demand skilled trade job in Canada for your skills and interests.

Step 1: Your Interests and Skills

Self-assessment is crucial. Explore resources like the Government of Canada's Skills Assessment and Career Planning Tool to identify your strengths and preferences. Research specific trades using the NOC system. Explore websites like Skills Canada for trade descriptions, salary ranges, and training requirements.

Step 2: Research Canada’s Provincial or Territorial Labor Market Needs

Demand for skilled trades can vary across provinces. Use provincial government websites and immigration pathways specific to skilled trades. For instance, Alberta's Trades site offers information on in-demand trades, apprenticeship programs, and licensing requirements.

Step 3: Use Job Boards

Websites like Canada’s Job Bank and Indeed house numerous skilled trade postings. Refine your searches using keywords like “electrician,” “plumber,” or specific trade titles and location filters. Consider specific job boards like BuildForce Canada for construction-related trades.

Step 4: Explore Apprenticeship Opportunities

Many skilled trades require apprenticeship training, combining on-the-job experience with classroom learning. Government websites like the Canadian Apprenticeship Service list registered apprenticeship programs with participating employers.

Step 5: Network and Connect

Reach out to trade associations, unions, and training institutions. Attend job fairs or industry events to connect with hiring managers and learn about current trends. Contact established tradespeople in your desired field for informational interviews to gain valuable insights.

How Can I Work in an In-demand Skilled Trade Job in Canada?

Obtaining a Canada work permit is crucial to work in an in-demand skilled trade job in Canada. Canada offers various pathways for foreign workers to enter the skilled trades sector. Firstly, individuals must identify their trade and ensure it is listed under the NOC system. Next, securing a job offer from a Canadian employer is typically required, as it demonstrates a genuine need for your skills in the country.

The Canadian employer may need to obtain a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) to support your Canada work permit application, affirming that hiring a foreign worker will not negatively impact the local job market. Once the job offer and LMIA, if applicable, are secured, you can apply for a work permit through Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).

Depending on the NOC classification of your trade and the duration of your job offer, you may be eligible for a temporary work permit or, in some cases, a pathway to permanent residency through programs like the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) or Provincial Nominee Program (PNP).

Learn how to make sure your Canadian work permit application is complete.

How Can I Get Permanent Residency as a Skilled Tradesperson in Canada?


Skilled tradespeople are in high demand in Canada, and the Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP) offers a clear path to permanent residency in Canada. The FSTP prioritizes applicants with job offers in trades listed on the NOC system. You'll also need at least two years of recent work experience in your skilled trade and meet minimum language proficiency requirements in English or French.

Compared to other immigration programs, the FSTP offers a faster and more straightforward application process. You can submit your Express Entry profile with your FSTP qualifications, increasing your chances of receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residency. Gaining permanent residency through the FSTP allows you to live, work, and study permanently in Canada. You can also sponsor family members for immigration and eventually apply for Canadian citizenship.

FAQs

Which Skilled Trade Jobs Are Expected to Grow the Most in Canada?

Electricians, plumbers, HVAC technicians, and welders are expected to see the most growth in Canada due to ongoing infrastructure projects, renovations, and the retiring workforce.

What Are the Benefits of Pursuing a Skilled Trade Career in Canada?

Looking for a skilled trade career in Canada offers benefits such as competitive salaries, job stability, opportunities for advancement into supervisory or entrepreneurial roles, and the satisfaction of working with your hands to create tangible results.

How Can I Stay Updated With the Latest Trends and Job Opportunities in Skilled Trades?

Stay updated by following industry associations, subscribing to trade publications, networking with professionals in your field, attending trade shows and conferences, and regularly checking online job boards specific to your skilled trade.
