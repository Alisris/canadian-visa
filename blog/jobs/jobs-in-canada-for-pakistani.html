Jobs in Canada for Pakistani

by 
Kelia Losa

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: May 12th, 2023

Canada is a land of opportunities with a thriving job market that attracts job seekers worldwide. The Canadian job market has seen steady growth over the years and is projected to continue growing in the years to come.

The current state of the Canadian job market is stable, with an unemployment rate of around 6%. The key industries in Canada are diverse, including Information Technology, Healthcare, Engineering, Finance, Education, and more. These industries offer various job opportunities to individuals with different skill sets and experience levels. In addition, the Canadian government has been investing in innovation, which has led to the growth of emerging industries such as Artificial Intelligence, Clean Technology, and Aerospace.

The economic outlook for Canada is positive, with the country ranking high in economic stability and overall quality of life. Canada's robust economy is driven by strong consumer spending, business investment, and exports. The government has been implementing measures to attract foreign investors and create a business-friendly environment.

Working in Canada as a Pakistani can be an exciting opportunity. Canada values diversity and is a welcoming country to immigrants worldwide. Pakistanis can take advantage of several immigration programs, including the Federal Skilled Worker Program, International Student Program, and Provincial Nominee Programs, to gain entry into the Canadian job market.

Top 5 Jobs in Canada for Pakistani

There are various job opportunities available for Pakistanis in Canada. This section will discuss some of the top jobs for Pakistanis in Canada, their salary expectations, and job prospects.

Information Technology

The Information Technology (IT) industry is one of the fastest-growing industries in Canada. It is a dynamic and exciting industry that offers various job opportunities, including software developers, database administrators, and network administrators.

The average salary for a software developer in Canada is around $75,000 per year, while a database administrator can expect to earn around $85,000 per year. Network administrators can earn around $65,000 per year.

According to Indeed, there are over 40,000 job postings for IT-related positions in Canada.

Healthcare

The healthcare industry is another fast-growing in Canada, with a high demand for healthcare professionals. Various job opportunities are available in this industry, including doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and other healthcare professionals. The average salary for a registered nurse in Canada is around $80,000 annually, while a physician can expect to earn around $200,000 annually. Pharmacists can earn around $100,000 per year.

According to Indeed, there are over 25,000 jobs in Canada for healthcare-related positions.

Engineering

The engineering industry is another thriving industry in Canada, with a high demand for engineering professionals. Various job opportunities are available in this industry, including civil engineers, mechanical engineers, electrical engineers, and more.

The average salary for a civil engineer in Canada is around $85,000 per year, while a mechanical engineer can expect to earn around $90,000 per year. Electrical engineers can earn around $80,000 per year.

According to Indeed, there are over 15,000 job postings for engineering-related positions in Canada.

Finance

The finance industry is another well-established industry in Canada, with a high demand for finance professionals. Various job opportunities are available in this industry, including accountants, financial analysts, and more.

The average salary for an accountant in Canada is around $60,000 per year, while a financial analyst can expect to earn around $70,000 per year.

According to Indeed, there are over 10,000 job postings for finance-related positions in Canada.

Unskilled Jobs

Plenty of unskilled job opportunities are available in Canada, including in the retail and hospitality industries. These jobs typically require little to no experience or specific skill sets.

The average salary for unskilled jobs can vary widely depending on the industry and the specific job position. According to Talent.com, the average hourly wage for a retail sales associate is around $15, while the average hourly wage for a food service worker is around $14.

According to Indeed, there are over 100,000 job postings for unskilled jobs in Canada.

Various job opportunities are available for Pakistanis in Canada, including in the Information Technology, Healthcare, Engineering, Finance, and unskilled job industries. The salary expectations for each job vary widely depending on the industry and the specific job position. Pakistani job seekers can take advantage of the high demand for skilled and unskilled workers in Canada and explore the various job opportunities available.

Tips for Job Searching in Canada

Searching for a job in Canada can be daunting, especially for newcomers unfamiliar with the Canadian job market. However, several tips can make the job search process easier and more effective. Here are some tips for job searching in Canada:

Resume and Cover Letter Tips

Your resume and cover letter are your first impression to potential employers, so it's important to make them stand out. Customize your resume and cover letter for each job application to highlight your relevant skills and experience. Use keywords and phrases from the job posting to demonstrate that you have the qualifications they are looking for. Also, make sure to proofread your documents for spelling and grammar errors.

Networking Tips

Networking is an important part of the job search process in Canada. Contact friends, family, and colleagues to see if they know of any job openings or can introduce you to someone in your field. Attend industry events and job fairs to meet employers and make connections. You can also join professional associations or groups related to your industry to network with other professionals.

Job Search Websites

Several job search websites are available in Canada, including Indeed, LinkedIn, and Monster. These websites allow you to search for job postings based on location and industry. You can also set up job alerts to receive notifications when new job postings that match your criteria become available.

In addition to job search websites, there are government-run job search portals such as Job Bank, which provide a list of job openings across the country. 

Immigration Pathways for Pakistanis in Canada

If you're a Pakistani looking to immigrate to Canada, several programs can help you achieve your goal. Here are some of the immigration opportunities available for Pakistanis in Canada:

Federal Skilled Worker Program

The Federal Skilled Worker Program is a popular pathway for skilled workers who want to move to Canada permanently. To be eligible, you must have at least one year of skilled work experience, meet language requirements, and score at least 67 points on the Canadian Express Entry immigration points system. The points are awarded based on age, education, work experience, language proficiency, and adaptability.

International Student Program

The International Student Program is designed for international students who want to study in Canada and eventually stay there after graduation. To be eligible, a Canadian educational institution must accept you, have sufficient funds to pay for your tuition and living expenses, and meet other requirements such as health and security checks. After graduation, you may be eligible for a post-graduation work permit, which can allow you to gain valuable work experience in Canada.

Provincial Nominee Programs

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) are designed to help Canadian provinces and territories attract skilled workers who can meet specific labor market needs. Each province and territory has its PNP with unique eligibility requirements and application processes. To be eligible for a PNP, you must have skills, education, and work experience that meet the specific needs of the province or territory. Some PNPs require a job offer from a Canadian employer, while others may allow you to apply directly without a job offer.

Whether you are a skilled worker, an international student, or have other qualifications, there may be a pathway to achieve your goal of living and working in Canada. Consider exploring these immigration opportunities in detail to determine which is best for your situation.

If you're considering immigrating to Canada from Pakistan, seeking the help of a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC) can be beneficial. RCICs are licensed and regulated professionals authorized by the Canadian government to provide immigration advice and services. They can help you navigate the complex immigration system, assess your eligibility for immigration programs, and guide you through the application process. With their expertise and knowledge of immigration laws and policies, RCICs can help you increase your chances of success in your immigration journey.

FAQs

Can I Apply for a Job in Canada While Living in Pakistan?

Yes, you can apply for jobs in Canada while living in Pakistan. Many Canadian employers are open to hiring international candidates and may conduct interviews remotely via video conferencing or phone calls. However, it's important to note that you may need a work permit or visa before starting work in Canada.

Are There any Job Opportunities in Canada for Unskilled Workers?

Yes, there are job opportunities in Canada for unskilled workers, although they may be limited. Common jobs for unskilled workers in Canada include working in the hospitality, retail, or agriculture industries. However, these jobs may not pay as well as skilled jobs and may not offer the same benefits and opportunities for career advancement.

How Can I Improve my Chances of Finding a Job in Canada as a Pakistani?

There are several ways to improve your chances of finding a job in Canada as a Pakistani. One way is to network with people in your industry and attend job fairs and networking events. You can also tailor your resume and cover letter to the Canadian job market and apply for jobs that match your skills and experience. Finally, consider seeking the help of a job placement agency or a career coach who can help you navigate the job search process and provide valuable advice and guidance.
