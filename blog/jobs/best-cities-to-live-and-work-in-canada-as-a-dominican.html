Best Cities to Live and Work in Canada as a Dominican

by Robynn. Updated: August 26th, 2020

7.5 million Canadians and permanent residents are foreign born with more than one fifth of Canadians identifying as people of colour. By 2036, it is estimated that this total will increase to about 31 - 36% of the Canadian population. Canada is one of the most multicultural countries in the world, welcoming foreigners from every corner of the globe.

According to the Dominican Embassy Canada 2016 consensus, 23,130 Dominicans are already living and working in Canada but finding the best city for you is key before you apply for a visa to Canada from the Dominican Republic.

Although the cost of living in Canada is significantly higher than in the Dominican Republic, the quality of life and average annual income in Canada is much much higher. Canada offers free healthcare and education to all Canadian citizens and permanent residents and is one of the most safe and secure countries to live in. While the average annual salary in the Dominican Republic is $34,682, the national average salary in Canada is about $52,600. Some of the most popular provinces for Latin Americans include Ontario, Quebec, Alberta and British Columbia, but which cities have the most prominent Dominican communities and work opportunities? Let’s take a closer look, shall we.



Top 5 Cities to Immigrate to Canada from the Dominican Republic


Most Dominicans position themselves in four of Canada’s provinces: Ontario, Quebec, Alberta and British Columbia not only because of the prominent Latin American communities but because of the fantastic job opportunities and immigration programs.

Let’s take a look at the top 5 cities to live and work in Canada as well as how to apply for a visa to Canada from the Dominican Republic.

1. Toronto, Ontario


Ontario is one of the most popular destinations for Dominicans to relocate to in Canada. According to the 2016 census, 47% of the Latin American community in Canada live in Ontario, 31% of which are living in Toronto. Life in Toronto is full of hustle and bustle, beautiful parks and beaches as well as plenty of great opportunities if you intend to live and work in Canada. Although the cost of living is higher in Toronto than other Canadian cities, the wage more than makes up for it with most Torontonians earning around $134,000 per year, according to Salary Explorer. This, of course, depends on your level of experience and occupation. Top in demand jobs in Ontario, Canada include:

  • Tech Professions eg. web programmers, IT analysts, software programmers;
  • Medical specialist eg. cardiologists, pathologists, critical care medicine;
  • Managerial Jobs eg. sales, restaurant, marketing, construction;
  • Labourers, especially farm and construction workers;
  • Administrative assistants;
  • Educators especially early childhood educators;
  • Cooks and bakers;
  • Home and Child Caregivers especially au pairs or nannies;

Best Immigration Pathways to Ontario
Express Entry; or
Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)

2. Montreal, Quebec


Montreal is the Paris of Canada and is full of French culture, art and design and has been voted a one of the most liveable cities in the world. About 27% of Canadian Latinos live and work in Montreal. The average annual salary in Montreal is approximately $134,000, according to Salary Explorer, but this of course depends on your profession. Top in demand jobs in Quebec, Canada include:

  • Tech Professions eg. web developers, data scientist, software developers;
  • Dental hygienists;
  • Medical orderlies;
  • Managerial Jobs eg. restaurant, supermarket, construction;
  • Trade workers, especially carpenters and mechanics;
  • Administrative assistants eg. receptionists;
  • Educators especially early childhood educators, tutors and English second language teachers;
  • Cooks;
  • Home and Child Caregivers especially au pairs or nannies;

Best Immigration Pathways to Quebec
Quebec Skilled Worker Program; or
Quebec Experience Class

Please note that to apply for a visa to Quebec, Canada from the Dominican Republic you will have to apply directly to the province of Quebec as its system does not fall under the federal economic immigration system.

3. Vancouver, British Columbia


13% of Latin Americans choose to reside in British Columbia, 9% of whom live and work in Vancouver, Canada. Why? Because it is one of Canada’s tech hubs, is clean and safe, has some of the best schools in the country and has a phenomenal cultural scene including various galleries, museums and restaurants. Although it is one of the most expensive cities to live in Canada it also offers attractive salaries with most Vancouverites taking home an average salary of $129,000 per year, according to Salary Explorer. Top in demand jobs in Vancouver, Canada include:

  • Tech Professions eg. CAD technologists, database analysts, web & software developers;
  • Graphic designers;
  • Nurse practitioners & Registered nurses;
  • Managerial Jobs eg. supervisors, restaurant, office, sales, advertising and account managers;
  • Trade workers, especially carpenters, welders, mechanics and glaziers;
  • Barbers & Hairstylists;
  • Labourers, especially painters & construction workers;
  • Administrative assistants eg. bookkeeping;
  • Educators especially college instructors & early childhood educators;
  • Cooks and sous-chefs;
  • Home and Child Caregivers especially homecare and nannies;

Best Immigration Pathways to British Columbia
Express Entry; or
British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program ( BC PNP)

4. Calgary, Alberta


Calgary is one of the sunniest and most picturesque cosmopolitan cities in Canada. 9% of Latin American expats live and work in Alberta, Canada. Life in Calgary is fun filled: you and your family can enjoy plenty of outdoor activities such as visiting Kananaskis country and Dinosaur Provincial Park. It is one of the most affordable places to live and work in Canada, and you won't have to compromise your income. You can expect to earn about $126,000 annually, according to Salary Explorer, depending on your occupation. Top in demand jobs in Calgary, Canada include:

  • Tech Professions eg. user support technicians, web & software developers;
  • Graphic designers;
  • Managerial Jobs eg. analysts, business development, office, manufacturing & restaurant managers;
  • Truck and bus drivers ;
  • Trade workers, especially gas fitters, mechanics & glaziers ;
  • Labourers, especially foods processing & manufacturing, construction & landscape;
  • Administrative assistants eg. payroll, office administrators & bookkeepers;
  • Educators especially professors, early childhood educators & daycare assistants;
  • Cooks and bakers;
  • Home and Child Caregivers especially homecare and nannies;

5. Edmonton, Alberta


Life in Canada can't get any better than living and working in Edmonton. It’s nickname is Festival City, offering entertainment throughout the year, has a pleasant annual climate, with mild summers and winters, and is also extremely affordable. With 9% of Edmontonians originating from Latin America, you’ll be able to find many Spanish communities to join and can expect to earn an average salary of $125,000 per year, according to Salary Explorer, depending on your job. Some of the most in demand jobs in Edmonton, Canada include:

  • Managerial Jobs eg. restaurant, sales, property, logistics supervisors;
  • Labourers, especially home renovation & construction workers, carpenters, tile setters, metal workers and machinists;
  • Truck drivers;
  • Administrative assistants eg. office and bookkeepers;
  • Educators especially elementary & secondary school teachers;
  • Cooks and bakers;
  • Butchers;
  • Home & Child Caregivers especially aides, nannies & daycare workers;

Best Immigration Pathways to Alberta
Express Entry; or
Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP)
