Work as a Mechanic in Canada and Earn up to $100,000 Per Year

by 
Wesley Allan

The automotive industry is an essential part of the Canadian economy, and with the increasing number of vehicles on the road, mechanic jobs in Canada are in high demand. The country's advanced automotive infrastructure offers numerous opportunities to break into the industry or expand its experience. This comprehensive guide will discuss the qualifications and certifications required for mechanic jobs in Canada, the various types of positions available, salaries, working conditions, and much more.

As a mechanic in Canada, you'll have the opportunity to work on a vast range of vehicles, from small passenger cars to heavy-duty trucks and buses. This diverse experience can help you develop a highly sought-after skill set, leading to increased job opportunities and higher salaries. Moreover, the Canadian government has recognized the need for skilled mechanics. It has implemented several programs to attract international talent to help fill the gap.

If you're considering a career as a mechanic and are interested in working in Canada, read on to learn about the exciting world of mechanic jobs in Canada and how you can start your journey toward a rewarding career in the automotive industry.

What is Working as a Mechanic in Canada Like?

Working as a mechanic in Canada is an incredibly rewarding experience. Still, you must understand all the factors that affect your job as a mechanic in Canada.

Another hugely important aspect to consider is some of the unique aspects of Canadian work culture. They are as follows:

What Qualifications Do You Need to Work in Canada as a Mechanic?

To work as a mechanic in Canada, you must possess specific qualifications and certifications. These requirements vary depending on the province and the type of mechanic job you're seeking. Generally, you'll need a high school diploma or equivalent and specialized automotive repair and maintenance training. This training can be obtained through vocational schools, community colleges, or apprenticeship programs.

In most Canadian provinces, mechanics must obtain a trade certification, such as a Red Seal endorsement recognized nationwide. To become a certified mechanic, you must complete an apprenticeship program and pass a certification exam. The Red Seal endorsement allows you to work in any province or territory in Canada without additional certification requirements.

A valid driver's license is also essential, as you'll need to test-drive vehicles for your job duties. Depending on the type of mechanic job, you may need additional licenses or certifications, such as an air conditioning and refrigeration license or a commercial vehicle inspection certification. 

If you have prior qualifications, you may need to validate them with an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA).

What Kind of Mechanic Jobs in Canada Are There?

Several mechanic jobs are available in Canada, each with unique responsibilities, required skills, and National Occupational Classification (NOC) Codes. Some of the most common mechanic positions include:

Automotive Service Technician (NOC 72410)

This is the most common type of mechanic job in Canada. Automotive service technicians diagnose, repair, and maintain various types of vehicles, including cars, trucks, and motorcycles.

Diesel Mechanic (NOC 72420)

A diesel mechanic works on vehicles with diesel engines, including cars, trucks, buses, and heavy machinery.

Heavy Duty Equipment Mechanic (NOC 72401)

These mechanics repair and maintain large vehicles and equipment, such as construction machinery, farming equipment, and heavy trucks.

Industrial Instrument Technicians and Mechanics (NOC 22312)

These mechanics install, maintain, and repair industrial instruments, such as gauges, thermometers, and pressure sensors.

Motorcycle Mechanic (NOC 72423)

Motorcycle mechanics focus on repairing and maintaining motorcycles and other two-wheeled vehicles.

Aviation Mechanic (NOC 72404)

Aviation mechanics work on aircraft, diagnosing and repairing issues with engines, landing gear, and other systems.

Electrical Mechanics (NOC 72422)

These mechanics specialize in repairing and maintaining electrical systems in vehicles, including cars, trucks, and boats.

Regardless of the specific type of mechanic job you pursue, staying up-to-date on the latest industry trends, technologies, and best practices is essential. This will ensure your skills remain relevant and in demand in the ever-evolving automotive repair and maintenance world.

How Much Do Mechanics in Canada Make?

The salary for mechanic jobs in Canada can vary widely based on several factors, including experience, location, and specialization. According to Talent.com, entry-level mechanics can expect to earn approximately CAD 47,585, while experienced mechanics can earn up to CAD 76,404 annually. Those with specialized skills or certifications, such as a Red Seal endorsement, can command even higher salaries.

What are the Working Conditions and Benefits of Mechanic Jobs in Canada?

Mechanics in Canada typically work in repair shops, dealerships, or for companies that maintain their fleet of vehicles. Working conditions vary depending on the job, but most mechanics work indoors in well-lit, well-ventilated environments.

However, some positions may require working outdoors or in noisy, dirty conditions, particularly for those working on heavy equipment or in the aviation industry.

Besides a competitive salary, many mechanic positions in Canada offer benefits such as health and dental insurance, retirement savings plans, and paid time off. Additionally, mechanics working for large companies or dealerships may have access to ongoing training and development opportunities, leading to career advancement and increased earning potential.

With experience and additional training, mechanics can move into supervisory or management roles or even open repair shops.

Additionally, the high demand for skilled mechanics in Canada means that job security is vital and employment opportunities are plentiful.

Where Are Mechanics Most In-Demand in Canada?

Mechanic jobs are in demand across Canada, but certain provinces and cities have more opportunities. Some of the best places to find mechanic jobs in Canada include:

Ontario

As the most populous province and home to the largest city, Toronto, Ontario, offers many job opportunities for mechanics. The province is also a hub for automotive manufacturing, making it an ideal location for those looking to work in the industry.

Alberta

With a strong economy based on natural resources and many vehicles per capita, Alberta has a high demand for mechanics, particularly in cities like Calgary and Edmonton.

British Columbia

British Columbia's largest city, Vancouver, is a popular destination for mechanics due to its mild weather and bustling automotive scene.

Quebec

Home to Montreal, Quebec, offers numerous job opportunities for mechanics, particularly those bilingual in English and French.

How to Work in Canada as a Mechanic

To succeed in the Canadian mechanic job market, standing out from the competition and being proactive in your job search are essential. Here are some tips for success:

Step 1: Prepare for the Canadian Job Market

Before you start looking for work in Canada, it's vital to ensure you present the best version of yourself to potential employers. Here are a few ways you can do so.

Tailor Your Resume 

Ensure your resume is updated and tailored to the specific mechanic job you're applying for. Highlight your relevant skills, certifications, and experience to demonstrate your suitability for the position. If you're unsure how to get your resume to Canadian standards, speak to one of our representatives, who can help curate your resume to ensure you present yourself precisely the way Canadian employers want.

Prepare for Interviews

Research common interview questions for mechanic jobs and practice your answers. Be prepared to discuss your experience, skills, and certifications in detail. If you are unsure how your interview skills will be fair in Canada, schedule a consultation with one of our immigration professionals to learn about our job interview preparation course.

Stay Current on Industry Trends 

Staying informed about the latest automotive technologies and trends will help you remain competitive in the job market. Attend industry events, read trade publications, and participate in online forums to keep your knowledge up-to-date.

Network 

Building a strong network of industry professionals can open doors to job opportunities and provide valuable advice and resources. Attend trade shows, conferences, and networking events to connect with others in the automotive industry.

Be Willing to Relocate 

Depending on the job market in your desired location, you may need to be flexible and willing to relocate to find the best opportunities.

Step 2: Find a Job in Canada

Most pathways to Canada, Online and offline, are several ways to find mechanic jobs in Canada. Some of the most common methods include:

Online Job Boards

Websites like Indeed, Workopolis, and Monster are excellent resources for finding mechanic job postings in Canada. Create a profile and upload your resume so employers can easily find you.

Company Websites

Many large automotive companies and dealerships post job openings directly on their websites. Be sure to check these sites regularly for new opportunities.

Networking

Connecting with professionals in the automotive industry can be a valuable way to learn about job openings that may not be advertised publicly. Attend industry events, conferences, and trade shows to build your network and stay informed about the latest trends and job opportunities.

Trade Associations

Joining a trade association, such as the Automotive Industries Association of Canada, can provide access to job postings, networking opportunities, and valuable industry resources.

Apprenticeship Programs

If you're starting in the industry, an apprenticeship program can provide hands-on training and lead to a full-time mechanic job.

By using a combination of these methods, you'll increase your chances of finding a job in Canada.

Step 3: Find Your Pathway to Canada

Once you've landed your job in Canada, it's time to determine which immigration or visa pathway is best for your goals. This depends on your intentions and qualifications. The following are three popular ways to move to Canada as a mechanic.

Work Permit

If you're an international candidate looking to work as a mechanic in Canada, you'll need to apply for a work permit. Two types of work permits are available:

  • The Employer-specific Work Permit
    • This allows you to work for a specific employer in Canada
  • The Open Work Permit 
    • This allows you to work for any employer in Canada.

To apply for a work permit in Canada, you'll need to provide several documents, including:

  • Proof of Identity
  • A job offer from a Canadian employer, 
  • Proof of your qualifications and experience, and 
  • Proof of your ability to support yourself while in Canada.  
  • Medical exam results and 
  • A police certificate.

Learn more about how to get a work permit in Canada here.

The Federal Skilled Trades Program for Mechanics

The Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP) is a Canadian immigration program that allows skilled tradespeople, including mechanics, to apply for permanent residency in Canada. To be eligible for the program, you must have at least two years of work experience in a skilled trade and meet the minimum language requirements.

To apply for the program, you must submit an Express Entry profile and be invited to apply for permanent residency. The application process can take several months, but you can permanently live and work in Canada if approved.

Provincial Nominee Programs

In addition to the FSTP, several Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) offer opportunities for mechanics to immigrate to Canada. Each province has its own PNP, and the requirements and application process vary by location.

Some of the best PNPs for mechanics include:

  1. Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)
    • The OINP offers several pathways for skilled tradespeople, including the Skilled Trades Stream, designed specifically for those in the skilled trades, such as mechanics.
  2. Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP)
    • The AAIP offers a Skilled Worker Category for those in skilled trades, including mechanics. The program requires a job offer from an Alberta employer and a minimum of two years of experience in the trade.
  3. British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP)
    • The BC PNP offers several pathways for skilled tradespeople, including the Skilled Worker Category, designed for those in skilled trades, including automotive technicians and mechanics.
  4. Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program (SINP)
    • The SINP offers a Skilled Worker Category for those in skilled trades, including mechanics. The program requires a job offer from a Saskatchewan employer and a minimum of two years of experience in the trade.

Research each province's PNP to determine which program suits your skills and experience best. Finding your route to Canada can be complex, so it's recommended to seek the advice of an immigration lawyer or consultant to ensure you meet all the requirements and provide the necessary documentation. One such expert is an RCIC. An RCIC can help you ensure your eligibility for the work permit and help you gather and validate your documentation to ensure you get your work permit as quickly and easily as possible.

FAQs

What is the Overall Job Outlook for Mechanics in Canada? 

The job outlook for mechanics in Canada is positive, with a high demand for skilled workers in the automotive industry. The Canadian government has recognized the need for skilled tradespeople, including mechanics, and has implemented programs to attract international talent to fill the gap.

What Skills Are Required to Be a Successful Mechanic in Canada? 

Successful mechanics in Canada possess various technical and interpersonal skills, including strong problem-solving abilities, attention to detail, and excellent communication skills. Additionally, keeping up-to-date with the latest automotive technologies and trends is essential to remain competitive in the job market.

Get Your Career Into Gear in Canada

Mechanic jobs in Canada offer a challenging and rewarding career path for those with a passion for automotive repair and maintenance. With a robust job market, competitive salaries, and opportunities for career growth, the automotive industry in Canada is an excellent place to build your career.

To start your journey to Canada as a mechanic, research the qualifications and certifications required, explore the various types of mechanic jobs available, and be prepared to network and apply for jobs through various channels. Consider applying for a work permit or permanent residency through the FSTP or a PNP, and be prepared to relocate if necessary.

One of the best ways to ensure your process starts correctly is to have an experienced immigration professional like an RCIC guide you. They have a thorough understanding of Canada's visa system and can provide you with valuable advice and guidance. Using an RCIC can increase your chances of success in your immigration journey.
