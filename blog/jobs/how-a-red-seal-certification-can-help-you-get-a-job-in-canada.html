How a Red Seal Certification Can Help You Get a Job in Canada

by 
Kelia Losa

Author:

Kelia Losa

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

5 Min Read

You may or may not have heard of red seal certification, but did you know that it could help you get trade jobs in Canada and immigrate to Canada without a job offer!

What Is a Red Seal Certification?

The Red Seal Program, formally known as the Interprovincial Standards Red Seal Program, is a collaborative effort between the federal government and provinces/territories in Canada. It establishes common standards to evaluate the skills of tradespeople across the country. Tradespersons who pass the Red Seal examination receive an endorsement on their provincial/territorial trade certificate, indicating they meet the national standard for their trade.3

3 Reasons Why You Should Get Red Seal Certified

Trade people are a very important part of the Canadian economy. To make sure that the skill levels required to fill certain trade jobs in Canada are met, Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) developed standards for Red Seal Trades. Meeting these standards will help you in three ways.

1. It adds credibility

A Red Seal certificate validates your trade skills, demonstrating your expertise even if acquired outside Canada. This enhances your job prospects and facilitates immigration by ensuring your skills meet Canadian standards, requiring the completion of a four-year apprenticeship.

2. Open up job opportunities across Canada

With a Red Seal certificate, you become eligible to work anywhere in Canada without additional certifications in different provinces or territories.

3. Qualify for higher-paying jobs

Red Seal Endorsement (RSE) holders often earn higher salaries than non-certified trade workers. For example, RSE trade workers in certain trades can earn more than double the national average.

How to Get Red Seal Certified: A Step-by-Step Guide

To become Red Seal certified, follow these steps:

Step 1: Check if Your Trade Qualifies & Meets Requirements

Visit the official website of your province/territory to verify if your trade qualifies for the Red Seal Program. Each region has specific criteria, and it is crucial to ensure you meet them. Be thorough in understanding the requirements outlined by the respective provincial or territorial authority.

Step 2: Complete and Submit Your Application

Once you confirm eligibility, initiate the exam application process. Pay attention to the language preference (English or French) for the exam. Fill out the necessary forms, providing accurate and complete information. Ensure that you submit all required documentation and adhere to any additional guidelines specified by the relevant authority.

Step 3: Pay Your Fees

After successfully completing the application, confirm the cost of the Red Seal exam and the accepted payment methods. Fees can vary between provinces, so it is essential to understand the financial aspect of the certification process. Some provinces may offer financial support, such as apprenticeship loans or taxable cash grants, so explore these options as well.

By diligently following these steps, you can navigate the process of getting Red Seal certified in your specific trade.

Top Canadian Immigration Programs for Trade Workers

  • Federal Skilled Trades Program: Requires language proficiency, 2 years of work experience, and a job offer or certificate of qualification (Red Seal Certification).
  • Provincial Nominee Program (PNP): Each province has its PNP with different requirements aimed at filling specific labor gaps.
  • Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP): Targets labor shortages in Atlantic provinces (PEI, Nova Scotia, Newfoundland & Labrador, New Brunswick) through three streams.

FAQs About Red Seal Certification

Do You Need a Red Seal to Work in Canada?

Some trades in certain provinces require certification. Although not mandatory in all provinces, it can limit job opportunities.

How Do I Get a Red Seal Certificate Outside of Canada?

While you can apply for the exam from outside Canada, certification is granted only once you're in Canada.

What is the Difference Between a Red Seal and a Journeyman?

A Red Seal is an endorsement indicating compliance with federal standards, allowing you to work as a journeyman without supervision in Canada.
