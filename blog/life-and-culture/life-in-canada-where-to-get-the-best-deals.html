How Can I Move to Canada?


Express Entry

The Express Entry is an online immigration system that manages three federal programs for skilled immigrants coming to Canada as permanent residents. The three federal programs are as follows:

  1. Federal Skilled Workers Program
  2. Federal Skilled Trades Program
  3. Canadian Experience Class

The Express Entry system is known as one of the biggest entry points for Canadian immigration. For the year 2021, the government has set a quota of inviting 105,500 immigrants to work and live in Canada as permanent residents. This goal works hand-in-hand with the government wanting to welcome over 1.2 million foreigners by the end of 2023.

The Express Entry system has many benefits including being able to apply without having a job offer, having the entire immigration process taking as little as six months and be able to stay within the Express Entry pool if you arenâ€™t successful the first time.

How do I apply through the Express Entry system?

Once you have determined that you have met all Express Entry requirements, you may start with your online profile. Good to know, that once you start with the online profile, youâ€™ll have 90 days to complete and submit it. The following is needed to complete your profile:

  • Passport or travel document;
  • Education credential assessment report;
  • Language test results;
  • Certificate of qualification in a trade occupation issued by a Canadian province or territory (if you have one);
  • Proof of work experience(if you have); and
  • Provincial Nomination (if you have one)

After completing your online profile youâ€™ll be placed into the Express Entry pool along with other eligible candidates. Youâ€™ll be given a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score which determines your ranking, as well as if you will receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA). Receiving an ITA means that you now have the opportunity to apply for Canadian permanent residency. Each Express Entry draw has its own CRS score to qualify for.

What is a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)?


The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) is a points-based system used by the government, within the Express Entry system, to assess and score applicants. Which ultimately determines their ranking in the pool. This means the higher your CRS score the more likely you are to receive an ITA.

The maximum score you can achieve is 1,200 points, this is made up of two sections. The core set of 600 points and the additional set of 600 points as well. All applicantâ€™s scores are based on factors such as age, education, language proficiency and more.
