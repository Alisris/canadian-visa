Why Fall is a Good Time to Move to Canada

by 
Shireen Fisher

Author:

Shireen Fisher

BTech Degree in Journalism, Diploma in Journalism

Shireen Fisher is a widely-published writer with nine years of experience. She started her media career in radio before heading back to university to pursue her National Diploma and BTech Degree in Journalism.

. Updated: September 23rd, 2024
7 mins

As the leaves turn golden and the air grows crisp, Canada in autumn becomes an ideal destination for those looking to make a fresh start. Fall in Canada offers newcomers a unique blend of natural beauty and practical advantages. From the vibrant colors of the changing foliage to the cozy atmosphere of Canadian Thanksgiving, this season has a special charm that makes it the best time for many to come to this land of maple leaves.

Immigrating to Canada during autumn has several benefits that can make the transition smoother. The mild weather conditions allow for a comfortable move, while the start of the school year makes it easier for families to settle in. Job seekers may find more opportunities as businesses gear up for the holiday season. The fall festivals and events also provide a perfect chance to explore Canadian culture and make new connections. Whether planning to work in Canada or start a new chapter, this season offers a golden opportunity to begin your Canadian journey. We take a closer look at why Fall is a good time to move to Canada.

Mild Weather Conditions

Fall in Canada offers mild weather with comfortable temperatures, making it an ideal time for newcomers. With pleasant days and cool nights, the season provides a welcome break from Summer heat and Winter cold. This moderate climate is perfect for outdoor activities, allowing newcomers to enjoy Canada's beauty easily.

Comfortable Temperatures

Fall brings comfortable temperatures. In September, cities like Toronto enjoy pleasant weather around 60 degrees Fahrenheit. As the season progresses, temperatures gradually decrease, creating a perfect balance between warm days and cool nights.

Fall weather in Canada is neither too hot nor too cold, offering a reprieve from the extreme conditions of other seasons. This moderate climate influences daily life, allowing for a smoother transition for those new to the country. The lack of extreme weather also means fewer weather-related challenges for newcomers.

Ideal for outdoor activities

The mild autumn weather impacts outdoor activities, making it an excellent time to explore Canada's natural beauty. With comfortable temperatures and less humidity, newcomers can enjoy a variety of fun activities, including:

  • Hiking,
  • Biking,
  • Strolling through colorful landscapes

Preparation for Winter

Fall in Canada offers a valuable opportunity for newcomers to prepare for the harsh winter months ahead. As temperatures gradually drop, it allows time to acclimate to the colder weather, set up essential utilities, and winter-proof homes. By taking these steps in advance, you can ensure a smoother and more comfortable transition into the Canadian winter.

Time to Adjust Before Harsh Weather

Autumn allows newcomers in Canada to adjust gradually to colder temperatures. This period influences the body's ability to adapt to the climate. As the weather cools, immigrants can slowly build tolerance to lower temperatures, making the transition to winter less shocking. This time allows for experimentation with layering clothing and finding the right balance of warmth and comfort before the harsh winter arrives.

Setting up Utilities

Fall has an impact on utility preparations for winter. Setting up essential services like electricity and heating before the cold sets in is crucial. You must contact utility providers at least two weeks before moving in to ensure a smooth transition. This process may involve credit checks and potential deposits. Some utilities might be included in rent, so clarifying responsibilities with landlords or property owners is essential.

Winter-proofing Your New Home

Preparing a home for winter influences comfort and safety. You must check for drafts around windows and doors, sealing any gaps to prevent heat loss. Inspecting and cleaning the furnace, changing air filters, and ensuring proper insulation in the attic are essential steps. It's also important to prepare for potential power outages by having flashlights, batteries, and candles on hand. These preparations help create a cozy environment in which to enjoy the Canadian winter.

Lower Moving Costs

Fall in Canada gives newcomers the advantage of lower moving costs, as it falls during the off-peak season. With reduced demand, moving companies often provide more affordable rates and greater availability, allowing for more flexible planning.

Off-peak Season Rates

Fall impacts moving costs, making it an attractive time to relocate to Canada. Moving companies often offer more competitive rates as the summer rush winds down. This price decrease is due to reduced demand for moving services during autumn. As a newcomer in Canada, you can take advantage of these off-peak season rates to save money on your relocation expenses.

More Availability With Moving Companies

The autumn season also brings increased availability with moving companies. With fewer people relocating during this time, you will have more flexibility in moving dates. This increased availability allows for better planning and smoother transitions for those immigrating to Canada.

Less Competition for Housing

During this season, newcomers in Canada have a distinct advantage when searching for housing, as there is less competition compared to the busy summer months. With fewer people looking for homes and a rise in available listings, the fall season provides more options and better opportunities to find suitable accommodation. Landlords are often more open to negotiations, allowing you to secure favorable lease terms and pricing.

Reduced Summer Rush

When you move to Canada during the Fall season, you'll find a decrease in housing competition. The summer real estate rush often fails to materialize, with sales remaining subdued and inventory rising. This trend influences the rental market, creating opportunities for newcomers. In Vancouver, August sales are known to be lower than July, while the Greater Toronto Area is known to show only a tiny increase. This reduced activity impacts housing options, making fall an ideal time for immigrants to find accommodation.

More Options to Choose From

Fall influences housing availability in Canada. Active listings have increased significantly, with some areas seeing jumps of up to 46% year-over-year. This surge in inventory impacts pricing, with some regions experiencing price drops. For newcomers, this means more diverse options and greater negotiating power. The increased supply allows immigrants to explore various housing types, from condos to family-sized properties, making finding a suitable home in their desired location easier.

Time to Negotiate Leases

The autumn season has an impact on lease negotiations. With more unit vacancies in the market, landlords are often more open to discussion. This timing allows you to secure better deals on long-term leases. It's advisable to research local rental averages and approach negotiations with confidence. Even a small reduction in rent can lead to significant savings over time, making fall an advantageous period for you to settle into your new Canadian home.

Job Market Opportunities

Autumn in Canada presents valuable job market opportunities for newcomers as companies ramp up hiring after the summer lull. With increased job postings across various industries, it's an ideal time to apply for positions. Fall offers numerous networking events to connect with professionals and explore job openings. Securing a role in the Fall also allows time to settle in before the holiday slowdown, allowing one to establish routines and prove oneself in a new job.

Learn more about in-demand jobs in Canada.

Post-summer Hiring Season

As the summer slowdown ends, many companies ramp up their hiring efforts. This post-summer period often sees an increase in job postings across various industries. You can definitely can take advantage of this surge in employment opportunities. It's an ideal time to apply for positions as businesses gear up for the busy Fall and Winter seasons.

Networking Events

Autumn in Canada brings a wealth of networking events. These gatherings have an impact your chances of finding employment. From tech meetups to industry-specific conferences, Fall offers numerous opportunities to connect with professionals and potential employers. You can attend these events to expand their network, learn about job openings, and gain insights into the Canadian work culture.

Time to Settle Before Holiday Slowdown

The fall season provides a window to settle into new roles before the holiday period. This timing has an influence on job stability for newcomers. Starting a job in autumn allows time to get used to the work environment and establish routines. It also offers an opportunity to prove yourself before the typical year-end performance reviews, potentially leading to a stronger position within the company.

Easier School Transitions

The Fall is an ideal time for newcomers to transition into the Canadian education system, as it marks the start of the academic year. This timing allows students to begin their studies alongside their peers, making it easier for them to integrate into Canadian schools and universities. Starting in the Fall provides a smoother adaptation for school-age children, while university students benefit from a broader range of programs and financial aid opportunities. Overall, Fall offers a seamless entry point into Canada's educational landscape.

Start of Academic Year

Fall impacts school transitions for newcomers to Canada. The September intake is the primary admission period for Canadian universities and colleges. This timing aligns with the start of the academic year, making it easier for students to begin their educational journey. 

Smoother Integration for Children

The fall season offers a chance for smoother integration of children into Canadian schools. As classes commence, newcomer students can start on equal footing with their peers. This timing allows children to adapt to the new environment, make friends, and become part of the school community from the outset. Canadian schools often have programs to support English learners, helping them catch up academically within a few years of arrival.

University Enrollment Timing

For those seeking higher education, the fall intake is highly preferred in Canada. Canadian universities offer a wide range of programs during this period, providing more options for international students. The autumn start allows students ample time to settle in and adjust to the academic environment before winter arrives. This timing often aligns with scholarship applications and other financial aid opportunities for newcomers pursuing their studies here.

Time to Establish Routines

As the leaves paint the landscape in vibrant hues, you'll find the perfect opportunity to settle into their new home. With milder weather, it's the ideal time to establish your necessities, stock up on essentials, and explore the neighborhood. Join in local community events and gatherings to forge new friendships and find your place in Canada's vibrant tapestry before the winter's chill sets in.

Setting up Before Winter Holidays

Fall in Canada allows you to settle in before the winter holidays. This period has influenced the creation of a comfortable living space. It's an ideal time to set up utilities and ensure functional heating systems. You can also stock up on winter essentials during end-of-season sales, saving money on necessities.

Exploring Neighborhoods

Autumn's mild weather has an impact on neighborhood exploration. New residents can use comfortable temperatures to familiarize themselves with their surroundings. This is an opportunity to discover local amenities, parks, and community centers. Exploring during Fall allows newcomers to experience the beauty of Canadian autumn while getting to know their new home.

Building Local Connections

Fall influences community engagement. As activities resume after summer, newcomers can join local events and meet neighbors. This season provides chances to build connections before winter sets in. Participating in fall festivals and community gatherings helps immigrants integrate into their new Canadian community, fostering a sense of belonging.

Fall Festivals and Events


This season of rust tones is filled with exciting festivals and events that allow newcomers to immerse themselves in local traditions and cultures. From the warmth of Thanksgiving gatherings to the spooky fun of Halloween, it offers something for everyone. Whether enjoying traditional foods, taking part in parades, or exploring haunted attractions, you'll have ample opportunities to celebrate Canada's diverse and festive spirit during the fall season.

Thanksgiving Celebrations

Canadian Thanksgiving, which is celebrated on the second Monday of October, has a significant influence on fall festivities. This holiday has its roots in expressing gratitude for safe voyages and bountiful harvests. Families gather to enjoy traditional meals, which vary by province.

A Jigg's Dinner is popular in Newfoundland, while Ontario savors sweet butter tarts. Across Canada, spicy pumpkin pie is a common dessert. The holiday also features parades, with the Kitchener-Waterloo Oktoberfest Thanksgiving Day Parade being the largest, showcasing over 120 floats.

Halloween Activities

Halloween in Canada brings a variety of spooky events. Haunted attractions like Fright Nights at Playland in Vancouver and Haunt Manor in Niagara Falls offer thrilling experiences. For families, the Haunted Pumpkin Festival at Prairie Gardens in Alberta provides over 50 activities, including scarecrow-making workshops and ghost trains. Cities across Canada host Halloween parades, costume contests, and pumpkin carving events, creating a festive atmosphere for newcomers to enjoy.

Cultural Festival

Fall in Canada has an impact on cultural celebrations. The Celtic Colors International Festival in Nova Scotia showcases Celtic music and culture through concerts and community events. The Mont Sutton Fall Festival celebrates the stunning fall foliage in Quebec with guided hikes, jazz music, and chairlift rides. The Fall Okanagan Wine Festival in British Columbia offers wine tastings, special dinners, and awards, highlighting the region's renowned vineyards and harvest. These diverse festivals allow newcomers to experience Canada's rich cultural tapestry during autumn.

FAQs

Can I work in Canada While I Wait for My Canadian Permanent Residency Application to be Processed?

Yes, you can apply for a Bridging Open Work Permit (BOWP), which will allow you to work in Canada legally while you await processing. This permit is available to certain permanent residence applicants already in Canada.

I'm Preparing to Study in Canada This Fall. Will I be Able to Work as an International Student in Canada?
