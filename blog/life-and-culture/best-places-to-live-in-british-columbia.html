Best Places to Live in British Columbia

by 
Wesley Allan

Author:

Wesley Allan

Bachelor's Degree in English Language and Literature and Film Studies

Wesley Allan has been writing professionally for over five years and about immigration for a year. With a BA in English Language and Literature and Film Studies.

. Updated: April 25th, 2023

British Columbia (BC), the westernmost province in Canada, is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse culture, and thriving economy. From the towering peaks of the Rocky Mountains to the lush rainforests of the Pacific Coast, British Columbia offers a unique and unparalleled living experience. With over 5 million people, British Columbia is home to diverse cultures, languages, and backgrounds. This article will explore what makes a living in British Columbia so special and introduce you to the top 10 cities for a high-quality life in this beautiful province.

The province is not only recognized for its breathtaking landscapes but also for its robust and stable economy. Various industries drive British Columbia's economy, including natural resources, tourism, technology, and film production. As a result, the province offers a wide range of job opportunities for locals and newcomers, making it an ideal place to live and work.

But what makes a city a great place to live? First, let's dive into the factors that contribute to the quality of life in a city, and then we'll introduce you to the top 10 cities in British Columbia.

Several factors come into play when determining the quality of life in a city. These factors include affordability, access to quality education and healthcare, a strong job market, a low crime rate, and a vibrant cultural scene. Additionally, access to outdoor recreational activities and a clean environment are essential for a healthy and happy life.

In British Columbia, you will find cities that meet these criteria and more. The province offers a perfect balance between urban and rural living, providing residents with both the excitement of city life and the tranquility of nature. Finding the right one for you is vital. To help, here are 10 of the best cities in BC.

The 10 Best Places to Live in British Columbia

Vancouver

Vancouver, the largest city in British Columbia, is consistently ranked among the top cities in the world for its quality of life. Known for its stunning natural surroundings, Vancouver boasts a mild climate, beautiful parks, and abundant outdoor activities. The city also has a diverse and thriving cultural scene, with numerous art galleries, museums, and theaters.

The job market in Vancouver is strong, with opportunities in various industries such as technology, film production, and natural resources. The city also offers excellent educational institutions and a top-notch healthcare system. However, it's worth noting that Vancouver has a high cost of living, particularly regarding housing prices.

Victoria

Victoria, the capital city of British Columbia, is known for its charming architecture, beautiful gardens, and mild climate. Located on Vancouver Island, Victoria offers a relaxed pace of life, with easy access to outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and whale watching. The city has a strong economy, with hospitality, technology, tourism, and government job opportunities. Victoria has Canada's warmest and mildest climates, making it a great place to enjoy the beaches and outdoor activities. It is perfect as Victoria is surrounded by natural beauty, making it a beautiful place to call home.

The cost of living in Victoria is more affordable than in Vancouver, making it an attractive option for many people looking to move to British Columbia.

Victoria is a safe and family-friendly community, making it a great place to raise a family. If you love the outdoors, nature, and a laid-back lifestyle, Victoria is a great place to live. It has a balanced age demographic, great education, and a friendly and fun city. Victoria is also a British-feel city, making it a unique place to live in Canada.

Kelowna

Nestled in the heart of the Okanagan Valley, Kelowna is a picturesque city surrounded by vineyards, orchards, and lakes. The city is known for its sunny climate, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Kelowna's economy is driven by agriculture, wine production, and tourism, providing residents various job opportunities.

Kelowna is a vibrant city with a unique blend of urban and outdoor living, offering a kind and united culture, excellent schools, healthcare facilities, and diverse neighborhoods filled with exciting activities, incredible views, and delicious food. The cost of living in Kelowna is more affordable than in Vancouver and Victoria, making it an attractive option for families and young professionals.

Whistler

Whistler, a world-renowned ski resort town, is just a short drive from Vancouver. Known for its stunning alpine scenery, outdoor recreational opportunities, stunning landscape, and pedestrian-only village, Whistler is a popular destination for tourists and residents. The town's economy is primarily driven by tourism, with hospitality, retail, and outdoor recreation job opportunities.

The cost of living in Whistler can be high, particularly for housing, but many residents find the benefits of living in this beautiful mountain town to be well worth the expense. 

Nanaimo

Located on the east coast of Vancouver Island, Nanaimo is a beautiful city known for its picturesque harbor and vibrant downtown area. With waterfront views, scenic parks, and beaches, Nanaimo is surrounded by nature and beauty. Additionally, Nanaimo's climate is mild throughout the year, making it comfortable and comparable to Mediterranean weather. This makes it perfect for those who love the outdoors, as the city offers residents numerous beaches, parks, and outdoor recreational opportunities. Nanaimo also has a range of job opportunities with a strong economy driven by industries such as forestry, technology, and tourism.

The city's central location and vigorous growth make it an affordable option. 

Fort St. John

Located in the Peace River Country of northeastern British Columbia, Fort St. John is a small city known for its natural beauty and strong economy. The city offers excellent career opportunities, especially in industries like oil and gas, with the highest household income in the province. Fort St. John offers a range of job opportunities in these fields, as well as in healthcare, education, and government. 

The city is known for its wildlife and warm summers, making it an excellent place for nature enthusiasts and those who enjoy outdoor activities. The community is also safe and welcoming, with friendly people who make it easy to build connections and feel at home. This allows residents to spend less on surviving and enjoy a higher quality of life. Also, the chance to experience the Northern Lights is another unique aspect of life in Fort St. John.

The cost of living in Fort St. John is more affordable than in larger cities like Vancouver, making it an attractive option for those looking for a quieter, more affordable lifestyle. 

Squamish

Located halfway between Vancouver and Whistler, Squamish is a small town known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities. The town is known for its world-class rock climbing, hiking, and mountain biking trails. With lakes like Brohm Lake, Alice Lake, and Cat Lake, you can enjoy swimming, paddling, and other water sports. The region also boasts diverse wildlife, making it a prime location for nature enthusiasts.

Squamish's stunning views can be experienced with the Sea to Sky Gondola, which provides backcountry trail access. In addition, the town has a vibrant craft beer scene, excellent dining options, and scenic flights offered by Sea to Sky Air. Moreover, Squamish is a year-round destination with activities like snowshoeing, backcountry skiing, and snowboarding available in the winter.

The town's economy is driven by industries such as tourism, outdoor recreation, and film production, providing a range of job opportunities for residents.

The town's proximity to Vancouver and Whistler makes it an attractive option for those looking for a quieter, more affordable lifestyle while still within easy reach of larger cities.

Kamloops

Located in the Thompson River Valley, Kamloops is a small city known for its balanced lifestyle, warm climate, and over 2,000 hours of sunshine annually. The city is surrounded by mountains, lakes, and rivers, making it an ideal location for outdoor enthusiasts. Kamloops's diverse economy offers great careers in financial services, technology, forestry, agriculture, and tourism. 

Penticton

In the Okanagan Valley, sandwiched between Okanagan Lake to the north and Skaha Lake to the south, Penticton is a small city known for its mild climate and outdoor recreational opportunities. It is a vibrant and growing city that offers something for everyone, from young professionals to families to retirees. The city is also becoming a hub for independent workers who value an active lifestyle with more flexibility. The city's economy is driven by industries such as wine production, agriculture, tourism, trades, service, forestry, and mining sectors, providing residents with various job opportunities.

Prince George

Prince George, British Columbia, is a mid-sized city in central BC with around 80,000 people. There are several reasons why someone might consider moving to Prince George. 

Firstly, living costs are lower than in other cities in British Columbia, such as Whistler, and people earn a higher-than-average income. As a result, buying a home in Prince George's is realistic, and nicer and newer homes sell for a small fraction of prices in other parts of Canada. 

The city is safe and has an embracing community, making it an excellent location for skilled immigrants. In addition, there are many opportunities for career advancement in Prince George, making it an excellent location for experienced professionals or those starting in the working world.

As a result, the city is seeing more immigrants. Its economy is experiencing steady growth with an influx of new businesses in the community. The city's economy is primarily driven by industries such as forestry, mining, and healthcare, providing residents various job opportunities.

Finally, the city offers a blend of all the amenities of a large city with the charm of a small town. Despite its growing economy, forests, lakes, and rivers surround Prince George, with multiple walking and hiking trails, making it an excellent location for nature lovers.

If you feel that one of these cities may be the future home you've been looking for, here are a few things you should know about British Columbia before you start your move.

To start your journey to a bright life in BC, you must be prepared. One of the best ways to start is to ensure you know which of BC's many immigration programs suits you. These programs differ based on eligibility, intentions, and the province's needs. The best way to find your pathway to Canada is to utilize the experience and expertise of a certified immigration professional like a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC). An RCIC can fully evaluate you to help you ensure you know exactly the program where you would have the highest chance of success.

How to Immigrate to British Columbia

Moving to British Columbia can be an exciting and life-changing experience. However, the immigration process can be complex and overwhelming. Fortunately, there are several immigration programs available to help you move to British Columbia.

Express Entry

Express Entry is a federal immigration program that allows skilled workers to immigrate to Canada. To apply for Express Entry, you must create an online profile and provide information about your skills, work experience, education, and language ability. If you meet the eligibility criteria, you will be entered into a pool of candidates and assigned a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score based on your profile.

Candidates with the highest CRS scores can apply for permanent residency through regular draws. Once you receive an invitation to apply, you will have 90 days to submit a complete application. If you want to find out what your CRS score may be, here's our CRS Calculator.

British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP)

The British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP) is a program that allows the province of British Columbia to nominate candidates for permanent residency. The program has several categories, including the Skills Immigration category, the Express Entry BC category, and the Entrepreneur Immigration category for the different types of workers required in BC.

To apply for the BC PNP, you must create an online profile and provide information about your skills, work experience, education, and language ability. You may be invited to apply for nomination if you meet the eligibility criteria. Once you receive a nomination, you can apply for permanent residency through the federal government. Learn more about the BC PNP and how you can apply here.

Business Immigration

British Columbia offers several programs for entrepreneurs and investors who want to start or invest in a business in the province. These programs include the Entrepreneur Immigration stream of the BC PNP and the Start-Up Visa program.

You must meet specific eligibility criteria for your business experience, net worth, and investment plans to apply for these programs. You can receive a work permit and eventually apply for permanent residency if approved. Learn more about Canada's business immigration streams here. 

FAQs

What are the Education Options in British Columbia?

British Columbia offers a range of educational options, including public schools, private schools, and international schools. The province's high-quality education system focuses on student-centered learning and experiential education. British Columbia is home to some of Canada's top Designated Learning Institutions (DLIs) like the University of British Columbia, the British Columbia Institute of Technology, and Simon Fraser University.

What are the Healthcare Options in British Columbia?

British Columbia's publicly-funded healthcare system provides residents access to essential medical services. The province also has various private healthcare options for those wanting additional services. Learn more about healthcare in Canada here.
