Discover the Hidden Gems to Explore During Summer in Canada

by 
Shireen Fisher
×

Author:

Shireen Fisher

BTech Degree in Journalism, Diploma in Journalism

Shireen Fisher is a widely-published writer with nine years of experience. She started her media career in radio before heading back to university to pursue her National Diploma and BTech Degree in Journalism.

. Updated: June 21st, 2024
7 min

Canada, with its vast landscapes and vibrant cities, is a dream destination for many. But for newcomers to Canada, or those seeking an escape from the well-trodden tourist path, uncovering unique experiences can be a challenge. Summer in Canada, however, unlocks a treasure trove of hidden gems across the country, offering something special for every adventurer.

This blog is your guide to exploring the lesser-known corners of Canada during the warm summer months. Whether you're a seasoned traveler or a newcomer settling into your new home, get ready to discover unforgettable experiences that will make you fall in love with Canada all over again.

Unveiling Canada's Summer Secrets

Summer in Canada is a season of vibrant energy. Long, sunny days melt away into warm evenings, perfect for outdoor adventures. National parks come alive with hikers and campers, while lakes and beaches call for refreshing dips. Cities host bustling street festivals and vibrant cultural events, showcasing the diversity of the nation.

But beyond the familiar tourist spots, a world of surprises awaits. Imagine exploring a secluded island haven, untouched by crowds, or embarking on a wildlife safari in a lesser-known national park. Perhaps you crave a unique cultural experience, attending a traditional summer festival or digging into the rich history of a forgotten town.

This summer, ditch the typical travel itinerary and embark on a journey to discover Canada's hidden gems.

Hiking, Camping, and Wildlife Encounters

Canada's wilderness boasts some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the world. Summer is the perfect time to explore this natural wonderland.

Hiking Adventures

For the intrepid explorer, consider venturing beyond Banff National Park's iconic trails. Hike the challenging yet rewarding Sigurd Olson National Park Lookout Trail on Isle Royale in Lake Superior, Ontario, offering panoramic views of the world's largest freshwater lake.

Alternatively, trek through the Náyar National Park in Manitoba, known for its rare orchid species and abundant birdlife. For a truly unique experience, tackle the Whistler Peak Trail in British Columbia, a challenging climb that rewards you with stunning vistas of the Coast Mountains and glaciers.

Camping Bliss

Imagine waking up to the call of loons or the gentle crash of waves on the shore. Pitch your tent in the heart of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve in British Columbia, where you can camp under the Milky Way and explore the ecological wonders of the region.

Alternatively, explore the Kejimkujik National Seaside Park in Nova Scotia, famous for its white-sand beaches, towering old-growth forests, and opportunities to kayak through hidden coves. For a truly remote experience, consider backpacking through Gros Morne National Park in Newfoundland, where you can camp beside dramatic fjords and hike amongst towering cliffs.

Wildlife Encounters

For animal lovers, Canada offers a chance to witness incredible creatures in their natural habitat. Embark on a whale-watching tour in Churchill, Manitoba, where beluga whales gather by the thousands in the summer months. Or, take a wildlife boat tour in Tadoussac, Quebec, where you can spot humpback whales, minke whales, and even the occasional fin whale.

You can also travel to Riding Mountain National Park in Manitoba for a chance to see majestic black bears and playful otters in their natural habitat.

Pristine Beaches and Coastal Charm

Canada's vast coastline is dotted with idyllic islands, each offering a unique escape. Here's a glimpse into some hidden gems waiting to be discovered.

East Coast Paradise

Canada's east coast is a treasure trove of island paradises, each offering a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and charming communities. Here, we set sail on a journey to discover some of the hidden gems gracing the country’s shores.

Magdalen Islands, Quebec

A small archipelago in the Gulf of St. Lawrence, the Magdalen Islands enchant visitors with their dramatic red cliffs, rolling green hills, and charming fishing villages. Explore the rich Acadian culture, indulge in the freshest seafood (especially those famous lobster rolls!), and soak up the sunshine on the islands' beautiful beaches.

Bribie Island, Nova Scotia

Step back in time on Bribie Island, known for its historic lighthouse, charming fishing shacks, and abundant birdlife. Hike along scenic trails, explore the island's rich history, and witness breathtaking sunsets over the Atlantic Ocean.

Fogo Island, Newfoundland and Labrador

For a truly unique experience, head to Fogo Island, known for its dramatic landscape, colorful fishing villages, and innovative artistic community. Hike the island's rugged coastline, explore the unique geology, and stay in a one-of-a-kind "shorefast" cottage – a modern take on a traditional Newfoundland fishing stage.

West Coast Wilderness

Canada's west coast is a realm of rugged beauty, where ancient forests meet the Pacific Ocean. Here, we explore a selection of islands that offer adventure, cultural immersion, and breathtaking scenery.

Salt Spring Island, British Columbia

Located in the Salish Sea, Salt Spring Island is a haven for artists, foodies, and nature lovers. Explore art studios and galleries, sample fresh produce at local farmers markets, and hike through lush forests or kayak the island's scenic coastline.

Mayne Island, British Columbia

Escape the crowds and discover the tranquility of Mayne Island. Hike or bike through rolling hills dotted with vineyards and orchards, relax on secluded beaches, and enjoy the island's laid-back atmosphere.

Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve, British Columbia

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Gwaii Haanas offers a true wilderness adventure. Kayak through mist-laden inlets, explore ancient Haida Gwaii archeological sites, and marvel at the abundant wildlife, including majestic bears, playful dolphins, and soaring eagles.

This is just a taste of the many island paradises waiting to be explored on Canada's coasts. So pack your swimsuit, grab your sense of adventure, and set sail for an unforgettable summer island hopping adventure.

Unique Festivals and Events Celebrating Canada's Diverse Culture

Unique Festivals and Events: Celebrate Canada's Diverse Culture Summer in Canada explodes with vibrant festivals and events, showcasing the rich tapestry of cultures woven into the nation's fabric. From indulging in culinary delights to celebrating music and history, there's an event waiting to ignite your senses.

Calgary Stampede, Alberta

This ten-day extravaganza isn't just about rodeo thrills. Dive into a global food fair, where international vendors tempt your taste buds with flavors from around the world. Don't miss Alberta's own iconic dishes, from sizzling steak to juicy Saskatoon berry pie./

Poutine Festival, Drummondville, Quebec

Poutine, a Canadian comfort food masterpiece of fries, cheese curds, and gravy, takes center stage at this unique festival. Sample countless variations, from classic to adventurous, and crown your favorite poutine creation the champion.

Taste of Niagara, Niagara-on-the-Lake, Ontario

Held amidst the picturesque vineyards of Niagara, this ten-day culinary celebration is a paradise for gourmands. Sample exquisite creations from top chefs, savor local wines, and enjoy live music under the summer sky.

Toronto International Jazz Festival, Toronto, Ontario

Music lovers rejoice! This world-renowned festival attracts top jazz musicians from around the globe. Immerse yourself in electrifying performances across various stages, transforming Toronto into a vibrant jazz haven.

Winnipeg Folk Festival, Winnipeg, Manitoba

Embark on a journey through global music at the Winnipeg Folk Festival. This multicultural extravaganza features hundreds of artists representing diverse genres, from traditional folk to contemporary world music. Celebrate the unifying power of music in a welcoming and inclusive atmosphere.

Celtic Colors, Cape Breton, Nova Scotia

Immerse yourself in the rich Celtic heritage of Cape Breton at this renowned festival. Live music fills the air as talented fiddlers, pipers, and singers showcase their artistry. Witness the passion and energy of Celtic music and dance performances that will leave you wanting more.

Festival of New France, Quebec City, Quebec

Travel back in time to Quebec City's colonial era at this captivating historical reenactment. Explore a recreated 17th-century village bustling with costumed actors portraying artisans, soldiers, and everyday people. Immerse yourself in the sights, sounds, and atmosphere of New France and gain a deeper appreciation for the city's rich history.

Shakespeare by the Sea, St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador

Set against the stunning backdrop of the Atlantic Ocean, Shakespeare by the Sea offers a unique take on the Bard's timeless plays. Enjoy open-air performances under the summer sky and experience the power of Shakespeare's words in a breathtaking natural setting.

Gold Rush Festival, Whitehorse, Yukon

Relive the excitement of the Klondike Gold Rush at this lively festival. Pan for gold, participate in historical reenactments, and soak up the spirit of this bygone era. Whitehorse transforms into a bustling gold rush town, complete with costumed characters and gold rush-themed activities.

Planning Your Canadian Summer Adventure

Planning Your Canadian Summer Adventure: Unforgettable Memories Await With Canada's vast landscapes and diverse experiences, planning your perfect summer adventure can be overwhelming. But fear not! Here's a guide to transform your dream into reality, ensuring you discover the hidden gems that resonate most with you.

Interests Take Center Stage

What sparks your travel fire? Are you an adrenaline-seeking hiker craving mountain vistas, or a culture vulture yearning for historical immersion? Perhaps a relaxing beach escape with fresh seafood is your ideal summer getaway. Identifying your interests will guide you towards the hidden gems that align perfectly with your travel dreams.

Research is Your Ally

Dive into the treasure trove of online resources and travel blogs to discover hidden gem destinations. Consider regional tourism websites and social media communities dedicated to exploring Canada's lesser-known wonders. Don't be afraid to get specific – search for keywords that reflect your interests, like "backpacking trails in Nova Scotia" or "kayaking with whales in British Columbia."

Accessibility Matters

Once you've shortlisted your hidden gems, consider travel time and accessibility. Are they easily reached by car, train, or plane? If public transportation is limited, renting a car might be the best option for ultimate flexibility, especially for exploring remote locations. Factor in travel time between destinations to ensure a smooth flow for your itinerary.

Embrace Local Gems

While exploring lesser-known destinations, consider incorporating nearby towns and villages into your itinerary. This allows you to experience the authentic charm of local communities and uncover hidden gems beyond the main attractions. Explore local farmers markets for fresh produce and unique souvenirs, or visit a family-run restaurant for a taste of regional cuisine.

Pack for Adventure (and Unexpected Weather)

Canada's summer offers warm days, but evenings can get surprisingly cool. Pack layers of clothing to adapt to changing temperatures. Comfortable hiking shoes or sandals are essential, along with a rain jacket and waterproof gear in case of unexpected showers. Don't forget sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to shield yourself from the sun's rays.

Summer in Canada can be unpredictable. While the weather is generally pleasant, occasional rain showers or cooler nights are a possibility. Pack accordingly and be prepared to adjust your plans if necessary. Embrace the opportunity to experience the diverse weather patterns that contribute to Canada's natural beauty.

Cultural Sensitivity

As you explore hidden gems, remember that you're a guest in local communities. Be respectful of local customs and traditions. Learn a few basic phrases in French if you're venturing into Quebec or other predominantly French-speaking regions. A friendly greeting and a genuine interest in the local culture go a long way in enriching your experience.

Leave No Trace

Canada's pristine natural beauty is a national treasure. Respect the environment by adhering to "Leave No Trace" principles. Pack out all your trash, minimize your campfire impact, and stay on designated trails to preserve the delicate ecosystems of these hidden gems.

With careful planning and a spirit of adventure, your Canadian summer adventure awaits. Follow these tips, delve into the hidden gems, and create unforgettable memories that will leave you yearning to return and discover more of Canada's magic.

FAQs

I’m Not a Newcomer to Canada but Want to Visit to Experience Summer There. Do I Need a Visa?

Yes. To enter Canada, you need a Canadian Visitor Visa. Before you apply for one, first check whether you qualify for an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA).

As a Newcomer to Canada, I Now Have Access to Universal Healthcare. Since I will be Traveling Around the Country, Will Ambulance Services be Covered, Should I Have an Emergency?

No. Ambulance services are not covered under Canada’s public healthcare services.
