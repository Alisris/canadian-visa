Living In Ottawa Vs Quebec

by 
Kelia Losa
×

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: June 22nd, 2023

Both located in Canada, Ottawa is the capital city, nestled on the banks of the Ottawa River in the province of Ontario, while Quebec City, with its rich history and European charm, is the capital of Quebec province. With a population of approximately 844,000, Quebec City offers a cozy and intimate atmosphere, while Ottawa-Gatineau boasts a larger population of around 1,437,000, making it a bustling cosmopolitan hub. Before you make a decision, join us as we explore education, job opportunities, and more in this detailed comparison between these amazing cities!

Job Opportunities

Ottawa

As the capital of Canada, the City of Ottawa is a bustling hub of job opportunities. With a population of over one million, it ranks as the fourth-largest municipality in the country. One of the key factors contributing to Ottawa's thriving job market is its vibrant technology sector, which offers a wide range of employment prospects.

Besides its strong tech industry, Ottawa boasts a high median household income and relatively affordable living costs, making it an attractive destination for professionals seeking a balance between financial stability and quality of life.

As of May 2023, the unemployment rate in Ottawa was 4.3%, with a slight increase of 0.1% from the previous month. However, it is important to note that this rate is 5.7% below the peak observed in July 2020 and remains below the long-run average, indicating a relatively stable job market.

On popular job search platforms like Indeed, there are currently 14,388 job listings available in Ottawa, spanning various industries and professions. This abundance of job opportunities reflects the city's diverse economy and the continuous demand for skilled workers.

Quebec City

Quebec City, known for its rich history and welcoming atmosphere, offers a thriving job market with numerous career prospects. The city is known for being safe, open to diversity, and providing its residents a high quality of life.

While Quebec City may be smaller in population than Ottawa, it still presents ample opportunities for professionals looking to establish themselves in various fields. According to Payscale, the average salary in Quebec City is CAD 61,234 per year or CAD 31.40 per hour, with entry-level positions starting at CAD 39,994 per year and experienced workers earning up to CAD 134,162 per year.

With its charm and all the advantages of larger urban centers, Quebec City attracts newcomers who seek a balanced lifestyle. The city's diverse job market provides options for various industries and sectors.

Platforms like Indeed currently offers 12,640 job listings in Quebec City, reflecting the availability of career opportunities and the city's commitment to fostering economic growth and development.

Whether you choose Ottawa or Quebec City, both offer promising job markets that cater to different industries and professional backgrounds. Consider your career aspirations and preferences, weigh the available in-demand job opportunities, and explore the unique offerings of each city before making a decision that aligns with your goals.

Ottawa vs Quebec Weather

When considering a move to a new city, it's essential to consider the climate and weather conditions. Ottawa and Quebec City experience distinctive seasons throughout the year, each offering unique charm.

Quebec City Weather

The cold season in Quebec City lasts approximately 3.3 months, from December 4 to March 13. During this period, the average daily high temperature remains below freezing point 0°C (32°F). The coldest month in Quebec City is January, with an average low temperature of -13°C (7°F) and a high of -6°C (21°F).

Winters in Quebec City are characterized by cold temperatures, and average highs in December and January struggle to reach -6°C (20°F). Snowfall is common during these months, creating a picturesque winter wonderland. In the summer, temperatures occasionally soar above 30°C (85°F), but the overall climate is mild, with cool nights. July often brings a five-week-long heatwave, with tropical-like days and nights.

Ottawa Weather

Ottawa experiences all four seasons distinctly, making it a year-round destination for those who appreciate seasonal variety. The hottest month in Ottawa is July, with an average temperature of 21.5°C (71°F). On the other hand, the coldest month is January, with an average temperature of -9.5°C (15°F).

Summer in Ottawa is characterized by hot and dry weather, with above-seasonal temperatures forecasted by Environment Canada for July, August, and September. The winter season in Ottawa typically begins in October and extends till late April. Snowfall can occur as early as November; even in May, visitors may encounter snowfall. Each season brings its unique beauty and experiences to enjoy in Ottawa.

Whether you prefer the crisp, snowy winters of Quebec City or the diverse seasons of Ottawa, both cities offer distinct weather patterns that add to their charm. Consider your preferences and the activities you enjoy to choose the climate that suits your lifestyle.

Cost of Living Comparison Between Ottawa and Quebec City

When considering a move to a new city, it's crucial to understand the cost of living and how it may impact your finances. Let's compare the cost of living between Ottawa and Quebec City using data from Numbeo, a crowd-sourced database of user-contributed information.

Indices Difference (Quebec City - Ottawa)
Consumer Prices (without rent) 4.2% lower
Consumer Prices Including Rent 15.7% lower
Rent Prices 40.7% lower
Restaurant Prices 1.9% lower
Grocery Prices 1.2% higher
Local Purchasing Power 2.4% higher

According to Numbeo's data, Quebec City's living cost is generally lower than Ottawa's. Let's delve into the specifics:

Consumer Prices (without rent) 

Consumer prices in Quebec City are 4.2% lower compared to Ottawa. This includes various expenses such as groceries, transportation, and entertainment.

Consumer Prices, Including Rent 

When considering the cost of living, including rent, Quebec City is 15.7% lower than Ottawa. Rent prices in Quebec City are significantly more affordable, with a difference of 40.7% compared to Ottawa.

Restaurant Prices 

Dining out in Quebec City is slightly more affordable, with restaurant prices being 1.9% lower than in Ottawa.

Groceries Prices 

While overall consumer prices are lower in Quebec City, grocery prices are 1.2% higher than in Ottawa.

Local Purchasing Power 

Quebec City boasts a 2.4% higher local purchasing power than Ottawa, indicating that individuals in Quebec City generally have slightly more disposable income.

It's important to note that these indices provide a general overview and individual circumstances may vary. Personal spending habits, housing choices, and lifestyle preferences will also impact the overall cost of living in each city.

Housing options are readily accessible in Ottawa and Quebec City. While rent prices may be relatively low, they are also not exorbitant like in other cities. Owning a house in Ottawa can be relatively affordable if you are flexible with the location. On the other hand, renting in Quebec City is inexpensive, and housing prices are generally relatively affordable.

Before deciding, carefully evaluate your financial situation and consider these cost of living factors to determine which city aligns best with your budget and desired standard of living.

Education in Ottawa versus Quebec City

When considering a city to live in, the quality of education available is essential, especially for families with children. Both Ottawa and Quebec City offer excellent educational opportunities, although different laws govern them due to being in different provinces.

In Ottawa, you will find a range of educational institutions, including some of the top universities in Canada, that cater to diverse needs:

Universities

  • University of Ottawa
  • Carleton University
  • Dominican University College
  • La Cité collégiale
  • University of Ottawa
  • Saint Paul University

Ottawa is home to numerous primary and secondary schools, both public and private. Some notable private schools include Ashbury College, Blyth Academy, Elmwood School, and Westboro Academy.

On the other hand, Quebec City offers a vibrant educational landscape:

Universities

  • Laval University
  • University of Quebec
  • University of Quebec at Rimouski
  • University of Quebec at Trois-Rivières
  • University of Quebec in Outaouais

For primary and secondary education, Quebec City boasts several public and private schools. Some notable private schools include Académie St-Laurent Academy, Académie Joan of Arc Academy, Macdonald-Cartier Academy, and OMS Montessori.

It is important to note that Ottawa and Quebec City have different education laws due to their respective provinces. These laws influence factors such as curriculum, funding, and governance.

When assessing the education standards in both cities, it is essential to remain objective and consider individual needs and preferences. Factors such as school performance, extracurricular activities, and community engagement should also be taken into account when making an informed decision.

Ultimately, it is recommended that individuals and families research and visit schools in both Ottawa and Quebec City to understand their specific offerings, programs, and atmosphere. This firsthand experience will help make an informed decision aligning with your educational goals and values.

Languages in Ottawa and Quebec City

Ottawa, Canada's capital city and located on the border of Quebec, has a unique linguistic landscape. While the primary language spoken in Ottawa is English, French is also significant because it is the official second language. As a government city, French has more prominence in Ottawa than other parts of Ontario or Canada. Both English and French are widely spoken, and it is common to find bilingual individuals in the city.

Besides English and French, Ottawa is home to various language communities representing Arabic, Chinese, German, Italian, Portuguese, Spanish, and Indigenous languages. The Indigenous population speaks Indigenous languages such as Anishinaabemowin, Algonquin, and Cree in Ottawa.

To live and work in Ottawa, proficiency in both English and French is advantageous. Many employers are required to provide services in both languages, reflecting the bilingual nature of the city. Learning some basic French phrases can be helpful when interacting with locals, and knowing French may benefit government-related work.

Quebec City, on the other hand, is predominantly French-speaking. It is the capital of the province of Quebec, where French is the majority language and the sole official language. Approximately 80% of Quebec's population has French as their mother tongue, with French being the primary language most residents speak. The Office québécois de la langue française (OQLF), also known as the language police, oversees the promotion and preservation of the French language in Quebec. You can immigrate to Canada as a French-speaker in various ways. 

While Ottawa is officially bilingual with a strong presence of both English and French, Quebec City is primarily French-speaking, reflecting its unique linguistic and cultural identity within the province of Quebec.

Health Services

Access to quality healthcare is a crucial aspect of life, particularly for individuals in different cities or provinces. Let’s compare the health services in Ottawa and Quebec City, shedding light on medical care, top hospitals, and the functioning of the public healthcare systems in both cities.

Medical Care in Ottawa

Ottawa boasts a robust healthcare system with various medical services available to residents. The city is home to several renowned hospitals, including:

  • The Ottawa Hospital
    • A leading academic health sciences center offering specialized care in various fields, such as cancer treatment, cardiac care, and neurosurgery.
  • CHEO (Children's Hospital of Eastern Ontario)
    • Focused on pediatric care, CHEO provides comprehensive medical services for children and youth.
  • Queensway Carleton Hospital
    • A community hospital providing various services, including emergency care, surgery, and rehabilitation.

Public Healthcare System in Ontario

Ontario operates under a publicly funded healthcare system known as the Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP). As a permanent resident, you become eligible for OHIP coverage after establishing residency in the province. OHIP covers medically necessary services, including doctor visits, hospital stays, and specialist services. It is important to note that there may be waiting times for certain procedures or specialist appointments.

Medical Care in Quebec City

Quebec City also offers its residents comprehensive medical care, focusing on French-speaking healthcare services. Prominent hospitals in the city include:

  • CHU de Québec-Université Laval
    • A major healthcare network of multiple hospitals specializing in various medical disciplines and research.
  • Hôpital de l'Enfant-Jésus
    • A renowned teaching hospital provides various services, including emergency care, surgery, and specialized treatments.
  • Centre hospitalier de l'Université Laval (CHUL)
    • Another major hospital affiliated with Université Laval, offering specialized care and cutting-edge research facilities.

Public Healthcare System in Quebec

Quebec operates under its public healthcare system, known as the Régie de l'assurance maladie du Québec (RAMQ). Like OHIP, RAMQ provides coverage for medically necessary services, including doctor visits, hospital care, and some specialist services. As a permanent resident, you become eligible for RAMQ coverage once you have obtained your Quebec Health Insurance Card (Carte d'assurance maladie du Québec).

Accessing Public Healthcare as a Foreigner

As a foreigner obtaining permanent residency in Ontario or Quebec, accessing public healthcare requires enrolling in the provincial health insurance plan. In Ontario, this involves applying for OHIP coverage, while in Quebec, you would need to obtain the Quebec Health Insurance Card (RAMQ). It is important to consult the official government websites or contact the relevant authorities to understand the specific requirements and procedures for enrollment.

Transportation: Ottawa vs Quebec City

Regarding transportation, Ottawa and Quebec City have unique characteristics and offerings. Let's compare the transportation systems in these two Canadian cities, including public transport options and the cost of owning a car.

Public Transportation

Both Ottawa and Quebec City have public transportation systems in place to cater to the needs of their residents and visitors. Ottawa boasts a bus and light rail system, while Quebec City also has an extensive bus network. However, there are some notable differences between the two.

In Ottawa, introducing the Light Rail Transit (LRT) system has significantly improved public transit options. The LRT connects various parts of the city, making commuting more efficient and convenient. The bus system in Ottawa is also well-regarded, particularly for daytime office workers traveling to and from Downtown and the suburbs. However, public transit across the provincial border can be challenging and inconvenient.

In Quebec City, the bus system is good, providing reliable transportation. Although there is no light rail system in Quebec City, the bus network efficiently serves the transportation needs of its residents.

Comparing Traffic

Traffic congestion is a common concern in many cities, but when comparing Ottawa and Quebec City, both cities fare relatively well. According to locals, while traffic can be bad during construction season, it is less severe than in cities like Toronto or Montreal. This is good news for commuters who value a more manageable traffic situation.

Cost of Owning a Car

The cost of owning a car can vary depending on several factors, such as the type of vehicle, insurance rates, and local regulations. While specific prices may fluctuate, getting a general idea of car costs in Ottawa and Quebec City is possible.

According to Kijiji, an online classifieds website popular in Canada, the average cost of buying a car in Ottawa ranges from around CAD 10,000 to CAD 30,000, depending on the vehicle's make, model, and condition. The average car cost in Quebec City falls within a similar range, depending on similar factors. However, it is important to note that car prices can be subject to change and can vary significantly based on individual preferences and circumstances.

Culture

When it comes to cultural diversity and vibrant traditions, Ottawa and Quebec City each have their distinct characteristics. Let's will explore the cultural aspects of both cities, including immigrant populations, predominant ethnicities, languages spoken, and notable festivals.

Immigration and Ethnic Diversity:

Approximately 185,000 people born outside of Canada reside in Ottawa, accounting for almost 18% of the metropolitan population. While specific ethnic statistics are not provided, Ottawa has a diverse population, including significant communities of Irish, Chinese, Asian, Indian, and Latin American backgrounds. This diversity contributes to the city's multicultural fabric and fosters a rich blend of traditions, languages, and cuisine.

On the other hand, Quebec City is home to many non-permanent residents, with around 346,000 individuals residing there as of 2022. As of January 1, Quebec hosted 57% of Canada's asylum seekers, 16% of its temporary workers, and 12% of its foreign students. While Quebec City has a significant immigrant population, French culture remains dominant, as the city is located in the predominantly French-speaking province of Quebec.

Cultural Highlights and Festivals:

Ottawa boasts a vibrant cultural scene with numerous festivals and artistic performances. The city is home to the National Arts Centre, which showcases live performances, including theater, ballet, opera, symphony orchestra, and live music concerts. Additionally, Ottawa hosts a wide range of festivals throughout the year, such as Blues Fest, Jazz Fest, Folk Fest, West Fest, the Tulip Festival, the Hot Air Balloon Festival, the Chamber Music Festival, the Festival of Lights (Fireworks), Hope Beach Volleyball, Winterlude, and Canada Day celebrations.

In Quebec City, cultural events and festivals are also an integral part of the city's identity. The city is known for its rich history and preservation of French heritage. Some notable festivals in Quebec City include the Quebec Winter Carnival, an iconic winter celebration with parades, snow sculptures, and outdoor activities. The Festival d'Été de Québec, one of Canada's largest outdoor music festivals, attracts renowned artists and music enthusiasts worldwide. Furthermore, the New France Festival showcases the city's colonial history through reenactments, traditional costumes, and period-inspired activities.

Language and Cuisine:

Ottawa is officially bilingual, with English and French being widely spoken. As the capital of Canada, it serves as a hub for both English and French-speaking communities. The city's diverse culinary scene reflects its multicultural population, offering many international cuisines alongside traditional Canadian dishes.

Quebec City, located in the French-speaking province of Quebec, predominantly speaks French. French language and culture are deeply rooted in the daily lives of Quebec City residents, contributing to a distinctly French atmosphere. Quebec cuisine is known for its unique dishes, such as poutine (fries topped with cheese curds and gravy), tourtière (meat pie), and maple syrup-infused treats.

Ottawa and Quebec City have cultural charm and appeal. Ottawa embraces its multicultural population, offering diverse traditions, languages, and festivals. With its strong French influence, Quebec City preserves and celebrates its rich heritage through events, language, and cuisine. Whether you prefer the vibrant multiculturalism of Ottawa or the distinct French ambiance of Quebec City, both cities have much to offer regarding cultural experiences.

Entertainment

When it comes to entertainment, both Ottawa and Quebec City offer a vibrant cultural scene and a wide range of activities to cater to different interests. Whether you're a fan of arts, music, sports, or nightlife, these cities have something to offer for everyone. Let's take a closer look at what each city offers regarding entertainment.

Ottawa

Parliament Hill 

One of the main attractions in Ottawa is Parliament Hill. Take a guided tour of the Parliament Buildings and witness the changing of the guard ceremony.

National Gallery of Canada

Art enthusiasts will be delighted by the National Gallery of Canada, which houses a vast collection of Canadian and international artwork.

Rideau Canal

The historic Rideau Canal offers a unique experience, especially during the winter when it transforms into the world's largest skating rink.

Nightlife in ByWard Market

Ottawa's ByWard Market is a lively neighborhood with restaurants, bars, and clubs. Enjoy a night out in town and experience the city's vibrant nightlife.

Major Festivals

Ottawa hosts several major festivals, including Winterlude, Canada Day celebrations, and the Canadian Tulip Festival. These events bring the city to life with music, art, and cultural performances.

Quebec City

Old Quebec

Step back in time and explore the charming streets of Old Quebec, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Discover historic landmarks, quaint shops, and cozy cafes.

Winter Carnival

Quebec City's Winter Carnival is a world-renowned event that attracts visitors from all over. Enjoy winter sports, ice sculptures, parades, and lively outdoor concerts.

Museums and Galleries

Quebec City boasts an array of museums and galleries, including the Musée de la Civilisation, Musée National des Beaux-arts du Québec, and the Musée de la Place-Royale.

Summer Festival

The Festival d'été de Québec is one of the largest music festivals in North America. With multiple stages and a diverse lineup of artists, it offers a fantastic musical experience.

Quartier Petit Champlain

This picturesque neighborhood is known for its charming boutiques, art galleries, and excellent dining options. It's the perfect place for a stroll.

Both Ottawa and Quebec City offer a rich tapestry of entertainment options, ensuring visitors and residents can find something to suit their preferences. Whether looking for history, art, music, or vibrant nightlife, these cities will surely leave you entertained and captivated.

Ottawa Taxes vs. Quebec

There is a notable difference between Ottawa in Ontario and Quebec regarding taxes. Your income tax can vary significantly depending on which province you choose to reside in. Let's compare the tax comparison between Ontario and Quebec based on the provided information.

According to New Canadian Life, the income tax difference is substantial for an annual income of CAD 75,000. In Ontario, you would pay CAD 16,561 in income taxes; in Quebec, the amount would be CAD 20,893 for the same income. This means a difference of more than CAD 350 per month.

It's important to note that these figures are based on a specific income level and may vary depending on individual circumstances such as deductions and other factors. However, it gives a general idea of the tax disparity between the two provinces.

While Quebec may have higher income and sales taxes than Ottawa, certain factors can offset these differences. Here are some insights from individuals sharing their experiences:

Cost of Living

Quebec generally has lower rent and housing prices compared to Ontario. This can help offset the higher taxes, especially if you have a family and can benefit from certain income tax breaks.

Value for Tax Bucks

Some individuals find that they get more value for their tax dollars in Quebec compared to Ontario. Services, infrastructure, and social programs the government provides may contribute to this perception.

Access to Healthcare

While Quebec may offer certain advantages, finding a family doctor in the province can be challenging. This could be a consideration for individuals prioritizing healthcare accessibility.

Gas Prices

It’s worth noting that gas prices are higher in Quebec than in Ontario. This is an additional cost to consider when assessing the overall financial impact.

Ultimately, the decision between Ottawa and Quebec regarding taxes depends on individual preferences, financial circumstances, and lifestyle considerations. It's important to carefully evaluate the pros and cons, weighing factors such as income tax differences, cost of living, and available services to make an informed choice.

How to Choose Where to Live

Canada is a vast and diverse country, offering numerous opportunities for prospective immigrants. When considering where to settle in Canada, it's important to consider various factors to find a place that suits your needs and aspirations. Here are five tips to help you make an informed decision:

Step 1: Research the Area

Conduct thorough research on different Canadian provinces and cities before making any decisions. Consider factors such as climate, job opportunities, healthcare, education, cultural diversity, and quality of life. Look for regions that align with your preferences and offer the resources and amenities you desire.

Step 2: Identify Your Desired Lifestyle

Think about the lifestyle you envision for yourself and your family. Are you looking for a bustling metropolitan area with a vibrant arts and culture scene? Or do you prefer a quieter, close-knit community with a strong sense of community? Consider factors like recreational activities, proximity to nature, access to amenities, and the pace of life.

Step 3: Prioritize Your Needs

List your priorities and rank them according to importance. For example, education might be a top priority if you have children. Job prospects and career opportunities might take precedence if you work in a specific industry. You can focus your search on areas that fulfill those requirements by identifying your needs.

Step 4: Consider Housing Costs

Housing costs can vary significantly across different regions in Canada. Research the average housing prices or rental rates in the areas you are considering. Consider the cost of living, property taxes, and utility expenses. Ensure that your housing budget aligns with the options available in your chosen location.

Step 5: Visit and Explore

If possible, plan a visit to Canada to experience different cities firsthand. This will give you a better sense of the culture, lifestyle, and overall atmosphere. Explore the neighborhoods, talk to locals, and immerse yourself in the community. It's also beneficial to attend job fairs or networking events to get a feel for the employment opportunities in the area.

FAQs

What is Québec City Known For?

Québec City is known for its rich history and charming European feel. It is renowned for its well-preserved Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage site, with narrow cobblestone streets and iconic landmarks like the Château Frontenac. The city hosts various festivals throughout the year, including the Quebec Winter Carnival, showcasing vibrant cultural traditions. Québec City is also known for its delicious cuisine, including poutine and maple syrup products. It offers a unique blend of history, culture, and scenic beauty.

Is Québec City Expensive?

Compared to some other Canadian cities, Québec City is generally considered to be more affordable. While prices may vary, overall, the cost of living, including housing, transportation, and everyday expenses, is relatively lower than in major urban centers like Toronto or Vancouver. Québec City offers a range of housing options at various price points, and dining out or entertainment activities are often more affordable compared to larger cities. However, it's essential to consider personal preferences and lifestyle when assessing affordability.

How do I Find Accommodation in Ottawa?

Finding accommodation in Ottawa can be facilitated through various resources. Websites like UsedOttawa.com and classifieds of local newspapers such as the Ottawa Citizen or Ottawa Sun provide listings for both rental and property purchase options. Another valuable resource is the Ottawa Real Estate Board's website, which offers comprehensive information on available properties for sale. Additionally, engaging with local real estate agents or property management companies can provide personalized assistance and access to a broader range of housing options that match specific requirements.
