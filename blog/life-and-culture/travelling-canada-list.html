Travel to Canada: Your To-Do List for a Perfect Trip

by 
Wesley Allan
×

Author:

Wesley Allan

Bachelor's Degree in English Language and Literature and Film Studies

Wesley Allan has been writing professionally for over five years and about immigration for a year. With a BA in English Language and Literature and Film Studies.

. Updated: August 30th, 2023

Are you planning to travel to Canada? If yes, then you are about to experience one of the most beautiful countries in the world with its stunning landscapes and vibrant cities. But before you embark on your journey, you must be well-prepared. Don't forget to check off everything on your pre-travel checklist before you go! From making sure your passport is up to date to packing the right accessories, there are several important things to consider before embarking on your Canadian adventure. This guide will help you master your to-do list, making your trip to Canada a truly memorable experience.

Your Travel to Canada Checklist

Make Sure Your Passport is in Order

Your passport is your ticket to the world, and ensuring it's in good standing before you start your journey is vital. Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months before departure. This is a common requirement for most countries, and Canada is no exception. So, take a moment to check the expiration date on your passport.

Besides having a valid passport, making copies of important documents such as your passport, travel insurance, and itinerary is also a good idea. These copies can be a lifesaver in case of loss or theft. You can also consider storing digital copies in a secure cloud service. This way, you can access them from anywhere in the world.

Purchase Travel Insurance to Protect Yourself and Your Belongings

When planning to travel to Canada, it's crucial to consider travel insurance. Travel insurance can protect you from unexpected incidents such as medical emergencies, trip cancellations, or baggage loss. A small investment can save you from significant financial losses and unnecessary stress.

Ensure you understand your insurance policy coverage. It should cover medical expenses, trip interruption, and luggage loss or damage. Also, remember to carry a copy of your insurance policy and the contact details of your insurance provider. Learn more about travel insurance in Canada.

Learn About the Local Customs and Etiquette

Understanding the local customs and etiquette is a significant aspect of traveling to Canada or any foreign country. Canadians are known for their politeness and respect for others. They value personal space and privacy. Awareness of these basic etiquettes can help you blend in and interact comfortably with the locals.

In Canada, tipping is customary. In restaurants, it is usual to tip between 15% to 20% of the total bill before tax. Tips are also expected in taxis, hair salons, and hotel room service.

Get Your Finances in Order

Before you travel to Canada, it's important to familiarize yourself with the Canadian currency. The official currency is the Canadian Dollar (CAD). Coins are available in denominations of 5 cents (nickel), 10 cents (dime), 25 cents (quarter), 1 dollar (loonie), and 2 dollars (toonie). Banknotes are available in denominations of 5, 10, 20, 50, and 100 dollars.

Keep an eye on the exchange rates as they fluctuate. It's a good idea to exchange some money before you arrive. However, you can also withdraw CAD from ATMs in Canada.

Credit cards are widely accepted in Canada, and having one for emergencies and convenience is a good idea. Visa and MasterCard are the most commonly accepted, but many places also accept American Express and Discover.

Before you leave, inform your bank and credit card companies about your travel plans. This will prevent them from flagging your card for unusual activity or blocking it due to suspected fraud.

If you are in Canada for a while, consider automating your bills. This will ensure you don't miss any payments while you're away. Most service providers offer an online bill payment option, which you can set up before your departure. Learn more about how to prepare your finances for Canada.

Create an Itinerary of the Places You Want to Visit and the Activities You Want to do

Canada is a vast country with many attractions. From the stunning Rocky Mountains in Alberta to the vibrant city life of Toronto, Ontario, the list of things to see and do is endless. Research the best places to visit in Canada and create an itinerary that suits your interests and travel style. To give you an idea, here are some of Canada's top adventure destinations if you're on a working holiday in Canada or just visiting.

Research the Best Options for Transport in Canada

Canada's transport system is efficient and extensive. Depending on the places you plan to visit, you might use a combination of flights, trains, buses, and car rentals. It's essential to research the best transport options and plan accordingly. Learn how to rent a car in Canada.

Pack Appropriate Clothing and Gear for the Weather and Activities You Have Planned.

Canada's weather can vary depending on the region and time of the year. So, pack your clothing and gear accordingly. Don't forget to pack a good pair of walking shoes, as you'll likely do a lot of walking or hiking.

Learn Some Basic French if you plan to visit Quebec

While English is widely spoken across Canada, French in Canada is the primary language in Quebec. Even if you're only visiting for a few days, knowing some French in Canada can enhance your travel experience.

Research the Best Restaurants in Canada and Local Cuisine to Try

Canada's culinary scene is as diverse as its landscape. There's much to explore, from poutine in Quebec to fresh seafood in British Columbia. So, research the best restaurants in Canada and local cuisine to try.

Check if You Need Any Vaccinations Before Traveling to Canada

Generally, no special vaccinations are required to travel to Canada. However, being up-to-date on routine vaccines is always a good idea.

Purchase Tickets for Popular Attractions in Advance to Avoid Long Lines

Some of the popular attractions in Canada can have long lines, especially during peak tourist season. To save time, consider purchasing tickets in advance. Learn more about Canada's top attractions.

Download Maps and Travel Apps to Help You Navigate Canada

Having a map of Canada can be handy for navigation. Consider downloading offline maps or travel apps on your smartphone. These can help greatly, especially in areas with limited internet connectivity.

Research a Canadian SIM Card

Depending on the length of your stay and your data needs, getting a Canadian SIM card might be beneficial. Research the different providers and plans available. Learn more about getting a mobile plan in Canada.

Learn About the Local Emergency Services and How to Contact Them if Needed

Knowing how to contact local emergency services in emergencies is crucial. In Canada, the emergency number for police, fire, and ambulance services is 911.

Pack a Universal Power Adapter for Your Electronic Devices

Electricity in Canada operates at 120 volts, and the frequency is 60 Hz. You'll need a power adapter or converter if your devices are incompatible. Pack this essential item to keep your devices charged and ready to use.

How to Apply for Canada Tourist Visa in 4 Steps

If you require a visa to travel to Canada, start the application process well in advance.

Step 1: Fill Out the Application Form Diligently

Fill out the application form on the Canadian government website, ensuring no errors or omissions could potentially jeopardize your application. This form includes sections on personal data, travel history, employment details, and the reason for your visit to Canada.

Step 2: Pay the Visa Fees

The Canadian Visa fee is a non-refundable amount set by the Canadian immigration authorities. Ensure this fee is paid through an approved payment method to avoid any complications down the line.

Step 3: Schedule Your Appointment

It's crucial to schedule your appointment promptly, as slots can be filled quickly due to high demand. While scheduling, choose a date and time when you can be fully prepared and available without distractions.

Step 4: Attend Your Visa Interview

This is a critical part of the process, where you present all necessary documents and answer questions regarding your intended trip.

If your application is successful, you will receive your tourist visa. Depending on your country, processing time can take 21 - 484 days. Learn more about Canada's visa processing times.

Remember, every detail matters during this interaction as it provides insight into your visit intent and credibility. So, rehearse possible questions beforehand and ensure all documents are in order before attending the interview. Trust this process, as it has been designed to ensure only legitimate visitors get approval for their Canada tourist visa applications. With our expert advice and guidance throughout each step, you'll navigate this journey with more confidence and less stress.

Preparation is key when planning to travel to Canada. By following this checklist, you can ensure a worry-free and enjoyable trip. So, start planning, pack your bags, and get ready to explore Canada's incredible beauty and diversity. Remember, Canada is more than just a destination; it's an experience waiting to be explored. So, embrace every moment, immerse yourself in the culture, and create memories that will last a lifetime. Happy traveling!

FAQs

What is the Best Time to Visit Canada?

The best time to visit Canada depends on the activities you want to do. For winter sports, January to March is ideal. The summer months, from June to August, are best for outdoor activities like hiking and wildlife viewing. Learn more about the top winter activities in Canada and the top summer activities in Canada.

Is Canada Expensive to Visit?

The cost of traveling to Canada can vary depending on your travel style, the regions you're visiting, and the time of year. However, with some planning and budgeting, it's possible to have an affordable trip. To help you get a good idea, learn more about the cost of living in Canada.

Do I Need a Visa to Visit Canada?

This depends on your nationality. Some visitors may need an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) or a visa to enter Canada. Check the official Government of Canada website for the most accurate information. Find out if you need a visitor visa for Canada.
