Are you looking for a unique international experience? Do you want to explore Canada and experience all the wonders that this country has to offer? If so, a Canadian Working Holiday Visa may be the perfect option!

The Canadian Working Holiday Visa, also known as the International Experience Canada (IEC) program, allows eligible applicants to come to Canada for up to two years for work and travel. With a Working Holiday Visa, you can explore Canada’s diverse cultures, landscapes, and cities and experience the great outdoors.

However, where do you start with all of Canada to explore? To help you plan your Working Holiday in Canada, we’ve compiled some must-see attractions and highlighted some of the best destinations for working holiday makers in Canada.

Work While You Earn in Canada

A Working Holiday Visa is a perfect way to explore Canada and experience all the country offers. A Working Holiday Visa allows you to work and travel in Canada for up to 24 months. This article explores the best destinations for an unforgettable working holiday experience in Canada, from budget-friendly cities to coastal towns. We’ve also provided a few tips to help you find accommodation and apply for a Working Holiday Visa.

If you’re considering a working holiday in Canada, why not talk to one of our RCICs today and start your journey? Our experienced RCICs can help you with the application process and answer any questions.

What is a Working Holiday in Canada?

The Canadian working holiday, also known as the International Experience Canada (IEC) program, allows eligible applicants to come to Canada for extended stays of up to two years. The IEC program is an incredibly popular option for travellers exploring Canada, allowing them to live, work, and travel for up to two years.

The IEC program is open to citizens of certain countries and territories. To be eligible, you must be a citizen of one of the eligible countries or territories and meet the other eligibility requirements. The IEC program also has different categories for work and travel experiences, such as working holidays, young professionals, and international co-op internships.

Must-See Attractions in Canada

When starting your working holiday in Canada, you first want to check out the most famous destinations. Sure, some of them may be touristy, but they’re touristy for a reason. These are some of Canada’s most unique and beautiful sites and are a perfect starting point for your working holiday.

Niagara Falls

One of the most iconic natural wonders in the world, Niagara Falls is a must-visit destination for anyone coming to Canada. Located in Canada’s most populous province, Ontario, the thunderous roar of the falls and the picturesque views of the cascading water make this a must-see attraction.

CN Tower

The iconic CN Tower is the tallest free-standing structure in North America. Located in Toronto, the CN Tower offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding landscape. At the top of the tower, visitors can explore the glass floor, the observation deck, and the Sky Pod.

Banff National Park

One of Canada’s most popular national parks, Banff National Park is famous for its stunning mountain scenery and fantastic outdoor activity opportunities. Banff National Park is a great place to explore Canada’s outdoor beauty, from skiing and snowboarding to hiking and camping.

Find out what adventures await you at Banff National Park.

Old Quebec City

Nestled in the centre of Quebec city, the old city of Quebec is one of the oldest European settlements in North America. The cobblestone streets, the grand architecture, and the charming atmosphere make it a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Canada.

Rideau Canal

The Rideau Canal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Canada’s most popular tourist attractions. The canal is used for recreational activities such as boating, fishing, and canoeing, and it also offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

How to Apply for a Working Holiday Visa in Canada

Applying for a Working Holiday Visa in Canada is relatively straightforward.

Step 1: Check Your Eligibility Requirements

First, you must meet the eligibility requirements. This includes being between 18 and 30 (or 35, depending on your country of origin) and having a valid passport.

Step 2: Apply Online

Once you have met the eligibility requirements, complete the online application form. This includes providing personal information, such as your name, address, and date of birth. You must also provide details about your previous travels, if applicable.

Step 3: Pay Your Fees

Once you have completed the online application form, you must pay the relevant visa fee. This fee varies depending on your country of origin.

Step 4: Receive Your Email

Once your application has been processed, you will receive further instructions. This will include a link to the visa portal to print your visa and start your working holiday in Canada.

To learn more about the application process and eligibility requirements, look at our Working Holiday Visa guide.

FAQs

Are you considering a working holiday in Canada? Here are some of the most commonly-asked questions about the experience.

How Long Can I Stay in Canada on a Working Holiday Visa?

You can stay in Canada for up to 24 months on a Working Holiday Visa. However, the length of your stay may vary depending on your country of origin.

Are There Any Restrictions on the Type of Work I Can Do?

Yes, there are restrictions on the work you can do while on a Working Holiday Visa in Canada. For example, you are only allowed to do casual and part-time work, and you are not permitted to work in certain industries.

Are There Any Age Restrictions?

Yes, there are age restrictions for certain countries. For example, people from the United Kingdom must be between 18 and 30 years old, while people from New Zealand must be between 18 and 35 years old.

Where Can I Stay on My Working Holiday in Canada?

Finding accommodation in Canada can be tricky, especially if you’re on a Working Holiday Visa. Here are a few tips to help you find the perfect place to stay.

  • Hostels or Short-term Accommodation
    • These are often cheaper than hotels and perfect for short-term stays.
  • Guesthouse or Bed & Breakfast.
    • These are often cheaper than hotels and can provide a homely atmosphere.
  • Private Residence.
    • This can be an excellent option for a more long-term stay.
  • Use online platforms such as Airbnb or Couchsurfing
    • These can be great options for those on a budget.

What Rules Must I Follow on My Working Holiday?

It’s important to remember that Working Holiday Visas are temporary visas, and you must abide by the regulations of Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). This includes working no more than 20 hours per week, not taking jobs away from Canadian citizens, and not working in specific industries.

It’s also important to remember that you must leave Canada at the end of your visa period. If you’re working illegally, you may be subject to fines and deportation.
