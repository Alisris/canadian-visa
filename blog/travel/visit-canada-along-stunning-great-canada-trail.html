Visit Canada Along The Stunning Great Canada Trail

by Stephan Basson. Updated: July 24th, 2018

The Great Canada Trail – an absolute must on your visit to Canada

The Great Canada Trail is a sprawling network of connected pathways, spanning 24,000 kilometers. Stretching from coast to coast, it is the longest recreational trail in the world. 

Filled with lush forests, sweeping landscapes, and awe-inspiring mountainous peaks, this trail offers travellers a picturesque journey through the majesties of Canada’s wilderness. A passion project spearheaded by locals, the great Canada Trail is an experience not to be missed when you visit Canada.

A trail of such grand scale can be overwhelming to navigate. With so much to see and do, our guide will direct you to the most spectacular sections of the Great Canada Trail. 

Top Three Sections of the Great Canada Trail

British Columbia

For those travelling from Vancouver, the Kettle Valley Rail Trail comes highly recommended. This 650km long trail, the longest in British Columbia, includes some of the most beautiful sights in all of Canada.

The Myra Canyon, a National Historic Site of Canada, is found here and is a must for sightseeing, hiking, and cycling.

For wine lovers, a stop along the famed Naramata region is essential. Allow for some time here as you soak up the natural landscape while sipping on some of the country’s finest wines.

Near Summerland, history buffs can appreciate a 90-minute trip on a restored 1912 Steam Locomotive. On selected dates a special train robbery event is held, promising to make this train ride an unforgettable one.

Ontario

A mere fifty-minute drive from Toronto, the Caledon Trailway boasts velvety green hills, creeks winding through valleys, luscious lilacs, and quaint little villages dotted with cozy B&Bs.

The Caledon trail also holds lush gardens with several spots perfect for picnicking. Do not miss the opportunity to explore Hamilton, a vibrant city bustling with a recent surge in arts and culture. 

Hamilton is the ideal stop for a quick turn to urban life as you make your way along the winding trailway.

Prince Edward Island

The coastal Confederation Trail is one of the most popular sections of the Great Canada Trail and with good reason.

Winding along the coast of Prince Edward Island, the trail darts between charming villages and broad bay seascapes. The trail makes its way through the heart of Charlottetown, a must stop for any traveler who wishes to visit Canada. 

Known as the birthplace of Canada, Charlottetown is a hub of all things cultural, coastal and culinary. Spend the day out on the boats, strolling through town, or snacking on renowned seafood dishes.
