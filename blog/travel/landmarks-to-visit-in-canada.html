5 Major Landmarks in Canada

by 
Themba Phongolo

Canada is known for its stunning natural beauty and stunning Canadian landmarks. From breathtaking national parks to iconic man-made structures, landmarks in Canada showcase the splendor of the vast and diverse North American nation.

We will take you on a journey to discover the landmarks of Canada. Whether you are an adventure seeker, a nature lover, or a history enthusiast, these famous landmarks in Canada are sure to leave you in awe.

Canada's rich socio-cultural history and varied topography ranging from tundra to arid plains are also renowned for its world-famous landmarks. Famous landmarks in Canada facts will be presented, as each of the famous landmarks in Canada has interesting natural or built environmental histories, beautiful scenery, impressionable experiences and recognizable, iconic imagery.

Home to some of the world’s most beautiful lakes, waterfalls, and mountains, Canada is surrounded by three major oceans, including the Pacific, Artic, and Atlantic.

Want to see what wonders Canada has to offer? Here are five major landmarks in Canada and the immigration visa pathways you can use to visit them.

Five Major Landmarks in Canada

The five major landmarks of Canada are outlined below as follows:

Banff National Park

Located in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, Banff National Park is a true natural wonder. With towering snow-capped peaks, crystal-clear lakes, and abundant wildlife, this park offers a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts.

Visitors can explore the park's extensive network of hiking trails, go wildlife spotting, or simply soak in the breathtaking views of this major landmark in Canada. For those seeking a unique, long-lasting, and memorable experience, visiting the Banff Upper Hot Springs is a must, where you can relax in the warm mineral-rich waters while surrounded by majestic mountain scenery.

Among the famous landmarks in Canada, Banff National Park is positioned as the country's flagship park for its national park system. It is part of the iconic Canadian Rocky Mountain range and has been honored by United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) as a World Heritage Site.

The park is renowned for its various natural wonders, including snow-capped mountains, great blue lakes and vast forests spanning thousands of square kilometers.

Due to it being at the heart of the Rocky Mountains Range in Alberta, Banff National Park offers a glimpse into the geological history of Canada’s prairie topography. For beautiful scenery you can feast your eyes on, the park's Turquoise crystal clear lakes and lush green trees that reach heavenly heights offer scenes that will take your breath away.

Known for Lake Louise, plenty of outdoor activities include swimming, fishing or a peaceful stroll along the shore. In winter, Banff transforms into a major winter sports area. Lake Louise freezes over, attracting skiers as it is one of Canada’s most prominent ski destinations.

Banff National Park has firmly stamped its place among the famous landmarks in Canada and presents the perfect opportunity to explore Canada’s bountiful wilderness. If you love the great outdoors, then the following activities are available to experience in Banff National Park.

  • Hiking Trail in Banff
  • Fossil Hunting
  • Banff’s Sightseeing Lift
  • Skiing for All
  • Backcountry Camping
  • Canoeing
  • Hot Springs

Learn more about how you can travel to Canada and experience its backcountry gems.

The CN Tower

A symbol of Canada's engineering prowess, the CN Tower is an iconic landmark in Toronto. Standing at a staggering height of 553 meters, it held the title of the world's tallest freestanding structure for over 30 years. The tower offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city and Lake Ontario from its observation deck, which is accessible through high-speed elevators.

For the thrill-seekers looking to make long-lasting memories from their experience of this major landmark of Canada, the EdgeWalk offers a unique opportunity to walk on a 1.5-meter-wide ledge encircling the tower's main pod, 356 meters above the ground.

Brave enough? Once you have completed your walk, you can treat yourself to something to eat at the CN Tower’s restaurant, where you can soak in Canada's premier city's breathtaking and commanding views! The CN Tower is not just a tourist attraction but also a telecommunications hub, broadcasting TV and radio signals across the region.

The CN Tower is one of the most globally recognized icons of Canada. Its huge frame dominates the skyline of Toronto, a visual so striking that it is often used on promotional and marketing material for tourism to Toronto and Canada.

Confederation Bridge

Stretching over 12.9 kilometers, the Confederation Bridge connects the provinces of New Brunswick and Prince Edward Island, making it singularly unique among the major landmarks in Canada. This engineering marvel is the longest bridge in Canada and one of the longest in the world. The bridge provides a vital transportation link for residents and visitors, eliminating the need for ferry services.

Driving across the bridge offers stunning views of the Northumberland Strait, shifting tides and picturesque coastal landscapes. As you cross this architectural masterpiece, you can appreciate the engineering ingenuity that went into its construction and the seamless connection it provides between the two provinces.

Built in 1977, this famous landmark in Canada has high economic value to the surrounding region, specifically to people within the trucking, fishing, tourism and construction companies. The construction history of Confederation Bridge offers an instructional guide into the history of long bridge construction in Canada and the country’s development of materials engineering.

Niagara Falls

No list of famous landmarks in Canada would be complete without mentioning the awe-inspiring Niagara Falls. Straddling the border between Canada and the United States, these massive waterfalls are a sight to behold and offer unforgettable beautiful scenery.

The sheer power and beauty of the falls are mesmerizing, as millions of gallons of water cascade over the edge every minute.

Visitors can experience the falls up close by taking a boat tour on the Maid of the Mist, which brings you to the base of the falls. For a bird's-eye view, the Skylon Tower offers an observation deck with panoramic views of the falls and the surrounding area.

Niagara Falls is a must-see attraction as a major Canadian landmark. It attracts millions of visitors each year, producing 2,271,247 liters of water every second.

Niagara Falls consists of 3 waterfalls, namely the:

  • American Falls
  • Horseshoe Falls
  • Bridal Veil Falls

These three waterfalls produce the highest flow rate of any waterfall on Earth. For the best view of this major landmark of Canada, Queen Victoria Park offers a direct view of the Falls.

Hopewell Rocks

Located on the Bay of Fundy in New Brunswick, the Hopewell Rocks are an extraordinary natural wonder. The Rocks’ composition provides a first-hand and tangible view into Canada's geological and maritime history.

These towering rock formations have been sculpted by the highest tides in the world, which rise and fall up to 16 meters twice a day. At low tide, visitors can walk on the ocean floor and explore the unique rock formations, some resembling flowerpots.

As the tide rises, the rocks become islands surrounded by water. The best time to visit is during either low or high tide to witness the dramatic transformation. The Hopewell Rocks offer a truly unforgettable experience that showcases the power of nature.

This major Canadian landmark was formed and shaped by tidal erosion. Its world-renowned iconic imagery ensured that it was designated a Marine Wonder of the World, holding the record for some of the world’s highest tides which reach up to 4 stories high.

Hopewell Rocks have many activities, including exploring the caves and walking through the maze during low tide, making it one of the most navigable of the major landmarks in Canada.

Hopewell Cave has been patiently sculpted by the massive tides of the Bay of Fundy for thousands of years, leaving behind one of the most impressive natural architectural wonders.

It has over twenty free-standing sea stacks peppered along two kilometers of shoreline. If you visit Atlantic Canada, we recommend Hopewell Rocks as a prime.

If you wish to visit Hopewell Rocks Park, please note that admission is valid for two consecutive days, a regulation measure instituted to ensure that you have the flexibility to see both high and low tides.

Please ensure you’re surefooted and wear sturdy footwear if you explore the trails and shoreline at this major Canadian landmark.

How Can You Visit Canada’s Major Landmarks

Looking to visit one of Canada’s major landmarks but don’t know how to? Here are some temporary immigration visa programs that you can use:

Canada Visitor Visa (Tourist Visa)

To visit Canada as a tourist and explore its major landmarks, you will need a Canada Visitor Visa. This visa allows you to enter the country for a temporary stay. The application process involves:

  • Submitting the required documents
  • Possession of a valid passport
  • Proof of financial support
  • Letter of invitation (if applicable)

Find out more about everything you need to know about Canada’s Tourist Visa.

IEC Working Holiday Visa

If you are a young adult from one of the participating countries, the International Experience Canada (IEC) program offers the opportunity to work and travel in Canada. The Working Holiday Visa allows you to explore the country's major landmarks while gaining valuable work experience.

The application process involves creating an online profile and being selected from a pool of candidates. Once accepted, you can apply for a work permit and start your Canadian adventure.

Learn why you should go on a Working Holiday in Canada.

Unveiling the beauty of Canada through its major landmarks is a journey that offers both natural wonders and architectural marvels. From the towering peaks of Banff National Park to the majestic power of Niagara Falls, each landmark has its unique charm that captivates visitors worldwide. So start planning your trip to Canada and prepare to be amazed by the beauty and splendor of these iconic landmarks.

If you wish to experience the multi-layered richness of Canada's landmarks, we recommend you contact an immigration consultancy company affiliated with Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants (RCICs).

An RCIC will help to facilitate your immigration to Canada through each stage of the application process, from taking the eligibility test to submitting your application with all of the required documentation and paying the necessary application fees to receive your visitor's visa.

FAQs

What is the Most Visited Natural Canadian Landmark?

Niagara Falls is the most visited natural Canadian landmark. Frequently renowned as one of the most beautiful natural wonders in the world, millions of local and international tourists visit Niagara Falls, drawn towards it by the need to witness its majestic falls.

What is the Most Visited Built Environment Canadian Landmark?

The CN Tower is the most visited built environment Canadian landmark. Towering above the Toronto skyline, the CN Tower is the most internationally recognized structure in Toronto. It has become a symbol of the enterprising force of the Canadian nation and a major source of tourism to Canada.

Are There any Other Major Landmarks in Canada Worth Visiting?

Canada has numerous other major landmarks, such as the Rocky Mountains, Parliament Hill in Ottawa, and Old Quebec City. Each region has its unique attractions that are worth exploring.
