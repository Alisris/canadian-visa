The 5 Best Places to Live in Yukon

by 
Wesley Allan
×

Author:

Wesley Allan

Bachelor's Degree in English Language and Literature and Film Studies

Wesley Allan has been writing professionally for over five years and about immigration for a year. With a BA in English Language and Literature and Film Studies.

. Updated: June 23rd, 2023

In the heart of the Great White North, where the Northern Lights dance across the sky and the wilderness stretch as far as the eye can see, lies Yukon, a territory as rich in beauty as it is in history. Living in Yukon is akin to dwelling in a landscape painting, every day offering a different shade of nature's palette. The Yukon River meanders through vast forests, serene lakes, and towering mountains, making for a picturesque backdrop that never ceases to amaze.

Known for its ruggedly beautiful terrain, Yukon is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether hiking through the Tombstone Territorial Park, fishing in the Yukon River, or exploring the historic Klondike Gold Rush sites, Yukon offers a unique blend of adventure and tranquility. The territory is renowned for its wildlife, and residents often spot bears, moose, and caribou in their natural habitat.

Beyond the natural beauty, Yukon boasts a vibrant arts scene, with numerous festivals and events throughout the year. The diverse indigenous culture is celebrated with pride, adding a rich tapestry to the region's identity. The sense of community is robust, with residents coming together to celebrate their love for this land. Life in the Great White North is charming, a blend of serenity, adventure, and cultural richness that is hard to find elsewhere.

Top 5 Most Livable Yukon Cities

As a territory, Yukon is home to several cities, each with unique character and charm. 

These cities in Yukon paint a vibrant picture of life in the territory. From the lively cultural scene in Whitehorse to the historic charm of Dawson City, the adventurous spirit of Haines Junction, the unique attractions of Watson Lake, and the serene lifestyle of Carmacks, there is a place for everyone in Yukon.

Whitehorse

Whitehorse, the capital and largest Yukon city is often called the "Wilderness City". Nestled on the banks of the Yukon River, Whitehorse is surrounded by mountains and forests, offering stunning natural beauty at every turn. Despite being in the heart of the wilderness, Whitehorse is a modern city with all the amenities one would expect, making it a great place to live.

The city offers a high living standard, excellent healthcare facilities, top-notch schools, and a thriving economy. Whitehorse is also home to a vibrant arts and culture scene, with numerous festivals and events throughout the year. The Yukon Arts Centre, the Old Fire Hall, and the annual Yukon International Storytelling Festival are just a few examples of the city's rich cultural offerings.

Outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty to love in Whitehorse. From hiking and biking trails to fishing and boating on the Yukon River, there are endless opportunities for adventure. The city is also home to the Takhini Hot Springs, a natural hot spring popular for relaxation and rejuvenation. With its perfect blend of modern amenities and natural beauty, Whitehorse stands out as a great place to live in Yukon.

Dawson City

Dawson City, once the heart of the Klondike Gold Rush, is a city that wears its history with pride. The city's heritage buildings, wooden boardwalks, and historic sites transport visitors back in time, offering a glimpse into the gold rush era. However, Dawson City is more than just a historic town - it's a vibrant community that offers a unique lifestyle.

Dawson City is known for its lively arts scene. The city is home to the Dawson City Music Festival, the Klondike Institute of Art and Culture, and the Palace Grand Theatre, offering a rich cultural experience. The city's culinary scene is equally vibrant, with various restaurants serving everything from traditional Yukon fare to international cuisines.

Nature lovers will find plenty to explore in and around Dawson City. With its stunning mountain landscapes, the Tombstone Territorial Park is a popular spot for hiking and camping. The Yukon River offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and wildlife watching. Despite its historic charm, Dawson City offers all the necessary amenities, making it a great place to live in Yukon.

Haines Junction

Haines Junction, situated at the edge of the Kluane National Park and Reserve, is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The town offers breathtaking views of the St. Elias Mountains, and the vast wilderness provides endless opportunities for adventure. Haines Junction is where nature's grandeur can be experienced up close.

The town is known for its close-knit community, with residents often coming together for community events and celebrations. The Da Ku Cultural Centre is a hub for cultural activities, showcasing the rich heritage of the Champagne and Aishihik First Nations. Haines Junction also hosts the annual Kluane Mountain Bluegrass Festival, attracting musicians and music lovers from all over.

Despite its small size, Haines Junction offers all the necessary amenities, including healthcare facilities, schools, and shopping outlets. The town's restaurants serve delicious local fare, and the Haines Junction Bakery is a must-visit for its fresh bread and pastries. With its stunning natural beauty and close-knit community, Haines Junction is a great place to live in Yukon.

Watson Lake

Watson Lake, located in the southeast part of Yukon, is a city that is as unique as it is appealing. Known for its Signpost Forest, a collection of over 77,000 signposts from around the world, Watson Lake is a city that welcomes everyone. The Northern Lights Space and Science Centre is another unique attraction, offering an immersive Northern Lights experience.

Watson Lake offers a relaxed lifestyle with a strong sense of community. The city has several annual events, including the Yukon Quest International Sled Dog Race and the Watson Lake Recreation Centre's annual Arts and Crafts Fair. Watson Lake has several recreational facilities, including a swimming pool, curling rink, and golf course.

The city's natural surroundings offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. There is always something to do, from fishing and boating on Watson Lake to hiking in the nearby forests. With its unique attractions and relaxed lifestyle, Watson Lake is an attractive place to live in Yukon.

Carmacks

Carmacks, a small village located on the banks of the Yukon River, offers a serene lifestyle that is hard to find elsewhere. Known for its simplicity and tranquility, Carmacks is a place where one can truly connect with nature. The village's traditional lifestyle is intertwined with the modern, offering a unique blend of the old and the new.

Carmacks is home to several cultural sites, including the Tagé Cho Hudän Interpretive Centre, which showcases the history and culture of the Northern Tutchone people. The village also hosts the annual Carmacks Fish Derby, a popular event that brings the community together.

Despite its small size, Carmacks offers all the necessary amenities, including a hospital, school, and shopping facilities. The village's natural surroundings, including the Yukon River and the nearby forests, offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. With its simplicity and tranquility, Carmacks is a great place to live in Yukon.

To make Yukon your home, you must be adequately prepared for the immigration process. One of the best ways to do this is to utilize the skills and experience of a certified Canadian immigration professional like a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC).

Why Live in Yukon?

Natural Beauty

Yukon's stunning natural beauty, vast wilderness, serene lakes, and towering mountains are a major draw. Living in Yukon is like a landscape painting, offering a unique blend of serenity and adventure. Yukon is also a vantage point for one of the seven wonders of the world - the Northern Lights!

Outdoor Adventure 

Yukon is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether hiking, fishing, boating, or wildlife watching, Yukon offers endless opportunities for adventure. Find out what you can get up to in Yukon.

Vibrant Arts and Culture Scene

Yukon boasts a vibrant arts scene, with numerous festivals and events throughout the year. The diverse indigenous culture adds a rich tapestry to the region's identity. 

Strong Sense of Community

The sense of community in Yukon is robust. Residents celebrate their love for this land, creating a warm and welcoming environment. Learn more about Yukon's community.

High Standard of Living 

Yukon offers a high living standard, excellent healthcare facilities, top-notch schools, and a thriving economy. Despite its rugged terrain, Yukon provides all the amenities of a modern city. Learn more about the Yukon economy.

How to Move to Yukon

Moving to Yukon can be a rewarding experience, but it requires careful planning and preparation. Here are some ways to make a move:

Yukon PNP 

The Yukon Provincial Nominee Program, or Yukon PNP, is a pathway for skilled workers and entrepreneurs to settle in Yukon. The program offers several streams, including the Skilled Worker, Critical Impact Worker, and Business Nominee streams. Learn more about the Yukon PNP.

Express Entry

Express Entry is a federal immigration program that selects skilled workers based on their skills and experience. Applicants who receive a provincial nomination from Yukon receive additional points, increasing their chances of receiving an invitation to apply for permanent residence. This video breaks down how you can move to Canada through Express Entry. 

Home Caregiver Pilots

This pilot program allows home care providers to immigrate to Canada, including Yukon. Applicants must have a job offer from a Yukon employer and meet other eligibility requirements. Find out more about Canada's caregiver pilots.

Temporary Resident Visas

Temporary Resident Visas, including Work Permits, a Working Holiday Visa, and Study Permits, allow individuals to live and work in Yukon temporarily. These visas can often be a stepping stone to permanent residence. Learn more about your temporary residence options.

FAQs

What is the Cost of Living in Yukon? 

The cost of living in Yukon is relatively high compared to other parts of Canada, mainly due to the high cost of goods and services. However, this is offset by higher wages and a lower tax rate. Learn more about Yukon's cost of living.

What are the Job Opportunities in Yukon?

Yukon has a diverse economy, with sectors such as mining, tourism, and government services offering job opportunities. Learn more about Yukon's in-demand occupations.

Is Yukon an Excellent Place to Raise a Family?

Yes, Yukon is a great place to raise a family. The territory offers a safe environment, excellent schools, and plenty of outdoor activities for kids. Learn more about education in Yukon.

Experience the Glory of the Great White North in Yukon

With its stunning natural beauty, vibrant cities, and a strong sense of community, Yukon offers a unique lifestyle that is hard to find elsewhere. Whether you choose the modern city of Whitehorse, the historic charm of Dawson City, the adventurous spirit of Haines Junction, the unique attractions of Watson Lake, or the serene lifestyle of Carmacks, you will find a place to call home in the Great White North. Experience the glory of Yukon and embrace the charm of living in the Great White North.
