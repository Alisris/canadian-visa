5 Reasons to Immigrate from the United Arab Emirates to Canada

by Stephan. Updated: September 4th, 2018

If you are living in the United Arab Emirates and you are looking for your next adventure, you’ve come to the right place. Canada is world-renown as a safe haven for the minorities of the world, it is a place where equality and diversity is encouraged and celebrated. It is the perfect place for you to change your future. In this article, we list the top 5 reasons why you should immigrate from the United Arab Emirates to Canada.

1) Citizenship

One of the biggest differences between being an expat in Canada and in the United Arab Emirates is citizenship. If you immigrate to Canada with permanent residence or when you earn permanent residence you are immediately treated like you belong and you are protected by the laws of Canada as an equal.

As a permanent resident, you can already own property, make investments, enjoy free healthcare and many other Canadian benefits. If you stay in the country for a minimum of four years out of six, you can even apply for Canadian citizenship. If you are successful, you earn precisely the same privileges as born Canadians.

If you are interested in becoming a citizen or permanent resident, we recommend reading our complete guide on how to immigrate from the United Arab Emirates to Canada here.

2) Civil Liberties

Canada is known for its great civil liberties. Many refer to the country as a tolerant and broad-minded society. Canadians have the right to freedom of religion, meaning everyone is free to practice whatever they believe without persecution.

Primary and secondary education is free for all citizens and permanent residents. Free public healthcare is also available to them. This is just more evidence that Canada cares about education and their people’s well-being. Not many countries can boast about the same privileges.

Canada is also the first non-European country to legalize same-sex marriage. Regardless of your nationality, race, gender or sexual orientation, Canada ensures that its people have a place in society where they are protected and treated with fairness and respect. In fact, over 40 sitting members of parliament were born abroad.

3) The People

Everyone should know by now how friendly Canadians are. Expect to hear the word “sorry” much more often than you are currently used to. Expats and visitors from around the world always comment on the friendliness and helpfulness of Canadian locals.

Canada is currently ranked as the 10th most friendly country for newcomers by the latest annual Expat Insider survey. According to the study, 45% of visitors consider staying in Canada forever. People who participated in the survey referred to locals as “open-minded, tolerant, and benevolent”. Four out of five people found that Canadians’ attitude towards foreigners is positive which is nearly twice the global average!

4) Climate

Most people only associate Canada with snow and winter, but the country actually has four distinct seasons. Spring runs from March to May, summer is from June to August, falls hits in September until November and winter only lasts from December to February.

It’s true that winter can be really frosty in Canada, but the warm and lovely summer days should not be overlooked. The extremes between seasons just means you have to visit and explore everything in Canada twice. You might visit Banff in winter and you’ll find it to be the picture perfect winter fairytale landscape, including frozen waterfalls. In summer, however, that same spot is filled with summer activities, like hiking and kayaking and then the waterfalls continuously flow into their endless crystal clear rivers and lakes.

5) Nature

Canada is the second largest country in the world and it is quite sparsely populated given its sheer size. Having so much open space means nature flourishes throughout the landscape. Travelling through the country you will encounter everything from glaciers, fjords, snow-capped mountains, evergreen forests to grasslands, beaches and natural hot springs.

There are over 40 national throughout Canada, meaning you have endless opportunities to explore the diverse wildlife and nature of the country. Here you will find many famous Canadian creatures like moose and bears roaming around.

If you are a nature lover or photographer, Canada is the perfect place for you. Driving around 40 km in any direction from a city will most likely land you in unspoiled nature far from any other person. For this reason, it's easy to find your own personal paradise in Canada.
