Canada's Population Milestone Puts the Country Ahead: Immigration Drives Economic Success

by 
Kelia Losa

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: June 21st, 2023

Canada has achieved a remarkable milestone, surpassing a population of 40 million. This extraordinary growth can be attributed to immigration, serving as a strategic advantage in the global labor race. The country's rapid population expansion bolsters the economy but also presents unique challenges in major cities. Canadas immigration-driven economic boom is reshaping the country and catalyzing a wealth of work opportunities.

Population Surge: Canada's Immigration Experiment to Counter Economic Decline

Canada's groundbreaking approach to immigration is propelling the country's growth and positioning it favorably amongst the global competition for skilled workers. Prime Minister Justin Trudeau's administration sees immigration as a means to broaden the labor market and expand Canada's international presence. With a population milestone of over 40 million, achieved on June 16, Canada is embracing immigrant workers, refugees, and foreign students, to sustain its momentum.

We have lots of space for people to come and occupy," emphasized Usha George, a professor specializing in Canadian immigration policy at Toronto Metropolitan University. "To expand our agricultural, industrial, and technological base, we need more people to come here.

Surpassing the 40 million mark depicts Canada as an attractive destination for individuals worldwide. Canada's rapid and unprecedented population growth highlights its dynamic and welcoming nature. Anil Arora, Chief Statistician at Statistics Canada, enthusiastically stated,

This is an exciting milestone for Canada. It is a strong signal that Canada remains a dynamic and welcoming country, full of potential."

While Canada continues to embrace newcomers, there is a pressing challenge of balancing growth in rural regions needing population influx while managing strains on urban centers already experiencing population surges. Immigration Minister Fraser acknowledged the government's efforts to address these strains by regionalizing immigration programs and prioritizing skilled professionals in high-demand sectors like healthcare and homebuilding.

This is going to help us bring the skills that we need into the economy to help alleviate some of the social pressures rather than exacerbate them," Fraser affirmed.

As Canada's population milestone highlights its economic strength and progressive immigration policies, the nation is ready to embrace its diverse and expanding population, contributing to the country's vibrant future.

Canada's Strategic Immigration System Results in Record-Breaking Inflows

According to Bloombergs article on Canadas Mass Immigration Experiment, the countrys strategic immigration system has yielded remarkable results, positioning the country as a global leader in welcoming newcomers. Since 1967, Canada has implemented a points-based system that evaluates immigrants based on age, education, employment prospects, and language proficiency, enabling the country to attract skilled workers. This approach has been integral to Canada's economic policy and has contributed to its reputation as an immigrant-friendly nation. in 2015, this developed inthat is used now, The Express Entry System. 

Under the leadership of the Trudeau administration, Canada has set an ambitious target of adding approximately half a million permanent residents annually. In addition, the country has seen substantial inflows of foreign students, temporary workers, and refugees, resulting in a record-breaking total of one million arrivals last year. This influx has propelled Canada's annual population growth rate to an impressive 2.7%, surpassing the growth rates of advanced economies and rivaling those of developing nations like Burkina Faso, Burundi, and Sudan.

Sean Fraser, Canada's immigration minister, emphasizes embracing immigration to avoid negative social and economic consequences. He highlights that,

...the ability to successfully integrate people in large numbers doesnt demand that you welcome fewer people; it demands that you advance smart immigration policies.

Canada's commitment to immigration is evident in its population composition, with nearly one in four people being immigrantsthe highest proportion among the Group of Seven nations. According to the Economic Times, the current pace of growth, Canada, the smallest G-7 country by population, is projected to double its residents within approximately 26 years. By 2050, it is anticipated to surpass Italy, France, the UK, and Germany in population size.

With its strategic immigration system and progressive policies, Canada continues to attract and integrate diverse talent, fueling economic growth and solidifying its position as a global leader in immigration.

For more Canadian Immigration news, like and follow up on our social media platforms below.

Immigrants Help Grow Canadas Future

Immigrants are vital in shaping Canada's future, contributing significantly to its labor force and economy. Across various sectors, their impact is evident. According to Statistics Canada, immigrants aged 25 to 54 hold substantial positions:

  • Over 36% are employed in the accommodation and food services sector.
  • Nearly 38% contribute to the transportation and warehousing sector.
  • Over 34% are engaged in professional, scientific, and technical services.
  • Over 20% are involved in the construction industry.

As the country's population milestone surpasses 40 million, the influx of immigrants brings fresh talent, skills, and entrepreneurial spirit. Immigrants are actively engaged in a wide range of sectors, from accommodation and food services to transportation and warehousing, from professional and scientific services to construction.

Their presence strengthens the labor force and stimulates innovation, productivity, and competitiveness. By attracting skilled workers through programs like Express Entry, Canada remains at the forefront of global talent acquisition. The diverse skill sets and expertise brought by immigrants enhance the country's economic resilience and provide a solid foundation for sustained growth.

Moreover, as the national population ages and retirements increase, immigrants offer a valuable solution to address labor market needs and maintain economic vitality. By welcoming and integrating immigrants, Canada secures a prosperous future where the economy thrives, and work opportunities abound for newcomers and existing residents.

The contributions of immigrants extend beyond their representation in specific sectors. They significantly bolster the Canadian economy as a whole. With an aging population and declining birth rates, the labor force needs a robust influx of workers. Immigrants strengthen the labor force and generate economic growth by actively participating in the workforce. Their tax payments and employment sustain the economy, offsetting the challenges an aging demographic poses.

Notably, Canada's Express Entry program attracts skilled immigrants who excel in various occupations. Among the occupations that are invited to immigrate are:

  • Software engineers,
  • Designers,
  • Information systems analysts,
  • Computer programmers,
  • Financial auditors,
  • Accountants,
  • Advertising,
  • Marketing, and
  • Public relations professionals.
  • Chefs
  • Welders
  • Plumbers

These skilled individuals bring expertise and innovation, further enhancing Canada's economic potential.

Furthermore, immigrants contribute to improving and delivering health and social services. Many immigrants, particularly refugees and refugee claimants, pay more into the healthcare system than they utilize, known as the "healthy immigrant" effect. Their contribution alleviates the burden on the healthcare system, ensuring its sustainability and accessibility for all.

Beyond their economic contributions, immigrants fully integrate into Canadian society. Their active involvement in volunteering and participation in social organizations is noteworthy. Approximately one-third of immigrants in Canada engage in volunteer work, fostering a sense of community and giving back. Statistics Canada highlights that immigrants and their descendants who actively participate in local personal networks and community activities show higher levels of trust, connection with neighbors, and a willingness to contribute through volunteering.
