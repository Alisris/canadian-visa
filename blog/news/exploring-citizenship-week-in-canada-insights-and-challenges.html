Exploring Citizenship Week in Canada: Insights and Challenges

by 
Kelia Losa

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: May 24th, 2023
5 min read

As Citizenship Week takes center stage in Canada, it serves as a poignant reminder of the significance of Canadian citizenship and prompts a closer look at recent trends and challenges in obtaining it. The latest statistics from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) reveal a decline in the number of new Canadian citizens during the first quarter of 2023. 84,720 individuals, representing a decrease of 11.9 percent compared to the previous year, embraced Canadian citizenship during this period. 

However, experts believe this to be a lasting effect of older COVID-19 policies, and while the decrease is concerning, Canadian officials continue to encourage newcomers to settle. In the 20232025 Immigration Levels Plan, Canada expects to welcome over 1.3 Million people by 2025.

These figures, recently released by the IRCC, shed light on the changing landscape of Canadian citizenship. Against this backdrop, Citizenship Week, observed from May 22 to 28, serves as an opportune time to reflect on the value and significance of Canadian citizenship and to address the obstacles that applicants face. Let us delve deeper into the data and explore the implications of this decline while also recognizing the importance of this annual celebration.

Celebrating Canadian Citizenship: Citizenship Week 2023

Every year, Citizenship Week is a special occasion for Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) to honor new citizens and reflect on the essence of Canadian identity. This significant week is commemorated nationwide from May 22nd to May 28th, 2023.

During Citizenship Week, the Minister will personally attend citizenship ceremonies in Halifax, Nova Scotia, and Vancouver, British Columbia, underscoring the importance of this momentous occasion. Thousands of individuals will be welcomed as new Canadians, embracing the rights and responsibilities of Canadian citizenship.

Citizenship ceremonies hold great meaning for those reaching this milestone, signifying their integration into Canadian society. As part of the celebrations, citizenship ceremonies will be held throughout the week, inviting everyone to join in and witness the transformative journey of becoming a Canadian citizen. Additionally, a livestream from Halifax on May 24th provides an opportunity for Canadians across the nation to partake in the festivities.

IRCC has been committed to modernizing its services, offering streamlined processes to enhance the citizenship experience. From online testing to virtual citizenship ceremonies and online application trackers, advancements have ensured accessibility and efficiency.

Canadian citizenship offers numerous advantages. Unlike permanent residents, Canadian citizens have no residency obligations, granting them the freedom to reside anywhere within or outside Canada. Moreover, Canadian citizens can obtain Canadian passports, enabling hassle-free international travel. Additionally, as citizens, they have the right to vote in municipal, provincial, and federal elections, allowing them to actively participate in shaping the nation's future.

Despite the recent decline in citizenship numbers, it is worth noting that Canada surpassed its citizenship goals last year, welcoming nearly 364,000 new citizens. In the first three months of 2023 alone, approximately 85,000 individuals have already become Canadian citizens. Indians remain the largest group, comprising approximately 19% of new citizens. Citizens from the Philippines, Nigeria, Syria, Pakistan, Iran, China, the United States, and the United Kingdom collectively account for 54% of this number, representing the diverse fabric of Canadian citizenship.

As Citizenship Week unfolds, let us come together to celebrate the richness of Canadian citizenship, acknowledging the achievements of new citizens and cherishing the values that define being Canadian.

Canadian Citizenship Trends: A Quarter of Decline in Early 2023

The first quarter of this year witnessed significant changes in the sources of new Canadian citizens, with notable drops seen in several nationalities. Among the standout declines, 1,408 fewer French nationals, 1,061 Iranians, 1,134 Filipinos, 2,075 Syrians, and 1,056 Ukrainians obtained Canadian citizenship compared to the corresponding period last year. These figures underscore a shifting landscape in the origins of new citizens.

However, it is important to note that while certain countries experienced declines, there were also noteworthy increases from other source countries. Notably, there were 1,169 more Indians and 1,196 additional Nigerians (46% increase) and Eritreans with 556 (102% increase) who became Canadian citizens in the first quarter of this year compared to the same quarter last year. While these gains are significant, they were insufficient to offset the declines in citizenship from other source countries.

These changes in the demographics of new Canadian citizens highlight the dynamic nature of the country's immigration patterns. As we explore the implications of these shifts, it becomes crucial to understand the underlying factors contributing to these trends and consider the potential impact on Canadian citizenship's overall diversity and composition.

To go from 75% of newcomers obtaining citizenship to only 45% is simply shocking, said Daniel Bernhard, CEO of the Institute for Canadian Citizenship.

Contextualizing the Decline in Canadian Citizenship Numbers

The recent decline in Canadian citizenship numbers should be viewed within the broader context of a preceding year marked by a surge in new citizens. The COVID-19 pandemic, which significantly impacted immigration processes, played a role in these contrasting trends.

In 2022, Canadian immigration officials worked diligently to address the backlog of applications, resulting in a substantial increase in the number of immigrants taking the citizenship oath. A staggering 375,355 individuals became Canadian citizens that year, surpassing 137,119 in 2021 and 110,987 in previous years. This remarkable surge can be attributed to the efforts made to catch up with the delays caused by the pandemic.

However, as Citizenship Week approaches, it is important to acknowledge the recent decline in citizenship numbers. This decline serves as a reminder that even with the successful recovery from the pandemic's impact, there are still challenges to address and factors influencing the acquisition of Canadian citizenship.

Citizenship Week itself provides an opportunity for all individuals in Canada to join in the celebration. The week-long event includes nationwide citizenship ceremonies, symbolizing the transformative journey of newcomers becoming Canadian citizens. As part of the festivities, a special livestream from Halifax on May 24th offers an inclusive platform for everyone to participate and share in the joy of Citizenship Week.

It is a great honour to participate in welcoming new members to our Canadian family, said Immigration Minister Sean Fraser this week. This outlook increases the chances for those looking to get Canadian citizenship.

The Transformative Power of Canadian Citizenship: Empowering Newcomers and Fostering Integration

Canadian citizenship profoundly and positively impacts newcomers, facilitating their integration into Canadian society. The acquisition of citizenship opens doors to a myriad of opportunities and benefits, empowering individuals to fully participate in the country's social, economic, and political fabric.

From accessing social services and healthcare to pursuing educational and employment opportunities, Canadian citizenship provides newcomers with a strong foundation for building a fulfilling and prosperous life. Additionally, citizenship fosters a sense of belonging, allowing individuals to fully engage in their communities, contribute to the nation's growth, and shape its diverse cultural landscape. By recognizing the transformative power of citizenship, Canada continues to create an inclusive and welcoming environment for all newcomers.

According to George Carothers, Senior Director, Ideas and Insights of the Institute for Canadian Citizenship, We should want those who are contributing to Canada to stay here and become citizens.

A Shift in Immigration Trends during Citizenship Week

As Citizenship Week unfolds in Canada, it is a moment to reflect on the evolving landscape of immigration and the diverse paths individuals take towards building their lives in this welcoming nation. While the numbers of Canadian citizenship may have witnessed a slight decline, there is a vibrant story of growth and opportunity when we look at the increased numbers of Permanent Residents (PRs), Visitor visas, Temporary Residents (TRs), and Study visas.

The latest data reveals a remarkable increase in the issuance of Permanent Residency permits, signaling a growing interest and trust in Canada as a destination for long-term settlement. 158,982 permits were authorized at the start of 2023, an 18% increase from 2022 same period. The rise in PRs showcases the confidence that individuals worldwidefrom around the world have in Canada's inclusive society, robust economy, and promising future. These individuals, through their Permanent Residency status, contribute to the country's cultural diversity, economic prosperity, and social fabric.

Additionally, the surge of 109% in Visitor visas, with a total of 414,916 permits, signifies Canada's appeal as a sought-after destination for tourism, family visits, and business opportunities. Visitors from various countries choose to explore t Canadashe beauty, hospitality, and vibrant experiences that Canada has to offer. Their presence not only enhances cultural exchange but also contributes to the growth of local businesses and the tourism sector.

Furthermore, a 50% increase in Temporary Residents (TRs) applications received, with a total of 1,431,551 and a 109% increase in approved Study permits demonstrates Canada's position as a premier educational hub and a destination for career advancement. In the latest report, it was revealed that India accounted for the highest number of study permits applications with 85,797, making up 44% of the total. Following closely behind were Nigeria with 20,755 study visas, showing an impressive growth rate of 255.64%. 

These two countries collectively represent a significant portion, amounting to 54% of all study permits and visas granted in Canada this year.

While the number of Canadian citizenship may have experienced a slight dip, the positive trajectory of Permanent Residency permits, Visitor visas, Temporary Residents, and Study visas indicates the enduring appeal and growing interest in Canada. This diverse influx of individuals contributes to the multicultural tapestry of the nation, fostering innovation, economic growth, and cultural exchange.

Canadian Citizenship in Numbers

The recent 2021 Census shed light on the composition of Canadian citizens, showcasing the diverse tapestry of the nation. With a total count of 33.1 million Canadian citizens, encompassing both individuals born in Canada and those who attained citizenship through naturalization, the population reflects the rich multicultural fabric that defines the country.

Among the overall population residing in Canada, approximately 3.2 million individuals were identified as non-Canadian citizens. These individuals held either permanent resident/landed immigrant status or were classified as non-permanent residents, highlighting the presence of individuals who have yet to acquire Canadian citizenship.

When examining the countries of citizenship reported by non-Canadian citizens in the 2021 Census, India and China emerged as the top sources. This reflects the substantial number of individuals from these nations who have chosen Canada as their new home.

Furthermore, the data from 2021 indicated that four out of every five eligible immigrants achieved naturalized Canadian citizenship. This statistic, with an impressive naturalization rate of 80.7%, underscores the significance of citizenship as a crucial step for immigrants in their journey of integration and belonging.

Notably, over 3.7 million Canadian citizens hold multiple citizenships, and a significant portion comprises naturalized Canadian citizens. This highlights the diversity and global connections that exist among Canadian citizens, illustrating the country's commitment to embracing individuals from various backgrounds.

These statistics demonstrate Canadian citizenship's dynamic and inclusive nature, encompassing both those born within the country's borders and those who have chosen Canada as their new home. The high rate of naturalization among eligible immigrants and the presence of citizens with multiple nationalities reflect the ongoing efforts to foster an inclusive society that celebrates diversity and welcomes individuals from around the world.
