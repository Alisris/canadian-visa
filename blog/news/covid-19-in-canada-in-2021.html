Covid-19 in Canada in 2021

by Theresa. Updated: June 18th, 2021

The Covid-19 pandemic has left a great mark on the world, one that makes people feel uncertain more than they have probably felt in the past decade, if not the last century.

On top of the uncertainty that fills the world, apart from the pandemic, there are many other reasons, such as political, social, and economical reasons that make people want to move from their countries to a safer, socially accepted place that welcomes one and all. Even though Canada is recognized as one of the top first-world countries to move to, immigrants are contemplating whether its safe or if they are even allowed to apply during this unforeseen period that doesnt seem to come to an end.

If you are wondering what the situation of Covid-19 in Canada is in 2021, you will be pleased to know that Canadas got everything under control. Immigrants, globally, are still allowed to enter, live, and work in Canada. To make things even more exciting, the country has also managed to make progress by vaccinating more people than any country in the world.

How Canada is Winning the Battle Against Covid-19

When the Covid-19 pandemic started, Canadian immigration was thought to slow down, yet it has managed to grow even bigger than it has before.

Canada was determined to make their immigration initiative for 2021 to 2023 work, regardless of the potential effects of the pandemic. The country still managed to accept 184,000 immigrants in 2020. Although this is the lowest number of immigrants they have welcomed to their country since 1998, the Great White North is back on track and plans to welcome over 1.2 million immigrants by 2023. Canada also expects to welcome over 320,000 immigrants through the Express Entry system only, among over a hundred other immigration programs.

Canadas government has managed and is planning to reach their prospects for the next three years with the help of the countrys federal government, which has implemented various measures to support immigrants during the pandemic.

With such a big need to live in a country that is not only secured against the risks the pandemic presents but also the dangers that other countries face due to the impact of the pandemic on economies, Canada is accepting as many immigrants as they can. That is, in the case that foreigners can prove that they can meet the standards to work in Canada, which is mainly focused on skilled professionals and their families.  

Compared to most other countries in the world, Canada has made significant progress in the battle against Covid-19. In just two months after recording more than 9,000 Covid-19 cases in one day, the countrys daily case count has shrunk by 90 percent. Close to 65percent of Canadians have also received the vaccine by June 2021, which shows just how focused the Canadian government is on looking after its residents. With so many people that have at least received one shot of the vaccine, Canada overtakes some of the top first-world countries that have also already implemented more than 40 percent of vaccinations in their countries.

While most residents have only been partly vaccinated in Canada at this point, the country is well on its way with nearly 20percent of residents already having received a full dosage of the vaccination.

Since Canada has the pandemic under control, immigrants can rest assured in knowing that they can apply for an immigration program, and with approval, move to Canada in as little as six months. At this rate, the country is well on its way to being completely vaccinated in no time, minimizing the risks of Covid-19 in the country, promising entry for immigrants without any constraints.

A Good Time to Make the Big Move


Canadian immigration has allowed many people from all parts of the world, particularly third-world countries, to create a better life for themselves and their families in one of the best countries in the world.

When you look to Canada for not only a better future but also a better life now, you wont be disappointed because Canada takes care of all its people, whether they are Canadian citizens or immigrants. The country values every person, and also what they can contribute to the country in exchange for a good, secure, and free life.

To find out more about life with Covid-19 in Canada or how to immigrate during the Covid-19 pandemic, get in touch with a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC) today.
