New Changes to Canada Immigration Operation System Announced

by 
Kelia Losa
×

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: October 4th, 2023

The Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) has been grappling with several challenges over the years. As Canada's immigration system evolves, it faces growing pains, inefficiencies, and the need for adaptation to changing global circumstances. Recognizing these issues, former IRCC Deputy Minister Neil Yeates published a comprehensive report that suggested critical improvements across four key areas: Organizational Structure, Governance, Management Systems, and Culture.

Making Immigration Processes Better

Neil Yeates' report provides a roadmap for IRCC's transformation, emphasizing that several steps must be taken to streamline its operations and better serve its diverse clientele. These steps encompass a major shift in organizational structure, governance reforms, strengthening management systems (particularly in planning and reporting), and fostering a culture that aligns with the department's objectives. Yeates also suggested reviewing the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act to harness the experience and expertise of IRCC's diverse staff groups.

One notable aspect of IRCC's transformation is its significant personnel growth. The department has expanded its workforce from 5,352 employees in March 2013 to 12,949 employees as of January 2023, reflecting the magnitude of the changes.

IRCC Reorganized to Business-Line Model

One of the major changes resulting from Yeates' recommendations is the recent reorganization of the department. IRCC has been divided into distinct sectors, each responsible for specific aspects of immigration and citizenship services. These sectors include:

  • Asylum and Refugees Resettlement
  • Citizenship and Passport
  • Chief Financial Officer
  • Chief Information Officer
  • Client Service, Innovation, and Chief Digital Officer
  • Communications
  • Corporate Services
  • Economic, Family, and Social Migration
  • International Affairs and Crisis Response
  • Migration Integrity
  • Service Delivery
  • Settlement Integration and Francophone Affairs
  • Strategic Policy

This restructuring aims to enhance the department's efficiency, clarity, and accountability.

Better Assistance for Refugees

One significant outcome of the reorganization is the department's enhanced ability to respond to global changes and humanitarian crises. For instance, the newly established International Affairs and Crisis Response sector allows IRCC to plan for and respond to emergencies proactively. This sector is crucial in formulating humanitarian action plans, particularly in light of recent events like the Ukrainian crisis and the resettlement of Afghan and Syrian refugees. Canada's Refugee Program is highly advanced and is only becoming better. 

Deputy IRCC Minister Christien Fox has implemented Yeates' recommendations under advisement. These changes are strategically designed to simplify immigration processes, making them more efficient and bringing IRCC closer to its immigration targets. By aligning its structure, governance, management systems, and culture with contemporary needs, IRCC is positioning itself to serve Canada's diverse immigrant population better.

Neil Yeates' comprehensive recommendations for IRCC are ushering in a new era for immigration processes in Canada. The transformation of the department's organizational structure, governance, and operational systems promises to make the immigration journey smoother for applicants while ensuring that Canada welcomes newcomers with open arms. As IRCC continues to adapt to evolving global realities, its commitment to facilitating immigration remains unwavering.

Medical Certificates for Express Entry

In a significant update this week, the Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) has introduced changes to the medical certificate requirements for Express Entry applicants. These updates aim to streamline the application process and make it more accessible for individuals seeking Canadian immigration.

Starting from October 1, 2023, a major change is coming into effect: upfront medical examinations will no longer be mandatory when applying for Express Entry. Instead, applicants are instructed to wait for specific guidance from the IRCC before scheduling a medical examination. This change is designed to simplify the process for applicants and reduce the initial burden on prospective immigrants.

The IRCC has empowered visa officers, immigration officers, and border services officers with the authority to issue medical examination instructions when necessary. They will initiate the process by conducting an integrated search to determine if an immigration medical examination (IME) is required based on the applicant's specific situation or any temporary public policies that may apply. It is worth noting that certain medical circumstances may impede you from applying for Canadian permanent residency. 

Instructions for Visa and Immigration Officers:

When deemed necessary, visa and immigration officers will provide applicants with a GCMS-generated form, IMM 1017E, along with detailed instructions. It's crucial to note that applicants must complete their medical examination within 30 days of receiving these instructions.

For instances where a client has been granted permission to use a non-panel physician, the visa or immigration officer will issue an additional form, IMM 5419E, along with the IMM 1017E. To differentiate this situation, the officer will add a comment at the top of the IMM 1017E, stating, "Permission given to use a non-panel physician." Furthermore, a visa office stamp will be placed over this comment for reference.

These changes are expected to make the Express Entry application process more straightforward and efficient for aspiring immigrants. By eliminating the upfront requirement for medical examinations and allowing officers to issue instructions as needed, the IRCC aims to enhance the overall experience for applicants while ensuring that the necessary medical assessments are carried out when appropriate.

Applicants are encouraged to stay updated with IRCC instructions and guidance to ensure a smooth immigration process as these changes come into effect on October 1, 2023.

Inadmissibility Laws Re-Defined

The Supreme Court of Canada has redefined the criteria for inadmissibility to Canada, emphasizing that individuals can only be deemed inadmissible if they pose a genuine threat to national security. This landmark immigration law ruling significantly narrows the powers of immigration officials and tribunals, particularly in cases involving foreign nationals without criminal convictions on security grounds. Canada has always stiputaled which crimes make you inadmissible to the country, and the Supreme Court Ruling further cements this. 

The focal point of this decision revolves around Section 34(1)(e) of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA), which stipulates that "a permanent resident or a foreign national is inadmissible on security grounds for engaging in acts of violence that would or might endanger the lives or safety of persons in Canada."

The Supreme Court's ruling clarifies that a person can only be classified as inadmissible under Section 34(1)(e) of the IRPA if their involvement in violent activities is directly linked to national security or the security of Canada.

This momentous verdict carries substantial implications, as it safeguards against overly broad applications of inadmissibility laws. Advocates and legal experts assert that the decision prevents authorities and government tribunals from casting an indiscriminate net to target individuals for deportation without valid national security concerns being at the core of their actions.

The ruling made as part of an ongoing court case, Mason v Canada (Citizenship and Immigration), underscores the importance of a clear and direct connection between an individual's actions and the safeguarding of Canada's security, thereby ensuring a fair and just application of inadmissibility laws. It represents a significant step towards a more equitable and balanced immigration system, where deportation decisions are made with careful consideration and adherence to the principles of justice.

These changes in immigration policies aim to streamline and improve the immigration process while upholding national security. Together, they seek to create a more efficient and just system for individuals seeking entry to Canada. Stay updated on immigration news by following us on social media for the latest developments.
