Canada Surpasses 220,000 ITAs in 2023, Nearing Halfway Mark of Immigration Target

by 
Kelia Losa
×

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: June 28th, 2023

On June 27, 2023, IRCC announced its latest Express Entry Draw results, distributing a substantial number of 4,300 ITAs. This recent draw has propelled the total count of ITAs for 2023 to an impressive milestone of 220,003. As we approach the halfway mark towards the ambitious immigration target of 465,000 for the year, Canada has been experiencing remarkable success in attracting newcomers and contributing to its growing population. In fact, only two weeks prior to this announcement, Canada proudly celebrated its achievement of reaching a population milestone of 40 million.

June 27 ITA Results: 4,300 Candidates Invited

In the latest Express Entry draw conducted by the Immigration Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) on June 27, 2023, a total of 4,300 candidates were invited to apply for immigration programs. This draw marked the fifteenth of its kind in 2023, and candidates were required to meet a minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score of 486 to qualify for an Invitation to Apply (ITA). Calculate your CRS points here. 

This draw followed closely after the previous one on June 8, which also invited 4,800 candidates in an all-program draw, with the same minimum CRS cut-off score. These recent draws demonstrate the ongoing commitment of Canada's immigration system to attract skilled individuals from around the world.

Meeting Immigration Targets

As Canada moves forward in its pursuit of a robust immigration system, achieving the annual immigration target of 465,000 for 2023 stands as a key objective. With the issuance of 220,003 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) so far in 2023, the nation is making significant progress in attracting and welcoming new residents.

The ITA count represents a remarkable milestone in the country's endeavor to meet its immigration goals. As the halfway mark is approached, the question arises: Will Canada successfully reach its ambitious target by the end of the year?

The issuance of 4,300 ITAs in the latest Express Entry draw on June 27, 2023, contributes to the growing number of newcomers. These invitations allow eligible candidates to pursue their immigration aspirations and contribute to Canada's social and economic fabric.

In an effort to broaden the immigrant pool and cater to labor market needs, Canada has introduced six new categories: healthcare, STEM professions, trades, transport, agriculture and agri-food, and strong French-speaking abilities. These categories aim to attract skilled individuals who possess relevant work or language experience, further enhancing the diversity and talent within the country.

While progress has been substantial, the path to achieving the 465,000 target is still ahead. The Canadian government remains committed to ensuring a fair and transparent immigration system that not only fulfills the economic demands of the country but also upholds the values of diversity, inclusivity, and social cohesion.

As the year unfolds, Canada continues to adapt and refine its immigration policies to address emerging challenges and opportunities. The collaboration between the federal government, provinces, territories, and stakeholders plays a vital role in ensuring the success of Canada's immigration objectives.

The positive momentum and dedication exhibited thus far instill confidence that Canada is on track to meet its immigration target for 2023. As the number of ITAs issued steadily rises, Canada's commitment to attracting skilled individuals and facilitating their integration into Canadian society remains resolute.

The continued efforts of IRCC, along with the nation's collective commitment to immigration, signal a promising future for newcomers and the country. Together, they contribute to Canada's reputation as a land of opportunity, where individuals can build fulfilling lives and contribute to the vibrant multicultural fabric that defines the nation.

For more Canadian Immigration News, like and subscribe to our Youtube Channel and follow us on our social media platforms below.

Celebrating Canada Day

As the immigration system progresses and the country continues to welcome newcomers, Canada gears up to celebrate its national holiday, Canada Day, on July 1st. This federal statutory holiday commemorates the anniversary of Canadian Confederation, which took place on July 1, 1867, marking the formation of Canada as a dominion within the British Empire.

Across the country, Canada Day is celebrated through various activities organized in communities nationwide. It presents an opportunity for both Canadians and newcomers to engage in festivities, forge connections, and create lasting memories with loved ones.

As the year unfolds, the IRCC's commitment to fostering immigration and facilitating the integration of newcomers remains steadfast. The achievements thus far and the forthcoming celebrations paint a promising picture of Canada's inclusive society and its ongoing pursuit of excellence in immigration and nation-building.
