Work in Canada With the New Innovation Stream Employer-specific Canadian Work Permit

by 
Shireen Fisher

Author:

Shireen Fisher

BTech Degree in Journalism, Diploma in Journalism

Shireen Fisher is a widely-published writer with nine years of experience. She started her media career in radio before heading back to university to pursue her National Diploma and BTech Degree in Journalism.

. Updated: September 25th, 2024
4 min

Canada's tech industry is experiencing unprecedented growth, fueled by a combination of innovation, investment, and a thriving startup ecosystem. The country has implemented a comprehensive Tech Talent Strategy to support this expansion and ensure that it remains competitive on the global stage. A vital component of this strategy is the Innovation Stream, a new immigration pathway designed to attract skilled tech workers worldwide.

Canada's Tech Talent Strategy: The Innovation Stream

The International Mobility Program (IMP) allows Canadian employers to hire foreign workers without a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA). The Innovation Stream, therefore, offers a streamlined process for obtaining a Canadian work permit for tech professionals. Applicants who have been offered a position in a high-skilled occupation by an employer involved in the Global Hypergrowth Project (GHP) now qualify to apply for an employer-specific work permit through this Stream.

This type of work permit allows you to work in Canada under certain conditions, including:

  • The specific employer you're permitted to work for
  • The duration of your employment
  • The location where you can work, if applicable

By eliminating the need for an LMIA, which can be time-consuming and costly, the program aims to expedite the immigration process and facilitate the recruitment of top talent.

Eligibility Criteria for the New Work Permit

To qualify for the Innovation Stream, applicants must meet several criteria:

  • A Job Offer: Applicants must have a valid job offer from a Canadian employer participating in the Global Hypergrowth Project (GHP).
  • NOC Classification: The offered position must fall under the National Occupational Classification (NOC) TEER categories 0, 1, 2, or 3, indicating a high-skilled occupation.
  • Education and Experience: Applicants must possess the necessary education and experience specified in the NOC for their intended occupation.
  • Language Proficiency: While not explicitly stated, applicants may be required to demonstrate a certain level of English or French proficiency, especially for positions that involve significant communication with clients or colleagues.

The Application Process

The application process for the Innovation Stream is relatively straightforward. Applicants must note that to apply for this Canadian Work Permit, their employer will first have to:

  • Provide an offer of employment
  • Make a payment of 230 CAD for the employer compliance fee
  • Provide an offer of employment number

Applicants will need to complete the following steps.

Step 1: Gather Required Documents

This includes the job offer, proof of education, work experience, and language proficiency (if applicable).

Step 2: Submit Online Application

Applicants can submit their application online through the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) portal.

Step 3: Provide Biometrics

If required, applicants may need to provide their biometrics (fingerprints and photograph) at a designated visa application center.

Step 4: Medical Exam

Applicants may also undergo a medical examination to meet Canada's health requirements.

Step 5: Apply Online

To apply online, applicants must have access to a scanner or camera to create electronic copies of documents and a valid credit or debit card.

Points to Note

Non-GHP Applicants Can Apply For Other Canadian Work Permits

Foreign workers who don't meet the above criteria might still qualify for a different type of Canadian work permit.

Applicants Can Bring Their Families

Spouses or common-law partners and dependent children may qualify for an open work permit as family members of a high-skilled worker.

You May Qualify For Quicker Processing

Applicants could qualify for expedited processing if their occupation falls under TEER categories 0 or 1 in the NOC system.

Level up Your Tech Career in Canada

Canada offers a wealth of opportunities for tech professionals, from startups to established corporations. The country's thriving tech ecosystem is characterized by a supportive government, world-class universities, and a diverse and talented workforce.

By choosing to work in Canada, tech professionals can enjoy:

  • Competitive Salaries: Canada offers competitive salaries and benefits for tech workers.
  • High Quality of Life: Canada consistently ranks high in international surveys for quality of life, with factors such as safety, education, and healthcare contributing to its appeal.
  • Work-Life Balance: Canadians are known for their emphasis on work-life balance, and the country offers a variety of outdoor activities and cultural experiences.
  • Career Growth Opportunities: Canada's growing tech industry provides ample opportunities for career advancement and professional development.
