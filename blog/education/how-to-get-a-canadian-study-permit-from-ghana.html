How to Get a Canadian Study Permit From Ghana

by 
Shireen Fisher

Author:

Shireen Fisher

BTech Degree in Journalism, Diploma in Journalism

Shireen Fisher is a widely-published writer with nine years of experience. She started her media career in radio before heading back to university to pursue her National Diploma and BTech Degree in Journalism.

. Updated: May 15th, 2024
7 mins

For many Ghanaian students, studying in Canada represents a dream a chance to gain a world-class education, experience a vibrant culture, and build a promising future. But before you pack your bags and book your flight to become a Ghanaian international student in Canada, you must know how to navigate the process of obtaining a Canadian study permit. This comprehensive guide will equip you with the knowledge and steps required to secure your Canadian Study Permit from Ghana.

Understanding the Canadian Study Permit

Immigration Refugees and Citizenship (IRCC) has made 485,000 approved study permits available for 2024. A Canadian study permit allows you, a foreign national, to enroll and attend a Designated Learning institution (DLI) in Canada. It's crucial to understand that a study permit doesn't equate to a visa. While a study permit authorizes your studies, a visa grants you permission to enter Canada. The IRCC will issue you a Visitor Visa (temporary resident visa) or Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA). This depends on your passport.

Eligibility Requirements For Ghanaian Students to Get a Canadian Study Permit

To pursue your studies in Canada, you'll need to meet specific criteria established by IRCC. Here's a breakdown of the key requirements.

Requirement Description
Enrollment at a Designated Learning Institution  Acceptance into a DLI is the cornerstone of your study permit application. DLIs are universities, colleges, and other institutions authorized to host international students.
Financial Stability Demonstrate sufficient financial resources to cover your tuition fees and living expenses in Canada. This can be proven through bank statements, scholarships, or documented support from a sponsor.
Planned Return Convince the immigration officer that you intend to return to Ghana upon completion of your studies. This can be established through strong ties to your home country, such as family or employment prospects.
Adherence to the Law Maintain a clean criminal record and comply with Canadian laws throughout your stay. You may be required to obtain a police certificate from your home country.
Good Health If necessary, undergo a medical examination by a physician approved by IRCC. This ensures you don't pose a health risk to Canada's public health system.

How to Apply For a Canadian Study Permit From Ghana

Now that you understand the eligibility criteria, you must follow these steps to apply for a Canadian Study Visa.

Step 1: Decide on a Designated Learning Institution

Research Canadian universities and colleges that align with your academic interests and career goals. Explore programs offered by DLIs and ensure they suit your aspirations. Once you find your ideal Canadian university, apply as soon as possible to get a letter of acceptance.

Step 2: Gather Everything You Need

Here's a checklist of documents you'll likely need for your application:

  • A valid Ghanaian passport and proof of identity
  • A letter of acceptance from a DLI
  • A provincial attestation letter (PAL) from the province or territory plan to study in
  • Proof of financial support
  • Medical exam results (if applicable)
  • A letter of explanation
  • Academic transcripts
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs
  • Proof of language proficiency (English or French)

Step 3: Read the Online Instruction Guide

IRCC provides detailed online instructions specific to Ghanaian applicants. Carefully review these instructions to ensure you understand the specific requirements and any additional documents you need.

Step 4: Prepare Your Answers

The online application form might include questions regarding your study plans, future career aspirations, and financial resources. Prepare clear and concise answers demonstrating your genuine intention to study in Canada and return to Ghana upon completion.

Step 5: Understand Which Fees Are Payable

There are associated fees with the study permit application process. These include the application fee, biometrics fee (fingerprints and photographs), and the cost of the medical exam (if required). Familiarize yourself with the current fee structure and ensure you have the necessary funds to cover them.

Step 6: Set up an Online Account

You can apply for your study permit online through the IRCC website. Setting up an online account allows you to submit your application electronically, upload documents, and track the application's progress.

Step 7: Submit Your Application

Once you've compiled all the required documents and completed the online application form, submit it electronically. Ensure all information is accurate and complete to ensure timely processing.

Beyond the Permit Application

IRCC works diligently to process your study permit application. Here's a breakdown of what to expect.

Biometrics and Medical Exam

After submitting your application, you might be instructed to provide biometric information (fingerprints and photographs) at a designated visa application center. You may also need to undergo a medical examination by an IRCC-approved physician.

IRCC Completeness Check

IRCC will meticulously review your application to ensure it includes all the necessary documents. Incomplete applications might be returned for resubmission or put on hold until missing documents are received.

Additional Information Requests

In some cases, IRCC may request further information or an interview with their officials in your home country to gain a clearer understanding of your application.

Medical Exams and Police Certificates

Depending on your situation, you might need to undergo a medical examination or obtain a police certificate from your home country. These requirements help ensure public health and safety in Canada.

Missing Documents

Don't worry if you couldn't submit everything initially. You can utilize your online account or a designated web form to submit missing documents later. Remember, a complete application is essential for timely processing.

Keeping Your Information Updated

If your contact details (address, phone number, etc.) change after applying, be sure to update your application promptly through the IRCC portal.

Application Approval

Upon receiving a study permit approval, you'll either receive it:

  • At the Port of Entry: If you're arriving in Canada for the first time.
  • By Mail: If you're already in Canada when your application is approved.

Applicants from Outside Canada

If you applied from abroad, IRCC will send you a "port of entry letter of introduction." This letter confirms your authorization to study in Canada, but it's not your study permit. Remember to carry this letter with you and present it to a border services officer upon arrival in Canada.

By understanding this process, you can ensure a smooth and efficient journey toward securing your Canadian study permit.

Why Canadian Universities Are Ideal For Ghanaian Students

Canada's reputation as a welcoming and multicultural nation extends to its educational institutions. Here's why Canadian universities could be the perfect fit for your academic journey.

World-Class Education

Canadian universities consistently rank high globally, offering a rigorous and internationally recognized education. You'll gain valuable knowledge and skills from esteemed professors at the forefront of their fields. Learn more about the top public universities for International students in Canada.

Diverse and Inclusive Environment

Canada celebrates diversity, and its universities reflect that. You'll encounter a vibrant mix of cultures and backgrounds, fostering a rich learning environment that broadens your perspective.

Focus on Practical Learning

Many Canadian programs incorporate practical learning experiences like internships or co-op placements. This allows you to gain valuable real-world experience that complements your academic studies and enhances your future employability.

Pathway to Permanent Residency

Canada offers immigration programs that can lead to permanent residency for international graduates with Canadian degrees and work experience. This opens doors to exciting career prospects and a chance to build a life in Canada.

A Post Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) helps you gain valuable Canadian work experience and unlocks your potential for permanent residency through the Express Entry's Canadian Experience Class (CEC) program.

The CEC streamlines the transition for international graduates with Canadian experience to become permanent residents. This program prioritizes individuals familiar with Canadian life who can contribute positively to the economy. Strong English or French language skills, coupled with qualifying work experience under your PGWP, become significant assets for a successful CEC application.

Safe and Stable Society

Canada boasts a high quality of life with low crime rates and a stable political system. This creates a secure and supportive environment where you can focus on your studies and personal growth.

Affordable Education

Compared to other popular study destinations, tuition fees at Canadian universities can be more affordable. The cost of living in Canada is also lower than in some other developed countries, so you will be able to live a comfortable student life in this land of maple leaves.

Scholarships and Funding Opportunities

Numerous scholarships and financial aid programs are available from Canadian institutions and organizations to support international students. Research these opportunities to lessen the financial burden of your studies.

FAQs

Can I Work in Canada While Studying?

Yes, international students in Canada can work part-time. You're allowed to work up to 20 hours per week during regular school semesters or while studying as part of an intensive program with no planned breaks. This restriction ensures your studies remain the primary focus.

What Kind of Healthcare Can I Get as a Ghanaian International Student in Canada?

Ghanaian students in Canada require health insurance. Many universities offer healthcare plans, while others require using a designated provider. Research these options and your own healthcare needs to make an informed decision. Explore provincial health insurance options and consider supplemental coverage if needed. By planning ahead, you can ensure access to quality healthcare throughout your Canadian studies.
