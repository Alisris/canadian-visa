Canada Student Visa Requirements Explained

by Kayla Peters. Updated: March 29th, 2023
5 min read

Reviewed by David Allon

Reviewed by:

David Allon

A professional and knowledgeable RCIC with a passion for migration and international policies. He will ensure that you are provided with the best immigration service and business guidance on your journey to Canada.

Editorial Guidelines
David AllonLearn More

Choosing Canada as your study destination can be more than just earning a qualification in one of the most educated countries in the world. It can also mean choosing your next permanent home. Thats right, you receive top-tier education as an international student and have the opportunity to become a permanent resident in Canada.

Three in ten international students who arrived in Canada after 2000 transitioned to permanent residency within ten years of receiving their first study permit. Check if you meet the Canada student visa requirements and push your education and life to the next level!

How Can I Study in Canada?

Regardless of your study program or the length of the program, all foreign nationals who want to study in Canada will need to get a study permit. To apply for the permit, youll have to meet the following requirements:

  • Proof of acceptance to a Designated Learning Institution (DLI).
  • Proof of Identity.
  • Proof of sufficient funds for tuition and living expenses for you and your family who come to Canada with you. Your return transport will need to be covered in the funds as well;
  • You should have no criminal record and obey the law. You may need to get a police certificate if requested;
  • You need to be in good health. You may also be required to get a medical exam; and
  • Lastly, you must prove to an officer that you will leave Canada once your study visa expires.

Additional documents that you may also need include:

  • A letter of explanation - this will help the officer understand why you want to study in Canada;
  • Certificat dacceptation du Qubec - this certificate is needed if you want to study longer than six months in Quebec;
  • A custodian declaration (for minors only) - A custodian is a responsible adult who is a Canadian citizen or a permanent resident who will take care of and support the minor child; and
  • Other documents - this may depend on your country or region.

A DLI is a school approved by the provincial or territorial government for international students.

When Should I Apply for the Spring 2022 Intake in Canada?

Start applying to universities or colleges from October 2021 to December 2021. Most spring intake schools in Canada 2021 will have the deadline set in the month from October to February. Its important to keep an eye on the schools website for updated May 2022 intake and ensure you apply before the due date.

What is the Student Direct Stream?

The Student Direct Stream (SDS) is a way of obtaining a study permit more quicker. The processing time can be as short as 20 calendar days. To apply through the SDS, you need to be a resident of one of the following countries:

Did You Know

International students studying full-time and with valid study permits may bring their spouse or common-law partners along. Not only that, but your partner will arrive in Canada on an open work visa, which allows them to work anywhere in Canada while you study.

Why do Canada Study Permits get Rejected?


Its very important to have a detailed study plan that is logical and convincing enough to show the officer that the main purpose of your coming to Canada is to study. The program you choose needs to make sense to the immigration officer, and youll need to explain how the qualification youre earning in Canada will help you when you return to your country of origin.

The failure to do the above is one of the most common reasons for refusing a study visa. Beyond the reason mentioned, proof of finance and lack of authentic documents are also why your visa can be denied.

David Allon, one of the Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants (RCICs) we work with, answers a frequently asked question below.

Question: What are some typical issues that I can prevent in order to increase my chances of getting the study permit?
Answer: You need to create as many ties to the country of residence or country of birth to convince the visa officer you will leave Canada after studying.

Top Courses to Study in Canada for International Students

Applying for a course with a higher success rate of getting a job after graduation is always a great idea. Many of the curriculums in Canada are very industry-oriented, making it much easier to get employed after graduation.

Below are the four most popular fields of study for international students:

  1. Business and Finance;
  2. Computer Science and IT;
  3. Health and Medicine; and
  4. Engineering

The careers mentioned above have occupations on one or more of the 11 provinces' in-demand occupations lists. This means your chances of finding a job after your studies are good.

International students who want to work in Canada after graduation can do so via the Post-Graduation Work Permit program.

Post-Graduation Work Permit


The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) allows international students to live and work in Canada with an open work visa. This visa will allow you to work in any location and for any Canadian employer. The PGWP is only for international students who have completed their studies and obtained a qualification from a DLI.

Your PGWP validity will be according to the length of your study program. Take a closer look at the table below to better understand the validity of your visa.

Determine the Length of Your PGWP
Length of Study Program Length of Work Permit
Eight months - two years You may receive a PGWP valid for up to the same length as your study program.
Two years or more You may receive a 3-year PGWP.
If you completed more than one program You may receive a PGWP valid for the combined programs' length. Each program must be PGWP eligible and at least eight months in length.

Did You Know?

International student graduates who worked during their time of study or after graduation were more likely to become Canadian permanent residents.

Many students use studying to work in or immigrate to Canada. Join over 530,000 international students by ensuring you meet the Canada student visa requirements.

How Studying in Canada Leads to Immigration

Having Canadian work experience is extremely valuable if you want to immigrate to Canada, as it makes you eligible for programs such as the Canadian Experience Class (CEC). The CEC is designed for foreign nationals seeking permanent residency in Canada who have gained Canadian work experience through a temporary work permit.

The CEC falls under the Express Entry system making it easy to apply from anywhere in the world, as Express Entry is an online system. You also wont need proof of settlement funds, reducing the processing time to three or four months.
