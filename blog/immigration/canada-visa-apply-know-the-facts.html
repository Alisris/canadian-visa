Canada Visa Apply When You Know the Fact

by Kelsey. Updated: August 22nd, 2018

Of course, if Canadian immigration was simple everyone would do it. Sadly immigration can be one of the most complicated processes if you do not know how it works. Canada Visa apply when you know the facts.

First of all, you must know if you need a visa. Some people do not need work visas when they come to Canada. This applies for people who are visiting either for short periods like:

  • Business people meetings with Canadian companies; or
  • Sports coaches that have come with their team to compete against Canadian teams

There are also some countries that have such a good relationship with Canada that they do not need a visa at all. The U.S. is one of these countries. All you need as a citizen of the U.S. is your ID to show to border officials.

eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization)

What is an eTA and what does it have to do with Canadian immigration? Check out the video below.

There are some countries that do not need this travel authorization, this is when you already have a work, study or visitor visa for Canada.

You will also not need one if you are a permanent resident of Canada, so long as your residency card has not expired.

Medical Check

If you plan to work, study or visit Canada for less than six months, you will likely not need a medical check.

But if you have lived longer than six months in any of these countries, you will most likely need a medical check to apply for your visa.

Just remember that you cannot go to just any doctor for this medical check. It must be done by medical professionals from the Panel of Physicians.

These tests will be valid for one year only so make sure that when you apply, your medical check will not expire before your application process ends.

Processing Times, Know How Long it Takes

If you have a specific visa in mind you must make sure you know how long the process can take.

Programs like Express Entry aim for a processing time of six months or less. While some programs like the Global Talent Stream aim for a two week processing time.

If you want to visit as a tourist, you should know that a Visitor Visa takes around one month to be completed from start to finish.

The best way to go forward is to know the requirements of each visa you want to apply for. This will save you time and money in the long run.

Budget Appropriately

Do you know how much the paperwork and application fees will be? Whether you immigrate to Canada alone or with your family you need to know how far your budget can stretch.

If for example, you are applying to move to Canada, but your children are staying with your family in Australia, you must prove you have enough funds to support them and yourself.

Just so you know, dependants are:

  • Under the age of 22; and
  • Not married; or
  • Physically or mentally dependant on you

Some programs will require that if you are a single adult, you must have at least CAD12,500 to support yourself until you become self-sufficient in the country.

Other programs will require you to complete things like an ECA (Educational Credential Assessment), medical checkups or even get your biometrics done.

Always make sure you know the costs involved before you go ahead with your application.

Authorized Representatives

RCICs (Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants) and immigration lawyers are the only professionals you can hire for paid immigration consultancy and services. They are called authorized representatives and are acknowledged by the Canadian government and other regulatory boards.

If your friend or someone you know offers to help with the application for a fee do not accept! Unless they are an authorized representative, your immigration application will be rejected by the IRCC (Immigration Refugee Citizenship Canada).

You can check that certain people are actually representatives by checking out registries like the ICCRC (Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council). Each RCIC has a number that you can use to identify them.
