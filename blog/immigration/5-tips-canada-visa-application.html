Editorial Guidelines
David AllonLearn More

So much can go wrong with your Canada visa application. Strict deadlines, heaps of documentation, tests and qualifications, even the smallest error with any of these can cost you your entire application. We know its very difficult to keep up with the ever-changing world of Canadian immigration.

Dont let the small things dash your dreams of a Canadian life. We have put together a list of 5 top tips guaranteed to improve your applications chance of success. Save your money, avoid mistakes and follow our advice. Lets get you to Canada.

Choose the Right Visa

There are over a hundred different immigration programs and streams available for prospective immigrants to Canada. Each of these pathways has specific requirements and eligibility criteria that need to be met if you would like to immigrate to Canada.

Maybe you dont want to immigrate, maybe you just want to visit or experience a working holiday in the Great White North. If that is the case, getting to Canada may be easier.

With the aforementioned example in mind, we recommend that you take a look at all the options available to you and narrow them down to the programs that will enable you to satisfy your needs of traveling or immigrating to Canada.

In particular, we recommend that you only apply to immigration programs and streams that youre eligible for as per the results you would have received after taking the eligibility test.

Do not apply for an immigration visa program that doesnt allow you to do what you want to do in the country and dont apply for a program that has very strict requirements which you cannot meet when there is another easier to qualify for option available to you. Put succinctly, make the right choice.

As a means to illustrate the different eligibility requirements for distinct immigration programs and how these influence an applicants decision with regards to choosing the right visa program, heres an outline of two of the most commonly used immigration programs: Express Entry and Provincial Nominee programs.

Express Entry

With application processing times as fast as six months, the Express Entry system is the most popular immigration program among prospective immigrants to Canada. It is largely open to workers that are able to pass the eligibility test on the basis of their personal demographic data such as:

  • Age
  • Education
  • Medical history
  • Work experience
  • Marital status
  • Links to Canada

As per the answers you provide in the eligibility criteria, a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score will be generated on your behalf, which plays a determining factor that will allow you to gauge which job category you're placed in within the National Occupational Classification (NOC) and TEER system.

Your placement on this system will influence your eligibility to apply either for a Federal Skilled Trades program, Federal Skilled Workers program and Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

Provincial Nominee Program

Provincial Nominee programs are used by Canadian provinces to address their labor shortages. Each province has a list of in-demand occupations which in collaboration with the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) they use to source eligible workers through the issue of invitations to apply (ITA).

To see if you fall within the purview of eligibility for one of the provincial nominee programs, please take a look at the following list of PNPs.

  • Alberta
  • British Columbia
  • Manitoba
  • New Brunswick
  • Nova Scotia
  • Northwest Territories
  • Newfoundland and Labrador
  • Ontario
  • Prince Edward Island
  • Quebec
  • Saskatchewan
  • Yukon

Get Familiar With PNPs

If you want to immigrate to Canada, then you will have to get to know the Provincial Nomination Programs (PNPs). Nearly all of Canadas provinces and territories have their own variation (with multiple subcategories) of this program.

Normally, PNPs enable provinces and territories to nominate people, who have the skillset and work experience needed in the province, to apply for permanent residence if they agree to living and working in the province for a certain amount of time.

There are a few different methods to follow in order to get nominated. One way is to get in contact with the province and express your interest- called an expression of interest (EOI) in living there in order to be considered for nomination.

Another way to get the attention of a province is to create a profile outlining your core human capital (language skills, work experience, education etc.) and hopefully, they will get into contact with you.

As always, each of the PNP programs and their various subcategories has specific requirements. You will need to get familiar with these in order to immigrate following this route.

Apply Early

This tip is simple enough to understand, apply early. The earlier you apply the more time you have to fix any mistakes you might have made. If you rush to complete documentation in order to meet the strict deadlines of Immigration, Refugees Citizenship Canada (IRCC).

Many dont know this, but if you are interested in immigrating through Express Entry, you can also benefit from applying early. As a federal immigration program that can boast about having application processing times that are as fast as 6 months, the sooner you apply for Express Entry the higher the chances your application has to be prioritized and possibly fast-tracked to success.

During some Express Entry draws, the tie-break rule is used to determine who gets Invitations to Apply for permanent residence. Basically, when a certain number of invitations are issued and a bunch of people have the minimum CRS (Comprehensive Ranking System) score, then the people who entered the Express Entry pool first will be issued invitations. In this way, you will benefit from applying early.

Use a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant

The importance of an RCIC cannot be stressed enough. An RCIC or Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant is a professional immigration expert who has the legal authority to assist you with your Canada visa application.

RCICs can be trusted for their professionalism if they are registered with the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants (CICC). The council regulates the professionalism and credibility of RCICs. If you are wondering whether you can trust your RCIC, simply run their number through the CICCs registry to find out if they are in good standing with the council.

What is the Most Effective tip you can use for Your Canada Visa Application?

Is There a Canada Visa Immigration Application Dedicated to Refugees?

Canada offers immigration services for refugees who wish to find a safe haven and refuge within Canada. This is possible through either sponsorship by a relative benefactor or through claiming asylum.

What is the Average Cost of a Canada Visa Application?

The average cost of a Canada visa application cost is usually divided between the cost of the specific immigration visa application or the general application fee. The general application is usually a flat rate of 100 Canadian dollars (C$).
