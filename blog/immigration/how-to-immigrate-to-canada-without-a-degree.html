How to Immigrate to Canada Without a Degree

by 
Kelia Losa

Editorial Guidelines
David AllonLearn More

Did you know that you don't necessarily need a degree to immigrate to Canada? That's right! Contrary to popular belief, moving to Canada doesn't require a degree. Canada actively seeks workers of all skill levels and sets, including semi-skilled and tradespeople. Immigration statistics reveal that the country values diversity and offers opportunities to individuals without formal qualifications to build a new life in Canada. Canada has plenty of options for you, no matter your skill set or work experience. The key is finding the right immigration opportunity for you. Moving to Canada with no qualifications is not only possible; there are specific programs that cater to potential workers and immigrants just like you.

Step-By-Step Guide to Immigrate to Canada Without a Degree

Are you interested in immigrating to Canada and working without a degree? While having a degree can be beneficial, it is not always required for certain occupations in Canada. This step-by-step guide will walk you through immigrating and working in Canada without a degree.

Step 1: Find Out if You Need a Work Permit

Not all Canadian occupations require a work permit. Some jobs are work permit exempted. If you are unsure whether your desired occupation requires a work permit, you can seek assistance to obtain information on these jobs. However, if you do need a work permit, you can secure one through the Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP) or the International Mobility Program (IMP).

Step 2: Prepare an Updated Resume

One of the crucial steps in your immigration journey is to prepare an updated and polished resume. Your resume serves as your passport to secure an interview, so showcasing your relevant skills and work experience is important. Since you don't have a degree, highlight your relevant skills to the position you are applying for. Additionally, make sure to format your resume according to the Canadian format.

Step 3: Start Scouting for Canadian Jobs That You Want to Apply For

Once your resume is ready, you can start searching for Canadian jobs that align with your skills and work experience. Utilize Canadian job portals available on the internet, such as Canada Job Bank and Career Builder. You can also check the official Job Bank of Canada for job opportunities.

Step 4: Start Your Application Today

The most important step is taking action. Start sending out your applications to Canadian immigration. If you need guidance throughout the process, canadianvisa.org can assist you. They can help you determine which visa program you qualify for and provide support throughout the application process.

Following these steps, you can begin your journey to immigrating and working in Canada without a degree. Remember, while a degree may not be a requirement, having relevant skills and a polished resume can greatly increase your chances of success. Start your application today and take the first step toward your Canadian dream!

Choose Your Immigration Route

Explore the diverse pathways available and pave your way to a new life in your dream destination. Embark on your immigration journey confidently as you select the best route tailored to your qualifications, skills, and aspirations.

The Express Entry System

The Express Entry system is aimed at helping highly-skilled foreigners immigrate to Canada in the fastest and most efficient way possible. The Express Entry System has three major programs:

  • The Federal Skilled Worker Program
  • The Federal Skilled Trades Program
  • The Canada Experience Class

While The Federal Skilled Worker Program requires a university degree, Express Entry's two other programs, namely The Federal Skilled Trades Program and The Canadian Experience Class, do not. Each program has its own set of requirements. However, your eligibility for the Express Entry system depends on the following factors:

Express Entry Eligibility Criteria
Nationality
Age
Language Ability
Education
Work experience
Links to Canada

Each criteria will allow you to score points. The higher your score, the better your chances of application success! Find out what your score could be with our CRS Calculator.

Provincial Nominee Programs

If you are a foreign worker who wants to immigrate to Canada and have a valid job offer of at least one year, then The Provincial Nominee Program may be the way forward for you. Eleven of Canada's provinces and territories have unique programs and requirements, including occupation in-demand programs that allow you to apply through Express-Entry linked programs, streamlining the application process. The key to success here is to find out where your job is in-demand in Canada. The more in demand it is, the higher your chances of receiving a Provincial Nomination (PN) to apply for permanent residency in Canada!

The Atlantic Immigration Pilot

The Atlantic Immigration Pilot is aimed at foreign workers who want to immigrate to one of Canada's Atlantic provinces.

  • Newfoundland & Labrador
  • Prince Edward Island
  • New Brunswick
  • Nova Scotia

To apply to live and work in Atlantic Canada through the AIP, you will need to meet the requirements of one of the following programs:

Atlantic Immigration Programs
Atlantic High-Skilled Program
  • Have a degree, diploma, or other qualification from a publicly funded Atlantic education institution
  • have lived in an Atlantic Canadian province for a minimum of 16 months out of the two years before obtaining your post-secondary qualification
  • Language test (English or French)
  • Sufficient funds to support you and your family members
Atlantic Intermediate-Skilled Program
  • Havehave at least one year of work experience in a TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 job in the last three years
  • at least a Canadian high school diploma or equivalent
  • language test (CLB or NCLC Level 4 in English or French)
  • Sufficient funds to support you and your family members
  • Valid full-time non-seasonal job offer of at least one year from an employer in Atlantic Canada
Atlantic Home Care Program
  • have at least one year of work experience in a TEER 3 occupation or as a registered nurse, licensed nurse, nurse's aide, or home support worker in the last three years
  • at least a Canadian high school diploma or equivalent
  • language test (CLB or NCLC Level 4 in English or French)
  • Sufficient funds to support you and your family members
  • Valid full-time non-seasonal job offer of at least one year from an employer in Atlantic Canada

The Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot

The Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot (RNIP) s your ticket to immigrate to Canada without a degree if you are interested in moving to one of 11 participating cities and towns in Ontario, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Alberta, and British Columbia. To qualify, you will need to meet the following eligibility requirements:

RNIP Eligibility Requirements
Have at least one year of work experience in the last three years
At least a Canadian high school diploma, post-secondary qualification, or equivalent
Language test in English or French (CLB/NCLC 6) for TEER 0 and 1; CLB/NCLC 5 for TEER 2 and 3, and CLB/NCLC 4 for TEER 4 & 5
Sufficient funds to support you and your family members
Valid full-time non-seasonal job offer from an employer in one of the 11 participating communities

If you're an international student in Canada, you do not have to meet the work experience requirements for this pilot. However, you must present a credential from a post-secondary program that was at least two years long or a master's degree or higher if your program is under two years.

Agri-Food Pilot

The Agri-food Pilot (AIP), which launched in May 2020, is aimed at helping skilled workers in the meat processing and manufacturing sectors, as well as the agricultural and animal production sectors, immigrate to Canada

To qualify, you must meet the following criteria:

AIP Eligibility Requirements
Have at least one year of full-time non-seasonal work experience in the last three years in an eligible occupation
A Canadian high school diploma or equivalent
Language test in English or French (CLB/NCLC 4)
Sufficient funds to support you and your family members
Valid full-time non-seasonal job offer in an eligible occupation

The Home Care Provider Pilots

Home care providers are in high demand in Canada. Because of this, the Canadian government has dedicated two specific Home Care Provider Pilots to help home support workers and home child care providers live and work in Canada.

The eligibility requirements are:

Caregiver Pilot Eligibility Requirements
Have at least two years of full-time Canadian work experience in the last three years in an eligible occupation or a valid full-time job offer in an eligible occupation
Relevant post-secondary educational qualification of at least 1 year
Language test in English or French (CLB/NCLC 4)
Sufficient funds to support you and your family members
Supporting documents showing you are capable of doing the job, i.e. proof of relevant work experience and education credentials

Are you still asking yourself, can I immigrate to Canada without a degree? You may have noticed that many degree-exempt programs require you to find a job first, so let's see what Canada offers.

Skill Levels Breakdown

There are various Canadian immigration programs to choose from, and a lot more for those without degrees than you may think. The three important factors when looking for the right immigration program are your skill level, work experience, and whether or not you have a job offer.

High-skilled jobs in Canada are usually classified at NOC TEER level 0, 1, or 2, while intermediate or low-skilled occupations are usually classified as TEER 3, 4 or 5.

Teer Level Description
TEER 1 Jobs for professionals like doctors, lawyers, and accountants
TEER 2 Technical or skilled trade jobs such as chefs, plumbers, or electricians
TEER 3 Jobs in the processing, transportation, and hospitality sectors, such as retail butchers, long-haul truck drivers, and roofers
TEER 4 Jobs like desk clerks, receptionists, and bartenders
TEER 5 Jobs like fruit pickers, cleaning staff, and food and beverage servers

Now that you know which skill level you fall under and what education requirement may be expected of you, moving to Canada with no qualifications shouldn't seem so far-fetched. Let's look at the top 6 ways to immigrate to Canada without a degree.

Best Jobs in Canada Without a Degree

Having a university degree or prior experience is often associated with a successful or well-paying career. However, it's worth noting that in Canada, many job opportunities don't necessarily require a degree or specific work experience in a particular field. By understanding the highest-paying professions available without a degree or experience, you can make informed decisions about your employment options. Canada values skills and experience, and as a result, you can explore various occupations where a university degree is not a prerequisite.

Miners

National average salary: 78,609 CAD per year.

In an underground setting, miners are in charge of mining materials, including ore, rocks, and coal. They also create supporting routes to facilitate their entry and exit from the mines. While moving the rocks and ore, they extracted to their ultimate places above ground. Miners carefully control explosives to shatter huge boulders or ores into smaller, easier-to-transport parts. In addition, they guarantee the safety of their coworkers and the area around them.

Maintenance Manager

National average salary: 75,003 per year

A maintenance manager develops protocols, puts them into practice, inspects facilities, spots problems, and fixes them. They are in charge of organizing and managing all construction-related repairs and installations.

Maintenance managers also maintain the hydraulic and electrical systems of a building. They also keep track of maintenance budgets, costs, and budgets for equipment, as well as connections with contractors and service providers.

Receptionists

National salary average: 33,150 CAD per year.

When visitors arrive at a building or institution, receptionists welcome them and point them in the right direction. They ensure the reception area is tidy, appealing, and has all the necessary supplies and stationery. Through phone calls and in-person interactions, the receptionist also gives correct basic office information.

Utilizing and maintaining office supplies like computers and copiers are among the technical abilities required for a receptionist. Additionally, receptionists need strong verbal and written communication skills, effective time-management and multitasking abilities, and positive customer service attitudes.

Warehouse Worker

National average salary: 32,175 CAD per year.

Orders are prepared and finished by warehouse personnel for pickup or delivery. This includes loading, packaging, wrapping, labeling, and shipping the order. They are also in charge of processing and receiving the merchandise for the warehouse. The same goes for tasks like unloading, labeling, and storing the goods.

Workers in the warehouse are also responsible for carrying out inventory controls and upholding a high quality for their audits. Strong organizational abilities, expertise with and knowledge of warehousing procedures and practices, and a current forklift license are some prerequisites for a warehouse worker.

Executive Chef

National salary average: 54,915 CAD per year

Among their many responsibilities, executive chefs organize and supervise the cooking process. To guarantee that the menus are of the highest caliber, they also work on tweaking or changing them. Similarly, senior chefs coordinate the tasks of the kitchen crew in addition to making plans for kitchen equipment purchases and repairs.

Finally, they address complaints or issues relating to meal services while providing their bosses with food expenses and requirements estimations. The position does not call for a degree.

Source: Talent.com

Moving to Canada with no Qualifications

Moving to Canada without qualifications can be challenging, but Canada is known for its welcoming and inclusive immigration policies. The country values diversity and recognizes the potential in individuals willing to contribute to society. While having qualifications can certainly improve your chances, Canada also provides opportunities for individuals without formal qualifications to start a new life.

Canada's immigration system is designed to attract skilled workers who can contribute to the country's economy and fill labor market gaps. However, Canada also acknowledges that qualifications aren't the sole measure of an individual's potential. When assessing applicants, they consider various factors, such as work experience, language proficiency, adaptability, and willingness to learn. If you demonstrate your commitment to building a life in Canada, your acceptance chances increase.

FAQs

Can I Get a Good Job Without a Degree in Canada?

Although a bachelor's degree is typically required for professional-level positions in Canada, candidates without a degree may still be eligible for a work visa. However, they must demonstrate skills, experience, education, and language proficiency to meet the employer's job requirements. There are also various highly-paid job opportunities in Canada in the hospital and mining industries

Can I Work in Canada With a Diploma?

Completing a certificate, diploma or degree at least eight months long qualifies you for a Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP), which enables you to stay in Canada and work full-time. You can work in any occupation in Canada, even one unrelated to your education, and you don't need a job offer to apply.

Can Foreigners Without a Degree Work in Canada?

Yes. Skilled work in Canada is always needed. Canada's job market is strained, not because of a lack of jobs still because there aren't enough local skilled workers to keep up with the number of available jobs in Canada, which is why Canada is looking to you, a skilled and experienced foreign worker looking to live and work in the Canadian community.
