Relocate to Canada from the U.A.E. and Get Permanent Residence for Your Family

by Robynn. Updated: November 11th, 2019

Due to the waning population, high retirement rate and job relocations to urban city centres and internal promotions, Canada are in urgent need of highly skilled workers, especially in engineering, managerial, medical, aerospace, financial and IT sectors.

Because these positions require highly skilled individuals and are in high demand, the job offers often come with attractive salary offers, making Canada a hotspot for motivated young professionals.

The Canadian government has not only made it easier to immigrate to Canada but has also sped up your visa application, making it both easier and quicker to have your application processed. The Express Entry system assists highly skilled individuals to immigrate to Canada in as little as 6 months, making it one of the most popular ways to migrate to Canada.

Canada has become a very popular destination for those looking to relocate, due to it’s welcoming nature, multiculturalism as well as benefits such as free public healthcare and schooling up until grade 12. If you are successful in your application and receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residence, your family members will also be able to enjoy these amazing benefits.

Canada has a very accommodating ex-pat community, especially in the Muslim community. Islam is the second-largest religion in Canada, making it extremely easy to integrate into the Canadian community. The Minister of Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship of Canada, The Honourable Ahmed Hussen, is Somali born and has been in charge of overseeing Canada’s federal immigration policies since 2017.

Interested in immigrating to Canada? Simply click the link below or keep reading to find out more about the Canadian visa application process.

1. Competitive Job Opportunities

Canada has a number of job opportunities available to highly skilled workers wanting to immigrate to Canada. Here are some of Canada’s top-earning occupations in 2019.

2. Free Public Healthcare and Education for You and Your Family

Canada offers an extremely high quality of life, including world-renowned public healthcare at no cost to all Canadian citizens and permanent residents. This benefit is also extended to your loved ones so that you can rest assured that your family will be well taken care of in the unfortunate event that they may need medical assistance.

Your children will also have access to free public education up until grade 12, which means that you will be able to save for their tertiary education and have the opportunity to send them to some of the top universities in the world.


3. Welcoming Expat Communities

Canada has an active Muslim ex-pat community. Most of Canada’s Muslim population resides in Ontario, Quebec, Alberta and British Columbia. There are many organisations designated to helping Muslim migrants adjust to their new homes abroad in Canada. For example, The Muslim Association of Canada is a non-profit organization offering educational and religious services to the Islamic community in Canada.
