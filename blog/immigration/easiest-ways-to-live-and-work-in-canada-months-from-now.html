Express Entry

The Express Entry system is not an immigration program but rather an online points-driven system that manages immigration applications: but why is Express Entry so popular? Because Express Entry is easy to navigate and yields quick results, making it one of the most preferred immigration pathways.

The Express Entry system has three versatile immigration options:

  • Canadian Experience Class
  • Federal Skilled Program
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program

Candidates would have to receive an Invitation To Apply (ITA)  before they can apply directly to programs. Whether or not you receive an ITA will depend on how thorough and truthful you are in the information you provide on your Express Entry profile. Those who qualify for the programs under Express Entry will have the option of applying for a temporary work permit.

Failure to remain truthful when providing your information can result in the rejection of your application. We therefore urge candidates to exercise integrity and honesty throughout the application process.

The Application Process under the Express Entry

If you wish to live and work in Canada, you can apply for permanent residency. You must, however, ensure that you're eligible to enter the country, as well as comply with the program criteria.

Relevant Pointers for those Making a Canada PR (Permanent Residency) Application through Express Entry:

  1. You would need a secure Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) account and sign in.
  2. Thereafter, you should find the Express Entry link to apply for permanent residence in Canada.
  3. Ensure all forms are filled out correctly and that no fields are omitted before submitting your application. This can cause a delay in processing times as your application will be sent back to you.
After Filling out Documents Manually, You Must:
  1. Fill out all fields correctly and ensure that the information is truthful.
  2. Scan and upload the documents.
  3. French or English documents may require a certified translator who should photocopy your original document that needed translation, and stamp it. The stamp should be in English or French.
  4. In the event of a translator unable to finish the translation, applicants can obtain an affidavit from the translator who managed to complete the translation.

The Provincial Nomination Program (PNP)

The Provincial Nomination Program (PNP)  is aimed at immigrants with various skill sets and skill levels. If you have what it takes to live and work in Canada, then this program is for you. The PNP is a province specific program, which means that foreign workers are selected based on the shortage of skills within the Canadian province.

With the right skills and experience for the job, you can be a successful nominee under PNP. Below we highlight the two methods in which you can apply for the PNP under Express Entry.

Option 1: Choosing a Province

Be sure to conduct the necessary research on the province before selecting a province. Once you are happy, choose a province of your choice. You can expect to be nominated according to the province you selected. After being nominated, your application will be evaluated for eligibility and you will be contacted for additional information.

Option 2: Creating an Express Entry Profile

Alternatively, you can create an Express Entry profile, showcasing the provinces/territories you would like a nomination from. Thereafter, you will be contacted via your Express Entry profile to inform you to apply through their stream. You can expect to be nominated electronically through your account.

Temporary Foreign Worker Program:

This program is ideal if youâ€™re seeking a job in Canada. Canadian employers will be maximizing the use of immigration programs, such as the Temporary Foreign Worker Program when seeking out global talent. Immigrants who are able to fill the skill shortage in a province or territory stand a good chance at succeeding.

Under this program, your employer will need to obtain a positive Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA), which serves as proof that your employer is authorized to hire an immigrant as a temporary worker.

Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP):

The AIP aims to target skilled foreign workers and international graduates who want to live and work in Canada and gain permanent residence. More so, if youâ€™re a skilled worker or an international student studying at a Canadian institution, you can live and work in one of Canadaâ€™s four Atlantic provinces. However, before applying, double-check the requirements to ensure eligibility.

Family Class Sponsorship:

If you are 18 years or older, you can bring your family members with you to live in Canada through family sponsorship. This is one of the most widely used streams, since the program makes it so easy to bring your spouse or sibling to Canada.

To sponsor a relative, you should be 18 years or older and be registered as a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, or be registered under the Canadian Indian Act. In addition to this, you should be able to financially support yourself and relatives by providing the necessary food, shelter, clothing and social assistance where needed.

The Express Entry system yields fast results and comprises three immigration programs that are highly versatile and accommodating: Canadian Experience Class, Federal Skilled Program and Federal Skilled Trades Program.

How Can I Get a Job in Canada Without Experience?

You can start by checking the available job vacancies in Canada. Check for your NOC 2021 code. Most job vacancies require you to have some form of experience or on-the-job training. While you can compromise on the level of education, you should have some experience or training within your occupation to work in Canada.

Can I Immigrate to Canada Without a Degree?

In most cases you would need to have a credential from a valid university or college. However, some jobs in Canada donâ€™t require a degree, such as some NOC C jobs may not require a degree but relevant on-the-job training.
