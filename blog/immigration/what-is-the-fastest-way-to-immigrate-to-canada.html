What is the Fastest Way to Immigrate to Canada?

Editorial Guidelines
David AllonLearn More

Are you wondering about the fastest way to immigrate to Canada? Look no further! Regarding swift immigration, Canada's Express Entry system is your best bet. By focusing on your skills and occupation, Express Entry allows you to enter the immigration pool, where you will be ranked based on a comprehensive points system.

However, remember that the "fastest" way to Canada ultimately depends on your circumstances. Your skills and occupation are vital in determining the most expedited path for you. So, if you're eager to embark on your Canadian journey, keep reading!

How to Migrate to Canada Fast

Discover how some immigration options offer quicker processing times and valuable insights to help you navigate the process efficiently. You could be on your way to Canada in just six short months! Make sure to check the IRCC processing times for your chosen immigration route.

The Express Entry System

The Express Entry System is one of the fastest and most efficient ways to immigrate to Canada. Once your application is accepted, it has an average visa process time of 6 months, and you can apply online, making it easily accessible for those who want to apply for permanent residence from anywhere in the world. There are 3 Federal Immigration Programs to choose from based on your age, language ability, education, skills, and work experience:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP);
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP); and
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

One of the great characteristics of the Express Entry system is that you only have to create an online profile once for the entire year, and you'll automatically be entered into draws every two weeks.

Top Tip:

If you qualify for Express Entry and have a job offer in a specific province, apply through one of Canada's 11 Express Entry Linked Provincial Nominee Programs. This can cut your application time by almost a third and reduce it to 6 months.

The Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP)

The AIP also has a 6-month processing time and has recently updated its application process by now allowing online applications for work permits! The AIP was designed to assist with Canadian immigration to Atlantic Canada, namely: Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Newfoundland & Labrador, and Prince Edward Island, through one of three programs:

  • Atlantic International Graduate Program;
  • Atlantic High-skilled Program; and
  • Atlantic Intermediate-skilled Program

Top Tip:

Visit our in-demand jobs in Canada pages to find out in which Atlantic Province your occupation is in demand to improve your chances of success in the application process. The more in-demand your job, the better your chances of getting hired and getting a visa through the AIP.

Start-Up Visa Canada

The Start-Up Visa Canada program offers a streamlined pathway for entrepreneurs and their families to immigrate to Canada and establish innovative start-up businesses. This program is designed to attract international entrepreneurs who have the potential to create jobs and contribute to the growth of Canada's economy.

The Start-Up Visa program requires applicants to have the support of a designated Canadian business incubator, angel investor group, or venture capital fund. Once accepted into the program, applicants can benefit from a fast-track application process, with an average processing time of 6 months.

Top Tip:

Research and connect with designated business incubators, angel investor groups, or venture capital funds to explore the opportunities available and improve your chances of success in the Start-Up Visa application process. This program offers an exciting pathway for entrepreneurs to bring their innovative business ideas to life in Canada.

Global Talent Stream: Temporary Foreign Work Permit

Now you may find that this doesn’t quite deserve to be on the list as it doesn’t technically qualify as immigration to Canada due to the temporary status you will get, but hear us out. Depending on your nationality and where you are applying, getting a temporary work permit can take 4 to 6 months. And once you have gained as little as six months of work experience, you could apply for permanent residence through programs such as the Saskatchewan Work Experience Stream, especially if you work in one of the following sectors:

  • Agricultural Sector;
  • Health Sector;
  • Hospitality Sectors;
  • Long Haul Truck Driver.

Other options include the Canadian Experience Class, managed by the Express Entry System, which requires one year of Canadian work experience to qualify to apply for permanent residence.

Top Tip:

If you are an exceptionally talented IT professional with at least five years of work experience, then you could ask your prospective employer to enquire about fast-tracking your application through the Global Talent Stream, as your application could be processed in as little as two weeks!

How to Fast Track Your Immigration to Canada

If you're looking to expedite your immigration process to Canada, here are some valuable tips to fast-track your application:

Ensure You Have The Correct Documentation

Ensure all documents, including education credentials, work experience certificates, and identity papers, are accurate, up-to-date, and properly prepared. This will help prevent delays or rejections due to incomplete or incorrect information.

Complete Language Proficiency Tests Early

Taking language proficiency tests such as IELTS (International English Language Testing System) or CELPIP (Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program) early on is crucial. High scores will strengthen your application and demonstrate your language skills to Canadian immigration authorities.

Obtain Certifications or Evaluations for Your Qualifications

If you possess specialized qualifications or professional certifications, consider getting them evaluated or accredited by relevant Canadian organizations. This step can help showcase the value of your skills and qualifications in the Canadian context.

Be Proactive in Job Search

Start researching and networking with potential employers in Canada well in advance. Being proactive in your job search can increase your chances of securing a job offer, which can positively impact your immigration application. Check out our 5-step guide on finding a job in Canada.

Seek Assistance from an RCIC

Engaging the services of a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC) can provide expert guidance throughout the immigration process. An RCIC can ensure that your application is prepared accurately, and they can assist you with navigating complex immigration procedures and regulations.

The Easiest Way to Immigrate to Canada

While the fastest immigration pathway may not always be the easiest, some routes offer more convenience and flexibility: The Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP). These programs provide a straightforward and accessible path to Canadian immigration.

With a wide range of Provincial Nominee Programs to choose from, the chances of your skills being in demand in one of the provinces are significantly higher. This increases your likelihood of receiving a nomination and successfully immigrating to Canada.

Each province and territory in Canada has its own Provincial Nominee Program, tailored to meet their specific economic and demographic needs. Here are some of the notable PNPs:

  • British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP)
    • Designed for skilled workers, international graduates, and entrepreneurs who wish to settle in British Columbia.
  • Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP)
    • Offers various streams for skilled workers, international graduates, and entrepreneurs looking to establish themselves in Alberta.
  • Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)
    • Provides pathways for skilled workers, international graduates, and business immigrants to settle in Ontario, Canada's most populous province.
  • Quebec Skilled Worker Program (QSWP)
    • Quebec operates its immigration system, including the Quebec Skilled Worker Program, which targets skilled workers intending to reside in Quebec.
  • Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program (SINP)
    • Offers options for skilled workers, international graduates, and entrepreneurs who want to live and work in Saskatchewan.
  • Nova Scotia Nominee Program (NSNP)
    • Designed for skilled workers, entrepreneurs, and international graduates who wish to settle in Nova Scotia.

These are just a few examples of the Provincial Nominee Programs available across Canada. By qualifying for and obtaining a nomination through a PNP, your chances of immigrating to Canada are significantly higher, as provinces actively seek individuals with specific skills and qualifications that align with their economic needs.

FAQs

Can I Apply to Multiple Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) at the Same Time?

Yes, you can apply to multiple PNPs simultaneously to increase your chances of receiving a nomination.

Applying to multiple PNPs allows you to explore various provincial pathways and increase your opportunities for nomination. Each province has its own criteria and selection process, so applying to multiple PNPs allows you to simultaneously showcase your skills and qualifications to different provinces.

By applying to multiple PNPs, you can receive invitations or nominations from multiple provinces, providing you with more options for immigration and increasing your chances of a successful immigration journey to Canada.

Are There Specific Occupations or Skills that Qualify for Fast Immigration?

The Express Entry system does not have specific occupations or skills that guarantee fast immigration. However, having in-demand skills or occupations can increase your chances of receiving an invitation to apply for permanent residence.
