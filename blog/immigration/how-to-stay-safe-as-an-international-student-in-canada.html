How to Stay Safe as an International Student in Canada

by 
Themba Phongolo

As an international student in Canada, you're provided with the exciting opportunity to explore a new country and advance your education. However, adjusting to a new country comes with challenges, particularly ensuring personal safety. This is why it's essential to get tips and practical advice to help protect yourself while studying in Canada. By preparing ahead, staying informed, and adopting good habits, you can focus on making the most of your academic journey with confidence.

Want to learn how you can remain safe as an international student in Canada? Then, take a look at our crucial safety tips as you pursue your studies in Canada.

How Can I Stay Safe in Canada as an International Student?

While studying abroad in Canada, it's essential to prioritize safety to ensure a positive and secure experience. From preparing for your arrival to understanding local laws and protecting yourself online, here's a guide to staying safe in Canada as an international student.

Preparing for Your Arrival in Canada

Before you arrive in Canada, take the time to research and prepare for your new environment. Familiarize yourself with local customs, emergency numbers, and basic Canadian etiquette. Ensure you have all necessary documents, including your Canadian student visa, medical insurance, and contact information.

It's also wise to connect with fellow students or join online forums related to your university to get insights and tips from those already in Canada. This preparation will help you transition smoothly and reduce potential stress upon arrival.

Learn what to do when you arrive in Canada.

Understanding Canadian Emergency Services

Canada's emergency services operate efficiently to ensure public safety. The primary emergency number across Canada is 911, which connects you to police, firefighters, and medical services. Remembering that 911 should only be used for genuine emergencies is crucial.

Each Canadian province and territory has local emergency services numbers for non-urgent situations. Familiarize yourself with the emergency protocols of your university, including the location of the nearest hospital and the procedures for evacuating campus buildings.

Familiarizing Yourself With Canadian Laws

Understanding Canadian laws is vital for safety and legal compliance. Canada has strict substance use, public conduct, and personal safety regulations. For example, the legal drinking age differs in each Canadian province or territory, but it is typically 18 or 19 years old. Cannabis is legal for recreational use, but there are restrictions on where and how it can be used.

Familiarize yourself with local laws regarding driving, noise levels, and any other regulations specific to your area. Following these Canadian laws will help you avoid legal issues and integrate smoothly into Canadian society.

Staying Safe While Getting Around

Canada is known for its safe and efficient transportation systems, but staying aware of your surroundings is essential. When using Canada's public transportation, such as buses and subways, be mindful of your belongings and avoid traveling alone late at night.

With a low crime rate of only 5,843 incidents per 100,000 people, according to Statistics Canada, you can feel relatively secure when getting around as an international student in Canada. However, criminal incidents can still happen at any time so it’s important to use well-lit and populated routes when walking, and consider using reputable ride-sharing services or taxis if you're unfamiliar with the area. Always let someone know your travel plans and expected arrival time if you're going out, especially if you're heading to a new or less familiar location.

Protecting Yourself at Home

Your safety at home is paramount, whether you're living in on-campus housing or off-campus accommodations. Ensure that your living space has functioning locks on doors and windows, and always secure these when you leave. Avoid sharing your living address or other personal information with strangers.

If you live with roommates, establish clear boundaries and communicate openly about shared responsibilities and safety concerns. Get to know your neighbors and local community to enhance security and create a supportive network.

Navigating Social Life Safely

While enjoying your social life is essential, maintaining safety is equally crucial. Be cautious about sharing personal information with new acquaintances, and be selective about who you trust. If you're attending social events or parties, consider going with friends and watching your belongings. It's also good to plan to get home safely, such as arranging transportation in advance or ensuring a sober friend can help.

Campus Safety Measures

Universities in Canada take campus safety seriously and offer various measures to protect students. Most campuses have security services available 24/7, including campus patrols and emergency response teams. Be sure to familiarize yourself with your campus's safety resources, such as emergency call boxes, security apps, and safe walk programs. Participate in safety workshops or training sessions your institution offers. Knowing how to access these resources and services will help you feel more secure while navigating campus life.

Financial Safety for International Students

Managing your finances securely is crucial to staying safe. Ensure you keep track of your financial transactions and check your bank accounts for unauthorized activities. Avoid having large amounts of cash and use debit or credit cards when possible. Be cautious of phishing scams and fraudulent schemes targeting students.

Ensure that financial transactions, such as paying for tuition or accommodation, are done through official and secure channels. If you have concerns about financial safety, seek advice from your university's financial assistance office or a trusted advisor.

Health and Wellness Safety

Your health and wellness are crucial components of overall safety. You must ensure adequate health insurance coverage to handle any medical needs. Get familiar with the nearest medical facilities and how to access healthcare services.

Maintain a healthy lifestyle by eating well, exercising regularly, and managing stress effectively. Stay informed about any health advisories or vaccinations required in Canada. If you experience health issues, get medical attention quickly to avoid complications.

Learn more about health and safety in Canada.

Digital Safety in Canada

In the digital age, protecting your online presence is essential for safety. You must use strong, unique passwords for your online accounts and two-factor authentication processes. Make sure you're aware of phishing emails and suspicious links that can compromise your personal information.

Regularly update your software and apps to protect against security vulnerabilities. When using public Wi-Fi, avoid accessing sensitive information or conducting financial transactions. Practice good digital hygiene to safeguard your data and privacy.

How Can I Build a Support Network to Ensure My Safety as an International Student in Canada?

Building a strong support network is essential for feeling safe and comfortable as an international student in Canada. Here are some tips to help you connect with others.

Connecting With Fellow International Students

Building a support network is crucial for your safety as an international student studying in Canada. Start by connecting with other international students who share similar experiences. Join international student associations or attend orientation events to meet peers from diverse backgrounds. These connections can provide valuable insights into navigating life in Canada and offer emotional support during challenging times.

Joining Campus Safety Groups

Many Canadian universities and colleges have campus safety groups that you can join. These groups often organize workshops on personal safety, self-defense techniques, and emergency preparedness. Participating in these activities will teach you essential safety skills and meet like-minded individuals who prioritize safety on campus. Campus safety groups are an excellent resource for staying informed about potential safety concerns and updates on campus security measures.

Establishing Emergency Contacts

Establishing a list of emergency contacts in Canada is vital. This should include your university's international student office, campus security, and trusted friends or roommates. Keep these numbers saved in your phone and written down in a safe place.

Don't hesitate to call 911 in an emergency. This service connects you to police, fire, and ambulance services throughout Canada. Remember, interpreters are available if English isn't your first language, ensuring you can always get help when needed.

FAQs

How Can I Protect Myself From Fraud or Scams as an International Student in Canada?

To protect yourself from fraud or scams, verify the legitimacy of any request for personal information. Don't share sensitive information like your bank account through unsecured channels or with unknown contacts. Report any suspicious activities to local authorities or your institution's international student office immediately.

What Are the Regulations That International Students Must Follow in Canada?

International students in Canada must comply with the conditions of their Canadian study permit, including maintaining full-time enrollment and making progress in their studies. They must also adhere to local laws, including employment and health insurance regulations.

How Can I Become an International Student in Canada?

To study here, you need a Canadian student permit. Secure a letter of acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) and apply through IRCC or a Canadian consulate. If necessary, provide proof of funds, a passport, and a medical exam. For faster processing, explore the Student Direct Stream (SDS). This option is available to students from certain countries.

Learn more about getting your study permit in just 20 days with the SDS.
