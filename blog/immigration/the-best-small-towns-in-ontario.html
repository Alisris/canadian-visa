The Best Small Towns in Ontario

by 
Shireen Fisher

Author:

Shireen Fisher

BTech Degree in Journalism, Diploma in Journalism

Shireen Fisher is a widely-published writer with nine years of experience. She started her media career in radio before heading back to university to pursue her National Diploma and BTech Degree in Journalism.

. Updated: February 6th, 2024
7 mins

If you want to go to the Great White North but would prefer to steer clear of the hustle and bustle of big city life, you're in luck. Ontario, Canada, boasts an array of quaint and picturesque small towns, each offering its unique charm and appeal. For those considering a move to Canada, these small towns in Ontario present an enticing option for settling down and embracing a relaxed lifestyle amidst stunning natural landscapes and vibrant communities. Let's explore the best small towns in Ontario that attract newcomers to Canada with their warmth and beauty.

The Best Small Towns in Ontario

Whether nestled amidst vineyards, along waterfronts, or amidst lush forests, the following small towns in Ontario are known to draw newcomers and travelers alike.

Stratford

Famed for its annual Stratford Festival, this town exudes cultural vibrancy and artistic flair. Set along the Avon River, Stratford boasts stunning gardens, Victorian architecture, and a thriving culinary scene. Whether wandering through its charming streets or catching a world-class theatrical performance, Stratford offers a delightful blend of sophistication and small-town charm.

Niagara-on-the-Lake

Nestled at the mouth of the Niagara River, Niagara-on-the-Lake is a gem steeped in history and natural beauty. Known for its wineries, historic sites, and picturesque waterfront, this town offers a serene escape amid vineyards and lush landscapes. Take a bike ride along the Niagara Parkway or indulge in a wine-tasting experience amidst the region's renowned vineyards.

Collingwood

Situated on the shores of Georgian Bay, Collingwood is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. From skiing and snowboarding in the winter to hiking and boating in the summer, Collingwood offers a myriad of recreational opportunities against the backdrop of the stunning Blue Mountains. Its vibrant downtown core is dotted with boutiques, galleries, and restaurants, making it an ideal blend of outdoor adventure and small-town charm.

Huntsville

Surrounded by pristine lakes and lush forests, Huntsville embodies the essence of Ontario's natural beauty. As the gateway to Algonquin Provincial Park, this town offers endless opportunities for outdoor exploration, including hiking, camping, and wildlife spotting. With its vibrant arts scene, quaint shops, and welcoming community, Huntsville provides a perfect retreat for those seeking a balance between adventure and relaxation.

Perth

Rich in heritage and charm, Perth is a picturesque town along the Tay River. Stroll along its historic streets lined with limestone buildings, visit its artisanal shops and galleries, or enjoy a scenic picnic by the river. With its close-knit community and array of cultural events, Perth offers a tranquil yet vibrant lifestyle that appeals to residents and visitors alike.

Port Hope

With its well-preserved 19th-century architecture and scenic waterfront, Port Hope is a hidden gem along the shores of Lake Ontario. Explore its charming downtown core, home to quaint shops, cafes, and heritage buildings, or take a stroll along its picturesque beachfront. With its strong sense of community and rich cultural heritage, Port Hope provides an idyllic setting for those seeking a slower pace of life.

Picton

Located in the heart of Prince Edward County, Picton is a quaint town known for its historic charm and scenic beauty. Explore its vibrant arts scene, sample local wines at nearby vineyards, or embark on a scenic drive along the county's winding country roads. With its laid-back atmosphere and stunning landscapes, Picton offers a perfect retreat for those looking to unwind amidst Ontario's natural splendor.

Niagara Falls (Ontario side)

While Niagara Falls is often associated with its iconic waterfalls, the town offers much more than breathtaking views. From thrilling attractions like the Niagara SkyWheel to charming boutiques and restaurants along Clifton Hill, Niagara Falls boasts a lively atmosphere that caters to visitors of all ages. With its proximity to the falls and array of entertainment options, this town is a popular destination for tourists and residents alike.

Gravenhurst

Set amidst the Muskoka region's picturesque landscapes, Gravenhurst is a quaint town known for its historic charm and outdoor recreation. Once you settle in Canada and move to Ontario, explore this town's scenic trails, visit the Muskoka Steamships & Discovery Centre, or simply unwind by the waterfront and soak in the serene surroundings. With its welcoming community and laid-back vibe, Gravenhurst offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Prince Edward County

Made up of a collection of charming towns and villages, Prince Edward County is a hidden gem on the shores of Lake Ontario. From the quaint streets of Wellington to the scenic beauty of Sandbanks Provincial Park, this Canadian town offers diverse attractions and experiences. Whether you're indulging in farm-to-table dining, exploring local wineries, or simply enjoying the stunning waterfront vistas, Prince Edward County promises a truly enchanting escape.

Discover the Charm of Ontario's Small Towns

Even though Ontario is Canada's most populous city, you'll find that it boasts many small towns that embody the essence of quaintness and charm, each offering a unique allure that captivates visitors and residents alike. From historic main streets lined with century-old buildings to breathtaking natural landscapes that beckon exploration, there's something enchanting about the simplicity and authenticity of small-town life in Ontario.

Historic Main Streets

One of the most charming aspects of Ontario's small towns is their historic main streets. Lined with charming storefronts, cozy cafes, and boutique shops housed in heritage buildings, these streets evoke a sense of nostalgia and timelessness. Taking a leisurely stroll down these quaint thoroughfares is like stepping back in time, where the pace of life is slower, and the sense of community is palpable.

Vibrant Arts and Culture

Despite their small size, many Ontario towns boast thriving arts and cultural scenes. From community theaters and art galleries to live music venues and craft markets, there's no shortage of creative expression to be found in these charming communities. Whether you're attending a local art exhibition, catching a live performance at a historic theater, or browsing handmade goods at a street festival, you'll discover an array of rich cultural experiences in Ontario's small towns.

Scenic Natural Landscapes

Ontario's small towns are often nestled amidst stunning natural landscapes, from rolling hills and lush forests to tranquil lakeshores and meandering rivers. Whether you're hiking along scenic trails, picnicking in a waterfront park, or simply taking in the panoramic views from a scenic lookout, the natural beauty of these towns never fails to inspire awe and wonder. For outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers, Ontario's small towns offer endless opportunities for exploration and adventure.

Close-Knit Community Spirit

The most charming aspect of Ontario's small towns is their close-knit community spirit. Residents take pride in their town's heritage and traditions, and a strong sense of camaraderie and neighborliness permeates everyday life. From community events and festivals to volunteer initiatives and local gatherings, there are countless ways to connect with fellow residents and become an integral part of the fabric of the community.

Culinary Delights

Despite their small size, Ontario's small towns boast a surprisingly diverse culinary scene, with cozy cafes, quaint bistros, and farm-to-table restaurants serving delicious fare made from locally sourced ingredients. Whether you're indulging in freshly baked pastries at a charming bakery, savoring gourmet cuisine at a cozy restaurant, or sampling artisanal cheeses, wines, and maple syrup at a local market, the culinary delights of Ontario's small towns are sure to tantalize your taste buds.

The Benefits of Living in Small Towns in Ontario

When you settle in Canada, youll find that living in a small town in Ontario has many advantages, offering residents a tranquil and fulfilling lifestyle. Let's explore some of the benefits in detail.

Sense of Community

Small towns in Ontario are known for fostering strong bonds among residents, creating a supportive and welcoming community atmosphere. Unlike the anonymity of big cities, small towns provide opportunities for meaningful connections and relationships. Whether it's participating in local events, volunteering for community initiatives, or simply chatting with neighbors at the corner store, living in a small town allows you to feel like a valued member of a close-knit community.

Affordability

Compared to larger cities, small towns in Ontario often offer more affordable housing options and a lower cost of living. Whether you're looking to buy a home or rent an apartment, small towns typically provide housing options that are more budget-friendly, allowing residents to stretch their dollars further. Additionally, everyday expenses such as groceries, dining out, and entertainment tend to be more reasonable in small towns, making it easier to live comfortably within your means.

Natural Beauty

One of the greatest perks of living in a small town in Ontario is the access to stunning natural landscapes. From rolling hills and lush forests to pristine lakeshores and meandering rivers, Ontario's small towns boast breathtaking scenery that provides endless opportunities for outdoor recreation and relaxation. Whether you enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, or simply soaking in the beauty of nature, living in a small town allows you to immerse yourself in the great outdoors right outside your doorstep.

Cultural Richness

Despite their size, many small towns in Ontario are rich in history and culture, boasting vibrant arts scenes, festivals, and local events celebrating their heritage. Whether it's attending a community theater production, exploring local art galleries, or participating in traditional festivals and parades, small towns offer a wealth of cultural experiences that showcase each community's unique character and identity. Living in a small Canadian town allows you to immerse yourself in this rich tapestry of culture and history, fostering a deeper appreciation for the place you call home.

Peace and Tranquility

Escape the noise and congestion of urban life and enjoy the peace and tranquility of small-town living in Ontario. Unlike the constant hustle and bustle of big cities, small towns offer a slower pace of life where you can unwind and recharge amidst serene surroundings. Whether it's walking along tree-lined streets, enjoying a quiet afternoon by the waterfront, or simply savoring a cup of coffee on your front porch, small-town living allows you to embrace a simpler and more relaxed way of life.

Choosing Your Ideal Small Town in Ontario

Choosing your ideal small town in Ontario requires careful consideration of several key factors to ensure that it aligns with your lifestyle and preferences. Here's a detailed exploration of what to consider.

Location

The location of a small town in Ontario significantly determines its suitability as your potential home. Consider factors such as proximity to major cities, accessibility to transportation networks, and ease of commuting to work or other destinations. Additionally, think about the surrounding natural environment and recreational opportunities available in the area. Whether you prefer a waterfront town, a quaint village nestled amidst rolling hills, or a town surrounded by forests and parks, choose a location that resonates with your lifestyle and interests.

Amenities

Evaluate the amenities available in the small town you're considering. Look for essential services such as schools, healthcare facilities, grocery stores, and recreational amenities like parks, libraries, and community centers. Additionally, consider the availability of cultural and leisure activities such as theaters, museums, sports facilities, and local events and festivals. Assessing the range of amenities in a small town will help ensure that it meets your daily needs and offers opportunities for leisure and entertainment.

Housing Affordability

Evaluate the housing affordability in the small town you're considering. Research the real estate market to understand the average property prices, rental rates, and housing options available. Compare the cost of living in the town to your budget and financial goals to ensure that it's within your means. Additionally, consider property taxes, utilities, and maintenance costs when assessing housing affordability. Choosing a small town with affordable housing options will allow you to live comfortably and enjoy a good quality of life without financial strain.

Employment Opportunities

Evaluate the job market in the small town and surrounding areas. Consider prominent regional industries, job availability, and potential career growth opportunities. If you're relocating for work, ensure that there are suitable job prospects aligned with your skills and qualifications. Additionally, assess the commute time to nearby employment centers or consider remote work options. Learn more about the in-demand jobs in Ontario.

Education System

For families with children or individuals pursuing further education, the quality of the education system is a crucial consideration. Research the local schools, including public, private, and alternative education options, as well as their academic performance, extracurricular activities, and resources available. Additionally, consider proximity to post-secondary institutions for those pursuing higher education or continuing education opportunities. Learn more about the top universities in Ontario.

Healthcare Services

Access to healthcare services is essential for maintaining physical and mental well-being. Evaluate the availability and quality of healthcare facilities in the small town, including hospitals, clinics, doctors' offices, specialists, and emergency services. Consider factors such as waiting times, availability of healthcare professionals, and range of medical services offered to ensure that your healthcare needs are adequately met.

The Ontario Healthcare Plan, also known as the Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP), is a comprehensive, publicly funded healthcare system that provides essential medical services to residents of the province. Under OHIP, eligible Ontario residents, including those with Canadian permanent residency, have access to a wide range of healthcare services, including doctor visits, hospital care, diagnostic tests, and emergency services, all without direct payment at the point of care.

Transportation Infrastructure

Assess the transportation infrastructure in the small town, including road networks, public transit options, and access to major highways or transportation hubs. Consider your commuting needs and access to essential services, amenities, and recreational destinations. Additionally, evaluate parking availability, cycling infrastructure, and walkability to determine the ease of getting around the town and surrounding areas.

Safety and Security

Safety and security are paramount when choosing a place to live. Research crime rates, police presence, and community safety initiatives in the small town to gauge its overall safety. Consider factors such as street lighting, neighborhood watch programs, and emergency response times to ensure that you feel secure and comfortable in your new home. Learn more about safety levels in Canada.

Environmental Sustainability

Consider the town's commitment to environmental sustainability and conservation efforts. Look for initiatives promoting green spaces, recycling programs, renewable energy sources, and environmentally friendly practices. Assess the town's environmental policies and community engagement in sustainability initiatives to ensure that it align with your values and commitment to preserving the environment.

Diversity and Inclusivity

Evaluate the diversity and inclusivity of the small town, including its demographic makeup, cultural representation, and support for marginalized communities. Consider factors such as ethnic diversity, LGBTQ+ inclusivity, accessibility initiatives, and cultural events and festivals. Choosing a town that embraces diversity and promotes inclusivity fosters a sense of belonging and acceptance for all residents.

FAQs

Does Ontario Have a Provincial Nominee Program?

Yes. The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) offers a streamlined pathway for obtaining provincial nomination, facilitating the immigration process.

There are two distinct methods for securing nomination through the OINP. Apart from the Express Entry route, you can directly reach out to Immigration Ontario and apply for the OINP. Upon receiving a provincial nomination, you can create a profile in the Canada Express Entry system and include their nomination credentials.

Do I Need to Speak French if I Plan to Settle in a Small Town in Ontario?

While English is the primary language, some eastern Ontario towns like Hawkesbury, Plantagenet, and Embrun have French-speaking communities.

Knowing basic French phrases is helpful but not essential. Find out more about learning French as an immigrant in Canada.
