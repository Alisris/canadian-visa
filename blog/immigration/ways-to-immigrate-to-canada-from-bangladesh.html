Top Ways to Immigrate to Canada from Bangladesh

by Nicky VZ. Updated: September 5th, 2020

Bangladeshis from cities like Dhaka, Chattogram, and Cumilla immigrate to Canada every year for better opportunities and to pursue a higher quality of life in the Great North. Canada’s lenient immigration policies and more than 100 immigration programs make it easy for skilled workers from Bangladesh and their families to earn permanent residency in Canada. The good news is that Canada aims to welcome more than 1-million newcomers by 2022! Do you want to be one of them?

Keep reading to discover the top ways how you can immigrate to Canada from Bangladesh here.

How to Immigrate to Canada from Bangladesh


With over 100 immigration and visa programs available to move to Canada - there is sure to be an option that you qualify for. The trick is finding the best path for you and your family to have a successful immigration journey.

Discover for which of more than 100 immigration programs you qualify with our comprehensive eligibility evaluation that is reviewed by certified professionals. Simply, fill in the form below to get started today!

Learn more about the top ways to earn permanent residency in Canada below! 

1. Express Entry: Immigrate to Canada Without a Job Offer


The Express Entry Immigration System is the most popular way for skilled workers to immigrate to Canada due to its speed and flexibility. Applications are processed in as little as 6-months and allow you to settle anywhere in Canada with the exception of Quebec.

The Express Entry manages three immigration programs namely the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP), Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP), and the Canadian Experience Class (CEC). Which type of workers can apply for Express Entry immigration? Find out below.


Express Entry Immigration
Skill TypesExample Job Positions
ProfessionalsMedical workers, accountants, architects, lawyers, teachers, scientific staff, etc.
ManagersHospitality, mining, legislation, finance, etc.
Trade WorkersCarpenters, welders, plumbers, cooks, chefs, etc,
Technical WorkersCivil engineers, broadcast technicians, computer network technicians, etc.

Express Entry Eligibility for Bangladeshis

You must meet the minimum requirements to apply for the Express Entry immigration program from Bangladesh. This means you must: 

  • Must be a skilled worker (See above)
  • Have 1 or 2 years of work experience (Inside or outside of Canada)
  • Be proficient in English or French (Language test is required)
  • Have a high school diploma (Education Credential Assessment (ECA) is required)

You can improve your chances of immigration success if you:

  • Arrange employment in Canada
  • Have higher education
  • Are between 20-29 years of age
  • Have a spouse with work experience and higher education
  • Have family living in Canada

2. Provincial Nomination Programs (PNPs): For Semi-Skilled Workers


If you don’t fit into the skilled worker categories mentioned above then you may qualify for a Provincial Nomination Program instead. Semi-skilled and intermediate skilled workers such as butchers, truck drivers, caregivers, and food and beverage servers are in-demand in a few regions in Canada. This does however mean that you may have to get a job offer in Canada and work in an in-demand job position for a predetermined time before you’re eligible to apply for permanent residency. Think of it as an internship for permanent residency in Canada. 

Other options for semi-skilled workers from Bangladesh include Immigration Pilots:

  • Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot
  • The Agri-Food Pilot
  • The Atlantic Immigration Pilot
  • The Home Care Providers Pilot

3. Study in Canada: Qualify For a PGWP


Canada is among the preferred destinations for Bangladeshi students to pursue higher studies. In fact, many students stay and work in Canada after their studies and earn permanent residency much easier because they have gained significant Canadian work experience.

Canada is home to many top universities and colleges with surprisingly lower education costs compared to the USA, UK, Australian, and New Zealand. If you study a course that’s at-least 8-months in length you could apply for a Post-Graduate Work Permit to work anywhere in Canada and kickstart your career, and new life in Canada! 

4. Business Immigration For Entrepreneurs


There are thousands of Bangladeshi owned businesses in Canada - big and small. From towing services, restaurants, tutoring academies to legal firms and you can find them all on the Banglatown.ca business directory.

You can immigrate to Canada from Bangladesh as a business owner or entrepreneur through the Start-Up Visa Program or a few Provincial Business Streams. Canada readily accepts newcomers who have the experience, and talent to start, manage, or buy a business in the country.

If you are successful with your business immigration application, you and your family can move to Canada together.

Reasons to Move to Canada from Bangladesh

How does Canada differ from Bangladesh? How will your life change when you become a Canadian permanent resident? Find out below!

1. Put Your Safety First in Canada


Canada is ranked the 6th safest country in the world and is notorious for its Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), who are the national police services of Canada. The country's low crime rate and secure neighborhoods make it the ideal place to raise a family. Canada is also a safer and more peaceful place to live if you are a woman. Less Canadian women compared to Bangladeshi women are affected by gender-based violence and equal rights are an integral part of the Canadian constitution.

2. Experience a Pristine Natural Environment


From its countless freshwater resources, clean air, and untouched natural environment, Canada is a spectacularly beautiful country that many people come from far to experience. Warm summers and snowy winters mean that you’ll be able to take part in a wide variety of new, and exciting activities like canoeing on Lake Louise and skiing at Whistler. Moving to Canada from Bangladesh means you’ll broaden your horizons and improve your quality of life.

3. Employment Opportunities and High Minimum Wage


Canada has a big, multi-cultural workforce with many immigrants from South Asian countries such as India and Bangladesh. Immigrants fill thousands of in-demand job positions in Canada left by a rapidly aging and retiring workforce. Working in Canada means you’ll be protected under the same labor laws as Canadians and enjoy better work conditions and earn a great salary. In Canada, the average minimum wage is $14 per hour!
