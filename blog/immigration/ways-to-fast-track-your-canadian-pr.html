Ways to Fast-Track Your Canadian PR

by 
Themba Phongolo

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: December 7th, 2023

The Canadian government has created various immigration policies and programs to attract skilled and unskilled workers to Canada to alleviate the labor gap that the country experiences. The fastest way to immigrate to Canada usually depends on your plans, qualifications, experience, career, and goals when you reach the country. However, there are several options for obtaining PR and immigrating to Canada quickly and easily.

Embarking on the next phase of your life in Canada? Discover how you can make this goal a reality quickly with our ways to fast-track your Canadian PR!

Permanent Residency in Canada (Canada PR)?

Canada is known for its welcoming attitude towards immigrants, and obtaining permanent residency in Canada, also known as Canada PR, is a dream for many. Canadian PR is a status granted to someone who is not a Canadian citizen but has been given PR status by immigrating to Canada.

A permanent resident has the right to live and work in Canada without any time limit on their stay. To become a permanent resident, a foreign national must apply to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) under one of several programs, including the Express Entry program, Provincial Nominee Program, and Business immigration programs.

Find out more about permanent residency in Canada.

3 Ways to Fast-Track Your Canadian PR

Use the Express Entry System

The Express Entry system is a popular and efficient way to fast-track your Canadian PR. It is an online system that manages applications from foreign skilled workers who want to settle in Canada permanently.

It administers three economic immigration programs, which include the:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP),
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP), and
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

By creating an Express Entry profile and meeting the eligibility requirements, you can enter the pool of candidates and receive an invitation to apply (ITA) for permanent residency. Maximizing your Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score is essential to increase your chances of receiving an invitation.

The CRS score is based on factors such as:

  • Age,
  • Education,
  • Marital Status,
  • Work experience,
  • Language proficiency in English or French, and
  • Ability to adapt and integrate into Canadian society.

Improving your language skills, obtaining a higher level of education, gaining more work experience, or securing a job offer from a Canadian employer can significantly boost your CRS score and increase your chances of getting an ITA. You can calculate your CRS score using our CRS calculator.

Learn more about the fast-track process of the Express Entry system.

Apply Directly to the Province You Want to Immigrate to Via Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) allow Canadian provinces and territories to nominate individuals who have the skills and experience needed to contribute to their local economies. Applying directly to the province you want to immigrate to through PNPs can be an excellent way to fast-track your Canadian PR.

Each province has its own set of eligibility criteria and selection processes, so it is crucial to research and understand the requirements of the specific PNP you are interested in. Some provinces have streams that prioritize candidates with job offers in high-demand occupations, while others focus on entrepreneurs or graduates from local educational institutions. To fast-track your PNP Canadian PR application, you must apply directly to the PNP stream, category, or pathway youre eligible to apply for.

By meeting the criteria and receiving a provincial nomination, you can also receive additional CRS points in the Express Entry system, significantly increasing your chances of receiving an ITA and expediting your application for permanent residency.

Find out more about Canadas Provincial Nominee Programs.

Buy or Establish a Business in Canada

Another way to fast-track your Canadian PR is by buying or establishing a business in Canada. The Canadian government encourages entrepreneurship and offers various programs to support business immigrants, with the Start-Up Visa Program being the most prominent.

The Start-up Visa program allows foreign entrepreneurs who have a viable business idea and sufficient support from a designated organization to immigrate to Canada. Canada prioritizes foreign immigration that can contribute to the countrys economy; therefore it fast-tracks the Canada PR application process for candidates who want to invest in or establish a business in Canada.

It is essential to thoroughly research the requirements, regulations, and market conditions before embarking on this path. Seeking professional advice and guidance from Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants (RCIC) or immigration lawyers specializing in business immigration can help you navigate the process smoothly.

Please Note

Ensure you have the required 75,000 CAD from a designated angel investor group or 200,000 CAD from a designated venture capital fund needed for your business immigration to Canada.

Learn more about business immigration in Canada.

Benefits of Canada PR

Obtaining Canadian PR comes with numerous benefits that make it an attractive option for immigrants from all over the world. Here are some key benefits of Canadian PR:

Work Opportunities in Canada

As a Canadian permanent resident, you have the right to work in any job or profession in Canada. You are not restricted to a specific employer or industry, giving you the freedom to explore different career opportunities and paths.

Find out more work opportunities in Canada via its in-demand occupations.

Mobility Rights in Canada

Canadian PR holders have the right to move and live anywhere in Canada, providing flexibility and the opportunity to settle in their preferred province or territory. This mobility allows individuals to benefit from the unique cultural, geographic, and economic advantages of different regions within Canada.

In contrast, if you are a temporary foreign worker in Canada via an employer-specific work permit, your life in Canada is tied to the location where your Canadian employer is headquartered.

Path to Citizenship in Canada

One of the most significant advantages of Canadian PR is the path it provides to Canadian citizenship. After residing in Canada for a specific period, meeting the residency requirements, and demonstrating knowledge of Canada's official languages and citizenship responsibilities, permanent residents can apply for Canadian citizenship. Canadian citizenship offers additional benefits, such as voting rights and access to Canadian passports.

Learn more about how to apply for Canadian citizenship.

Security and Protection in Canada

Canada is known for its strong social and legal systems, providing a safe and secure environment for its residents. Permanent residents enjoy the same legal rights, privileges, and protections as Canadian citizens, ensuring their safety and well-being in Canada.

Find out more about safety and security in Canada.

Social Benefits in Canada

Canadian PR holders are eligible for various social benefits and programs via Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC). This includes healthcare coverage through Canada's publicly funded healthcare system. Additionally, permanent residents can access social assistance, retirement benefits, and education subsidies, ensuring a high quality of life for themselves and their families.

Education Benefits in Canada

Canada is renowned for its excellent education system, with world-class universities, colleges, and schools. Canadian PR holders can access affordable education opportunities, including subsidized tuition fees and scholarships. This benefit is particularly attractive for individuals seeking to pursue further studies or provide their children with a high-quality education.

Learn more about Canadas education system.

FAQs

How Can I Request Urgent Processing of a Permanent Resident Card?

If you require urgent processing of a Permanent Resident Card (PR card), you can request expedited processing by providing a valid reason and supporting documentation. Valid reasons for urgent processing include humanitarian or compassionate grounds, urgent travel, or urgent employment reasons.

Carefully follow the instructions provided by IRCC and submit all required documents to support your request.

Can I Bring my Family Members With me to Canada?

Yes, you can bring your family members with you to Canada when applying for a fast-track immigration program. However, they must provide the necessary documents and meet the eligibility requirements for your specific program.
