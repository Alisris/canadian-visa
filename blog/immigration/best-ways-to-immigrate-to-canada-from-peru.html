Best Ways to Immigrate to Canada from Peru in 2023

by Nadine Wolfe. Updated: January 18th, 2023
7 mins

Did you know that people from around the world, including Peru, immigrate to Canada every year? Here you’ll find out how to immigrate to Canada from Peru in 2023 as well as why it’s a great choice to move here.

For example, Canada is a diverse country and embraces different cultures and languages including Spanish. It’s also one of the safest countries in the world and one of the most financially secure.

That’s only the beginning! Before we get to the countless reasons why Canada is a great place to call home, let’s look at the best ways you can get there.

Let’s Go to Canada!

To make it easier for people from around the world to immigrate to Canada, the Canadian government has developed a number of immigration programs and visas, over 100 in fact. However, to make the choice simpler, here are the top three immigration programs that you can consider when planning to move to Canada from Peru.

Express Entry

What is Express Entry? Well only one of the fastest ways to get to Canada! Express Entry is a system that processes visa applications in as little as six months.

There are three programs that operate under the Express Entry system, namely: Federal Skilled Worker Program, Federal Skilled Trades Program and Canadian Experience Class.

If your application for one of the programs is successful, you’ll be able to obtain permanent residency in Canada.


Federal Skilled Worker Program

The Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP) is geared toward people who have qualifications, skills or work experience that can help contribute to the growth of the Canadian economy.

To be eligible to apply for the Federal Skilled Worker Program, you need to meet the following criteria:

You must have skilled work experience in one of the following positions. These positions must also be listed in the National Occupation Classification (NOC):

  • Managerial
  • Professional
  • Trade/technical occupation

If your application for the FSWP is approved, you’ll be able to obtain permanent residency in Canada.

Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)

The Federal Skilled Trades Program is designed for skilled foreign trade workers who have experience in the eligible trade occupations that can contribute to the growth of the Canadian economy. Like with the Federal Skilled Worker Program, if your application for the Federal Skilled Trades Program is approved, you’ll be able to gain permanent residency in Canada.

Let’s see if you qualify to apply. Requirements to apply for the FSTP are as follows:

You ‘ll need:

  • A full-time job offer from a Canadian employer for a total period of at least 1 year OR a certificate of qualification from a provincial, territorial, or federal governing body
  • Language proficiency score in French or English measured by the Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) or the Niveaux de compétence linguistique Canadiens (NCLC). (Level 4 for writing + reading and level 5 for listening + speaking)
  • At least 2 years of full-time work experience within the last 5 years
  • Ability to demonstrate that you meet the job requirements of your skilled trade
  • You must show that you have enough money for you and your family to settle in Canada unless you have a valid job offer or can legally work in Canada

Skilled trade occupations eligible under the Federal Skilled Trades Program are organized in the following major National Occupational Classification (NOC) groups. These groups are further divided into different occupations, and they are all skill type B. You must prove that you have experience performing the duties of the occupational description in the NOC.

National Occupation Classification Categories

Major Group 72

  • Industrial
  • Electrical
  • Construction

Major Group 73

  • Maintenance
  • Equipment Operation

Major Group 82

  • Supervisors and Technicians in Natural Resources
  • Agriculture

Major Group 92

  • Processing and Manufacturing
  • Utility Supervisors
  • Central Control Operators

Minor Group 632

  • Chefs and Cooks

Minor Group 633

  • Butchers and Bakers

Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

If you’re seeking permanent residency but are already living in Canada, then look no further. The Canadian Experience Class was developed for permanent resident applicants who are already living in Canada and have gained work experience here through a temporary work permit.

If you want to apply through the CEC, check if you meet the necessary requirements.

Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)


Canada’s Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) consists of 11 participating Canadian provinces and territories. You can choose which province to apply for and if your application is successful, you’ll be able to obtain permanent residency in that particular province.

Here are the 11 provinces you can choose from:

  1. Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP)
  2. British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP)
  3. Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program (MPNP)
  4. New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NW PNP)
  5. Newfoundland & Labrador Provincial Nominee Program (NL PNP)
  6. Northwest Territories Nominee Program (NTNP)
  7. Nova Scotia Provincial Nominee Program (NS PNP)
  8. Ontario Provincial Nominee Program (OINP)
  9. Prince Edward Island Provincial Nominee Program (PEI PNP)
  10. Saskatchewan Provincial Nominee Program (SINP)
  11. Yukon Nominee Program (YNP)

Each program has its own set of requirements you need to meet to be able to apply.

Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)


The Atlantic Immigration Program was developed for skilled workers who are interested in living and working in one of Canada’s four Atlantic provinces and wish to gain permanent residence there.

What are Canada’s Atlantic provinces? These are provinces along the coastline such as:

  1. Newfoundland and Labrador
  2. Prince Edward Island
  3. New Brunswick and
  4. Nova Scotia

The AIP is also open to international students who have graduated in one of the four Atlantic provinces.

There are three programs that operate under the Atlantic Immigration Program, namely:

  1. Atlantic International Graduate Program
  2. Atlantic High-Skilled Program
  3. Atlantic Intermediate-Skilled Program

Each of these programs have their own set of requirements:

Atlantic International Graduate Program

  • This program is for people who have attained a qualification from a publicly-funded institution in one of the four Canadian Atlantic provinces;
  • You will need to have lived in the province for at least 16 months in the two years before attaining your qualification;
  • You will have to take a language test, regardless of the language you completed your studies in;
  • You must be able to prove you will be able to support yourself as well as any dependents that will be staying with you

Atlantic High-Skilled Program

To be eligible to apply for this program, you must:

  • have at least 1 year of experience working in management, professional or skilled job;
  • You must have a foreign degree or diploma equal to a Canadian credential, for which you will need an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) report done;
  • You will need to take an approved language test to prove you are proficient enough in English or French to live and work in Canada; and
  • You need to have proof of funds that meet the minimum required amount to support yourself as well as any dependents regardless of whether they are joining you in Canada or not

Atlantic Intermediate-Skilled Program

To apply for this program, you must:

  • have at least 1 year's experience working in a position that requires a high school education or job-specific training;
  • You must have a high school diploma equal to a Canadian credential, for which you will need an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA report);
  • You will need to take an approved language test to prove you are proficient enough in English or French to live and work in Canada; and
  • You need to have proof of funds that meet the minimum required amount to support yourself as well as any dependents regardless of whether they are joining you in Canada or not

FAQs

1. What is the maximum age to immigrate to Canada?

While there is no set age limit requirement for any Canadian immigration program, in most economic immigration categories, applicants between the ages of 25 and 35 receive the maximum points. However, that doesn't mean that applicants who are older cannot be approved.

2. Can you move to Canada without a job?

To put it simply, yes. Canada provides immigration pathways to allow foreigners to immigrate without having a secured job offer first, such as Express Entry and the Provincial Nominee Program.

3. Is immigrating to Canada worth it?

There are many reasons why immigrating to Canada is worthwhile. Here are some of the top reasons:

Employment opportunities

Job opportunities available are a great reason to immigrate to Canada from Peru in 2023. Due to an aging and retiring population, Canada is experiencing a shortage of skilled labour in various industries.

To combat this, the Canadian government is encouraging foreign workers who have the skills, qualifications, or work experience to contribute to the growth of the Canadian economy, to immigrate to Canada.

Quality, free education

Canada offers free public education for citizens and permanent residents from kindergarten through to the last year of high school in grade 12. Canada also has a number of prestigious universities if you wish to pursue a tertiary education, such as the University of Toronto, University of British Columbia and McGill University to name a few. What’s more, Canada is the most educated country in the world with 56 percent of adults in the country meeting the criteria for the OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development).

Multicultural society

Did you know that 21.5 percent of Canada’s population are of foreign origin? People from around the world have come to the Great North and chosen to call this their home. This makes for a diverse country where various languages are spoken and an array of cultures are celebrated.

Growing tech sector

Technology is the future. That’s why it’s a great idea to settle in a place that is moving forward in the tech sector. As Canada’s tech sector is fast growing, there is a need for tech workers. So if this is your field of expertise, there’s yet another reason to come over from Peru!

Safety

Finally, a great reason to immigrate to Canada from Peru in 2023 is safety. Canada is the sixth safest country in the world according to the Global Peace Index. Safety and security is simply something that everyone needs. You will be able to rest assured that you and your loved ones are settling in a safe and peaceful environment.

Now that you’re familiar with some of the best immigration programs you can choose from, you can start your visa application process. Although there are many great programs available, applying for a Canadian visa can be overwhelming and confusing. Fortunately, we work with Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCICS) who are ready to offer you a step by step guide through the visa application process and better your chances of being approved.
