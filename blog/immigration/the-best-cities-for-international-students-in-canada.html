The Best Cities For International Students in Canada

by 
Themba Phongolo

Canada is a top destination for international students, known for its high-quality education and welcoming environment. With a diverse range of cities offering unique opportunities and experiences, choosing the right one can be exciting and challenging for international students in Canada. Each city has distinct advantages, from Toronto's busy metropolis to Vancouver's scenic beauty. Whether you prioritize world-class education, cultural diversity, or affordable living, there's a city that matches your needs.

Looking for a study-abroad destination with exceptional educational institutions, vibrant cultural scenes, and unique opportunities? We explore the best cities for international students in Canada.

What Are the Top Cities for International Students in Canada?

Canada is a popular study destination for international students, offering a high quality of life, diverse culture, and world-class education. Below is an explorative overview of the seven of Canada's top cities for international students.

Toronto

Toronto, Canada's largest city, is a global hub of education and culture. It hosts some of the country's most prestigious institutions, such as the University of Toronto, Ryerson University, and York University. These universities are renowned for their diverse academic programs and cutting-edge research opportunities. Toronto's vibrant multicultural atmosphere enhances the student experience, offering exposure to various cultures and perspectives.

The city's extensive public transportation system makes it easy to navigate, and its numerous cultural events, from film festivals to art exhibitions, provide ample extracurricular activities. However, the cost of living in Toronto is relatively high. However, it is still less expensive than being a student in Vancouver. The city's strong job market and numerous networking opportunities can offset these costs for many students.

Vancouver

Vancouver is known for its breathtaking natural landscapes and high-quality educational institutions. Home to the University of British Columbia (UBC) and Simon Fraser University (SFU), Vancouver offers students a world-class education in an environment that combines academic diligence with outdoor adventure. The city is situated between the Pacific Ocean and the Coast Mountains, providing numerous opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and kayaking.

Vancouver's commitment to sustainability is evident in its green spaces and environmental initiatives. The city's diverse population adds to its cultural richness, making it an exciting place for students worldwide. However, the cost of living in Vancouver is among the highest in Canada, especially regarding housing. Despite this, the city's high average monthly net salary compared to other major Canadian cities like Montreal and job opportunities make it a popular choice for many international students.

Montreal

Montreal stands out for its bilingual education system, offering programs in both French and English. This unique feature makes it an attractive destination for students seeking to enhance their language skills while pursuing higher education. Institutions such as McGill University and Université de Montréal are internationally recognized for their academic excellence.

Montreal's vibrant cultural scene, characterized by its festivals, museums, and culinary diversity, provides a rich backdrop for student life. The city's relatively affordable cost of living compared to Toronto and Vancouver adds to its appeal. Montreal's emphasis on cultural diversity and its friendly, inclusive atmosphere make it a top choice for many international students. The city's public transportation system is efficient, making it easy for students to explore and commute.

Calgary

Calgary, situated in Alberta, is becoming a notable destination for international students due to its strong economy and high quality of life. The University of Calgary and Mount Royal University offer diverse academic programs and research opportunities. Calgary's location near the Rocky Mountains provides students with stunning natural scenery and outdoor activities like skiing and hiking.

Calgary's lower cost of living than Toronto and Vancouver is attractive, as it allows students to manage their expenses more effectively. Calgary's economy, known for its energy, technology, and finance strengths, offers students valuable internship and job opportunities. The city's community-oriented atmosphere and growing educational reputation make it an appealing choice for those seeking a balanced student experience.

Ottawa

As Canada's capital, Ottawa offers a blend of academic excellence and political significance. The University of Ottawa and Carleton University are prominent institutions that provide a range of programs, including specialized courses in government and international relations. Studying in Ottawa allows students to access numerous internships and career opportunities related to government and diplomacy.

The city's historic landmarks, such as Parliament Hill and various national museums, add to its unique character. Compared to Canadian cities like Toronto and Vancouver, Ottawa's moderate cost of living makes it a more budget-friendly option for students. The city's cultural festivals and public events provide a vibrant student life, enhancing the educational experience. Ottawa's academic opportunities and cultural experiences make it a top destination for international students interested in government and public affairs.

Edmonton

Edmonton, the capital of Alberta, is increasingly recognized as a top destination for international students due to its growing educational and economic opportunities. The University of Alberta is a leading institution that offers a wide range of programs and research opportunities. Edmonton's vibrant cultural scene includes numerous festivals, art galleries, and music events, contributing to a dynamic student life.

Edmonton's relatively low cost of living compared to major Canadian cities, such as Toronto, Calgary, and Vancouver, makes it a more affordable option for students. Edmonton's growing economy and focus on innovation provide valuable internship and job opportunities, particularly in sectors such as technology and healthcare. The city's friendly atmosphere and supportive community enhance the student experience, making Edmonton an appealing choice for those seeking academic and personal growth.

Halifax

Halifax, located on the east coast of Canada, offers a charming and supportive environment for international students. Dalhousie University and Saint Mary's University are prominent institutions in the city, known for their strong academic programs and research opportunities. Halifax's coastal beauty, characterized by its waterfront and historic sites, provides a picturesque setting for student life. The city's manageable size and friendly communities create a more personalized and engaging experience than larger cities.

Halifax's cost of living is relatively low compared to other Atlantic coastal cities like Charlottetown, making it an affordable option for students. The city's emphasis on community involvement and its welcoming atmosphere contribute to a positive educational experience. Halifax's academic excellence, coastal charm, and affordability make it an excellent choice for international students seeking a balanced and enhancing student experience.

How Can I Study to the Top Cities for International Students in Canada?

Moving to Canada to study involves several important steps, primarily obtaining student permits and visas. You'll need a Canada student permit to move to one of Canada's top cities for international students.

Canada Student Permit

A Canada student permit is essential for international students wishing to reside and study in Canada. To apply for a Canadian study permit, you must have, among other required documents, proof of acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI), sufficient funds, and a clean criminal record.

Student Direct Stream

The Student Direct Stream (SDS) offers an expedited application process for students from select countries. The SDS is available to students from countries such as China, India, and the Philippines, and the aim is to streamline the study permit application process. The standard Canada student permit and the SDS require you to demonstrate your intention to return to your home country after completing your studies.

Learn about the new English language tests accepted for Canada's SDS.

Can I Stay in Canada After Graduating as an International Student?

Many international students aspire to extend their stay in Canada post-graduation. One popular pathway is the Postgraduate Work Permit (PGWP).

Postgraduate Work Permit

The PGWP allows graduates of eligible DLIs to work in Canada for up to three years, depending on the length of their study program. To qualify, your program of study must be at least eight months long and completed at a DLI. You must apply for the permit within 180 days of receiving confirmation of your graduation.

The PGWP provides valuable work experience that can enhance your skills and improve your chances of obtaining Canadian permanent residency through programs like the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) within the Express Entry system. This program has certain restrictions, such as the requirement that the program of study must have been completed full-time and at a recognized institution.

Learn how to get permanent residency in Canada after graduation.

FAQs

How do I Choose the Best City in Canada for my Studies?

To choose the best city in Canada for your studies, consider factors such as the quality and reputation of local educational institutions, the cost of living, and the city's cultural and recreational opportunities. Research each city's academic programs, student support services, and overall lifestyle to find the best fit for your personal and academic needs.

Which City is Best for International Students Looking for Job Opportunities After Graduation?

Toronto and Vancouver are top choices for international students seeking job opportunities after graduation due to their robust economies and numerous job openings in various sectors. Both Canadian cities have thriving job markets and offer extensive networking opportunities, which can help graduates find employment in their field of study.

Learn about the best cities in Canada to get a job after graduation.

Can International Students Work Part-time While Studying in Canada?

Yes, international students in Canada can work part-time while studying, typically up to 20 hours per week during academic sessions and full-time during scheduled breaks. Students must hold a valid study permit to work off-campus, including authorization for part-time work.
