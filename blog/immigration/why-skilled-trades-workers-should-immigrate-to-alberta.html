Why Skilled Trades Workers Should Immigrate to Alberta

by 
Themba Phongolo
×

7 min read

Alberta is rapidly emerging as a top destination for skilled trades workers seeking new opportunities and a vibrant lifestyle. Against the backdrop of urban amenities paired with stunning natural landscapes, the province offers a wealth of job prospects for skilled trade workers in various fields. Alberta also presents an opportunity for growth and fulfillment if you’re a skilled trades worker ready to take your career to the next level.

Eager to learn more about why Alberta is a great place to move to as a skilled trades worker? Then, look at the exceptional opportunities Alberta offers skilled trades workers.

Why Should Skilled Trades Workers Move to Alberta?

Alberta has emerged as a prime destination for skilled trades workers looking for lucrative careers and an exceptional quality of life. Here are some reasons to move to Alberta as a skilled trades worker.

Moving Bonus Incentive for Skilled Trades Workers in Alberta

Beginning in April 2024, the Government of Alberta launched the "Alberta is Calling" program to attract skilled tradespeople to the province. This initiative offers a one-time refundable tax credit of $5,000 to up to 2,000 eligible applicants. To qualify for this program, candidates must meet several requirements. First, they must be at least 18 years old at the time of application. Applicants need to be Canadian citizens or permanent residents.

They must relocate to Alberta between May 1 and December 31, 2024, and establish tax residency in Alberta by December 31, 2024. Furthermore, applicants should have been Alberta residents for at least 12 consecutive months by the time they apply. The program encourages skilled trades workers to make Alberta their home, contributing to the province's growing economy while taking advantage of the tax credit incentive.

Economic Growth in Alberta

Alberta's economy has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by its robust energy sector, agriculture, and manufacturing industries. This economic growth has created a strong demand for skilled trades professionals across various sectors, from construction and infrastructure to oil and gas.

Competitive Wages

Skilled trades workers in Alberta enjoy competitive wages that often exceed the national average. The province's high demand for skilled labor has led to a favorable job market, offering workers increased earning potential and job security. Moreover, the province's diverse industries ensure a steady flow of projects, reducing the risk of employment gaps.

According to Numbeo, Alberta’s premier city, Calgary, has a higher annual monthly net salary after tax than its competing prairie cities, Winnipeg, Manitoba, and Saskatoon, Saskatchewan.

Major Prairie Canadian Cities Average Monthly Net Salary After Tax (CAD)
Calgary, Alberta 4,851.83
Saskatoon, Saskatchewan 3,425.56
Winnipeg, Manitoba 3,357.45

Job Security

Alberta’s dynamic economy and ongoing infrastructure projects ensure job security for skilled trades workers. With continuous development in areas like oil and gas, renewable energy, and transportation, there is a steady demand for skilled professionals. The province’s commitment to expanding its infrastructure and investing in new technologies further solidifies job stability for trades workers.

Infrastructure Development in Alberta

Infrastructure development is a cornerstone of Alberta’s economic strategy. Major projects such as the Trans Mountain Expansion, the Edmonton LRT expansion, and numerous public construction initiatives contribute to a robust job market for tradespeople. The province’s dedication to modernizing and expanding its infrastructure means that skilled trades workers will find ample opportunities to contribute to and benefit from these large-scale projects.

Diverse Industries in Alberta

Alberta’s economy is characterized by diversity, including industries beyond oil and gas. Jobs in Alberta thrive in the following economic sectors.

  • Technology
  • Agriculture
  • Healthcare
  • Manufacturing

This industrial variety offers skilled trades workers the chance to explore different career paths and apply their skills in various fields. For example, the growing tech industry in Alberta requires skilled technicians for data centers and technological infrastructure, presenting new avenues for trade professionals.

Affordable Housing in Alberta

Compared to other major Canadian cities like Vancouver and Toronto, housing in Alberta offers more affordable options. Canadian cities such as Calgary and Edmonton provide a range of housing choices, from urban apartments to suburban homes, at relatively lower prices. This affordability extends to the cost of living, making housing in Alberta a financially possible option for skilled trades workers looking to relocate with their families.

According to the Canadian Real Estate Association (CREA), housing in Alberta costs approximately half what it does in major Canadian provinces such as British Columbia and Ontario, as indicated in the table below.

Major Canadian Provinces Average Housing Cost (CAD)
British Columbia 1,371,493.61
Ontario 1,215,029.01
Alberta 691,544.89

Alberta’s Natural Beauty

Beyond economic and professional advantages, Alberta’s natural beauty is a significant draw. The province is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, including the Rocky Mountains, pristine lakes, and expansive forests. Outdoor enthusiasts will appreciate the various recreational activities, including:

  • Hiking,
  • Skiing,
  • Fishing, and
  • Camping.

This connection to nature enhances the quality of life and provides a perfect balance to a rewarding career.

Alberta’s Supportive Immigration Policies

Alberta is committed to attracting skilled workers worldwide through its supportive immigration policies. The province’s immigration programs are designed to streamline the process for skilled trades professionals. The Alberta Advantage Nominee Program (AAIP) offers pathways for tradespeople to obtain Canadian permanent residency, making the transition to life in Alberta smoother and more straightforward.

Alberta Advantage Nominee Program

The AAIP is a key component of Alberta’s immigration strategy. It allows the province to nominate individuals with the skills and experience needed to fill labor shortages. Skilled trades workers can benefit from various AAIP streams specifically targeting trade professionals. By obtaining a provincial nomination, applicants can accelerate their immigration process and gain access to Canadian permanent residency.

Employer Sponsorship

Another significant advantage is Alberta’s emphasis on employer sponsorship. Many provincial businesses are eager to support skilled trade workers by offering sponsorships and work permits. This support not only facilitates the immigration process but also ensures that workers have job security and support from their employers as they settle into their new environment.

Cultural Diversity in Alberta

Alberta is known for its cultural diversity and inclusive communities. The province is home to a vibrant mix of cultures and ethnicities, contributing to a rich and welcoming environment for newcomers. This diversity is reflected in Alberta’s festivals, community events, and cultural organizations, providing trades workers with opportunities to connect with people from various backgrounds and integrate smoothly into their new community.

Strong Education System

The province’s robust education system benefits families relocating to Alberta. Alberta boasts high-quality educational institutions, including well-regarded public schools and post-secondary institutions. The education system emphasizes academic excellence and provides ample opportunities for students to succeed, ensuring that children of skilled trades workers receive a top-notch education.

What Are the Top 10 In-demand Skilled Trades Jobs in Alberta?

Alberta's thriving economy and significant infrastructure projects have created a robust demand for skilled trades workers in Alberta. Here are the top 10 in-demand skilled trades jobs in Alberta, with National Occupational Classification (NOC) codes and annual average salaries from Canada’s Job Bank.

Welder (NOC 72106)

With a booming construction industry and ongoing energy projects, welders are in high demand across various sectors.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 96,396.89 CAD.

Electrician (NOC 72200)

Both residential and commercial construction projects require skilled electricians for wiring, installation, and maintenance.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 96,420.05 CAD.

Plumber (NOC 72300)

The construction and maintenance of plumbing systems in residential, commercial, and industrial settings create a steady demand for skilled plumbers.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 93,741.72 CAD.

Carpenter (NOC 72310)

From framing houses to building commercial structures, carpenters are essential to the construction industry.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 74,984.91 CAD.

Heavy Equipment Operator (NOC 73400)

Alberta's vast landscape and infrastructure projects require skilled operators for machinery like excavators, bulldozers, and cranes.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 85,697.35 CAD.

Boilermaker (NOC 72103)

The oil and gas industry relies heavily on boilermakers to construct and maintain industrial equipment.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 120,037.59 CAD.

Pipefitter (NOC 72301)

Installing, assembling, and maintaining piping systems in oil and gas facilities is crucial and requires skilled pipefitters.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 112,510.12 CAD.

Industrial Mechanic (NOC 72400)

Maintaining and repairing heavy machinery used in oil and gas operations is vital.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 100,971.01 CAD.

Cook (NOC 63200)

Alberta's growing population and diverse food scene have increased the demand for skilled cooks in restaurants, hotels, and other food service establishments.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 45,532.52 CAD.

Hairstylist (NOC 63210)

The beauty industry is thriving, and skilled hairstylists are in demand in salons and barber shops across the province.

Annual average salaries in Alberta: 48,285.10 CAD.

What is the Best Way to Immigrate to Alberta as a Skilled Trades Worker?

For skilled trades workers looking to immigrate to Alberta, the most common pathway is through the AAIP. This provincial program is designed to attract skilled workers to meet the labor demands within the province. The AAIP offers several streams, but the most relevant for skilled trades workers is the Alberta Opportunity Stream.

To be eligible, you typically need a job offer from an Alberta employer, meet specific education and work experience requirements, and demonstrate language proficiency in English or French. Another option is the federal Express Entry system, which includes the Federal Skilled Trades Program.

While not specific to Alberta, it's a popular choice for skilled tradespeople. You'll need to create a profile, meet eligibility criteria, and be invited to apply for permanent residence based on your Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score. If successful, you can apply for a provincial nomination from Alberta to increase your CRS score.

Now that you have a better understanding of why skilled trades workers move to Alberta, you can begin your journey to become an Albertan skilled trades worker with the guidance of a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC).

FAQs

Is it Easy to Integrate Into Alberta's Community as a Newcomer?

Yes, integrating into Alberta's community is generally smooth for newcomers. The province is known for its welcoming atmosphere and diverse population, making it easy for new residents to connect and build relationships. Community support services and social groups help newcomers adjust and feel at home.

What is the Lifestyle Like in Alberta?

Alberta offers a high-quality lifestyle with urban convenience and natural beauty. Residents enjoy vibrant cities with numerous amenities, outdoor recreational opportunities, and a strong sense of community. The province also boasts affordable living costs compared to other major Canadian cities.

Can my Family Accompany me if I Immigrate to Alberta?

Yes, your family can accompany you when you immigrate to Alberta. Immigration pathways like the AAIP allow family members to join the primary applicant. This ensures you and your loved ones can start a new life together in Alberta.
