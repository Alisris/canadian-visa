Get Hiring in Canada - Top Canadian Immigration Options for HR Managers

by Nicky VZ. Updated: August 15th, 2020

Are you an experienced hiring manager wondering what your Canadian immigration options are? The good news is that as a skilled worker with managerial experience, you already have one foot in the door. Now it‚Äôs only a matter of finding the right skilled worker program for which you qualify! The bad news? There are over 100 immigration and visa programs available in Canada, most of which are aimed at economic immigrants. Luckily, at CanadianVisa.org, we know what it takes for you to have a successful immigration application.

In this blog, we uncover three of your best Canadian immigration options to take you from where you are now to living your best life in the Great North.

Reasons to Pursue Canadian Immigration


The People

Meeting new people and interacting with many different individuals is a big part of an HR manager‚Äôs daily life. Pursuing your career in Canada means that you‚Äôll greatly benefit from the friendly, hospitable, and sweet nature of Canadians - and no, it‚Äôs not just a stereotype. Canadians are ranked as some of the nicest and most welcoming people in the world. Who wouldn‚Äôt want to work with them?

The High Quality of Life

Canada ranks well above the average for quality of life. We have affordable housing for as low as $143, 000 for a 2-bedroom house in places outside of major cities. Your subjective well-being and health are a high priority in Canada and therefore, each citizen and permanent resident has access to free, quality healthcare. Furthermore, Canada is one of the safest countries and places emphasis on environmental quality through many green initiatives. Canada also recently added 419, 000 new jobs with generous earnings and work-life balance.

Top Canadian Immigration Options for HR Managers


There are three great options for HR managers from overseas to consider if they are serious about immigrating to Canada. Canadian immigration relies heavily on skills transferability. That means your knowledge, skills, and experience as an HR manager is very much valued and will increase your chances of immigration success.

1) The fastest option

HR managers could immigrate to Canada in as little as 6-months through the Express Entry immigration system. What‚Äôs it all about? The Express Entry uses a points-based system known as the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) to rank candidates based on the competitiveness of their online profiles. This means if you have a high score for all the eligibility requirements you will receive an Invitation to Apply for permanent residency during one of routine draw rounds, sooner rather than later.

Check below if you meet the minimum requirements of the Federal Skilled Worker Program - an immigration program managed by the Express Entry system:


Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)
Eligibility RequirementDescription
Work ExperienceOne year within the last 10 years
Language SkillsExcellent English or French language proficiency
Job OfferNot required but can increase your CRS points
EducationMinimum of a high school diploma or higher

Express Entry Immigration Tip

If you want to have one of the most competitive profiles in the Express Entry draw pool then you should try to achieve a provincial nomination which equates to a whopping 600 CRS points! A provincial nomination is given to candidates who possess qualities that are sought after by a provincial government in Canada. For example, Ontario is vying for French-speaking skilled workers from overseas.

2) The employer support option


This option should be a walk in the park for you since you know the insights of the hiring process best of all. Securing a job in Canada and working in a human resources position for at least 1-year before applying for permanent residency could be a better plan, and here‚Äôs why:

Canadian work experience opens the door to more immigration options and increases your CRS points. Secondly, you are also more likely to achieve a provincial nomination from the province in which you gained your work experience in Canada. And lastly, you won‚Äôt have to blindly settle in Canada. You get to acclimate to life in the Great North first, get a feel for where you want to live and make friends along the way. This option may be slower but it eases you into a life-changing journey.

3) The option of settling in Manitoba


You must be thinking: why Manitoba of all places in this big, bountiful country? Well, Manitoba has a shortage of HR managers and have even listed HR managers on their list of in-demand occupations for 2020. This means you can easily achieve a provincial nomination by applying to Manitoba‚Äôs own immigration pathway known as the Skilled Worker from Overseas Stream.

Learn more: How to Move to Manitoba, Canada

The great news is not only are HR managers highly sought after but they can also earn a generous annual salary of $110, 036 in the capital city of Winnipeg! This is well above the national average. What‚Äôs more, Manitoba is far more affordable than the other more popular cities in Canada, like Toronto and Vancouver.
