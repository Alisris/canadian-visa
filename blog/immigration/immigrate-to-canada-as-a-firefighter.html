Immigrate to Canada as a Firefighter

by 
Themba Phongolo

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: June 28th, 2023

Immigrating to a new country can be difficult for anyone for many reasons. One of the main reasons is that the actual process can be tough regarding requirements and any other obstacles you may need to get through to immigrate.

Are you a dedicated firefighter looking for new opportunities in Canada? If you are, then you may have considered the question of how to become a firefighter in Canada as a foreigner?.

Canada, known for its excellent quality of life, is experiencing an increase in the demand for firefighter jobs in Canada for foreigners. In response, Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) has accommodated foreign firefighters who want to immigrate to Canada through several immigration visa pathways.

With over 80 immigration visa pathways, choosing the right immigration visa program, pilot, or stream can be difficult.

Therefore, we will walk you through the various pathways available for immigrating to Canada as a firefighter, the best provinces for firefighter jobs, and how to become a firefighter in Canada. Seize the Opportunity to get a Firefighter jobs in Canada with this helpful guide about how to immigrate to Canada as a Firefighter.

How to Immigrate to Canada as a Firefighter

Moving to Canada is an option for you with or without a job offer. This is made possible through the Express Entry system and the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP). Seize the Opportunity to get a Firefighter Canada job in Canada with this helpful guide about how to immigrate to Canada as a Firefighter.

Express Entry System

One of the main pathways for skilled workers, including firefighters, to immigrate to Canada is through the Express Entry system. This system manages applications for permanent residence under three federal economic immigration programs:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

Find out more about Canadas Express Entry system.

Federal Skilled Worker Program

The Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP) is designed for skilled workers with at least one year of full-time work experience in a professional occupation. As a firefighter, your experience and skills will be highly valued. To be eligible, you must meet the minimum requirements for:

  • Language proficiency
  • Education
  • Work experience
  • Adaptability factors

Learn more about Canadas Federal Skilled Worker Program.

Canadian Experience Class

If you have already gained work experience in Canada as a firefighter, you may be eligible to apply for permanent residence through the Canadian Experience Class. This program is specifically for individuals with Canadian work experience and aims to retain skilled workers who have adapted to the Canadian labor market.

Find out more about the Canadian Experience Class.

Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

Besides the federal programs, many provinces and territories in Canada have the PNPs that allow them to nominate individuals with specific skills or work experience for permanent residence. Some provinces have specific streams or categories for firefighters, recognizing the demand for this profession.

Learn more about Canadas provincial and territorial PNPs.

Immigration Pilots for Firefighters

To address regional labor market needs, Canada has introduced specific immigration pilots for certain occupations, including firefighters. These pilots aim to attract skilled workers to specific regions in Canada with a need for more workers in these occupations.

Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP)

The Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP) is a partnership between the Government of Canada and the four Atlantic provinces: Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, and Prince Edward Island. This pilot program allows employers in the Atlantic region to hire foreign workers, including firefighters, and support their immigration to Canada.

Find out more about the eligibility requirements and how to apply for the AIP.

Work Permits for Firefighters

If you are looking for temporary work opportunities in Canada as a firefighter, there are several options available to you.

Temporary Foreign Work Permit

A Temporary Foreign Work Permit (TFWP) allows you to work in Canada for a specific employer and duration. To obtain this permit, you will need a job offer from a Canadian employer and a positive Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA), which demonstrates that there are no Canadian citizens or permanent residents available to fill the position.

Find out more about the Temporary Foreign Work Permit.

Bridging Open Work Permit

If you are already in Canada with a valid work permit and have applied for permanent residence through the Express Entry system, you may be eligible for a Bridging Open Work Permit (BOWP). This permit allows you to continue working in Canada while your permanent residence application is being processed.

Learn more about the Bridging Open Work Permit.

Find out more about Canadas different types of work permits.

3 Best Provinces for Firefighter Jobs Canada

If you're looking for the best provinces in Canada for firefighter jobs, consider the following:

Yukon Territory

The Yukon Territory offers excellent opportunities for firefighters. With its vast wilderness and unique challenges, firefighters in the Yukon Territory play a crucial role in protecting communities and natural resources. The Yukon government actively recruits firefighters outside the territory to address the demand.

According to Canada Job Bank, The annual average base salary for firefighters in Yukon is not yet determined but estimates put it closer to the national average of 87,750 CAD.

Quebec

Quebec is another province with a high demand for firefighters. The province has a diverse population and a unique cultural heritage. Firefighters in Quebec are responsible for protecting both urban and rural areas, and the profession is highly respected.

The annual average base salary for firefighters in Quebec is 74,997 CAD, according to Canada's Job Bank.

Alberta

Alberta is known for its oil and gas industry but also has a strong demand for firefighters. With its growing population and expanding cities, Alberta requires skilled firefighters to ensure public safety. The province offers competitive salaries and excellent benefits for firefighters.

The annual average base salary for firefighters in Alberta is 95,550 CAD according to Canada's Job Bank.

What do I Need to Become a Firefighter in Canada?

The specific requirements to become a firefighter in Canada may vary depending on the province or territory. However, there are some general guidelines and qualifications that most fire departments look for in candidates.

  • You must be 18 years of age or older when you apply
  • Can work for any employer in Canada on a legal basis
  • Have a Valid First Aid Certificate and CPR Level HCP
  • Have no criminal convictions
  • Posess a Pleasure Craft Operator Card
  • Grade 12 Diploma or equivalent qualification
    • It must be validated by the World Education Services or other eligible organization (OSSD in Ontario)
  • Unrestricted and Validated Ontario "G" class license (or equivalent for other provinces)
  • Uncorrected 20/30 vision
  • Physical Fitness Assessment that is validated (Varies by province)
  • Standard, normal color vision
  • Unaided normal hearing
  • Provide proof of immunization from:
    • Rubella
    • Red Measles
    • Covid-19
    • Tetanus
  • Be able to work rotating shifts
    • including nights, holidays, and weekends
  • Be able to work under high-pressure

Benefits of Working as a Firefighter in Canada

Working as a firefighter in Canada comes with numerous benefits. Here are some of the advantages you can expect:

Firefighter Employment Benefits

Firefighters in Canada are entitled to benefits like health and dental insurance, pension plans, and paid vacation time according to Fire Recruitment Canada. Moreover, many firefighters in Canada are part of unions, which affects their salary and benefits. According to forces.ca, in you enroll as a firefighter in the Canadian Forces you will be entitled to receive paid education of a minimum 27,000 CAD, a monetary signing bonus and competitive pay rates that go up with each promotion.

High Demand

Firefighters are in high demand across Canada. As the population grows and communities expand, the need for skilled firefighters increases. This high demand provides excellent job security and opportunities for career growth.

Learn more about Canadas in-demand occupations.

Path to PR in Canada

Many pathways for immigration to Canada as a firefighter lead to permanent residence. Once you obtain permanent residence, you can enjoy all the rights and benefits of being a Canadian citizen, including healthcare, social services, and access to education.

High Quality of Life

Canada consistently ranks among the top countries in the world for quality of life. With its excellent healthcare system, low crime rates, and beautiful natural landscapes, Canada offers a safe and enjoyable environment for firefighters and their families.

Opportunities for Skills Development

Working as a firefighter in Canada provides numerous opportunities for professional development and skills enhancement, especially in Canadas largest cities. Fire departments invest in training and education for their firefighters, ensuring they have the latest knowledge and skills to handle emergencies effectively.

Immigrating to Canada as a firefighter can be exciting and rewarding. With various pathways to permanent residency and a high demand for skilled firefighters, Canada offers ample opportunities for individuals looking to start a new life chapter.

Whether you pursue permanent residency or work temporarily as a firefighter, Canada welcomes your skills and contributions to the firefighting profession.

FAQs

What are the Firefighter Jobs in Canada?

Firefighter jobs in Canada involve responding to emergencies, including fires, medical incidents, and hazardous materials incidents. Firefighters play a critical role in protecting lives and property, conducting rescue operations, and educating the community about fire safety.

What is the Average Base Annual salary for Firefighters in Canada?

While salaries for firefighters in Canada can vary depending on the province, experience, and other factors, the average base annual salary for a firefighter in Canada is 87,750 CAD according to Canada's Job Bank. Please note that this figure does not include overtime pay or additional allowances, which can significantly increase a firefighter's total income.
