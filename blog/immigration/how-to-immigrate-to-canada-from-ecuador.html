How to Immigrate to Canada from Ecuador

by Nadine Wolfe. Updated: November 10th, 2021
5 min read

Thousands of people from all over the world have chosen to immigrate to Canada and call this their second home. Canada plans to welcome over 400,000 new permanent residents in 2021 and 25,000 immigrants living in Canada are from Ecuador. If you’re an Ecuadorian and are interested in immigrating, Canada should be at the top of your list.

Many people move to Canada from Ecuador to create a better life for themselves and their families. If this is something you wish to have, you won’t be disappointed. Canada is known as the best country in the world when it comes to quality education, healthcare, safety and diversity.

Canadian Citizens and immigrants alike practice tolerance and are accepting of different cultures and belief systems. This makes Canada one of the best places to settle while still being able to hold onto the things you hold dear and live freely.

There are over 100 visa and immigration programs available. Let's look at the immigration programs that will give you the best chance of successfully applying for permanent residence in Canada.

4 Ways to Get to Canada from Ecuador

If you want to move to Canada from Ecuador, you need to apply for permanent residence status. There are a number of immigration programs that you can apply through to obtain permanent residency in Canada, namely:

  • Express Entry
  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSW)
  • Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)
  • Atlantic Immigration Pilot

Get There in a Flash with Express Entry


You guessed it, it’s in the name! Express Entry is the fastest way to immigrate to Canada, with a processing time of just six months. The Express Entry system was developed to allow Canada to meet labor market needs by filling in demand job opportunities. Canada needs foreign workers to fill these in demand employment opportunities and help the economy grow.

Express Entry is used to select candidates to move to Canada through various Federal Economic Immigration programs. You are most likely to be successful at applying for Express Entry if you meet the following criteria:

  • Are under 30 years old
  • Are able to prove that you have moderate to high English or French language proficiency
  • Have at least 3 years of skilled work experience

Check out the complete list of requirements you need to meet if you want to immigrate to Canada from Ecuador through Express Entry.

Federal Skilled Worker Program


If you have tertiary education qualifications, work experience or skills, you can immigrate from Ecuador through the Federal Skilled Worker Program and contribute to the Canadian Economy.

To qualify for this program you need to prove that you meet the necessary requirements. These requirements include having work experience as a professional, or experience in a trade or technical occupation. If you are successful in your application, you will obtain permanent residency in Canada.

If this sounds like a good fit for you, take a look at the list of requirements.

To qualify for the Federal Skilled Worker Program, you must:

  • Have 1 year of full-time experience (or equivalent hours made up in part-time work) that you acquired recently (no longer than 10 years ago.
  • Your job needs to be classified as skill level 0, A or B on the National Occupation Classification list
  • Have at least a secondary education
  • Take the Canadian Language Benchmark test which will assess you English language abilities in four categories: writing, reading, speaking and listening, and
  • Obtain a minimum score of Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) 7 in all 4 abilities

The factors mentioned above can also increase your CRS score.

To determine if you’re eligible for a Canadian Skilled Worker Visa, you will be assessed. But don’t worry, we know what it’s on:

  • Your level of English or French proficiency
  • Your level of education
  • Years of work experience
  • Whether you have a job offer or not
  • Your age


Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)

So, you want to move to Canada from Ecuador, but do you have a certain province in mind? If you do, this program could be for you.

Did you know that Canada has planned to welcome 64,000 to 81,500 immigrants through its Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) in 2021? There are over 80 provincial nominee programs in Canada. PNPs enable participating provinces and territories to nominate eligible immigration candidates for permanent residency in Canada.

There are 11 Provincial Nominee Programs which each have their own requirements.

Atlantic Immigration Pilot


Imagine yourself sinking your feet into crisp sand, looking at clear blue waters and feeling warm air on your skin as the sun sets. Well, with the Atlantic Immigration Pilot this can be your reality. The Atlantic Immigration Pilot is directed toward people who want to live and work in one of Canada’s four Atlantic provinces.

Build a stable life in Canada while living on the borders of the ocean. You can have your pick from Newfoundland and Labrador, Prince Edward Island, New Brunswick and Nova Scotia.

If you are from Ecuador, or elsewhere in the world, but have completed your studies in one of these four provinces, you can start your life here; the pilot is open to you too! The Atlantic Immigration Pilot welcomes foreign graduates who have completed their studies in one of these four Atlantic provinces.

So how do you grab this awesome opportunity? Here are three different programs offered by the Atlantic Immigration Pilot.

Atlantic International Graduate Program

This program is directed toward people who have a qualification from a public tertiary institution in one of Canada’s Atlantic provinces.

To apply for this program you will need to meet the following criteria:

  • You will need to have lived in the province for at least 16 months in the two years before attaining your qualification
  • You must take a language test to assess your proficiency in English or French
  • You must prove that you are able to support yourself as well as any dependent children or family members that will be accompanying you

Atlantic High-skilled Program

To apply for this program, you must:

  • Have at least 1 year’s experience working in a management position, professional or skilled occupation
  • You must have a foreign degree or diploma that is equal to a Canadian credential. To prove this, you will need to have an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) report done
  • You will need to take an approved language test that will assess your English and/or French language proficiency
  • You will need to provide evidence that you have enough financial resources to support yourself and any dependents whether they are accompanying you to Canada or not

Atlantic Intermediate-skilled Program

Here are the criteria to apply for this program:

  • Have at least 1 year’s experience working in a position that requires a high school education or job-specific training
  • Have a high school diploma that is equal to a Canadian credential. To prove this, you will need to obtain an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) report
  • You will need to take an approved language test that will assess your English and/or French language proficiency
  • You will need to provide evidence that you have enough financial resources to support yourself and any dependents whether they are accompanying you to Canada or not

Once you have found the immigration program for you, it’s time to start applying. Instead of taking on the visa application process by yourself, invite us on your journey to immigrate to Canada from Ecuador, and receive help every step of the way.
