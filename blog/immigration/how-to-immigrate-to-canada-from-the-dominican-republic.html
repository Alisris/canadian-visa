How to Immigrate to Canada from the Dominican Republic

by 
Wesley Allan

Author:

Wesley Allan

Bachelor's Degree in English Language and Literature and Film Studies

Wesley Allan has been writing professionally for over five years and about immigration for a year. With a BA in English Language and Literature and Film Studies.

. Updated: July 18th, 2023

Dreaming of a vibrant, prosperous life in the land of the maple leaf? You're not alone. Every year, thousands of individuals from the Dominican Republic make the life-altering decision to immigrate to Canada, a land known for its robust economy, high standard of living, and welcoming multicultural society. This journey, while exciting, can be filled with complexities and challenges. It requires careful planning, preparation, and a thorough understanding of the immigration process. This guide will walk you through the essential steps to make your Canadian dream a reality. Canada has what you need whether you seek better educational opportunities, career advancements, or a fresh start!

How to Immigrate from the Dominican Republic to Canada

Immigrating to another country is a significant decision that involves many steps and processes. Here's a simplified guide to help you navigate the Dominican Republic to Canada journey.

Express Entry Canada

The Express Entry Canada system is the nation's primary method of attracting skilled immigrants. If you're a skilled worker, this could be your ticket from the Dominican Republic to Canada. You must ensure you're eligible for one of the three Express Entry Programs: 

  • The Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)
    • This program is for professionals with at least one year of experience from outside Canada in jobs classified under Canada's National Occupational Classification (NOC) system's Training, Education, Experience and Responsibility (TEER) Levels 0 or 1, often requiring a university degree, like a doctor, lawyer, or software developer.
  • The Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)
    • This is for skilled tradespeople, such as plumbers, electricians, and chefs, with at least two years of work experience in a skilled trade Classified NOC TEER 2 or 3 that often requires a diploma or job-specific training.
  • The Canadian Experience Class (CEC)
    • This is for skilled workers with at least one year of work experience in Canada.

Start by creating an Express Entry Canada profile online and enter details like:

  • Education,
  • Marital status,
  • Work experience,
  • Age,
  • Language proficiency in English or French and
  • Connections to Canada.

The government of Canada evaluates your details based on the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) to create a CRS score. All CRS scores enter into an Express Entry Draw, and applicants with scores higher than a set number receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residence. If you're an ideal candidate, you may receive your Canadian permanent residence in as little as six months. To determine your eligibility for this program, you can use a CRS calculator to give you an initial idea. If you feel your score isn't quite where you need it to be to get an ITA, you can learn more about improving your CRS score. 

PNP Canada

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP Canada) is another pathway for emigration. Each Canadian province or territory has its PNP, targeting specific skills needed in that region. If you have a skill in demand in a particular province, you could be nominated for immigration. Remember, being nominated through PNP greatly improves your Express Entry ranking.

Pilot Program Canada

Canada's Pilot Programs offer unique immigration pathways. These include the following:

  • The Atlantic Immigration Pilot
    • This pilot program Canada connects employers in Newfoundland & Labrador, Prince Edward Island, Nova Scotia, and New Brunswick with skilled foreign workers and international graduates, providing a pathway to permanent residence for over 6,000 newcomers each year.
  • The Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot
    • This community-driven program spreads the benefits of economic immigration to smaller communities in Ontario, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Alberta, and British Columbia by creating a path to permanent residence for skilled foreign workers who want to work and live in one of the participating communities.
  • The Agri-Food Immigration Pilot
    • This pilot program Canada provides a pathway to permanent residence for experienced, non-seasonal workers in specific industries and occupations. 
  • The Home Caregiver Pilots
    • These pilots enable home childcare providers and support workers to immigrate to Canada.

Each program has specific requirements and targets certain regions or industries. If you meet the criteria, these pilot programs offer a direct route to permanent residence. Learn more about Canada's pilot programs. 

Temporary Residence to Permanent Residence (TR to PR) Pathway

The Canadian government introduced the TR to PR pathway in 2021. The TR to PR pathways were introduced through special public policies to grant permanent status to temporary workers and international graduates with the skills and experience needed for Canada's economy. It is aimed at approximately 90,000 skilled workers, their families, and foreign graduates already residing in Canada. To apply for the TR to PR immigration pathways, applicants must meet certain eligibility requirements, including being in Canada, having valid status in Canada, and planning to live outside of Quebec. Learn more about Canada's TR to PR pathway.

If unsure which pathway is right, you can hugely benefit from talking to a Canadian immigration expert like a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC).

Why Move From Dominican Republic to Canada

Canada has much to offer immigrants from the Dominican Republic. Here are some reasons why you should consider making the move.

Canada's Diverse, Tolerant Culture and Commitment to Supporting Human Rights

The Canadian government takes immense pride in its ability to welcome individuals from various walks of life, particularly from diverse ethnicities, cultures, languages, and religions. This is a testament to the country's unwavering commitment to cultural diversity and human rights. Every person who steps on Canadian soil is guaranteed an environment that treats them with dignity and respect, a value highly upheld in this nation. 

As a result, people from places as diverse as the Dominican Republic find it easy to call Canada their new home. The harmonious cohabitation within this countrys borders is not just a point of pride but a symbol of unity and strength. The government of Canada continues its efforts to foster this unity, recognizing it as the backbone of its strong society. This commitment has made Canada globally recognized for its welcoming embrace to all who seek refuge or a fresh start within its borders. Learn more about Canada's welcoming multiculturalism.

World-class Universal Healthcare in Canada

Canada has the significant advantage of having a world-class universal healthcare system accessible to all Canadian citizens and permanent residents. The healthcare system is publicly funded and administered primarily by the country's 13 provinces and territories, each with its insurance plan. The federal government provides cash assistance to each province and territory per capita.

All citizens and permanent residents receive medically necessary hospital and physician services free at the point of use. The Canada Health Act of 1984 sets national standards for medically necessary hospital, diagnostic, and physician services. The primary objective of the Canadian healthcare policy is to "protect, promote and restore the physical and mental well-being of residents of Canada and to facilitate reasonable access to health services without financial or other barriers." Learn more about Canada's world-class healthcare system.

Over 800,000 Vacancies for Jobs in Canada

Canada's economy is robust and consistently growing, creating numerous job opportunities for immigrants. There are over 800,000 job vacancies across various sectors, providing a vast field for you to find a job that suits your skills and experience. These jobs range through all industries so that opportunities may await you. Learn how to land a job in Canada.

However, not all jobs are equal in Canada. Canada often suffers skill shortages due to their aging population. As a result, workers in several specific occupations are in higher demand in Canada than others. Not only that, but these in-demand occupations differ between each of Canada's provinces. This map details the in-demand occupations for each of Canada's provinces.

 

The Government of Canada is Stable and Well Functioning

Canada's government is renowned for its stability and efficiency, providing a conducive environment for residents. The government actively invests in infrastructure, education, healthcare, and social services, ensuring its citizens' high quality of life. Learn more about Canada's government structure. 

Canada is One of the World's Safest Countries

Many immigrants choose Canada because of the country's safety. Ranked among the world's safest countries, Canada offers a peaceful and secure environment for you and your family. This is due to the following factors: 

  • Low Crime Rates
    • Canada has lower crime rates than many other countries.
  • High-Quality Healthcare
    • Canada's healthcare system is highly regarded worldwide, and all Canadians, including those with permanent resident status, have access to free family doctor and hospital services throughout the country.
  • Safe Communities
    • Due to the low crime rates, high levels of education, and quality government services and provisions, most Canadians feel safe walking around their communities.
  • Strong Immigration Policy
    • Canada has a reputation for welcoming immigrants and valuing multiculturalism; foreign-born people comprise about one-quarter of the population.

Learn more about Canada's safety levels.

Immigrating from the Dominican Republic to Canada is a significant decision but has many benefits. The process may seem daunting, but it's achievable with the right information and guidance. Remember, Immigration experts can also provide support throughout your immigration journey.

FAQs

How Do I Know if My English or French is Good Enough to Move To Canada?

To immigrate to Canada, you must demonstrate proficiency in English or French as per the Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB). You can take a recognized language test such as IELTS for English or TEF for French. The scores needed depend on the immigration program you're applying through.

What is the Dominican Expat Community Like in Canada?

The Dominican community in Canada is vibrant and welcoming, offering a sense of home away from home. Several Dominican community organizations across the country organize cultural events and offer support to new immigrants. Most Dominicans in Canada reside in major cities like Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver. These cities offer various opportunities and have active Dominican communities.
