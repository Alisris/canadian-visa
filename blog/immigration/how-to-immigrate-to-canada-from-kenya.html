How to Immigrate to Canada from Kenya in 2023

by 
Kelia Losa
×

Are you considering migrating to Canada from Kenya in 2023? You won’t be alone. Canada is a top destination for Kenyan expats. According to the IRCC monthly updates, 39 808 temporary resident permits were issued to Kenyans between 2020 and the first quarter of 2023. Additionally, 7774 study permits and 2714 permanent residencies were granted in the same period.

Discover the essential steps and requirements for a successful immigration process. From understanding the available immigration programs to preparing your application and fulfilling the necessary criteria, this guide will provide valuable insights on migrating to Canada from Kenya. Explore Canada's opportunities and embark on your journey towards a new life in this diverse and vibrant country.

How to Move to Canada from Kenya

Canada is a popular destination for individuals and families looking to immigrate and start a new life. With its diverse culture, strong economy, and numerous opportunities, Canada attracts immigrants from all over the world, including Kenya.

Express Entry System

Canada faces a labor shortage crisis in various occupations, including professional and intermediate jobs. To address this shortage, Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) has created three skilled worker categories to facilitate immigration for skilled international workers. The Express Entry system, established in 2015, has three immigration programs.

Express Entry is designed to enable skilled and semi-skilled foreign workers and their families to become permanent residents in Canada as quickly as possible. The processing time for Express Entry applications is as little as six months, which is crucial for meeting the urgent need for skilled workers in various industries across Canada.

How Does the Canada Express Entry System Work?

To obtain permanent residency through the Express Entry system, applicants must create an Express Entry profile. The profile requires specific details about the applicant, including age, education, language skills, work experience, marital status, and links to Canada. These details calculate a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score out of 1200.

The CRS score is determined based on skills transferability, French language skills, Canadian education, Canadian job offer, and Provincial Nomination. The higher the CRS score, the better the chances of receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residency.

Provincial Nominee Program

The Provincial Nominee Program allows provinces and territories in Canada to nominate immigrants with the required work experience, skills, and education that meet their specific needs. Each province or territory has its own set of requirements and streams under the PNP.

The PNP offers an alternative pathway to permanent residency for individuals who may not meet the eligibility criteria of the federal programs like Express Entry. Provinces and territories actively seek individuals who can contribute to their local economy and address labor market demands.

Family Sponsorship

Family reunification is an essential aspect of Canadian immigration. The Family Sponsorship Class is a program designed to help reunite families by allowing Canadian citizens and permanent residents to sponsor their eligible family members for immigration to Canada.

Under the Family Sponsorship program, eligible sponsors can sponsor their spouse, common-law partner, conjugal partner, dependent children, parents, grandparents, and other eligible relatives. Sponsors must meet certain financial and sponsorship obligations to support their sponsored family members.

Business Immigration

Canada offers fast and efficient business immigration streams for foreign nationals who are business-savvy and have a strong business plan to implement in Canada. The country has experienced growth in major industries such as oil, gas, consumption, housing, agriculture, and business investment.

Business immigration programs in Canada aim to attract entrepreneurs, investors, and self-employed individuals who can contribute to the Canadian economy. These programs provide pathways to permanent residency for individuals with the necessary business experience, investment capital, and entrepreneurial skills.

Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP)

The Atlantic Immigration Pilot is designed to attract skilled workers and international graduates to the Atlantic provinces of Newfoundland and Labrador, Prince Edward Island, Nova Scotia, and New Brunswick. This pilot program aims to support the economic growth of these provinces by addressing labor market gaps and retaining skilled workers in the region.

Through the AIP, employers in the Atlantic provinces can hire eligible foreign workers and graduates who meet specific criteria. The program offers a pathway to permanent residency for individuals who secure a job offer from a designated employer and receive a commitment to support their settlement and integration in the Atlantic region.

Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot (RNIP)

TheRural and Northern Immigration Pilot focuses on supporting the economic development of smaller communities in Canada's rural and northern regions. The program aims to address the demographic challenges faced by these communities by attracting skilled workers willing to settle and contribute to their growth.

Under the RNIP, participating communities can recommend eligible foreign workers for permanent residency. Applicants must meet the community-specific criteria and secure a job offer from an employer in the participating community. This pilot program offers a unique pathway for individuals seeking to establish their lives and careers in smaller, close-knit Canadian communities.

Work in Canada from Kenya

Working in Canada offers numerous benefits and opportunities for individuals from Kenya. Canada's strong economy, high standard of living, and diverse job market make it an attractive destination for professionals seeking career growth and a better quality of life.

Jobs in Canada

Canada provides a wide range of job opportunities across various industries. Individuals should explore the in-demand jobs list to identify sectors with high demand for skilled workers. Websites like Indeed, which currently has just under 500,000 job listings, can be valuable resources for job seekers.

By researching and applying for jobs in their respective fields, individuals from Kenya can take advantage of Canada's vibrant job market and secure employment that aligns with their skills and qualifications.

Find out how you can apply for a job in Canada in just 5 easy steps.

Work Visa in Canada

Canada offers different types of work visas to individuals who wish to work in the country. The two primary categories are open work permits and employer-specific work permits.

An open work permit allows individuals to work for any employer in Canada, providing flexibility and the ability to explore different job opportunities. On the other hand, an employer-specific work permit is tied to a specific employer and job, requiring individuals to have a job offer from a Canadian employer before applying for a work permit.

To apply for a work visa in Canada, individuals need to meet specific requirements, including:

  • Demonstrating eligibility for the chosen work visa category.
  • Providing necessary documentation, such as a valid passport, proof of educational qualifications, and relevant work experience.
  • Submitting the completed application form and the required fees.
  • Obtaining a job offer from a Canadian employer (for employer-specific work permits).
  • Meeting health and character requirements.
  • Demonstrating sufficient financial resources to support themselves and any accompanying family members during their stay in Canada.
  • Adhering to the application submission guidelines provided by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) or the nearest Canadian visa office in Kenya.

It is important to carefully follow the application process and fulfill all the necessary criteria to increase the chances of a successful work visa application.

By understanding the available work visas and following the application process, individuals from Kenya can pursue employment opportunities in Canada and take a significant step towards building a successful career there.

Life in Canada as a Kenyan

Immigrating to Canada from Kenya offers a fresh start and a multitude of reasons that make the journey worthwhile. Canada provides a safer and more secure environment for individuals and families seeking refuge from daily life's uncertainties. With a robust social system and strict adherence to human rights, Canada offers a gentle and inclusive society that allows immigrants worldwide to unyoke themselves from the burdens they carried in their homeland. The peace of mind from living in a country where personal safety is less of a concern is truly liberating.

The economic aspect also plays a significant role in the decision to immigrate. While the cost of living in Canada may be higher compared to Kenya, the opportunities for financial stability and career growth are abundant. Although finding work as a newcomer may take time, the potential for long-term success is promising. It is crucial to bring a substantial financial safety net to navigate the initial settling period in a new country.

Canada's multicultural fabric is another captivating aspect that draws Kenyan immigrants. The diverse tapestry of cultures coexisting harmoniously in Canadian cities like Halifax is a testament to the nation's embrace of its immigrant population. Interacting with people from all corners of the globe fosters a sense of belonging and enriches the overall Canadian experience. The availability of a wide range of cuisines and the opportunity to connect with fellow expatriates contribute to a vibrant and inclusive Canadian society.

The freedom to express ideas and contribute to the Canadian social landscape allows Kenyan immigrants to bring fresh perspectives and innovative solutions back to their homeland. Immigrants can reflect upon their experiences and develop new understandings of the challenges faced in Kenya, enabling them to advocate for positive change and contribute to the growth and development of their home country.

The decision to immigrate from Kenya to Canada is driven by the desire for a better quality of life, enhanced personal safety, economic opportunities, cultural diversity, and the ability to make a difference in Canada and Kenya. It is an opportunity for growth, empowerment, and the chance to build a brighter future for yourself and future generations.

Cost of Living

To maintain the same standard of life as Toronto, you would need approximately 2,697.6CCAD (285,948.9KSh) in Nairobi (assuming you rent in both cities). This calculation uses the Cost of Living Plus Rent Index, considering net earnings after income tax. Please note that you can adjust the amount in this calculation.

Rent Prices 79.0% lower than in Toronto
Restaurant Prices 66.2% lower than in Toronto
Groceries Prices 58.3% lower than in Toronto

While the cost of living in Nairobi may be lower than in Toronto, it's important to consider other factors. Wages in Canada are significantly higher, contributing to a higher standard of living. Additionally, Canada boasts a high safety index thanks to its stable infrastructure, economy, political stability, and accessible healthcare. It is a global melting pot of cultures, making it an ideal place to call home.

Culture and Integration

Canada is known for its multicultural society, where diversity is celebrated and respected. The Canadian culture is a unique blend of indigenous traditions and influences from various immigrant communities. As a Kenyan immigrant, you will find a welcoming and inclusive environment encouraging cultural integration.

Similarities in Culture:

  • Respect for diversity
    • Canada values cultural diversity and promotes equality and inclusivity for all individuals, regardless of their background.
  • Hospitality and politeness
    • Canadians are known for their friendly and polite nature, making it easier for newcomers to feel welcomed and accepted.
  • Strong sense of community
    • Canadians often prioritize community engagement and volunteerism, creating opportunities for Kenyan immigrants to connect and contribute to their local communities.

Differences in Culture

  • Social norms and customs
    • While there may be differences in social etiquette and customs between Kenya and Canada, these differences can be understood and embraced through cultural exchange and adaptation.
  • Weather and lifestyle
    • Adapting to the Canadian climate, especially in regions with colder winters, may require adjustments to clothing, activities, and daily routines. However, it also presents opportunities for new experiences like winter sports and festivals.

Kenyan Community in Canada:

The presence of a significant number of Kenyan immigrants in Canada demonstrates the successful integration and thriving community. Between 2020 and the first quarter of 2023, 39,808 temporary resident permits, 7,774 study permits, and 2,714 permanent residencies were granted to Kenyans. This sizable Kenyan population provides a support network, cultural events, and opportunities for maintaining and celebrating Kenyan traditions.

As a Kenyan immigrant in Canada, you will have access to resources and support networks to help you navigate and integrate into Canadian society. Embracing Canadian culture while preserving your Kenyan heritage will enable you to create a unique identity and contribute to the rich multicultural fabric of Canada.

Public Services

Canada takes has an incredibly efficient public service system, providing its residents with exceptional access to education, healthcare, police services, and more. The nation's commitment to delivering high-quality public services has contributed to its reputation as one of the best countries to live in.

Public Education

  • Universal access: Public education is accessible to all residents, ensuring equal opportunities for personal growth and advancement.
  • High academic standards: Canadian schools consistently rank among the top in international assessments, reflecting the commitment to educational excellence.
  • Multicultural environment: Canada's diverse society is reflected in its classrooms, fostering cultural understanding, empathy, and global perspectives.
  • Supportive learning environments: Well-qualified teachers, modern facilities, and comprehensive resources enable students to thrive academically and socially.

Public Healthcare

  • Universal coverage: Every Canadian is entitled to essential healthcare services regardless of their socioeconomic status, ensuring equal access to medical care.
  • Primary care focus: Emphasis on preventive medicine and primary healthcare helps promote overall well-being and early disease detection.
  • Well-equipped facilities: State-of-the-art hospitals, clinics, and healthcare centers are equipped with advanced medical technologies to deliver efficient and effective care.
  • Health promotion and disease prevention: Public health initiatives and awareness campaigns aim to improve the overall health of the population and reduce the burden of preventable diseases.

Public Safety

  • Professional policing: Police departments across the country are known for their professionalism, adherence to human rights, and community-oriented approach.
  • Effective emergency response: Well-trained emergency services, including paramedics and firefighters, are readily available to handle any crisis or disaster.
  • Crime prevention and community engagement: Collaborative programs and initiatives foster partnerships between law enforcement agencies and communities, promoting crime prevention and public trust.

Migrate to Canada

Now that you have gained valuable insights into the process of moving to Canada from Kenya, you are well-equipped to embark on your journey. Whether you seek new job opportunities, a higher standard of living, or a multicultural experience, Canada offers a welcoming environment for Kenyan immigrants.

Remember, you don't have to navigate this path alone. Various resources and assistance are available to guide you through the immigration process, including government agencies, immigration consultants, and online platforms dedicated to providing reliable information.

From exploring the different immigration programs like Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Program, and Family Sponsorship to understanding Canada's cost of living, culture, and public services, you are better prepared to make informed decisions.

FAQs

Is Canada Welcoming to Immigrants?

Over the past 50 years, Canada has built a reputation for its inclusive approach towards immigrants and its appreciation for cultural diversity. With foreign-born individuals comprising approximately one-fifth of its population, Canada boasts one of the highest proportions of immigrants among industrialized Western nations.

Is Canada a Convenient Place to Settle in After Immigration?

As a developed country with a stable government and economy, Canada offers favorable conditions for immigrants to settle. The country's multicultural environment attracts students from around the globe, as Canada is a preferred destination for studying abroad.

Additionally, Canada provides programs for studying, working, and eventually obtaining permanent residency, allowing individuals who stay for a few years to qualify for long-term settlement.

Is Living in Canada Expensive?

Despite having one of the highest median incomes globally, Canada is widely recognized as one of the most expensive countries to live in. The average salary in Canada is approximately CAD 37,800, placing it among the top 20 countries in the world in terms of income.
