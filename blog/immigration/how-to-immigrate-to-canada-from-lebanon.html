How to Immigrate to Canada from Lebanon

by Robynn. Updated: September 10th, 2020

Immigration to Canada for Lebanese people offer more than 100 ways to make the move. Almost 1,800 Lebanese citizens have already chosen to immigrate to Canada over the past five years. With between 200,000 to 400,000 people originating from Lebanon, it no surprise why.

The main reason why newcomers choose Canada as their destination is that it’s a safe and welcoming environment to raise a family has a phenomenal school and healthcare system free of charge to all permanent residents and citizens, high-paying jobs, as well as a democratic and liberal government that is not only taking care of its people but is extending a helping hand in these times of uncertainty. There just no question why Canada is such a great place to move to with your family.

But with over 100 ways to move to Canada, how can you be sure you’ve chosen the best immigration option for you? Let’s take a look at some of the best Canada immigration options from Lebanon.



How Can I Apply for Canada Immigration from Lebanon


1. Take the Fast-tracked Path: Express Entry System

The Express Entry system is a popular way to immigrate to Canada mainly because it’s both fast and easy to access online. The average express entry visa application processing time is as little as 6 months. You will be ranked according to the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) according to your age, language ability, skills, education, job offer (if you have one) and ability to settle in Canada. 

Thousands of skilled workers are invited to apply (ITA) for permanent residence every 2 weeks through Canada’s Express Entry draws!

There are three main programs that you can apply through are:

  • The Federal Skilled Worker Program;
  • The Federal Skilled Trade Program; and
  • The Canada Experience Class.

There are, however, two of these programs that have been issuing tremendous amounts of ITAs lately: The Canada Experience Class and Express Entry-linked Provincial Nominee Programs.

2. Provincial Nominee Program (PNP): Express Entry-Linked Canada Immigration Programs


Most people are unaware that almost every Canadian province has an Express Entry-linked Provincial Nominee Program: 11 to be exact. What makes these Canada immigration programs so great it that it can boost your chances of receiving an ITA. By expressing interest in a particular province where your skills and experience are in-demand, you are increasing your chances to receive a provincial nomination (PN) to apply for permanent residence. Why is this so important? A PN is worth an incredible 600 permanent residence (PR) or Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) points, which will give you the best chances of success with your Canada visa application!

For example, in the latest Express Entry linked draw the number of PR points needed was 771, which means that with a PN (worth 600 points) you would only need 171 points to qualify for an ITA. Take a look at just how easy it is to improve your CRS score and get those extra points that you need to succeed.

3. Work in Atlantic Canada: Atlantic Immigration Pilot


The Atlantic Immigration Pilot (AIP) is a great way to apply to immigrate to Canada if you are from Lebanon. All you will need is a job offer from a designated employer in one of the following Atlantic Canadian Provinces: New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island or Newfoundland & Labrador. The AIP has also recently updated its visa application process. By updating their online portal, you can now apply for a Canada work permit online, which means that the application processing times have been reduced to as little as 6 months!

4. Post Graduate Work Program: Why You Should Study in Canada


The Post Graduate Work Program is designed to help students who have studied at a Designated Learning Institution stay in Canada and gain enough Canadian work experience so that they can apply for permanent residence under one of the many Canada immigration programs, such as the Canada Experience Class. The most important thing to consider when you choose to study in Canada is to also choose your college or university carefully as well as to consider the length of your program. There are over 300 DLI’s to choose from and we recommend that you choose a program or course that is at least 8 months to 1 year long. If you are eligible to apply for PGWP permit it will be valid for the same length as your study program was and to be able to apply for permanent residence through the CEC, you will need at least 1 year of Canadian work experience to qualify.

5. The Road to Permanent Residency: Temporary Foreign Work


Now, this may seem a little odd but it’s important to not rule out temporary jobs for foreigners when thinking about Canada immigration programs. The reality is that immigration is competitive and not everyone will be able to meet the requirements to get permanent residence right away. The good news is that if your are an experienced worker and are willing to work in Canada on a temporary work permit, you’ll find a number of doors could open for you in as little as 6 months to a year!

By taking a temporary job in Canada you will not only be able to get to Canada quicker but it will also give you the opportunity to test the waters and see which places in Canada are best for you and your family. The International Mobility Program has an average processing time of about 18 weeks and will allow your employer to hire you without a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) which usually requires them to post the job locally for about 3 months first before approaching international applicants. You will also be able to gain the work experience that you need to apply to immigrate to Canada permanently through provincial programs such as the Saskatchewan Experience Streams: for experienced workers, in industries such as healthcare, hospitality and transportation, allowing you to apply for permanent residence after just six months of working in Canada.
