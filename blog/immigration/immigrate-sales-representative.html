How to Immigrate to Canada as a Sales Representative

by 
Themba Phongolo
×

. Updated: July 24th, 2023

Sales representative jobs in Canada for immigrants are becoming more prevalent. This is due to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) making the Canada work permit more accessible through its various immigration visa pathways.

Canada’s welcoming immigration policies and diverse employment opportunities have made sales jobs in Canada more attractive to prospective foreign eligible immigrants. Due to the rise in demand for sales representative jobs in Canada for immigrants, several immigration visa pathways are available for prospective foreign sales representatives who want to know how to become a sales representative in Canada.

If you want to seize opportunities for sales representative jobs in Canada for immigrants, discover how you can do so by using the following immigration visa pathways to immigrate to Canada as a sales representative!

Most Suitable Immigration Visa Pathways for Sales Representatives

Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) is a popular immigration pathway for sales representatives who wish to settle in a specific province in Canada. Each province has its own Canada PNP, and they have different eligibility criteria and application processes.

Under the PNP, sales representatives may be nominated by a particular province or territory based on their skills and work experience. This nomination can then be used to apply for permanent residency in Canada. PNP streams that foreign sales representatives can use to immigrate to Canada are outlined in the table below:

Atlantic Intermediate-Skilled Program

The Atlantic Intermediate-Skilled Program is a specific stream under the Atlantic Immigration Pilot Program (AIPP) designed for sales representatives and other intermediate-skilled workers.

This program allows employers to hire foreign workers to fill labor gaps and support economic growth in the Canadian Atlantic provinces of:

  • Nova Scotia
  • Newfoundland and Labrador
  • Prince Edward Island
  • New Brunswick

Sales representatives offered a job by an employer in one of the Atlantic provinces can apply for permanent residency through this program.

Temporary Foreign Worker Permit (TFWP)

The Temporary Foreign Worker Permit (TFWP) is another option for temporary sales representatives who wish to work in Canada. Under this program, employers in Canada who possess a Labor Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) can hire sales representatives from other countries if they cannot find a Canadian citizen or permanent resident to fill the position.

The various TFWP streams that can be used to immigrate to Canada include:

  • High Wage Worker Stream
  • Low Wage Worker Stream
  • Agriculture Worker Stream
  • Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program
  • Global Talent Stream
  • Home Care Provider Stream
  • Foreign Academics

International Mobility Program

Foreign sales representatives whose countries have a trade agreement with the Canadian government can use the international mobility program (IMP) to get hired more easily by Canadian employers since they are exempt from LMIA hiring requirements. Sales representatives can apply for a work permit through the TFWP and gain valuable work experience in Canada.

The international mobility program has a group of immigration streams that eligible immigrants can use to move work in Canada temporarily, such as:

  • International Free Trade Agreements
  • Postgraduate Work Permit (PGWP)
  • Bridging Open Work Permit (BOWP)

Find out more about how to apply for a Canadian Work Permit.

IEC Working Holiday Program

The International Experience Canada (IEC) Working Holiday Program allows young individuals from participating countries to work and travel in Canada for a certain period.

Sales representatives eligible for the IEC Working Holiday program can obtain an open work permit to explore employment opportunities in Canada. This program is a great way to gain international work experience and potentially secure a job offer in Canada, which may open doors to permanent residency.

Learn more about Canada’s top 7 working holiday destinations.

Which Provinces in Canada Are Sales Representatives Most In-demand?

While sales representatives are needed across Canada, there are a few provinces where the demand for sales representatives is particularly high. If you are considering immigrating to Canada as a sales representative, these provinces should be on your radar:

Manitoba

Manitoba is known for its strong job market and growing economy. The province’s thriving sales industry makes it an ideal destination for sales representatives. With its diverse range of industries, including manufacturing, technology, and retail, sales representatives in Manitoba can find ample employment opportunities.

According to Canada’s Job Bank, sales representatives in Manitoba can earn an average annual salary of 36 385,68 CAD.

Nova Scotia

Nova Scotia is another province in Canada where sales representatives are in high demand. The province has a vibrant business community and a growing economy, which creates a need for skilled sales professionals. Nova Scotia sales industry spans various sectors, including tourism, manufacturing, and healthcare, providing sales representatives with abundant employment options.

Nova Scotian sales representatives earn an average annual salary of 37 282,99 CAD, according to Canada’s Job Bank.

Prince Edward Island

Prince Edward Island (PEI) may be one of Canada's smallest provinces, but it offers great job opportunities for sales representatives. The province has a strong focus on entrepreneurship and small business development creating a demand for sales representatives to help drive sales and growth. PEI's friendly community and high quality of life make it attractive for sales professionals.

The annual average salary for sales representatives in Prince Edward Island is 37 282,99 CAD according to Canada’s Job Bank.

Yukon Territory

The Yukon Territory is a great option for sales representatives seeking a unique and adventurous experience. Yukon offers a tight-knit community and a supportive business environment. Sales representatives in the Yukon can work in various sectors, including tourism, mining, and construction.

Sales representatives in Yukon can earn an annual average salary of 43,748.55 CAD, according to Canada’s Job Bank.

Learn more about the top 3 provinces to find mining jobs in Canada.

What is the Average Annual Salary for Sales Representatives in Canada?

The average annual salary for sales representatives in Canada can vary depending on experience, industry, and location. However, generally speaking, on average, sales representatives in Canada earn around 39,139.47 CAD per year, according to Canada’s Job Bank.

Why Move to Canada as a Sales Representative?

After deciding to move to Canada as a sales representative, you have the following perks and benefits to look forward to:

High Salary

One of the key reasons to move to Canada as a sales representative is the high salary potential. Sales representatives in Canada can earn competitive salaries, especially if they perform well and have a track record of success.

The high salary potential allows sales representatives to enjoy a comfortable lifestyle, raise their families and have financial stability. The provincial and national annual salary for sales representatives in Canada statistical figures mentioned above reflect the competitiveness of the living wage sales representatives receive in Canada.

Learn how to get a better wage through the 5 top tips to negotiate your salary in Canada.

High Demand for Sales Representatives

Canada has a strong and growing economy, which creates a demand for skilled sales professionals. Sales representatives are needed in various industries nationwide, including retail, manufacturing, technology, and healthcare. The high demand for sales representatives means ample job opportunities and room for career growth in Canada.

Varied Employment Opportunities

Sales representatives in Canada have the advantage of working in diverse industries. Whether you have technology, healthcare, or retail expertise, employment opportunities are available in your field.

This diversity allows sales representatives to explore different industries and gain valuable experience in various sectors. Here are the top and different employment opportunities along with their sales representative National Occupational Classification (NOC) codes:

  • Retail Sales Supervisors (NOC 62010)
  • Technical Sales Specialists (NOC 62100)
  • Retail & Wholesale Buyers (NOC 62101)
  • Insurance Agents & Brokers (NOC 63100)
  • Real Estate Agents & Salespersons (NOC 63101)
  • Financial Sales Representatives (NOC 63102)

Permanent Residency Pathway

Moving to Canada as a sales representative can also open doors to permanent residency. Many immigration pathways, such as the Provincial Nominee Program, can lead to permanent residency status in Canada.

This means that sales representatives can enjoy the benefits of working in Canada and have the opportunity to settle permanently and enjoy all the rights and privileges of being a Canadian citizen.

Find out more about the top 3 ways to get Canadian permanent residency.

Immigrating to Canada as a sales representative offers numerous benefits, including high salary potential, diverse employment opportunities, and a pathway to permanent residency.

With the various immigration visa pathways available, sales representatives can explore different options based on their goals and preferences. If you’re a foreign sales representative looking to make a positive career change, consider Canada your next destination!

FAQs

What is the Quickest Way to Move to Canada as a Sales Representative?

The quickest way to move to Canada as a sales representative is through the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP). Each province has its own PNP, and if you meet the eligibility criteria and are nominated by a province, you can apply for permanent residency. The PNP is designed to expedite the immigration process for skilled workers, including sales representatives.

Can You Work as a Sales Representative While a Student in Canada?

Yes, international students in Canada who possess a valid Canadian study permit are allowed to work part-time while studying. As a sales representative, you can seek employment in the sales industry, gaining valuable work experience while pursuing your studies. However, it's important to ensure you comply with the Canadian government's regulations and restrictions regarding international students' work permits.

Learn more about how to work in Canada as a student.
