5 Reasons to Immigrate to Canada from Nigeria

by 
Themba Phongolo

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: May 16th, 2024
5 min read

Considering a fresh start abroad from Nigeria? If you are, consider Canada, a country full of promise and opportunity, offering a home for Nigerians looking for a brighter future. Canada provides a standard of stability and prosperity to immigrants worldwide, from its renowned healthcare system to its inclusive society.

Whether it's the many economic prospects, world-class education, or simply the warmth of a welcoming community, our guide provides everything you'd want to know to immigrate to Canada from Nigeria.

Learn more about the top 5 reasons to immigrate to Canada from Nigeria, and find out what makes the country an ideal destination for Nigerians looking to settle abroad.

Strong Economy and Job Opportunities in Canada

Canada has a strong and diversified economy, ranking in the top 10 of the world's largest economies according to the World Population Review and various other rankings. This presents a healthy job market for Nigerian newcomers to Canada. Here's a breakdown of the economic advantages.

Demand for Skilled Workers

Several sectors in Canada are experiencing skilled labor shortages, creating opportunities for qualified Nigerians. These sectors include:

  • Engineering,
  • Technology,
  • Healthcare, and
  • Skilled trades.

Nigerians with qualifications and experience in these areas are well-positioned to find employment in Canada.

Learn more via the top in-demand jobs in Canada.

Competitive Salaries and Job Security

Salaries in Canada are generally higher compared to Nigeria, particularly in high-demand fields. This is evident below in the average monthly net salary after tax figures from Numbeo.

Countries Average Monthly Net Salary After Tax (CAD)
Canada 3,933.92
Nigeria 226.65

Strong Canadian labor laws ensure minimum wage standards and fair treatment for employees. Administered by Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC), the country's Employment Insurance (EI) provides financial support for eligible workers who lose their jobs, offering a safety net and contributing to overall job security.

The country's commitment to innovation and entrepreneurship further enhances its appeal, offering professional growth and success.

High Quality of Life in Canada

Canada consistently ranks high in global quality-of-life indexes, placing third according to the World Population Reviews US News and World Report. This creates a standard of living that many Nigerians find attractive. Here are some key aspects.

Universal Healthcare

Canadians enjoy access to high-quality public healthcare through a single-payer system. This eliminates dependence on private health insurance or concerns about affording medical care, a significant benefit that eliminates the challenge of accessing affordable healthcare.

Learn more about healthcare in Canada.

Education System

Canada boasts a world-class education system, with well-funded public schools and renowned universities. This offers excellent educational opportunities for Nigerians looking to further their studies or provide a high-quality education for their children.

Learn what you need about Canada's education system.

Safe and Clean Environment

Canada enjoys a reputation for having clean air, water, and sanitation systems. Together with low crime rates, this contributes to a healthy and secure living environment, a significant advantage over the environmental and security challenges sometimes faced in Nigeria. Canadian National Parks offer some of the best opportunities to experience Canada's safe and clean environments through visits to:

  • Banff National Park,
  • Nahanni National Park,
  • Fundy National Park,
  • Jasper National Park, and
  • Yoho National Park.

Learn about employment opportunities in a Canada clean tech hub.

Multicultural and Welcoming Society in Canada

Canada is filled with many vibrant cultures and ethnicities, creating a welcoming environment for newcomers to Canada. Here's how this inclusivity benefits Nigerians.

Sense of Belonging

Multiculturalism is a core value in Canada, and immigrants are encouraged to retain their cultural heritage while integrating into Canadian society. This welcoming atmosphere can ease the transition process for Nigerians, helping them feel accepted and valued.

Rich Cultural Diversity

Canada's diverse population allows Nigerians to experience many cultures, traditions, and cuisines. This exposure can broaden your horizons and create a more enriching living experience. The country's policies and programs promote inclusivity, creating a sense of belonging and community among newcomers to Canada.

Learn to celebrate Canada's diversity through cultural festivals and attractions.

Path to Permanent Residency and Citizenship

Canada's immigration system, including the Express Entry system, allows skilled workers to obtain permanent residency and citizenship based on factors like education and language skills, benefiting Nigerians with strong qualifications. Through programs like the Provincial Nominee Program, Start-up Visa, and Family Sponsorship, eligible Nigerians can immigrate to Canada based on various criteria, ultimately granting them equal rights and benefits as Canadian citizens upon obtaining permanent residency.

Canadian citizenship grants numerous benefits, including the right to vote, access to social programs, and the ability to sponsor family members for immigration. This path to long-term security and building a future in Canada can be highly attractive for Nigerians.

Learn more about the requirements for becoming a Canadian citizen.

Safety and Security in Canada

Canada has a reputation for being a safe and secure country with low crime rates. Here's how this strong sense of security can be a major advantage for Nigerians:

Low Crime Rates

Canada consistently ranks high in global safety rankings, with a significantly low crime rate of 5,668 per 100,000 people, according to Statistics Canada. This sense of security in Canada can provide peace of mind for Nigerians concerned about personal safety and the safety of their families.

Learn more about safety levels in Canada.

Stable Political System

Canada enjoys a stable and democratic political system, offering predictability and a sense of the rule of law. This can be a major factor for Nigerians seeking a secure environment to raise their families. For Nigerians who value security or stability in their country of permanent residence or citizenship, Canada offers a reassuring sense of security and protection under the law.

How Can I Immigrate to Canada From Nigeria

To immigrate to Canada from Nigeria, individuals have several pathways available, each with eligibility criteria and application procedures. One common route is through the Express Entry system, which assesses candidates based on a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) using factors such as:

  • Age,
  • Education,
  • Work experience,
  • Language proficiency, and
  • Adaptability to Canada.

Learn which Express Entry program is best for you.

Furthermore, the Provincial Nominee Program allows provinces and territories to nominate individuals with the skills and experience needed to contribute to their local economies. Family sponsorship is another option for those with relatives who are Canadian citizens or permanent residents.

Moreover, entrepreneurs with innovative business ideas may qualify for the Start-up Visa Program, provided they get support from designated Canadian investors. Regardless of the chosen pathway, applicants must go through medical examinations and security screenings and provide supporting documentation to demonstrate their eligibility for immigration to Canada.

Learn more about how to immigrate to Canada from Nigeria.

FAQs

Are There any Support Services Available For Nigerian Immigrants in Canada?

Yes, there are numerous support services available for Nigerian immigrants in Canada, including settlement agencies, community organizations, language classes, employment assistance programs, and cultural integration initiatives.

Learn more about immigration associations in Canada.

Will my Nigerian Qualifications be Recognized in Canada?

The recognition of Nigerian qualifications in Canada varies depending on the profession or field of study. However, Canada has processes for assessing and recognizing foreign credentials, which may require credential evaluation or additional training to meet Canadian standards.

Learn how to get your foreign qualifications recognized in Canada via the Education Credential Association (ECA).

How Can I Find Employment Opportunities as a Nigerian Immigrant in Canada?

Nigerian immigrants in Canada can find employment opportunities through various channels, including online job boards, professional networking events, employment agencies, government job portals, and professional associations. Some immigration programs may require a job offer from a Canadian employer before applying.

Are There Any Age Restrictions For Immigrants From Nigeria to Canada?

While there are no specific age restrictions for most immigration programs, certain programs like the Express Entry system may favor younger applicants due to the points system that considers age as a factor. However, individuals of all ages can explore various immigration pathways to Canada based on their qualifications and circumstances.
