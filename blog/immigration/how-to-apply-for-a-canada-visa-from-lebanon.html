How to Apply for a Canada Visa from Lebanon

by Rueben. Updated: September 16th, 2020

This year has been very difficult for a lot of people. Many countries have struggled with the economic effects of the global pandemic. Canada has weathered the storm better than most, and that is why many people are looking to immigrate to Canada. 

It is becoming increasingly well known that the Canadian government wants skilled foreign workers to come live and work in Canada. The population is too small for such a big country, by inviting hard-working people from all over the world they are offering not only a chance for a better future for you and your family but also a better future for Canada.

The rules are strict and immigrating legitimately can be challenging, so you must know everything about how to apply for a Canada visa from Lebanon before you start the immigration process. 

If you are ready to start your immigration journey today, follow the link below.



How to Move to Canada from Lebanon

You can think of applying for your Canada visa as a three-step process. The first step is to determine which program you are suitable for, there are over 100! The second step is to apply to the program that is best suited to you and the third is to pack your bags and move to Canada once you have received your visa! But as we know its never as simple as three short sentences, so lets take a look at each of those stages in some more detail.

Which Visa Program Should You Apply For?

Depending on your age, qualifications, skills, experience, family size and intentions when you arrive in Canada, you will need to choose a Canada visa program that will suit all your needs as well as your unique profile. Some of the most popular immigration programs are:

  • Express Entry System - one of the most popular choices, this system is for qualified individuals with at least one year of experience working in their profession. There are three programs in the system for different types of professions, namely:

    • Skilled Worker Program - for people with experience and qualifications in managerial and professional roles; doctors; for example accountants, IT professionals, managers, architects and designers.
    • Skilled Trades Program - if you are a qualified journeyman, good news; trades workers are some of the most in-demand professions in Canada. You will require authentication from a recognized body (such as a red seal) and at least one year of experience working as a tradesman. Popular skills urgently needed in Canada include carpenters, pipefitters, welders, mechanics, plumbers and electricians.
    • Canada Experience Class - if you answered yes to either of the above two programs and already have or intend to obtain at least one year of experience working in Canada before applying for permanent residency, then this is your choice. The Canada Experience Class gets preference over the other two because simply put, the applicants have already shown that they are capable of living and working in Canada.

  • Provincial Nomination Program - this is one of the most multi-layered immigration programs. Within this program, there are at least forty to fifty different visa options. The reason there are so many? Each Canadian province has its own Provincial Nomination Program, and each of those has its own unique streams or visas for Canadian immigration. 

For example, the Manitoba Provincial Nomination Program has four options; Skilled Workers in Manitoba, Skilled Workers Overseas, the Business Investor Stream and the International Education Stream. So if you know where you want to live in Canada, you can apply directly to the provincial government. If you are successful in your application you will receive a provincial nomination, which so long as you meet the eligibility criteria for the program you are applying to, guarantees your visa for permanent residency in Canada.



  • Pilot Programs - the Immigration and Refugee Council of Canada is continuously experimenting with different visa types to meet the needs of the Canadian labour market. For example, the four provinces of Atlantic Canada; New Brunswick, Newfoundland & Labrador, Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island are facing a shortage of both semi-skilled and skilled workers. 

As a matter of fact, they are facing a shortage of people, period. For this reason, the Atlantic Immigration Pilot was created to help address these shortages by allowing applicants with a valid job offer in Atlantic Canada to apply for permanent residency through this special program.

 

  • Another example is the Rural & Northern Immigration Pilot, a program that was created to allow smaller cities and communities who arent as much of a magnet as say Toronto or Vancouver for immigrants to attract hard and talented foreign workers. There are currently eleven towns and small cities participating in this Canadian visa program that allows applicants who can secure a job offer in the community to apply for permanent residency, and the community will support the application in return. Positions can vary from barbers to food counter attendants to nurses. 

Much like the provinces of Atlantic Canada, these communities are looking to grow by increasing their populations with young and motivated foreigners. There are a couple more pilot programs we havent been able to touch on, but if you want to learn more follow the link at the bottom of the article.


Applying for Your Canadian Visa

Once you know which Canadian visa program will give you the best chance of being able to live in Canada, you will need to complete and submit your application with any and all necessary supporting documentation. Some of the requirements are applicable for every visa program, such as a minimum score for a language test. Lets take a closer look at regular eligibility requirements.


Canada Visa Eligibility Requirements
RequirementDescription
Admissible to Canadayou cannot have a criminal record
Experience/Qualificationif you are applying to become a permanent resident, you will typically have to show you are able to perform the duties as laid out in your job offer
Educationevery program has a minimum requirement of at least a Canadian high school diploma or the equivalent.
Proof of fundsif you arent already working in Canada, you must show you can pay for your cost to move and settle in Canada
Language Requirementsdepending on your visa type, you may need to get a minimum score on one of the Canadian Language Benchmark tests
Medical Examinationyou may be required to have a doctors examination to show you dont have any terminal/life-threatening illnesses.

Move to Canada

It depends on one which immigration program you apply through, but for nearly every visa type (except tourist, Express Entry and IEC) you will require a valid job offer. You will use your job offer to get a Canada work permit and apply for permanent residence. So, it is a lot easier to move to Canada if you can secure a job offer before you go. Canada can be expensive without employment. When you arrive you don't want to struggle, being forced to spend all your savings and relying on others. Have a plan, do everything the right way, and you will feel much more safe and secure in your future knowing no short-cuts may come back to bite you.
