What Are the Requirements For an IELTS Score For Canada PR?

Editorial Guidelines
David AllonLearn More

Are you considering working, studying, or permanently moving to Canada? If yes, you must take an International English Language Testing System (IELTS) test. Favorable results from the IELTS test demonstrate that you can communicate well in English as a foreigner. This is an essential part of the immigration assessment process because it will help you find a job in Canada, become a valued member of Canadian society, and help you easily navigate your studies and social interactions. Scoring well also increases your chances of getting a Canadian visa. In this article, we go through everything you need to know about achieving a good IELTS score for Canada Permanent residency (PR).

What is IELTS?

If you plan on immigrating to Canada, you must get a Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) to prove that you are proficient in English or French. Canada uses IELTS as an English examination tool for immigrants applying for immigration, work visas, or study permits. Whether you apply via the Express Entry System, the Provincial Nominee Program, or any other immigration pathways, an IELTS General Training or IELTS Academic test will support your application for Canadian immigration. It is accepted by Immigration, Refugee, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) and tertiary institutions around the country. Your English proficiency will be tested on a scale of 0 to 9 in four areas: speaking, listening, reading, and writing.

What Are the Minimum IELTS Requirements For Canada Permanent Residency?

To be eligible for various immigration programs and visas, you must prove your English language (or French) abilities. The required level of proficiency for non-native English speakers depends on the organization they are employed by or the institution they study at. Each of these should establish their own minimum IELTS score, according to their needs. There are, however, specific requirements for different immigration programs set by IRCC. The minimum IELTS score for Canada PR is as follows:

Express Entry Program CLB Level Required
Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP) CLB 7 for a first official language and CLB 5 for second official language
Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP) CLB 5 for speaking and listening skills and CLB 4 for reading and writing skills
Canadian Experience Class Program (CEC) CLB 7 for TEER 0 or 1, CLB 5 for TEER 2 or 3

Express Entry Language Update

According to the IRCC, you can earn as much as 50 extra points for strong French language skills (even if French is not your first language). So, if you score Niveaux de Compétence Linguistique Canadien (NCLC) 7 or higher on all four French sections, you can obtain 25 extra points if you scored CLB 4 or lower in English and 50 extra points if you scored CLB 5 or more on all four English areas.

How is Your IELTS Test Assessed?

There are four different sections in an IELTS assessment: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. You will receive points for each section and then an overall band score. Each section is important, as it will give you an idea of how you will deal with real-life situations in Canada.

There are two kinds of IELTS tests: the General Training Test and the Academic Test:

  • The IELTS General Training test examines English proficiency through practical exercises that portray everyday situations. This is the test you are required to take to work in or immigrate to Canada.
  • The IELTS Academic test checks the English proficiency required for formal learning environments. This test can be taken if you solely wish to study in Canada.

Speaking

Speaking is as big a part of life as breathing, so naturally, it will be the skill you utilize the most. Because Canadians are a pretty friendly bunch, you will spend a lot of time chatting while getting to know your community and settling in. How you communicate will play a role in how quickly you become comfortable within your surroundings while exposed to a new culture and lifestyle.

The IELTS Speaking test will prepare you to converse with people around you at work, at a post-secondary institution, and in your community. It will also guide you in adopting a conversation style that sounds natural, clear, and easy to understand, even if you have a heavy accent.

Listening

Being able to grasp what someone says to us during a conversation fully is a major part of adapting to a new country. You cannot plan where to find yourself each day because life is unpredictable. The moment you set foot in Canada, you will deal with an immigration officer at the airport or listen out for details on a connecting flight to another province; you'll eventually start your new job or university, need to ask for directions, or order a coffee.

Understanding a new accent or keeping up with the speed at which someone speaks a different language can be stressful. In the listening section of the IELTS General Training test or IELTS Academic test, you will have to demonstrate that you can understand what is being said during conversations or monologues, and you also have to respond. Tasks are delivered in different accents and contexts to prepare you for different settings, according to real social interaction possibilities.

Reading

Reading is another integral part of our daily lives, whether checking ingredients on food labels, a work document, or directions on a map. When you can read, you can fully understand things even if they are unclear when spoken. In other words, reading allows you to process things without speaking.

The IELTS General Training Reading test will take you through the different reading styles, from scanning through things to probing for important information to understanding the writer's context. You will examine different types of reading material, from newspapers to magazines, company literature, and advertisements to realistically prepare for everyday reading.

Writing

In Canada, moments will call for written communication. You may have to leave a note for your neighbor, send a thank you card or provide feedback on a product or service. When it comes to your career, you'll probably have to put together supporting documents for a job application, or if you're a student, you'll have tons of papers to submit in English. Being able to write in a clear, concise manner is, therefore, of great importance.

The IELTS General Training test will prepare you for various situations that require writing, including filling out paperwork or adding your voice to issues that affect the general public.

What Does Band in IELTS Mean?

The IELTS test results are meant to be fairly easy to interpret. Results are released as band scores on a scale from 1, the lowest, to 9, the highest. It should be noted that there is no set' pass mark' for IELTS. The IELTS score required is set by companies, universities, and immigration authorities.

You need to determine the score you will need before starting your university course or job or depending on your immigration channel. You'll need to think about the level of English you'll need for your specific undertaking, so if you are dealing with highly academic literature or are doing very complicated training in your job, you will probably need a higher score.

IELTS is among the most important boxes to tick off in your Canadian Immigration application process. A clear understanding of what your IELTS score for Canada PR should look like is essential, as it will affect your immigration application status. You will have to ensure that this aligns with your chosen pathway, career, and university or college requirements.

Remember, there is no need to be intimidated by the IELTS test. By taking it, you'll be able to gauge how you will cope with engaging with your new community in Canada and finding your way around your new city and province.

FAQs

Where do I Take an IELTS Test?

There are over 1600 test sites around the world. Find the one closest to you and book your test today. You can also do an online preparation course to ensure you are well prepared for your test.

How do I Register For an IELTS test?

Registering for an IELTS test is simple. You must find an IELTS location closest to you, register for the test, and pay online or at your test center. Make sure you submit a copy of your identity document as well.

Will I be Able to Retake an IELTS Test?

If you didn't achieve the IELTS score you wanted, you can take the test again once you are ready. It is important to remember that your score may only improve if you work on the areas that need improvement.

Tip: Do some sample tests before you take the actual one. Also read up on how best to prepare so you do not miss any steps.
