Best Places to Live in Alberta

by 
Kelia Losa
×

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: April 18th, 2023

Located in western Canada, Alberta is a province known for its stunning natural beauty, thriving cities, and friendly communities. With a population of over 4 million people, Alberta is home to a diverse range of cultures and lifestyles, making it an exciting and welcoming place to live. Its capital, Edmonton, and largest city, Calgary, offer a range of opportunities for employment, education, and entertainment. But with so many wonderful places to call home in Alberta, where should you consider living? In this article, we'll explore the best places to live in Alberta, taking into account factors such as affordability, safety, and quality of life.

5 Best Places to Live in Alberta

Edmonton

Edmonton is the capital city of Alberta, and has a population of over 1.5 million people, making it the second-largest city in the province. In terms of affordability, Edmonton is considered to be more affordable than other major Canadian cities like Vancouver and Toronto, with a lower cost of living and lower housing prices. In terms of safety, Edmonton has a relatively low crime rate compared to other major Canadian cities, although like any urban center, it is not completely crime-free.

Edmonton is known for its thriving arts and culture scene, with numerous festivals and events throughout the year, as well as its proximity to nature, with easy access to parks, lakes, and wilderness areas. Edmonton offers a high quality of life with a range of amenities and opportunities for new residents.

Edmonton is home to the largest shopping mall in North America, the West Edmonton Mall, which covers an area of 5.3 million square feet and features over 800 stores and services.

Calgary

Calgary is the largest city in Alberta,with over 1.6 million people. While it is more expensive than Edmonton, it is still more affordable than other major Canadian cities like Vancouver and Toronto. In terms of safety, Calgary has a relatively low crime rate and is considered to be one of the safest cities in Canada.

Calgary is known for its vibrant and diverse economy, with a strong focus on industries such as oil and gas, technology, and agriculture. The city also offers a range of cultural and recreational opportunities, including museums, art galleries, parks, and sporting events. Calgary is also located near the Canadian Rockies, offering easy access to stunning natural scenery and outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and camping.

Calgary is home to the world-famous Calgary Stampede, a 10-day annual rodeo and festival that attracts over one million visitors each year and features a range of events such as bull riding, chuckwagon racing, and live music performances.

Banff

Banff is a charming town located within Banff National Park in Alberta. With a population of approximately 10,000 people, Banff offers a unique quality of life for those who love the outdoors. The town is surrounded by stunning natural scenery and offers a range of outdoor activities for residents and tourists alike. While it is considered to be more expensive than other towns in the region, it offers a sense of community and small-town charm that is hard to find elsewhere. 

Banff is a popular destination for tourists from around the world, and it is home to a range of boutiques, restaurants, and souvenir shops. However, it also offers a family-friendly atmosphere and is welcoming to people of all ages and backgrounds. In terms of safety, Banff has a relatively low crime rate, although it is important to be aware of wildlife hazards in the surrounding area.

Banff is Canada's highest town, with an elevation of 4,537 feet (1,383 meters) above sea level.

Camrose

Camrose is a city situated in central Alberta, Canada, surrounded by Camrose County. With a population of approximately 19,000 people, Camrose is an affordable option for those seeking a quieter and more laid-back lifestyle, with a lower cost of living compared to larger cities like Edmonton or Calgary. The city is also known for its relatively low crime rate, making it a safe place to live and raise a family. Camrose offers a range of amenities and activities for its residents, including parks, trails, and recreational facilities. 

The city has a strong sense of community and hosts numerous events and festivals throughout the year, bringing residents together. Camrose is located about 90 km (56 mi) from Edmonton and is situated in a transitory region of Alberta between prairie and boreal forest known as aspen parkland. It serves as a major economic center for many small farming communities in the surrounding area. The Stoney Creek runs through the city, adding to the beauty of the natural landscape. Camrose has a humid continental climate with warm summers and long, cold winters. Camrose offers a high quality of life with a small-town feel and a range of opportunities for those seeking a quieter and more affordable lifestyle.

Camrose is home to the University of Alberta's Augustana Campus, a small liberal arts and sciences university that offers undergraduate degrees in a range of disciplines, including fine arts, social sciences, and natural sciences.

Red Deer

Red Deer is a mid-sized city located in central Alberta, Canada, serving as a hub for various industries including healthcare, retail trade, construction, oil and gas, hospitality, manufacturing, and education. Surrounded by Red Deer County and Lacombe County, the city lies within the rolling hills of the aspen parkland region, next to the Red Deer River. Red Deer has a rich history, having been inhabited by First Nations such as the Blackfoot, Plains Cree, and Stoney, prior to the arrival of European fur traders. 

The city has since developed into a diverse community, with a population of 100,844 people as of 2021, according to the Census of Population conducted by Statistics Canada. Red Deer's population is diverse, with 15.2% identifying as visible minority, and 7.1% reporting North American Aboriginal Origins. The city has a moderate crime rate, but has taken steps to improve safety and security in recent years. For residents, Red Deer offers a range of amenities and activities including parks, trails, recreational facilities, and a vibrant arts and culture scene with numerous events and festivals throughout the year showcasing local talent.

Red Deer is known for being a major transportation hub in Alberta, as it is located at the intersection of the QEII Highway and the Calgary-Edmonton Corridor, which connects the city to major centers throughout the province and beyond.

Alberta for Foreigners: Why It’s a Great Place to Call Home

Alberta is a province in Western Canada that is quickly becoming a popular destination for immigrants from around the world. In fact, according to recent statistics, Alberta is one of the top provinces in Canada for immigration, with over 60,000 newcomers arriving in the province each year. So, what makes it so popular? To find out, let’s dig into why Alberta is a great option for foreigners to immigrate to, the leading factors behind immigration to Alberta, and the benefits of living in Alberta as a newcomer, including settlement programs.

Why Alberta is a Great Option for Foreigners

There are many reasons why Alberta is an attractive destination for foreigners looking to immigrate to Canada. First and foremost, the province has a strong economy with a low unemployment rate, making it an ideal place to find work and build a career. Additionally, Alberta is home to a diverse population, with people from all over the world calling the province home. This creates a welcoming and inclusive community for newcomers, where they can connect with others who share their culture and experiences. Find out more about Canada’s amazing multiculturalism.

Another factor that makes Alberta an attractive destination for immigrants is its natural beauty. The province is home to stunning mountain ranges, vast prairies, and picturesque lakes and rivers, offering a range of outdoor activities for those who love nature and adventure. Additionally, the province's cities, such as Edmonton and Calgary, offer a vibrant and diverse cultural scene, with museums, galleries, and festivals celebrating the arts and culture. Be ready to a large variety of outdoor activities, too. 

Leading Factors Behind Immigration to Alberta

There are several factors that contribute to the high levels of immigration to Alberta. One of the biggest drivers of immigration is the province's strong economy, which offers a range of job opportunities in various industries, including oil and gas, agriculture, and technology. Canada has plenty of job opportunities and an in-demand occupations list that makes moving more accessible. 

Alberta's welcoming and inclusive community, diverse population, and high quality of life make it an attractive destination for those seeking a new start in Canada.

Why Live in Alberta as a Newcomer

There are many benefits to living in Alberta as a newcomer. The province offers a range of settlement programs and services designed to help immigrants settle into their new community and succeed in Canada. For example, the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP) offers a range of immigration pathways for skilled workers and entrepreneurs who want to live and work in the province. Additionally, the province offers language training programs, job search support, and cultural orientation programs to help newcomers integrate into their new community.

In terms of quality of life, Alberta offers a range of amenities and activities for residents, including parks, trails, and recreational facilities. The province's cities offer a vibrant arts and culture scene, with museums, galleries, and festivals that celebrate the diversity of the community. Additionally, the province is known for its high standard of living, with affordable housing, excellent healthcare, and a range of public services that support the well-being of residents.

Choose Alberta

If you want to live in Alberta, there are several pathways available for skilled workers and entrepreneurs. One of the most popular options is the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP), which allows the province to nominate candidates for permanent residence based on their skills and experience.

To apply for the AINP, you will need to meet certain eligibility requirements, such as having a job offer from an Alberta employer or having experience in an occupation that is in demand in the province. You will also need to meet the requirements of one of the AINP's immigration streams, which include the Alberta Opportunity Stream, the Alberta Express Entry Stream, and the Self-Employed Farmer Stream.

Navigating the immigration process can be complex and time-consuming, which is why many people choose to work with a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (RCIC). An RCIC is a licensed professional authorized by the Canadian government to provide immigration advice and assistance.

Working with an RCIC can help you navigate the immigration process more efficiently and effectively. They can help you determine which immigration stream is best for you, prepare and submit your application, and communicate with government officials on your behalf. They can also provide guidance and support throughout the process, answering any questions and ensuring that you are fully informed about your options.

If you're considering immigrating to Alberta, be sure to explore all of your options and seek the support of a qualified immigration professional.

FAQs

What is the Cheapest Place to Live in Alberta?

The cost of living in Alberta can vary depending on the city or town you choose to live in. Generally speaking, smaller towns and rural areas tend to be less expensive than major cities like Calgary and Edmonton. Some of the more affordable places to live in Alberta include Camrose, Lethbridge, Medicine Hat, and Red Deer.

Is Alberta a Good Place to Live?

Yes, Alberta is a great place to live. It has a strong economy, low unemployment rates, and a high standard of living. The province is known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, and friendly communities. Additionally, Alberta offers its residents excellent healthcare, education, and other public services.

Where is the Best Place to raise a Family in Alberta?

Alberta offers many great options for families, with a range of cities and towns that provide a safe and supportive environment for children to grow and thrive. Some of Alberta's best places to raise a family include St. Albert, Strathcona County, Okotoks, and Cochrane. These communities offer excellent schools, parks, recreational facilities, and access to healthcare and other important services.
