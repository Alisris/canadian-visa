How to Immigrate to Canada as an Athlete

by 
Themba Phongolo
×

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: January 12th, 2024

Canada, known for its picturesque landscapes and diverse communities, is a coveted destination for athletes seeking new horizons. Whether you're a world-renowned athlete or an aspiring prodigy, the process unfolds in four distinct steps, each designed to ensure a seamless transition to the vibrant and sports-loving Canadian environment.

In Canada, Ice Hockey reigns supreme, transcending sport to become a cultural phenomenon, with intense rivalries and iconic teams capturing the nation's fervor. Beyond the rinks, Canada's sporting tapestry extends to winter pursuits like skiing and curling, showcasing a deep-seated love for both competition and the pristine, snow-covered landscapes that define this sporting nation.

Explore the dynamic opportunities and essential steps for athletes aspiring to immigrate to Canada, unlocking a pathway to pursue their sports career in this welcoming and diverse nation!

How to Immigrate to Canada as an Athlete

Navigating the immigration process may be a challenging task. Still, with the help of our guide, you will learn the four pivotal steps that will pave the way for you to make Canada your home turf for athletic success.

Step 1: Gain The Relevant Athletic Experience

The first stride in your journey involves showcasing your prowess in the athletic arena. Whether you're a seasoned professional or an emerging talent, Canada values the experience you bring to the field. Recognition from international sports organizations and achievements in your sport will play a key role in establishing your eligibility.

The athletic experience needed to qualify to immigrate to Canada as an athlete generally includes participating in cultural activities or athletics at a world-class level or being a self-employed person in cultural activities or athletics in your own country for at least two years. This experience must be gained within the last 5 years before submitting your application to the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).

For athletes, this relevant experience can be broken down as:

  • Being self-employed in athletics for 2 one-year periods,
  • Participating at a world-class level in athletics for 2 one-year periods, or
  • A combination of 2 one-year periods being self-employed in athletics or participating at a world-class level in athletics.

Step 2: Meet the Business Immigration Points System Requirements

Canada operates on a points-based system for business immigration, including athletes. As you embark on your immigration journey, familiarize yourself with the points system, which takes into account factors such as:

  • Age,
  • Education,
  • Experience,
  • Language proficiency in English or French, and
  • Adaptability.

The more points you accumulate, the smoother your transition to becoming a Canadian athlete will be. To qualify to immigrate to Canada as an athlete, you must meet the maximum points requirements outlined below:

Athletic Immigration Selection Criteria Maximum Points Required
Education 25
Experience 35
Age 10
Ability in English and/or French 24
Adaptability 6
Total Accumulated Points 100

Learn more about the eligibility criteria for business immigration to Canada.

Step 3: Demonstrate Your Financial Self-Sufficiency

Ensuring financial self-sufficiency is a crucial aspect of the immigration process. Canada seeks athletes who can contribute to the economy and support themselves. Providing evidence of your financial stability, whether through savings, sponsorship, or a combination of both, is vital to demonstrate your ability to thrive in your new Canadian home.

Find out more about settlement and proof of funds for Canadian immigration.

Step 4: Gather And Submit the Necessary Application to the Self-Employed Program

Under the Self-Employed Program, athletes find a fitting avenue to establish themselves in Canada. This program caters to individuals with relevant athletic experience who can contribute to Canada’s cultural or athletic activities.

As you gather the required documents, including proof of athletic achievements and plans for cultural contributions, you're one step closer to making Canada your sporting haven. To submit your application, you must create a Permanent residence online application portal account. Ensure to include the following forms in your application:

  • Generic Application Form for Canada (IMM 0008),
  • Schedule A – Background/Declaration (IMM 5669),
  • Additional Family Information (IMM 5406),
  • Supplementary Information - Your travels (IMM 5562),
  • Schedule 5: Economic Classes—Declaration of Intent to Reside in Quebec [IMM 0008 Schedule 5]
  • Schedule 6A Business Immigrants – Self-employed persons [IMM 0008 SCHEDULE 6A],
  • Separation Declaration for Minors Travelling to Canada [IMM 5604],
  • Statutory Declaration of Common-law Union [IMM 5409],
  • Use of a Representative [IMM 5476] (if you applied with a representative).

You will have to pay the application fee of 2,140 CAD.

Find out what to expect after submitting your application.

What Are The Benefits of Being an Athlete in Canada

Canada not only opens its doors to athletes but also offers a myriad of benefits that enrich both sporting and personal experiences. Here are some of the major benefits of being an athlete in Canada.

Canada’s High-Quality Sports Facilities

Immerse yourself in top-notch sports facilities across the country. Canada's commitment to athletic excellence is evident in its state-of-the-art training centers, ensuring you have the tools and environment needed to hone your skills to perfection. Prominent sports centers in Canada include the:

  • Rogers Centre,
  • Spruce Meadows,
  • WinSport, and
  • H2O Adventure + Fitness Centre.

Supportive Sports Culture in Canada

Experience the warmth of Canada's supportive sports culture. From passionate fans to a welcoming community, athletes in Canada find themselves embraced by a nation that recognizes and celebrates their dedication to their craft.

Canada’s National and Provincial Funding Programs

Avail yourself of various funding programs designed to support athletes. Whether at the national or provincial level, financial assistance is available to cover training costs, travel expenses, and other essentials, allowing you to focus wholeheartedly on your athletic journey. Some of these funding programs are offered by the following organizations include:

  • National Sport Organization,
  • National Multisport Services Organization (MSO),
  • Canadian Sport Centre,
  • Community Sport for All Initiative,
  • Innovation Initiative, and
  • Sport for Social Development in Indigenous Communities (SSDIC).

Canada’s Inclusive Sports Policies

Canada takes pride in its inclusive sports policies. Athletes from diverse backgrounds and abilities are welcomed, creating an environment where everyone has the opportunity to excel and contribute to the rich tapestry of Canadian sports.

Find out more about sports in Canada.

Opportunities for Sponsorship and Endorsements

Successful athletes often attract sponsorships and endorsements. In Canada, a market for these opportunities exists, providing financial stability and the chance to build a personal brand while pursuing your athletic dreams. This is a major advantage for athletic immigrants coming from developing countries with less financial backing opportunities for athletes.

FAQs

Are There Specific Resources or Organizations That Can Assist Athletes in Their Immigration Journey to Canada?

Absolutely! Organizations like the Canadian Olympic Committee, AthletesCAN, and Provincial Sport Organizations (PSOs) offer valuable guidance and support throughout the immigration process.

Can Athletes Receive Financial Support or Scholarships For Their Immigration Process?

While direct financial support for immigration might be limited, athletes can explore scholarships, grants, or sponsorship opportunities provided by sports organizations, governments, or private entities to cover associated costs.

How Long Does The Immigration Process Take For Athletes?

Processing times can vary, but starting the immigration process well in advance and seeking professional guidance ensures a smoother and more efficient journey to Canada.

Embark on your athletic adventure in Canada - where the pursuit of sporting excellence aligns seamlessly with the warmth of a nation that celebrates diversity, talent, and the unyielding spirit of athletes. Welcome to your new home on the global stage!
