How to Improve Your Chances of Securing Canadian Permanent Residency as an International Student in Canada

by 
Themba Phongolo

Author:

Themba Phongolo

Bachelor's Degree in English and sociology

Themba has been writing for CanadianVisa.org since January 2023. Writing about immigration excites him due to the promise it presents, that people can immigrate and earn a better life for themselves.

. Updated: October 3rd, 2024
5 min read

As an international student in Canada, you're in a unique position to pursue permanent residency. The Canadian government values the contributions of foreign graduates to its economy and society. Your Canadian education and work experience can give you an edge in the immigration process. However, understanding the path to permanent residency as an international student in Canada can be complex and competitive.

If you want to learn how to enhance your chances of gaining permanent residency in Canada as an international student, our guide below offers tips to make you stand out and increase your odds of making Canada your permanent home.

Get Your Studies in Line With In-Demand Occupations

Choosing the right field of study can significantly impact your chances of securing Canadian permanent residency as an international student in Canada. By aligning your education with in-demand occupations, you can position yourself for success in the Canadian job market and immigration process.

Researching Labor Market Needs

Understanding Canada's labor market dynamics is crucial for making informed decisions about your career path. Stay updated on employment trends and skills shortages across different sectors. Websites like Canada's Job Bank and provincial labor market information portals can provide valuable insights into current and future job prospects.

STEM Fields

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) occupations are highly sought after in Canada. The country's Express Entry system now prioritizes candidates with STEM expertise, including data scientists, software developers, and engineers. By pursuing a STEM degree, you can increase your chances of receiving an invitation to apply for Canadian permanent residency through category-based Express Entry draws.

Healthcare Professions

Canada's healthcare sector is experiencing significant growth, with a demand for skilled professionals. As an international student in Canada, considering a healthcare career can open up many opportunities for Canadian permanent residency. In-demand occupations include:

  • Registered nurses,
  • Physicians,
  • Medical laboratory technologists, and
  • Allied health professionals.

Many provinces also have specialized immigration pathways for healthcare workers, making transitioning from study to work to Canadian permanent residency easier.

Optimize Your Post-Graduation Work Permit

Optimizing your Post-graduate Work Permit (PGWP) is crucial for maximizing your work experience in Canada and enhancing your permanent residency application. A PGWP is a work permit that allows international students who have graduated from a designated learning institution (DLI) in Canada to work there for a specified period. It's designed to enable graduates to gain Canadian work experience and improve their skills before applying for permanent residency. Here are some of the critical things to do to optimize your PGWP.

Eligibility Criteria

As an international student in Canada, you can apply for a PGWP if you've completed a study program at a DLI that's at least eight months long. To be eligible, you must have maintained full-time status during each academic term, with some exceptions for your final semester or approved leaves. You have 180 days after graduating to apply for your PGWP, and your study permit must have been valid at some point during this period.

Maximizing Work Experience

Your PGWP allows you to work for any Canadian employer, giving you valuable Canadian work experience. To maximize this opportunity, aim to gain at least one year of skilled work experience in occupations classified under the National Occupational Classification (NOC) TEER categories 0, 1, 2, or 3. This experience is crucial for improving your chances of permanent residency through programs like the Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

Transitioning to Express Entry

You can create an Express Entry profile after gaining enough work experience. This system manages applications for several economic immigration programs, including the CEC. Take an approved language test to strengthen your profile and aim for high scores. If you receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA), you can submit your permanent residence application, typically processed within six months.

Build a Competitive Express Entry Profile

As an international student in Canada, building a competitive Express Entry profile is crucial to increase your chances of securing permanent residency. The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) evaluates and ranks candidates, assigning a score out of 1200 points. To boost your profile, focus on the following critical areas.

CRS Score Optimization

To optimize your CRS score, aim to excel in multiple factors. Language proficiency, work experience, education, and Canadian connections all play vital roles. Strive to gain skilled work experience, especially in Canada, as it can significantly impact your score. Remember, meeting minimum requirements isn't enough; you must stand out among other candidates.

Check your CRS score using our CRS calculator.

Education Credentials Assessment

Obtaining an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) from a recognized organization like World Education Services (WES) is essential if you've completed education outside Canada. This assessment compares your foreign credentials to Canadian standards. An ECA is mandatory for Express Entry under the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP). Even if you're eligible under other programs, getting an ECA can help you claim additional CRS points for your education.

Learn more about Canadas ECA.

Improving Language Scores

Language proficiency is a critical factor in your CRS score. Take approved language tests like the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) and the Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program (CELPIP) for English or Test d'Evaluation de Franais (TEF) for French. Aim for a Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) 9 or higher in each area to maximize your points. Consider improving your English and French skills, as bilingualism can earn extra points. Remember, you can retake these tests to improve your scores and update your profile.

Explore Alternative Pathways to PR

You have several alternative pathways to permanent residency beyond the Express Entry system as an international student in Canada. These options can increase your chances of staying in Canada in the long term.

Start-Up Visa Program

The Start-Up Visa Program offers a unique opportunity for entrepreneurial international students. If you have an innovative business idea, you can apply for permanent residency by starting a qualifying business in Canada. To be eligible, you must secure support from a designated organization, such as a venture capital fund, angel investor group, or business incubator. This program allows you to contribute to the Canadian economy while pursuing your entrepreneurial dreams.

Atlantic Immigration Program

The Atlantic Immigration Program is designed to attract skilled workers and international graduates to Atlantic Canada. You may qualify for this program as an international student who has graduated from a recognized post-secondary institution in Atlantic Canada. It offers a streamlined path to permanent residency if you have a job offer from a designated employer in one of the Atlantic provinces. This program helps address labor shortages in the region while allowing you to settle in Atlantic Canada.

Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot

If you're open to living and working in smaller communities, the Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot could be an excellent option. This program aims to spread the benefits of economic immigration to smaller communities by creating a path to Canadian permanent residency for skilled foreign workers.

As an international student, you may be eligible if you have graduated from a publicly funded post-secondary institution in the participating community. This pilot program offers a chance to experience life in Canada's rural and northern regions while securing your permanent residency.

Find out everything you need to know about the Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot (RNIP).

FAQs

What is the Quickest Method for International Students to Obtain Permanent Residency in Canada?

The fastest way for international students to obtain permanent residency in Canada is through the Express Entry system, specifically the Federal Skilled Worker Program or the Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP). This method bypasses the requirement of prolonged work and residence in Canada.

What Will Change for International Students in Canada Regarding The Post-Graduation Work Permit in 2024?

According to the Canadian government, from November 1, 2024, only international students graduating from public college programs linked to high labor market demand areas will be eligible for a three-year PGWP. Degree graduates from universities will continue to qualify for the three-year permit.

What Are The Primary Ways an International Student Can Transition to Permanent Residency in Canada?

International students can transition to Canadian permanent residency through several routes: self-petitioning as a person of extraordinary ability, obtaining employer sponsorship, adjusting status to a dual-intent visa, becoming an investor, or marrying a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident.
