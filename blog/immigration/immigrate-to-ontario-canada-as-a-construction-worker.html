Immigrate to Ontario, Canada as a Construction Worker

by 
Kelia Losa

Author:

Kelia Losa

Masters Graduate in Journalism and Media Studies

Kelia Losa Reinoso is a qualified content writer with a Master of Arts degree in Journalism and Media Studies

. Updated: February 6th, 2023

If you are a qualified construction worker interested in immigrating to Ontario, Canada, you have come to the right place. Ontario is home to a large and vibrant construction industry, with many opportunities for skilled workers. With a number of international airports and a thriving economy, Ontario is the perfect place to call home.

The Canadian construction industry is highly competitive and requires a certain level of skill and experience. However, if you have the right qualifications and experience, you have the potential to find a well-paying job and settle down in Ontario.

The Canadian government has put in place a number of initiatives to make the process of immigrating to Ontario easier. Through programs like Express Entry, skilled workers can quickly and easily apply for permanent residency and begin the process of settling down in Canada.

Once you have obtained permanent residency, you will be able to access job opportunities within the construction industry. You will be able to use your experience and skills to gain meaningful employment, and you can start to build a career within Canada.

With its economy growing, and its construction industry booming, Ontario is the perfect place to immigrate to as a construction worker. With its welcoming communities, vibrant culture, and abundant job opportunities, Ontario is the perfect place to build a new life.

Top 3 Ways to Moving to Canada

Moving to Canada as a construction worker is no easy feat, but with the right planning and knowledge, it can be done. Here are three of the most viable pathways to make the transition to life in Canada as a construction worker:

Express Entry

Express Entry  is a point-based system created by Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) in 2015. It is designed to make the immigration process simpler, faster, and more efficient. To be eligible for Express Entry, you need to meet certain criteria, such as language proficiency, work experience, and education level. You must also have the necessary funds to settle in Canada.

Once you meet the requirements, you will submit an Expression of Interest (EOI) to the IRCC. Your application will be ranked against other applicants, and the highest-scoring candidates will be invited to apply for permanent residency.

Ontario Provincial Nomination Program

The Ontario Provincial Nominee Program (OINP) is a Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) offered by the Canadian province of Ontario. This program allows Ontario to nominate individuals for permanent residency who have the skills and experience to contribute to the economy of the province.

In order to be eligible for this program, you must have a job offer in the province or have applied for a job in the province. You must also meet the criteria of one of the PNP streams such as the Express Entry or Employer Job Offer streams.

In-Demand Skills Stream Requirements

  • You must attain 9 months of full time paid work experience in Ontario as a construction worker.
  • You must earn a minimum score of 4 on the Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) test.
  • You must have the equivalent of a Canadian secondary school diploma. You will need to get an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) to show that your qualification meets the Canadian standards.
  • You must be able to show you have enough money to settle yourself and support your family members in Canada. This can be shown through the annual earnings of your job offer.
  • You must be able to show that you intend to settle and live in Ontario after obtaining permanent residency there. This can be done a number of ways such as having an ongoing job, leasing or buying property, studying, volunteering or having personal and professional relationships with other permanent residents.
  • Legal status within Canada is only required if you are already working in Canada. This will mean you must have a valid work permit at the time of your application. If you are applying from outside of Canada, make sure you apply for your work permit at the same time as you apply for this stream. After nine months you will submit your proof of experience and your application for permanent residency will be processed.

Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP)

The Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP) is a program that allows employers in Canada to hire foreign workers. The program is designed to fill gaps in the labour market when qualified Canadian workers are not available.

In order to be eligible for the TFWP, you must have a valid job offer from an eligible Canadian employer. You must also meet the necessary requirements of the program such as having the necessary work experience, education, and language proficiency. Once your application is accepted, you can begin working in Canada on a temporary basis.

These are three of the ways to move to Canada as a construction worker. With the right preparation, you can make a successful transition to life in Canada as a construction worker.

Canadas new Policy

The Canadian government recently announced a new policy designed to improve working conditions for construction workers in the country. The program, known as the Temporary Foreign Worker Program, is a pathway for foreign workers to obtain a temporary work visa for up to two years. The program will also offer job training and language classes to help workers gain the skills necessary for the job.

Additionally, employers in the construction industry will be required to adhere to higher standards of workplace safety and health, which will ensure better working conditions for their employees. Finally, the program will also provide a fund to cover medical and dental expenses for workers who become injured on the job. This new program is an effort to ensure that construction workers in Canada have access to better working conditions, job security, and the potential for higher wages.

Canadas Construction Demand

Construction workers play an essential role in Canadas economy. From building new infrastructure and essential services, to renovating and maintaining existing structures, construction workers are essential to the countrys ability to keep running smoothly and safely.

The construction industry is a major employer in Canada, with over 2 million people employed in the sector. Its estimated that the industry contributes more than $50 billion to Canadas gross domestic product annually.

Construction workers are responsible for creating and maintaining roads, bridges, buildings, and other structures in Canada. They install, maintain, and repair a variety of structures and materials, from high-rises to tunnels and pipes. This requires a wide range of skills, including carpentry, electrical work, welding, and operating heavy machinery.

Given the importance of this sector to Canadas economy, there is a need for more construction workers in the country. This is especially true as the population grows, and more infrastructure and services are needed. As a result, there are many job opportunities in the industry, and the demand for skilled workers is high.

In addition to the need for skilled workers, there is also a need for a safe and healthy working environment for construction workers. This is important because workers are often exposed to hazardous conditions and materials, and must take necessary precautions to protect themselves.

All in all, construction workers are essential for keeping the country running smoothly and safely, and for meeting the growing demand for infrastructure and services. As such, there is a need for more construction workers in Canada.

Single-detached urban starts recorded a 11% decrease to 44,858 units, while multi-unit urban starts dropped 4% to 182,850 units, and rural starts were estimated to be at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 20,917 units. However, CMHC's Chief Economist Bob Dugan stated that the rate of new construction was still at an elevated pace in 2022, ending the year at 240,590 units, similar to levels observed in 2021. Despite additional housing supply, demand for housing in the country will continue to grow.

Living and Working in Ontario

Living and working in Ontario as a construction worker can be an exciting and rewarding experience. With its diverse range of projects, great pay, and the opportunity to work with some of the best professionals in the industry, Ontario has become a popular choice for those looking to pursue a career in construction.

The province of Ontario is home to a wide variety of construction projects, from major infrastructure projects to smaller renovations and repairs. No matter what type of construction youre looking for, theres bound to be a suitable project in Ontario. In addition, the diverse range of projects in the province means that there are always opportunities to learn new skills and be exposed to new technologies.

The pay in Ontario is also very competitive, with many construction workers earning an average hourly wage of over $30. In addition, there are many additional benefits and bonuses available to workers, such as overtime and vacation pay.

Another great benefit of working as a construction worker in Ontario is the opportunity to work with some of the best professionals in the industry. Many construction firms in the province are world-renowned and employ highly experienced professionals who can offer invaluable advice and guidance. Working with these professionals can help you learn valuable skills and gain valuable experience.

Overall, living and working in Ontario as a construction worker can be a great experience. With its diverse range of projects, great pay, and the opportunity to work with some of the best professionals in the industry, Ontario is a great choice for those looking to pursue a career in the construction industry.

FAQs

What type of construction work is available in Canada?

Canada is home to a wide variety of construction opportunities. Through both private and public sector employers, construction jobs are available in areas such as engineering and architecture, civil and electrical construction, roofing and siding, and residential and commercial construction.

What qualifications do I need to work in Canada as a construction worker?

Depending on the type of construction job you are looking to get, you may need certain qualifications. For instance, qualifications for an engineering position may include a 4-year degree in engineering or a related discipline. However, qualifications for a roofing or siding position may only require a high school diploma and specialized training.

How can I find construction work in Canada?

There are a number of ways to find construction jobs in Canada. You can search online job boards, contact construction companies directly, or search classifieds in your local newspaper. You can also contact local trade unions and labour organizations to inquire about job opportunities in your area.
