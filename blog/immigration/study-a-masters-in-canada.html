Study a Master's in Canada

by 
Shireen Fisher

Author:

Shireen Fisher

BTech Degree in Journalism, Diploma in Journalism

Shireen Fisher is a widely-published writer with nine years of experience. She started her media career in radio before heading back to university to pursue her National Diploma and BTech Degree in Journalism.

. Updated: February 27th, 2024
7 mins

Are you considering advancing your education by pursuing a Master's degree? Canada, renowned for its high-quality education system and diverse cultural landscape, is an attractive destination for ambitious individuals seeking academic enrichment and professional growth. Lets have a look at why you should complete your Master's Degree in Canada and explore the country's Post Graduate Work Permit (PGWP).

Choose a Master's Degree in Canada

Canada boasts an impressive array of universities and institutions offering various Master's programs across various disciplines. Whether you aspire to delve into the realms of business, engineering, healthcare, or the arts, Canadian universities provide ample opportunities to pursue your academic passions. With world-class faculties, state-of-the-art facilities, and a commitment to excellence, studying for a Master's degree in Canada promises a transformative educational experience.

Benefits of Completing a Masters Degree in Canada

Canadian universities are recognized globally for their academic excellence. Earning a Master's degree in Canada equips you with:

  • Advanced knowledge and skills: Master's programs in Canada delve deeper into specific areas, providing you with specialized expertise sought after by employers globally. Whether you're interested in cutting-edge research, innovative technologies, or specialized business practices, Canadian universities equip you with the cutting-edge knowledge and practical skills to excel in your field.
  • Enhanced career opportunities: A Master's degree significantly enhances your resume, making you a highly competitive candidate for diverse job roles. From leading corporations and research institutions to government agencies and international organizations, a Canadian Master's degree opens doors to lucrative career paths and positions you for rapid career advancements.
  • International recognition: This is your chance to gain global recognition and enhance your employability worldwide. Canadian degrees are highly respected and valued by employers across the globe. Earning your Master's in Canada equips you with a globally recognized credential, significantly increasing your competitiveness in the international job market and opening doors to exciting career opportunities beyond national borders.

Finding the Right Master's Program in Canada

Finding your perfect Master's program in Canada is an exciting first step toward achieving your academic and career goals. To ensure you find the ideal fit, here's a guide for navigating the selection process:

Align Your Interests and Aspirations

Self-reflection is key. Start by exploring your academic passions and career aspirations. What field ignites your curiosity? What career path do you envision for yourself? Identifying your interests and goals will guide you toward programs that resonate with your ambitions.

Program Duration and Eligibility

Once you've identified relevant fields, delve deeper into specific programs. Consider the program duration, ensuring it aligns with your timeline and PGWP eligibility (minimum eight months at a DLI). Pay close attention to entry requirements, including academic qualifications, standardized test scores, and relevant work experience.

University Reputation and Location

When looking for your ideal Canadian university, consider the university's academic reputation, research focus, and faculty expertise in your chosen field. Explore each university's campus facilities, student support services, and career development opportunities. Think about the location. Do you prefer a vibrant urban setting or a quieter academic environment?

Contact Admissions Offices

Connect directly with the admissions office of your chosen universities. They can provide detailed information about specific programs, application deadlines, and additional requirements. Don't hesitate to ask questions and clarify any doubts you may have.

The Most Popular Universities in Canada for Master's Students

While Canada boasts numerous outstanding universities, some consistently attract Master's students due to their strong reputation and diverse program offerings. Here are some of the most popular universities in Canada for Master's students, based on various university ranking lists.

University of Toronto

Consistently ranked among the top universities in Canada and the world, the University of Toronto (U of T) offers a wide range of highly regarded Master's programs across various disciplines, including medicine, law, business, engineering, and humanities.

University of British Columbia

Located in Vancouver, the University of British Columbia (UBC) is another top-ranked university in Canada. It is known for its strong research focus and excellent Master's programs in various fields, including science, engineering, management, and forestry.

McGill University

Situated in Montreal, McGill University is a prestigious university with a rich history and reputation for academic excellence. It offers a diverse range of Master's programs, including medicine, law, engineering, and business.

University of Alberta

The University of Alberta is set in Edmonton, Alberta. It is a leading research university with a strong focus on science, technology, engineering, and medicine. It offers a variety of Master's programs in these areas, as well as in social sciences, humanities, and law.

McMaster University

Based in Hamilton, Ontario, McMaster University is known for its innovative and interdisciplinary approach to education. It offers a range of well-regarded Master's programs in health sciences, engineering, business, and social sciences.

Note: These are just a few of the many excellent universities in Canada offering Master's programs. The "best" university for you will depend on your individual academic interests, program preferences, and other factors.

Do Master's Students Need to Apply For a Canadian Study Permit in Canada?

The answer is yes. You need a Canadian study permit to pursue a Master's degree in Canada. A study permit is a document issued by the Canadian government that allows non-Canadian citizens or permanent residents to study at a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) in Canada.

All international students planning to study in Canada must obtain a Canadian study permit before arriving in the country unless they qualify for an exemption based on the course length or other special circumstances.

Who Can Apply for a Canadian Study Permit?

If you're wondering whether you're eligible to apply for a Canada study permit, you should note that you must tick the boxes below.

Eligibility Requirement Description
Enrollment at a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) To be eligible for a study permit, you must first be accepted into a program offered by a DLI in Canada. Ensure that the institution you choose is recognized by Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).
Financial Proof You must demonstrate that you have sufficient funds to cover your tuition fees, living expenses, and return transportation for yourself and any accompanying family members. This financial evidence is crucial to supporting yourself financially while studying in Canada.
Compliance with the Law You must have a clean legal record and abide by Canadian laws. This includes providing a police certificate, if required, which verifies that you have no criminal history.
Good Health You must demonstrate that you are in good health. Depending on your home country and the duration of your stay in Canada, you may need to undergo a medical examination conducted by a designated physician.
Intention to Leave Canada You must convince the immigration officer reviewing your application that you intend to leave Canada upon the expiry of your study permit. Providing evidence of strong ties to your home country, such as family, employment prospects, or property ownership, can support your case.

Understanding the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) is an open work permit issued to international students who graduate from a designated learning institution (DLI) in Canada. It allows you to gain work experience in any field, anywhere in the country, after completing your studies.

This permit is like a key that opens the door to different job opportunities across Canada. It allows you to work in cities or even in quieter areas, depending on where you want to live and work.

With the PGWP, you can work in fields related to what you studied. It's a chance to learn new things, meet new people, and be a part of Canadian life. It's also a way to contribute to Canada's economy by working and paying taxes.

The PGWP is not just about getting a job, though. It's about growing personally and professionally. It helps you improve your skills, find out what you're good at, and start building your career in Canada.

In short, the Post-Graduation Work Permit is more than just a piece of paperit's an opportunity for you, as an international student, to work, learn, and thrive in Canada after completing your studies.

Canada's Recent Policy Change For Master's Students

In a recent policy shift, the Canadian government announced a significant change for international students pursuing Master's degrees. The IRCC recently announced that as of February 15, 2024, graduates of Master's programs lasting under two years are now eligible for a three-year PGWP, previously only available to graduates of programs longer than two years.

Why the Change Matters

Before the recent change, only graduates of Master's programs lasting at least two years were eligible for a three-year PGWP. Graduates of shorter programs received permits that matched the length of their study program, limiting their time to gain work experience. The extended work permit duration provides Master's students with more time to:

  • Secure full-time employment: This allows them to gain relevant Canadian experience and build their resumes, making them more competitive candidates in the job market.
  • Contribute to Canada's skilled workforce: Graduates can fill crucial gaps in specific industries and contribute their skills and knowledge to the Canadian economy.
  • Explore permanent residency options: With extended work experience, international students have a stronger chance of qualifying for programs like the Canadian Experience Class (CEC), facilitating their transition to permanent residents.

Eligibility Requirements for Obtaining the 3-year Work Permit

To qualify for a PGWP, international students need to meet the following criteria:

  • Successfully complete a program of study that lasts at least eight months at a DLI approved by the Canadian government.
  • Attend a program that results in a degree, diploma, or certificate.
  • Maintain full-time student status throughout each academic session of the program, as noted on the PGWP application, with some exceptions allowed.
  • Obtain an official transcript and a letter from the DLI confirming the completion of the program, both of which must be submitted with the PGWP application.
  • Graduate from one of the following institutions:
    • A college
    • Trade or technical school
    • University
    • CEGEP (in Quebec)
    • Private post-secondary institution (in Quebec) following public school regulations
    • Canadian private school authorized to grant degrees under provincial law (such as Associate, Bachelor's, Master's, or Doctorate degree) if enrolled in a degree program approved by the province.

Applying for the PGWP

The process of applying for a PGWP can be carried out from within Canada or another country, provided that you meet the eligibility criteria. It's important to note that you have a window of up to 180 days after graduation to initiate your PGWP application.

Step 1: Verification

During the application process, you are required to verify the following details:

  • Attendance and Completion: Confirm that you have attended and successfully completed your program of study.
  • Program Details: Provide information regarding the name and duration of your program.

You can verify these aspects by submitting any of the following documents:

  • Degree or Diploma
  • Transcript
  • Official Letter from Your School

Step 2: Apply Online

Create a profile. Then, apply online for your PGWP on the IRCC website.

Step 3: Pay Fees

Pay the appropriate fees once you apply for your PGWP. This will be $255 for the permit and $379 if you need to restore your student status.

Due to system limitations, you must pay the student status restoration fee online and attach a copy of the receipt to your PGWP application. There is no need to submit a separate application for student status restoration.

It's important to note that you cannot commence working until both your study permit and work permit have been approved. If you fail to apply within the 90-day window or if the 90 days have already elapsed, you must depart from Canada, in line with Canadian immigration regulations.

FAQs

Can I Get a PGWP if my Study Program Duration in Quebec is Under Eight Months?

If this is the case, you will not be eligible for a PGWP.

My Canadian Study Permit is About to Expire, and I Have Not Received My Results. What do I do if I Need to Apply for a PGWP?

Suppose your study permit is about to expire while you are waiting for exam results. In that case, you can do one of two things. You can either apply for a Visitor Record to extend your stay in Canada or leave Canada and submit your PGWP application from your home country.

If your Canadian study permit expires before you get the chance to apply for the PGWP, you will be given a grace period of up to 90 days after the expiry date to apply for a PGWP.
