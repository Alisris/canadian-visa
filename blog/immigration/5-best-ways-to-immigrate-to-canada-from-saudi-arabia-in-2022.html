5 Best Ways to Immigrate to Canada from Saudi Arabia in 2022

by Beatrice Visser. Updated: January 13th, 2022
5 min read

Canada is full of thousands of opportunities that are just waiting to be snatched. Opportunities such as: work opportunities, study opportunities and even living in Canada permanently. If you want to immigrate to Canada from Saudi Arabia in 2022, here are 5 ways to do exactly that.

5 Amazing ways to get to Canada from Saudi Arabia in 2022

1. Express Entry Programs

Express Entry is a system that allows foreign nationals to live and work in Canada as skilled workers. It was introduced to enable long term economic growth for the country and to fill the gaps in the labour market. There are three Express Entry programs:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP);
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP); and
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

In order to apply you will need to complete an online profile and submit supporting documents. When your Express Entry profile has been completed you will receive a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score out of 1,200 points. If you qualify you will receive an ITA for permanent residency in Canada. The score of every draw is different and only those who have the highest scores will qualify.

Once you receive your ITA you only have 60 days to fill in and complete the Express Entry application with all the documentation required to give you your best shot to live in Canada. The documents you will need are:

  1. Passport;
  2. National Occupation Classification (NOC);
  3. Education credential assessment report;
  4. Language assessment tests;
  5. Proof of funds;
  6. Police clearance certificate;
  7. Medical exam;
  8. Relevant and reliable references;
  9. Canadian job offer (if you have one);
  10. Provincial Nomination (if you have one).

Once all your documents are sorted, the last thing to do is to submit your application and pay the processing fee of $1,325

2. The Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

The PNPs enable provincial governments to nominate foreign nationals with a particular skill set needed within that province or territory. If you have a provincial nomination, the chances of you receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residency is that much greater.

Here are the different PNPs:

  • Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP)
  • British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BCPNP)
  • Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program (MPNP)
  • New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NBPNP)
  • Newfoundland and Labrador Provincial Nominee Program (NLPNP)
  • Northwest Territories Nominee Program (NTNP)
  • Nova Scotia Provincial Nominee Program (NSPNP)
  • Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)
  • Prince Edward Island Provincial Nominee Program (PEI PNP)
  • Quebec Skilled Workers Program (QSWP)
  • Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program (SINP)
  • Yukon Nominee Program (YNP)

Before you think about your move to Canada, it’s important to know that each province has different criteria that will need to be met for their PNP, and they will differ from province to province.

3. Family Class Sponsorship

If you have family that

live in Canada, they could make immigrating to Canada from Saudi Arabia that much easier. Your family member will be able to sponsor you if they are a permanent resident or citizen of Canada, and have adequate resources to provide financial support to the candidate.


Who is eligible for sponsorship?

  1. Spouse, Common law partner, and Conjugal partner.
  2. Dependent children
  3. Parents
  4. Brothers and sisters
  5. Grandparents
  6. Grandchildren
  7. Other relatives, e.g. orphaned niece or nephew

Candidates who get sponsored as children have to be under the age of 18, unmarried and shouldn’t be involved in a common-law relationship.

4. LMIA Work Visa

Most applicants first get Canadian job offers, apply for a work permit and thereafter immigrate to Canada from Saudi Arabia in 2022. The LMIA involves securing a job offer in Canada, having the Canadian employer apply for a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA), and then have the applicant apply for the work permit after the LMIA is approved. This process can lead to permanent residence.

Here are the top 10 jobs in Canada that require a LMIA:

  1. Truck Driver
  2. Farm Supervisor
  3. Caregiver
  4. Software Engineering
  5. Construction Managers
  6. Oil and Gas Drilling Professionals
  7. Registered Nurses
  8. Web Developers
  9. Electrician
  10. Structural Engineer/Civil Engineer

5. Study visa

Canada ranks fourth in the world for one of the best education systems in a country, so it’s not difficult to think why many international students flock here. Canada has a rich cultural diversity and is very welcoming to all it’s students with different backgrounds. Here are five easy steps to apply for a study visa in Canada:

Step 1: Apply to a Canadian university. Apply to a few so that if your first option is not successful, you’ll have other opportunities to fall back on.

Step 2: Apply for a study visa. You will need a study visa if you wish to study a course longer than six months. By applying for a study permit, you will be given a visa, which will either be a visitor visa or Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA).

Step 3: Create an online profile to start the application process. You can also apply by submitting a hard copy of your application and your supporting documents to your nearest Visa Application Centre. This might take a bit longer than the online application.

Step 4: Gather your supporting documents. The supporting documents you will need are:

  • Proof of identity
  • Proof of acceptance from the institution you have enrolled in
  • Proof of financial support

Proof of funds should be shown in the following ways:

  • Canadian bank account in your name
  • Proof of payment for housing and tuition fees
  • Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC)
  • Proof of student or education loan from your own bank
  • Bank overdraft that can be converted to Canadian dollars
  • Proof of funding paid in Canada, should you be the recipient of a scholarship

Step 5: Assess Your Language Requirements. You must be able to communicate effectively in French or English. You will have to show proof of this by taking one of these tests:

  • TCF Canada: Test de connaissance du français
  • TEF Canada: Test d’évaluation de français
  • IELTS: International English Language Testing System
  • CELPIP: Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program

FAQS

Can I extend my work visa from inside Canada?

Yes, you can. You should extend your work permit at least 30 days before your previous one is set to expire. As long as your passport doesn’t expire before the end date of your work visa, you should be able to extend it and live on in Canada.

How can I work in Canada after graduating?

An option to work in Canada after graduating would be to apply for the Post Graduate Work Program (PGWP).

Here are the requirements:

  • You should have proof that you completed your program successfully;
  • Your study permit should still be valid before you apple for a work permit;
  • You have applied for a work permit 90 days after you have graduated from your program;
  • Proof that you have studied continuously for 18 months while being in Canada.

This is one great way to stay in Canada if you're planning to move to Canada long term.


When do you not qualify as a sponsor?

You cannot qualify to be a sponsor if you:

  • Are unable to support yourself;
  • Are unable to provide for the basic needs of your dependents;
  • Are a permanent resident living outside of Canada;
  • Are currently in jail/penitentiary, reformation or prison;
  • Are bankrupt;
  • Were sponsored by someone and became a permanent resident less than 5 years ago;
  • Are a Canadian citizen who does not plan to live in Canada during the ‘sponsorship phase;
  • Are unable to pay the performance bond, immigration loans and family support payments.

What are the documents required for a Canada PR Visa

It’s crucial that the correct documents are submitted when applying for a PR visa otherwise it can affect the candidates eligibility before they even move to Canada, as well as the processing time for the application.

Documents that are required for the PR visa are:

  1. ECA Certificate: This certificate will serve as proof that foreign education that has been completed by the candidate is the equivalent of the Canadian standard;
  2. Language Test Score: IELTS Score;
  3. Medical Report and Police Clearance Certificate;
  4. Proof of Settlement funds;
  5. Proof of work experience;
  6. Copy of travel documents (passports etc.);
  7. Provincial Nomination Certificate (optional);
  8. Valid job offer (optional);
  9. Marriage Certificate (Optional);
  10. Divorce Certificate (if separated)

Now that you’ve seen the different ways that you can immigrate to Canada from Saudi Arabia in 2022, it’s important to remember that this process can be tricky to master on your own. Let us assist you on your journey to greatness.
